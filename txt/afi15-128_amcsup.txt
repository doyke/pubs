AFI 15-128, 7 February 2011 is supplemented as follows. This publication applies to the Air
National Guard (ANG). This publication does not apply to the Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC). This publication applies to AMC and AMC-gained weather organizations. This
supplement establishes roles and responsibilities for Air Mobility Command (AMC) weather
operations and weather support at AMC installations. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form
847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the
appropriate functional chain of command to HQ AMC/A3AW, 402 Scott Drive, Unit 3A1, Scott
AFB, IL 62226-5302 or email requests to AMC.A3AW@us.af.mil. Ensure that all records
created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with
Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance
with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at http://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

responsibilities.

2.1.2.6. (Added) Weather units will request on-site or virtual Staff Assistance Visits (SAV)
through Wing gatekeepers. Wing Gatekeepers will forward Wing/TACC commander approved
SAV requests to the AMC Gatekeeper (AMC IG/IPS).

2.1.2.7.1. (Added) AMC/IG will conduct weather functional evaluations IAW AFI 90-201, and
AFI 90-201 AMCSUP, Inspector General Activities.

2.1.2.7.2. (Added) AMC/A3AW will augment AMC/IG Combined Unit Inspections, to include
Tailored Compliance Inspections of the 618 AOC (TACC) and provide subject matter expertise
in establishing weather inspection requirements.

2.1.2.11. (Added) Assist the AMC Operations Support Squadron (OSS) Functional Area
Manager (FAM) in the development of Designed Operational Capabilities (DOC) statements for
AMC OSSs.
2.1.2.12. (Added) Develop weather Mission Essential Task Lists (METLs) for AMC A-Staff,
Air Force Forces staff, 618 AOC (TACC)/XOW, and OSSs.

2.1.2.13. (Added) Assist AMC Contingency Response FAM in the development of weather
METLs for contingency response forces.

2.2.2.14. (Added) Review Status of Resources and Training Systems (SORTS), Air and Space
Expeditionary Force (AEF) Reporting Tool (ART), and Defense Readiness Reporting System
(DRRS) reports for trends and issues which require resolution.

5.3.8. (Added) Conduct self-assessments IAW AFI 90-201 and AFI 90-201 AMCSUP. 618
AOC Director of Weather will create a new self-inspection in MICT within 45 days of assuming
the position.

5.3.9. (Added) Submit a Personnel Activity Report (PAR) IAW the template from the

5.6. (Added) AMC Applicability. The requirements of this paragraph apply to weather
personnel assigned to AMC CRGs, to include Mobility Support Advisory Squadrons (MSAS),
and to ANG CRG and Airlift Control Flights (ALCF) when gained by AMC. Perform actions
described in paragraphs 5.6.1. – 5.6.5. as required.

5.6.6. (Added) Within statutory and fiscal limits, MSAS weather personnel will assist in
training partner-nations in weather functions in support of air mobility operations.

5.6.7. (Added) Conduct self-assessments two times per year IAW AFI 90-201 and AFI 90-201
AMCSUP. Use the AMC CRG checklist within the Management Internal Control Toolset
(MICT) to conduct self-assessments.

5.6.8. (Added) Initial certification will include CAT 1 Contingency Response training IAW
AMCI 10-202, Volume 4, Expeditionary Air Mobility Support Operation. Consider utilizing the

host wing weather flight equipment and trainers as an additional resource when conducting

5.6.9. (Added) Develop a weather support planning template for deployed operations. The
intent and purpose is to quickly develop, document, and implement weather support for each
specific deployment, regardless of whether the deployment team includes weather personnel. To
the extent practical, define and document the items in AFMAN15-129V2 paragraph 1.3. Ensure
weather watch/warning issue and dissemination responsibilities are appropriately documented
for each deployment. The 621st Contingency Response Wing Weather Functional Manager will
ensure consistency of deployed weather support planning across the wing.

5.7.4. (Added) Conduct and document training and certification in the AFTR IAW the 1W0XX
and 15WX CFETP.

6.2.15. (Added) Newly arrived personnel should complete qualification training in no less than
two weeks and no more than four weeks after completing initial in-processing actions. Notify
AMC/A3AW of situations requiring longer than four weeks certification.
6.2.16. (Added) Conduct and document training and certification in the AFTR IAW the
1W0XX and 15WX CFETP.

6.3.1.1.1. (Added) AMC weather flights will publish a weather support instruction to
consolidate weather support documentation as specified in AFMAN 15-129 Volume 2, Air and
Space Weather Operations – Exploitation. AMC/A3AW maintains an installation weather
instruction template instruction at https://eim.amc.af.mil/org/a3/A3A/A3AW/default.aspx that
contains required information not readily documented elsewhere.

6.3.1.1.2. (Added) Weather flight leadership will assist the host/installation logistics plans
office in developing Chapter 35 of the installation In-Garrison Expeditionary Site Plan (IGESP)
by identifying available resources and weather services provided.

6.3.1.3. (Added) Weather flight leadership will assist the Installation Commander and
Emergency Management personnel in educating installation agencies on the types of severe
weather threats in the local area and on the purpose, applicability, and operating procedures of
the warning and watch system IAW AFI 10-2501.

6.3.2. (Added) AMC units will notify HQ AMC/A3AW, via the organizational email account
amc.a3aw@us.af.mil, when any of the following occur:

6.3.2.1. (Added) Any weather system/equipment outage lasts or is projected to last 72 hours or
more.

6.3.2.2. (Added) Any weather system/equipment which does not allow the unit to meet its
operational mission requirements. Include current and historical outage information. Be
cognizant of classification issues; use secure communications (e.g., SIPRNet, Secure Terminal
Equipment) when discussing mission-specific operational impacts as a result of the outage.

6.3.5. (Added) AMC weather flights complete self-inspections twice per year IAW AFI 90-
201AMCSUP and parent wing timelines. Additionally, senior WF leadership will create a new
self-inspection in MICT within 45 days of assuming the position. A change in senior WF
leadership is defined as a change in the person in charge of day-to-day operations that is
projected to last longer than 90 days.

6.3.17. (Added) Submit a Personnel Activity Report (PAR) IAW the template from the
AMC/A3AW SharePoint website: https://eim.amc.af.mil/org/a3/A3A/A3AW/Personnel%20
Activity%20Reports%20PAR/Forms/AllItems.aspx. Units without access to SharePoint will
email their report to the AMC/A3AW organizational email box. The PAR will be submitted
NLT the 5th of every month and will be marked “For Official Use Only (FOUO)” when it is
filled in.
