This instruction implements AFPD 32-30, Explosive Ordnance Disposal. It assigns responsibilities and
prescribes procedures for EOD to assist civil authorities with incidents involving commercial explosives
or improvised explosive devices (IEDs). It implements applicable portions of AFJI 32-3002, Interservice
Responsibilities for Explosive Ordnance Disposal, AFI 31-202, Military Working Dog Program, AFI
32-3001, Air Force EOD Program, AFMAN 91-201, Explosive Safety Standards, and AMCI 32-3001,
Air Mobility Command EOD Program. It applies to all personnel on McGuire Air Force Base and tenant
unit personnel as specified. This instruction does not apply to AFRC and ANG personnel. Ensure that all
records in this supplement are maintained and disposed of in accordance with AFMAN 37-123, Manage-
ment of Records, and the Air Force Records Disposition (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
Changes have been made to bring this supplement into compliance with Air Force Standards. The follow-
ing updates were made: paragraphs 1.1., 1.1.1., 2.5., 3.1., and 5.1. changed the terminology of installation
commander and support group commander to the specific 305 AMW/CC and 305 MSG/CC; paragraph
1.3., changed to “respond off-base and to off-road situations”; paragraph 1.7. changed legal office repre-
sentative to “SJA or JAG officer”; paragraph 2.3. changed from “individual receiving the call” to “The
EOD technician receiving the call…”; and paragraph 2.5.1. changes SJA to JAG. A bar (|) indicates
changes from the previous edition.


1. General Information and Responsibilities.
   1.1. The installation Commander (305 AMW/CC) delegates authority to the Mission Support Group
   Commander (305 MSG/CC) or designated representative to dispatch EOD teams in response to emer-
   gency incidents off-base.
       1.1.1. The McGuire EOD Flight may provide assistance to civil authorities in the interest of pub-
       lic safety, or when requested by the Army EOD Area Control Center when authorized by 305

       MSG/CC or designated representative. McGuire EOD assistance to civil authorities is normally
       limited to local communities and must not interfere with AMC mission.
    1.2. In accordance with AFI 32-3001, Explosive Ordnance Disposal Program, the first service to dis-
    cover a transportation accident or incident involving Federally owned or controlled explosive ord-
    nance must:
       1.2.1. Using the fastest mode of transportation available, ensure an EOD team arrives at the scene
       as soon as possible (within 4 hours).
       1.2.2. Try to prevent or limit loss of life, injury, property damage, and minimize public inconve-
       nience.
    1.3. Vehicle Maintenance will ensure EOD response vehicles are highly reliable and afforded priority
    maintenance in order to respond off-base and to off-road situations, as required.
    1.4. Civil authorities are primarily responsible for the safe handling and disposal of improvised explo-
    sive devices (IED), nonmilitary commercial explosives, or similarly dangerous articles outside DoD
    installations.
    1.5. Installation commanders may, on request from civil authorities, provide EOD assistance in accor-
    dance with AFJI 32-3002, Interservice Responsibilites for Explosive Ordnance Disposal. In accor-
    dance with AFI 32-3001, Explosive Ordnance Disposal Program, para 2.1.2, the senior EOD
    representative and the on-scene commander make a joint assessment and assign an incident risk cate-
    gory to the situation.
    1.6. When the decision is made to provide assistance to civil authorities, the 305 MSG/CC or a desig-
    nee qualified to make the required on-scene command and risk decisions, should accompany the EOD
    team off-base. The 305 MSG/CC may designate the EOD Flight Chief or NCOIC to fulfill this role.
    1.7. The Staff Judge Advocate (SJA) or other Judge Advocate General (JAG) Officer, will provide
    legal advice in order to avoid possible violation of the Posse Comitatus Act and to protect U.S. Air
    Force personnel and interests. The on-call JAG may provide legal advice on the anticipate matters.

2. Notification Procedures.
    2.1. Direct requests from civil authorities will be referred to the McGuire EOD Flight during duty
    hours and the McGuire Command Post after duty hours.
    2.2. Requests from the U.S. Army’s 63rd EOD Control Detachment are received by the McGuire
    EOD Flight during duty hours and the McGuire Command Post after duty hours.
    2.3. The EOD technician receiving the call during duty hours will record all pertinent data regarding
    the request. After duty hours, the Command Post will record the necessary data and contact the pri-
    mary EOD person on standby.
    2.4. Upon receipt of the call, the EOD technician will notify the EOD Flight Chief/Superintendent or
    305 CES/CC and in turn the 305 MSG/CC.
    2.5. The senior EOD person will contact the SJA or other JAG officer who will then contact the
    305 MSG/CC to provide information on the legal aspects of EOD’s support to civil authorities.

       2.5.1. Any further requests for legal consultation shall be directed to the SJA or other JAG officer
       via the base legal office during normal duty hours. The on-call JAG officer can be reached by
       contacting the McGuire Command Post after duty hours.
   2.6. The senior EOD person will notify the McGuire Public Affairs Office to provide information
   regarding the nature of the response.
   2.7. A backup EOD team will be placed on standby, for base support, until the primary team returns.
   2.8. The EOD team may request a Military Working Dog Team to accompany them off-base. This
   request will be channeled through the Law Enforcement Desk.

3. Response Procedures.
   3.1. With the approval of the 305 MSG/CC, the McGuire EOD team will respond to incident site.
   3.2. The senior EOD technician will:
       3.2.1. Formulate a render safe procedure or a plan of action to abate the hazard, and brief the
       on-scene commander, the command’s representative, or senior civilian authority on the proposed
       actions and possible damages that may occur.
       3.2.2. Limit exposure of EOD personnel to risk of injury or loss of life, unless the threat to the
       civilian populace is so severe that the risk is clearly justified.
   3.3. The EOD team will not make any statements to the news media. All news media requests for
   information will be directed to Public Affairs.

4. Limitations.
   4.1. Non-DoD toxic or hazardous materials are not taken to military installations for storage or dis-
   posal unless:
       4.1.1. The situation is deemed an emergency or lifesaving operation by the senior EOD represen-
       tative and on-scene commander.
       4.1.2. Formal agreements exists with other Federal agencies.

5. After Actions.
   5.1. Upon returning to McGuire AFB, the EOD team chief will notify the Command Post, and debrief
   305 MSG/CC or designated representative on assistance provided.
   5.2. EOD personnel subpoenaed to provide knowledgeable testimony will seek legal advice from the
   base legal office prior to appearing in court.
   5.3. All necessary reports are prepared and sent to HQ AMC.
