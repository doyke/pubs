This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Air and Space
Maintenance and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101, Aircraft
Equipment Maintenance Management. It is consistent with AFOSH STD 91-100, Aircraft Flight
Line – Ground Operations and Activities and AFOSH STD 91-501 Air Force Consolidated
Occupational Safety Standard. It provides safety guidelines and designates responsibility for,
training, qualification, and documentation for personnel who operate hangar doors and hangar
door awareness for personnel working in the hangar door facilities # 630, #647, #750 and #751.
Coordination/approval at the 920 RQW will be accomplished by an electronically signed AF
Form 673, Air Force Publication/Form Action Request (AF Form 673). Do not use e-mails for
final coordination/approval, but all e-mails will be maintained as part of the record set. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Form 847s from the field through Major Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms
managers. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management
System      (AFRIMS)         Records     Disposition     Schedule      (RDS)       located      at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm

1. General: The purpose of this instruction is to ensure the safety of all personnel during
hangar door operations. It addresses hangar door hazards, responsibilities and training
requirements.
 2                                                        920RQWI91-308 22 OCTOBER 2012


2. Hangar Door Hazards: Hangar door operation and movement creates a series of pinch
points and crush points. If proper precautions are not adhered to, damage to equipment and
injury or death to personnel can occur.
3. Responsibilities:
     3.1. Supervisors:
        3.1.1. Will ensure all personnel who work in hangar facilities receive initial and refresher
        Hangar Door Awareness Training and are documented (MIS, AFTRS, AF Form 55,
        etc…) See paragraph 4 for details.
        3.1.2. Will ensure only properly trained and qualified personnel operate hangar doors.
     3.2. Hangar Door Operators:
     3.2.1. Will report malfunctions and discrepancies immediately to a work center supervisor
     and building custodian, appropriately tag door with Hazard/Caution tag.
     3.2.2. Will ensure doors are operated IAW local checklists posted on the hangar doors being
     operated.
4. Hangar Door Awareness Training (HDAT):
4.1. All personnel who primary duty section is within hangar facilities will receive initial and
refresher HDAT; recurring requirement with a 3 year frequency.
4.2. HDAT is available on the S: Drive under Quality Training/Hangar Door Training folder and
available on CD for check out at 920MXG/MXQ Hangar 750 Room N202/ Tel. 494-7463.
5. Hangar Door Awareness Training (HDAT) Documentation:
     5.1. 920th Maintenance Group personnel will submit an AF Form 2426 with course code #
     29110 Hangar Door Awareness to MOF/MXOT for update in MIS after training is
     completed.
     5.2. Squadron Commanders or company representatives that have personnel working within
     920MXG hangars will forward an AF Form 2426 or letter of completion to 920MXG/MXQ
     for record purposes (see attachment 1).
6. Hangar Door Operator Training (HDOT):
     6.1. HDOT for 920MXG personnel is available by work center supervisor request.
     6.2. HDOT for non 920MXG personnel: Commanders or company representatives will
     forward a request to 920MXG/MXQ (see attachment 2).
     6.3. HDOT Certifiers will be appointed by 920MXG/CC.
     6.4. HDOT Trainer certification will be provided by 920MXG/MXQ.
6.5. Trainers will provide HDOT with approved lesson plan, operating checklist and
demonstrate all safety items.
7. Hangar Door Operator Training (HDOT) Documentation:
7.1. HDOT Trainers will submit AF Form 2426 with Hangar Door Operator Electric # 29111 to
920 MOF/MXOT for update and courtesy copy to 920 MXG/MXQ for record purposes.

8. Quality Assurance and Group Training Offices:
   8.1. MOF/MXOT & MXG/MXQ will jointly develop HDAT and HDOT programs.
   8.2. MXG/MXQ will be the OPR for outlining responsibilities and procedures for safe
   operation of hangar doors to include operating checklist.
   8.3. MXG/MXQ will generate a letter for MXG/CC approval for operators outside of MXG.
