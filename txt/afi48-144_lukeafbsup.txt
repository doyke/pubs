Air Force Instruction (AFI) 48-144, Drinking Water Surveillance Program, is supplemented as
follows. This supplement applies to all personnel, military and civilian, responsible for
implementation of the Drinking Water Surveillance Program for Luke AFB. This publication
applies to the U. S. Air Force Reserve units and members attached or assigned to Luke AFB.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication has been revised and should be completely reviewed. Updates numbering
to reflect changes in the recently published AFI 48-144, Drinking Water Surveillance Program.
1.9.3.7.1. (Added) Civil Engineer (CE) Utilities or CE Construction Project Manager will
notify Bioenvironmental Engineering (BE), Public Health (PH), and affected Facility Managers
two weeks in advance of a scheduled water outage, if possible.
 2                                                   AFI48-144_LUKEAFBSUP 18 MAY 2012


1.9.3.7.2. (Added) CE Customer Service Desk will utilize email to coordinate the notification
to interested parties. The email will include the following information: Type of Outage,
Affected Building(s), Purpose of Outage, Start Date, End Date, Estimated Start Time, and
Estimated Duration.
1.9.3.7.3. (Added) PH/BE reviews the plans for scheduled outages for sanitation concerns that
may dictate a need for temporary rest facilities or alternate drinking water sources such as bottled
water. PH will contact the facility manager listed on the outage routing sheet to provide
instructions to facilities as appropriate (ex. Child Development Center or food service locations).
1.9.3.7.4. (Added) BE/PH will determine if event requires “Boil Water Notice,” to discourage
consumption prior to confirmation of bacteriological results. This is in the event that the affected
portion of distribution system cannot be effectively isolated from the rest of system and or
potability concerns dictate.
1.9.3.7.5. (Added) In the event of an emergency or critical lapse in the distribution system, in
consultation with BE/PH, CE will notify the wing commander and initiate base-wide notification
as appropriate (ex. signs at base gates, Luke_All email, etc), with Mission Support Group
Commander approval and coordination with Fighter Wing Public Affairs.
3.1.3.1. (Added) Disinfection and flushing will be performed in accordance with American
Water Works Association standards for CE and Maricopa Association of Governments Uniform
Standard Specifications Public Works Construction for work performed by a contractor. CE will
provide a POC for any contract work to be performed. Prior to disinfecting and flushing, this
POC will notify BE (via DSN 896-7521 or the BE on-call pager (623) 203-6852 after hours) and
PH via DSN 896-6176/6174. The contact must verify the location of the work performed, and be
available to assist if requested.
3.1.3.2. (Added) BE will sample, within 3 hours of notification, for the presence/absence of
total coliforms.
3.1.3.3. (Added) BE will notify CE Customer Service of the sample results after the 24-hour
incubation period. If sample results are positive for coliforms, BE will request disinfection and
flushing procedures be repeated. BE will recommend that the water main return to service after
negative results are achieved.
