This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive 44-1, Medical Operations. This
instruction implements clinic intake forms that are properly formatted to be stored in the medical
record if needed. This publication applies to all personnel assigned, attached, or under contract
to the Pediatric Clinic. This publication requires the collection and or maintenance of
information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by 10 U.S.C. 55, Medical and
Dental Care, and E.O. 9397 (SSN). The applicable SORN F044 AF SG D, and Automated
Medical/Dental            Record            System             is          available           at:
http://dpclo.defense.gov/privacy/SORNs/SORNs.html.             This instruction applies to all
personnel assigned, attached, or on contract to the 59th Medical Wing (59 MDW). This
instruction does not apply to the 959th Medical Group, 359th Medical Group, Air National
Guard, or Air Force Reserve. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication
to the Office of Primary Responsibility using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication. Requests for waivers must be submitted to the OPR listed above for consideration
and approval. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management
System Records Disposition Schedule.

1. Tri-Service Workflow (TSWF).
   1.1. These documents have been modified from the original Tri-Service Workflow (TSWF)
   intake forms provided by the Air Force Medical Operations Agency that were not approved
   through a 59 MDW Instruction. They have been revised to include appropriate patient
   identification, form number and relevant patient history. The appropriate form is chosen by
   the front desk staff based on the reason for the visit and given to the patient/parent to

    functioning, the entire encounter can be documented on the form by the technician and

2. 59 MDW Form 176.
    2.1. 59 MDW Form 176, Screening Questionnaire Newborn-3 month Visit, is an intake form
    for well newborns to be completed by the parent prior to the visit. The information provided
    is entered into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
3. 59 MDW Form 177.
    3.1. 59 MDW Form 177, Screening Questionnaire 4-11 month Visit, is an intake form for
    well infants to be completed by the parent prior to the visit. The information provided is
    entered into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
4. 59 MDW Form 178.
    4.1. 59 MDW Form 178, Screening Questionnaire 12-23 month Visit, is an intake form for
    well children to be completed by the parent prior to the visit. The information provided is
    entered into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
5. 59 MDW Form 179.
    5.1. 59 MDW Form 179, Screening Questionnaire 2-5 year Visit, is an intake form for well
    children to be completed by the parent prior to the visit. The information provided is entered
    into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
6. 59 MDW Form 180.
    6.1. 59 MDW Form 180, Screening Questionnaire 6-11 year Visit, is an intake form for well
    children to be completed by the parent prior to the visit. The information provided is entered
    into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
7. 59 MDW Form 181.
    7.1. 59 MDW Form 181, Screening Questionnaire 12-18 year Visit, is an intake form for
    well adolescents to be completed by the patient/parent prior to the visit. The information
    provided is entered into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
8. 59 MDW Form 182.
    8.1. 59 MDW Form 182, Pediatric Asthma Visit, is an intake form for routine asthma visits
    to be completed by the patient/parent prior to the visit. The information provided is entered
    into AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
9. 59 MDW Form 183.
    9.1. 59 MDW Form 183, Pediatric Acute/EST Visit, is an intake form for acute visits or
    other concerns [not asthma or attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)] to be
    completed by the patient/parent prior to the visit. The information provided is entered into
    AHLTA by the technician on the appropriate TSWF template.
