This mission directive implements AFPD 10-1, Mission Directives. It prescribes the mission and
responsibilities of the 505th Command and Control Wing (505 CCW). This mission directive
applies to Air National Guard (ANG) when published in the ANGIND 2. It also applies to Air
Force Reserve Command (AFRC) units and members when published in the AFRESIND 2.
Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Contact
supporting records managers as required. Send comments and suggested improvements to this
publication on AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, through channels to
HQ ACC/A3Y, 205 Dodd Blvd, Langley AFB VA 23665-2792.

1. Mission. The mission of the 505 CCW is to improve warfighter capability through command
and control testing, tactics development, and training.
2. Command. The 505 CCW is an Air Combat Command (ACC) Wing organized under the
United States Air Force Warfare Center (USAFWC).
3. Responsibilities. Commander, 505 CCW will:
   3.1. Provide training for Component Numbered Air Force (C-NAF) Headquarters.
       3.1.1. Produce Basic Qualified crew members for Air and Space Operation Centers
       (AOC) by conducting Initial Qualification Training. Make this training available to other
       Service, Joint and Coalition Liaison Officers and Duty Officers.

       3.1.2. Develop Operational Art/excellence through the conduct of air operations
       leadership training.
           3.1.2.1. Train AOC Division Chiefs and select MAJCOM/NAF-level Directors.
           3.1.2.2. Provide training for senior officers covering AOC, Air Force Forces
           (AFFOR) Staff and Air Component Coordination Element academics.
           3.1.2.3. Support the Air University with systems academics, as well as AOC training.
           3.1.2.4. Provide Intermediate-level AFFOR Staff training to develop foundational
           skills necessary to execute warfighting headquarters staff responsibilities on behalf of
           the COMMAFFOR.
       3.1.3. Train joint and coalition personnel possessing appropriate clearance and access,
       who are assigned to or interface with an AOC to perform Service liaison or other duties.
       3.1.4. Conduct advanced Command and Control (C2) and Intelligence, Surveillance &
       Reconnaissance (ISR) training for AOC/AFFOR Staff Senior Leaders.
       3.1.5. Provide Continuation Training (CT) opportunities for C-NAF Headquarters.
           3.1.5.1. Develop, execute, control, and assess during BLUE FLAG or other
           operational-level exercises.
           3.1.5.2. Provide AOC CT opportunities during VIRTUAL FLAG (VF) exercises.
           3.1.5.3. Provide AOC CT opportunities during RED FLAG (RF) exercises.
    3.2. Provide training opportunities for C2 and ISR systems and personnel/crews, in addition
    to C-NAF.
       3.2.1. Develop, execute, control, and assess Warfighter Focused Events (WFE), VF and
       Joint Kill Chain Events (JKCE) using a combination of Live, Virtual, and Constructive
       (LVC) capabilities.
       3.2.2. Provide support to the USAF Weapons School Mission Employment (ME) phase
       by providing AOC representation and integration opportunities.
       3.2.3. Develop and enhance integration between the Distributed Mission Operations
       Center, Combined Air Operations Center-Nellis and Hurlburt LVC capabilities in support
       of RF, VF, WFE, JKCE, and Weapons School ME.
    3.3. Provide liaison and exercise support to the Combined Arms Center (CAC) on air, space
    and cyberspace power employment with direct support to the Battle Command Training
    Program.
       3.3.1. Produce exercise support and execution plans to train Higher Headquarters (HHQ)
       directed and CAC-assigned training audiences.
    3.4. Provide Joint National Training Capability Air, Space, and Cyber employment liaison to
    U.S. Joint Forces Command directed training, joint/coalition service training and other events
    as directed by USAFWC.
    3.5. Support ACC implementation of Operational Warfighter initiatives as directed by the
    Operational Warfighter/Coal Warfighter Roadmap.

  3.6. Support development of Distributed Mission Operations capabilities for the AF and
  support integration of multi-level security/cross-domain solutions to allow full participation
  by AF assets and in support of joint and coalition participation in testing, tactics, and
  training.
  3.7. Enhance operational-level C2 through the tactics development program.
  3.8. Provide support to C2 and ISR capability development through participation in forums
  such as the AOC Operational Review Board, AOC Requirements Review Board, and the
  Combat Air Forces Realistic Training Review Board.
  3.9. Support C2 and ISR evolutionary acquisition and Operational Test for C2 and ISR
  weapons systems, including: E-3 B/C AWACS, E-8C Joint STARS, AOC, TBMCS FL,
  TBMCS UL/UC2, PR C2, Tactical Air Control Party (TACP), Control and Reporting Center
  (CRC) and Distributed Common Ground System (DCGS).
     3.9.1. Provide reports on the operational effectiveness, suitability, net-centricity and
     impact for C2 and ISR weapons systems, including: E-3 B/C AWACS, E-8C Joint
     STARS, AOC, TBMCS FL, TBMCS UL/UC2, PR C2, TACP, CRC and DCGS that
     allow decision-makers to make informed decisions about materiel and non-materiel
     solutions to warfighter needs.
  3.10. Provide live-fly planning, modeling and simulations, control, and assessment support
  to Joint Expeditionary Force Experiment and other experimentation as directed by
  USAFWC.
  3.11. Monitor, evaluate and optimize long-range radar in support of geographic Combatant
  Commanders as tasked by HHQ.
  3.12. Integrate Air, Space and Cyber into 505 CCW test, tactics development, training, and
  exercise planning and execution events.
  3.13. Form Adopted: AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication
