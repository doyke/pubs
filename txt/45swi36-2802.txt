This instruction establishes uniform policy and procedures for nomination, selection and award
of the 45th Space Wing (45 SW), Patrick AFB and Cape Canaveral AFS (PAFB/CCAFS)
recognition programs; applies to all personnel assigned or attached to the 45th Space Wing,
Patrick AFB, Cape Canaveral AFS, 333d Recruiting Squadron (333 RCS), Air Force Technical
Applications Center (AFTAC), Defense Equal Opportunity Management Institute (DEOMI),
Joint Surveillance and Target Attack Radar System (JSTARS), 920th Rescue Wing (920 RQW)
or other associate units. If the Air Force (AF) and/or Headquarters Air Force Space Command
(HQ AFSPC) make policy changes affecting this program, changes will supersede this
instruction. The overall objective is to recognize outstanding performers and individual
achievements on a continuing basis.

Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Records Information Management Systems
(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.       Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF 847, Recommendation
for Change of Publication; route AF 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain
of command. This instruction requires the collection and maintenance of information protected
by the Privacy Act of 1974. Executive Order 9397, FDR on Social Security-Numbering System
for Federal Accounts Relating to Individual Persons, 22 November 1943, authorizes using the

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Military
Recognition Program: Added Field Grade Officer (FGO) category along with updated Annual
Award submission requirements (i.e., AF 1206, Biography, Statement of Intent, 7 Action Photos,
and Official Photo). Civilian Recognition Program: Changed the category headings in the AF
1206 to match those outlined in AFI36-2802. Updated guidance for Annual Award submission.
(i.e., AF 1206, Biography, Statement of Intent, 7 Action Photos, and Official Photo). Volunteer
Recognition Program: Added The Airmen and Family Readiness Center (A&FRC) as OPR for
this category. Team Recognition Program: Added instructions on what is required for award
submissions (i.e., AF 1206 and Photos), along with the addition of Award Score Sheet
(Attachment 9). Attachment 2: Dates updated along with the addition of the FGO category.
Attachment 3: Added FGO category to the awards letter. Attachment 7: Added Team and FGO
categories to the Annual Awards checklist.

1.3. Military Nominee Eligibility: The recognition program is administered on a calendar year
basis. Individuals will compete in the grade they held for the majority of the award period.
Members must have been assigned to the organization for at least 50 percent of the award period
to compete. Individuals must not have disciplinary action pending. Grade criteria are as
follows:
     1.3.1. O1 through O3 for Company Grade Officer (CGO).
     1.3.2. O4 for Field Grade Officer (FGO).
     1.3.3. E-7 through E-8 for Senior Noncommissioned Officer (SNCO).
     1.3.4. E-5 through E-6 for Noncommissioned Officer (NCO).
     1.3.5. E-1 through E-4 for Airmen (AMN).
     1.3.6. E-7 through E-8 with AFSC 8F000 for First Sergeant.
     1.3.7. E-1 through E-7 for Honor Guard.
     1.3.8. All military ranks for Volunteer.

     determine the winner of the 45 SW and the individual moving forward to compete at the
     installation level, and the other to determine the installation winner.

     each category to the wing/base quarterly recognition boards.

     1.4.4. Quarterly award winners are not eligible to compete during concurrent (back-to-back)
     quarters of the same calendar year.

1.6. Board Member Selection Criteria:
   1.6.1. The 45 SW/CCC or designated representative will serve as a staff advisor for the
   selection process.
   1.6.2. The 45 SW/CCCA will provide a board schedule to each participating organization.
   Those organizations will in turn solicit for volunteers to serve as board members and return
   the selections to the 45 SW/CCCA by the established suspense date.
   1.6.3. Wing/base boards will consist of at least five members and a president. The president
   is a non-voting member, except in the case of an overall tie.
   1.6.4. Board members will be rotated among base organizations and will include joint
   service members when appropriate.
   1.6.5. Board member grade requirements are as follows:
      1.6.5.1. AMN Category: SSgt or TSgt (President: MSgt).
      1.6.5.2. NCO Category: MSgt or SMSgt (President: CMSgt).
      1.6.5.3. SNCO Category: CMSgt or Gp Supt (President: 45 SW/CCC).
      1.6.5.4. CGO and FGO Category: Lt Col or Col (President: 45 SW/CV).
      1.6.5.5. First Sergeant Category: 45 SW/CCC and two CMSgt’s.
      1.6.5.6. Honor Guard Member: HG Flight Chief and OIC (President: CMSgt).

                         CIVILIAN RECOGNITION PROGRAM:

2.1. Objective: Recognize outstanding appropriated and non-appropriated fund (NAF)
civilians.
2.2. Responsibilities:
       2.2.1. The 45 FSS/FSMP (Awards and Decorations) acts as the board president and
       oversees the selection process.
       2.2.2. The board president will review the award nominations to ensure they are in
       accordance with (IAW) this instruction.
       2.2.3. Empowered by the 45 SW/CC, the awards committee selects the quarterly and
       annual award winners. The committee quorum will consist of three voting members.
       2.2.4. Each unit will be required to complete appropriate paperwork for granting a
       Notable Achievement Award or Time-Off Incentive Award for appropriated fund
       winners. A Request for Personnel Action (RPA) should be forwarded to the 45
       FSS/FSMP for review and final approval/processing. The original award justification
       should be maintained in the AF Form 971, Supervisor’s Employee Brief. The Standard
       Form 50, Notification of Personnel Action, can be printed by the employee by accessing
       their Electronic Official Personnel Folder (EOPF) from the Air Force Personnel Center
       (AFPC) secure website at: http://ask.afpc.randolph.af.mil/. The service and mission
       support squadrons will follow guidelines in 45 FSS OI 34-600, Human Resources Office
       Non-Appropriated Fund (NAF) Awards Program, for non-appropriated funds civilian
       winners.
       2.2.5. The 45 SW/CCCA will ensure appropriate award elements (e.g., plaques, trophies,
       etc.) are prepared and delivered to their respective events.
2.3. Nominee Eligibility: The unit’s appropriated and non-appropriated fund civilians who
meet the following criteria may be considered for an award.
   2.3.1. Individuals assigned or attached to the awarding unit for 6 months and serving on a
   permanent appointment.
   2.3.2. Individuals will be nominated in the category that corresponds to the grade/position
   they held for the majority of the period.
   2.3.3. Individuals must not have disciplinary action pending.
