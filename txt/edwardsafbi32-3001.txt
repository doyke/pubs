This publication implements Air Force Instruction (AFI) 32-3001, Explosive Ordnance Disposal
(EOD) Program. It establishes procedures to follow during operations on the Explosive
Ordnance Disposal (EOD) Open Bum/Open Detonation (OB/OD) and during Precision Impact
Range Area (PIRA) target clearance operations. This regulation applies to all Edwards Air Force
Base (EAFB) activities and tenant units providing EOD support. This publication cannot be
supplemented at any level. This instruction does not apply to the Civ Air Patrol and/or the
National Guard. No waivers may be granted for any part of this publication. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFM AN) 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of IAW Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located in the Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS). Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AFIMT
847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF IMT 847s from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command.

1. Responsibilities. It is the responsibility of all EOD personnel and the 412th Test Wing
Range Safety Officer (when applicable) to ensure these procedures are followed.
2. General
   2.1. Safety. To ensure the safety of all personnel involved in target clearances and EOD
   support to EAFB munitions related test, all live or unexpended ordnance item s found on the
   PIRA will be disposed of in-place. At no time will EOD personnel attempt to manually
   remove or disassemble explosive components (fuses, initiators, etc.) from impacted test

     Officer will give a safety briefing.
     2.2. Personnel Limits:
        2.2.1. Keep the number of personnel involved in OB/OD and range clearance operations
        to a minimum, consistent with a safe and efficient operation. However, the number of
        EOD personnel will never be less than two (minimum of three for OB/OD operations)
        with one having an Air Force Specialty of 3E871 or higher.
        2.2.2. Keep number of personnel involved in training operations to a minimum
        consistent with a safe and efficient operation. However, the number of EOD personnel
        will never be less than two with one having an Air Force Specialty of 3E851 or higher.

     2.4. Transportation. All explosives will be transported from the munitions storage area to
     the specific range via an authorized explosive movement route. All explosive items will be
     transported and protected IAW AFMAN 91-201, Explosive Safety Standards, AFI 31-101,
     Integrated Defense (FOUO), EODOI 91-4, OB/ OD Facility Operations and EODOI 91-2,
     Vehicle Transportation of Explosives.
     2.5. Communications Requirement. Two way radio communications with 412th Range
     Squadron “DOWNFALL” will be available during all PIRA or OB/OD operations. The EOD
     team leaders will ensure the Personnel/Equipment Tracking System unit is activated once
     within PIRA boundaries. EOD team leaders will notify DOWNFALL when teams arrive and
     depart from specific range locations. EOD team leaders will notify DOWNFALL when
     demolition/disposal operations have begun and when operations have been completed.
3. Emergency Actions. In case of a fire or medical emergency, immediately contact
DOWNFALL. Provide the details of the emergency and DOWNFALL will contact the
appropriate agency directly.
4. Procedures. EOD Operating Instructions (EODOI 9 1-2, EODOI 91-3, and EODOI 91-4)
will provide detailed procedures for all EOD PIRA range operations.

  4.1. All EOD range residue clearance/decontamination operations will be coordinated with
  the PIRA supervisor (5-6148) prior to any operation on the PIRA.
  4.2. EOD Operations Control Center (OCC) will always contact DOWNFALL (5-5603) to
  verify scheduled range time and obtain airspace ceiling required for explosive operations.
  DOWNFALL will notify the appropriate agencies to ensure the area will be clear.
  4.3. The EOD team leader will contact DOWNFALL (5-5603) prior to initial entry of the
  PIRA or OB/OD range and upon departure.
