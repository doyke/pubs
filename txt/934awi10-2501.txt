This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 10-25, Emergency Management,
Air Force Instruction (AFI) 10-2501, Emergency Management (EM) Planning and Operations
and Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 10-2507, Readiness and Emergency Management (R&EM)
Flight Operations. It establishes responsibilities and procedures for the 934th Airlift Wing (AW)
EM program. It defines the functions of units within the 934 AW that contribute to the program.
This instruction applies to all 934 AW units at Minneapolis-St Paul ARS, Minnesota. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. This revision
incorporates changes from AFI 10-2501, Emergency Management Planning and Operations to
include Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Supplement, 6 February 2009 and Interim Change
IC-2, 06 April 2009 requirements throughout the document. Additional abbreviations and
acronyms have been updated and added to the glossary and attachments added.

1. General: 934 Airlift Wing Commander has overall responsibility for the Emergency
Management (EM) Program.
2. Responsibilities:
    2.1. The 934 AW Commander will:
       2.1.1. Establish an EM Working Group (EMWG) to be composed of the following
       members: 934 MSG/CC (chairperson), 934 OG/CC, 934 LRS/LGRR, Exercise
       Evaluation Chief, 934 MSG/CEX, 934 LRS/LGRS, 934 MSG/SGPB, 934 SF, 934 CF,
       934 ASTS, 934 SVS, and tenants units. The EMWG will meet semiannually or as
       directed by the chairperson.
          2.1.1.1. EMWG meetings will cover:
          2.1.1.2. Wing Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear and High-Yield Explosive
          (CBRNE) training stats.
          2.1.1.3. Status of CBRNE Equipment.
          2.1.1.4. Staff Assistant Visits (SAV) and operational readiness exercise (ORE)
          trends.
          2.1.1.5. Status of base wide exercise evaluation program.
          2.1.1.6. PEC 55166 requirements.
       2.1.2. Ensure that the following units establish a Unit EM Program to support the
       Emergency Management Program:
          2.1.2.1. 934th Airlift Wing
          2.1.2.2. 934 MXG/CC – MXS, MOF, MXG
          2.1.2.3. 934 AMXS
          2.1.2.4. 27th Aerial Port Squadron
          2.1.2.5. 934th Aeromedical Evacuation Squadron
          2.1.2.6. 96th Airlift Squadron
          2.1.2.7. 934th Civil Engineer Squadron
          2.1.2.8. 934th Communications Flight
          2.1.2.9. 934th Logistics Readiness Squadron
          2.1.2.10. 934th Operations Support Flight
          2.1.2.11. 934th Security Forces Squadron
          2.1.2.12. 934th Services Flight
          2.1.2.13. 934th Aero-medical Staging Squadron
          2.1.2.14. 934th Mission Support Group

     2.1.3. Ensure the following functions establish Unit Control Centers (UCC) to support
     the Minneapolis-St Paul IAP, ARS, Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan
     (CEMP) 10-2, see requirements in paragraph 5.
        2.1.3.1. Command Post
        2.1.3.2. Security Forces Control Center
        2.1.3.3. Base Operations Services (BOS) Civil Engineer/Emergency Management
        2.1.3.4. BOS Transportation Control Center
        2.1.3.5. Maintenance Operations Center
        2.1.3.6. Airfield Operations Center
        2.1.3.7. Emergency Family Assistance Control Center (EFACC)
        2.1.3.8. Communication Control Center
     2.1.4. Appoint an Exercise Evaluation Team (EET) chief and ensure all exercise
     requirements are met IAW AFI 10-2501, Emergency Management Planning and
     Operations, as supplemented. See Attachment 3 for copy of appointment letter.
     2.1.5. Appoint the Disaster Response Force (DRF) from those agencies listed in 934 AW
     CEMP 10-2, Annex A. See Attachment 2 for sample appointment letter.
  2.2. Each Unit Commander will:
     2.2.1. Assign a primary and alternate Unit EM Representative (UEMR). In order to
     work EM issues during the week at least one of the EM representatives must be a full
     time employee.
     2.2.2. Forward a copy of appointment letters to 934th CES/CEX, and retain one copy in
     the unit's files. The UEMR is required to maintain a copy.
     2.2.3. Ensure as a minimum one representative remains current in UEMR training and
     attends at least one EM rep meeting per year.
     2.2.4. Ensure Unit Training Managers are trained in, and utilize, the Automated Civil
     Engineering System-Personnel Readiness (ACES-PR) module for scheduling and
     tracking CBRNE training.
     2.2.5. Ensure task qualification training (TQT) is conducted and properly documented as
     outlined in paragraphs 2.7.2.1. – 2.7.2.4.
     2.2.6. Ensure Facility Management kits are inspected at least annually and missing or
     expired items are replaced. Send a copy of the inspection results to CEX.
  2.3. The 934 R&EM Flight will:
     2.3.1. Conduct training that supports both military and civilian work force, to include:
     Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear and High-Yield Explosive (CBRNE)
     survival skills training (SST), UEMR, Base Emergency Preparedness Orientation
     (BEPO), State-side Contingencies, Readiness Support Team (RST) and Contamination
     Control Team (CCT.)

       2.3.2. Conduct SAV’s to each agency as identified in part 2.1.2. of this instruction, and
       AFMAN 10-2507 Chapter 5, R&EM Program Management.
           2.3.2.1. Conduct the SAV not to exceed 24 months.
           2.3.2.2. Provide a SAV checklist and a visit schedule to each unit.
           2.3.2.3. Schedule the SAV at least two unit training assembly’s (UTA) prior to the
           visit.
           2.3.2.4. Prepare a SAV report and forward the report to the unit no later than (NLT)
           1 UTA after visit.
           2.3.2.5. Review all checklists that support wing plans (i.e. 934 AW CEMP 10-2,
           IGESP) and support/emergency responses (i.e. DRF, EET, CCT and National
           Disaster Shelters, Emergency Operations Center (EOC)).
       2.3.3. Be responsible for maintaining all lesson plans and checklists in support of the
       base CEMP 10-2.
       2.3.4. Conduct a self-inspection in accordance with (IAW) 934AWI90-201, Self
       Inspection Program.
    2.4. Unit EM Representatives will:
       2.4.1. Act as the point of contact for the unit and meet all requirements set by AFI 10-
       2501 AFRC Sup, IC-2, in this instruction.
       2.4.2. Be present during unit SAV.
       2.4.3. Coordinate in-briefing and out-briefing (optional) with their unit commander.
       2.4.4. Track open SAV deficiencies until closed, provide estimated completion date.
       2.4.5. Maintain Unit EM Handbook according to the table of contents that is provided by
       the Readiness & Emergency Management office.
       2.4.6. Conduct a semi-annual self inspection using the 934 CES/CEX R&EM Flight
       SAV checklist and forward a copy of self inspection to 934 CES/CEX. Contact
       CES/CEX R&EM for the most current copy of the SAV checklist.
       2.4.7. Attend UEMR training/meeting sessions (minimum of one representative must
       attend to keep current).
       2.4.8. Bring the UEMR Handbooks to all training/meeting sessions.
       2.4.9. UEMR should coordinate staff assistance visit schedule with the Unit Ancillary
       Training Manager to ensure sufficient time is scheduled.
       2.4.10. ENSURE THAT ALL OPERATIONAL MASKS/EQUIPMENT ARE
       STORED SEPARATELY FROM THE TRAINING C1-BAG. Masks can be stored
       in the same area as long as the training equipment is identified. (MASK WILL NOT
       BE STORED IN OR ON TOP OF THE C-1 TRAINING BAG).
       2.4.11. Ensure each person on mobility is assigned their own operational MCU- 2A/P
       protective mask. Individuals are responsible for cleaning, inspecting, and operational
       status of their mask.
934AWI10-2501 23 MARCH 2010                                                                 5


     2.4.12. ENSURE MASKS WITHIN EACH UNIT ARE NOT SHARED. There is a
     potential (low risk) blood borne pathogen hazard. Before the mask is reissued to a new
     individual, it must be washed with a bleach solution as directed by T.O. 14P4-15-1.
  2.5. The DRF will:
     2.5.1. Maintain equipment in the event of a rapid response, along with:
         2.5.1.1. Checklist(s) IAW/supporting the CEMP Plan 10-2.
         2.5.1.2. Communications capability with unit control center, as applicable.
         2.5.1.3. Inclement weather gear.
         2.5.1.4. Road maps for the states of Minnesota and Wisconsin.
         2.5.1.5. Crash Grid maps with overlay.
     2.5.2. Receive training IAW AFI 10-2501, Emergency Management Planning and
     Operations, and other directives as supplemented.
  2.6. The EET Chief will:
     2.6.1. Follow the established guidelines IAW AFI 10-2501.
     2.6.2. Be responsible for the Wing Exercise Program. Units conducting their own
     exercise, (i.e., bivouac, medical) will require the EET involvement.
     2.6.3. Notify local outside emergency response agencies prior to an exercise.
     2.6.4. Develop an annual schedule in accordance with the Emergency Management
     Exercise program as outlined in AFI 10-2501 and AFRC SUP. Annual schedule must be
     approved by the 934 AW Commander. Personnel assigned to a mobility position must
     participate in wing exercises.       NOTE: AFI10-2501 states to ―…emphasize
     participation…‖ but does not establish a percentage of unit personnel who must
     participate.
     2.6.5. Ensure EET members are trained according to AFI 10-2501.
     2.6.6. Brief EMWG on status of wing exercise program.
  2.7. The Unit Ancillary Training Manager will:
     2.7.1. Be the point of contact (POC) for CBRNE Awareness Course, Improvised
     Explosive Device (IED) computer based training (CBT) on the Advanced Distributed
     Learning Services (ADLS) and SST. See paragraphs 4.1 – 4.1.8.
         2.7.1.1. Until ACES-PR is available and operational, training slots will be requested
         by contacting the R&EM Flight no less than three (3) days prior to SST. All
         scheduling and tracking of CBRNE/SST will be maintained on ACES-PR once it is
         operational.
     2.7.2. Ensure TQT is accomplished and documented correctly.
         2.7.2.1. Functional area manager/expert will select and compile training objectives
         (core tasks) from CFETP on to an AF IMT 1098, Special Task Certification and
         Recurring Training (or like product).
6                                                             934AWI10-2501 23 MARCH 2010


           2.7.2.2. TQT training will focus on the core tasks selected. Document the training
           electronically or on hard copy identifying who and what training was accomplished.
           2.7.2.3. Personnel accomplishing TQT will remain in MOPP4 for one (1) hour.
           2.7.2.4. Exercises (ORE, ORI, etc) can be used for TQT credit.
    2.8. The Accountable Officer (934 LRS/LGRS) will:
       2.8.1. Be the POC for operational chemical equipment.
       2.8.2. Maintain a status of chemical warfare defense equipment (i.e. inspection listings,
       shelf life items.)
       2.8.3. Brief the unit commander concerning shortfalls, actions taken to correct shortages,
       and budget requirements to replace unserviceable or expired equipment.
3. Unit EM information program.
    3.1. The EM rep will ensure briefings on tornadoes, floods, winter storms, and Attack
    Actions are conducted as needed or when provided by R&EM Flight.
    3.2. Post the following visual aids in a well traveled and visible location:
       3.2.1. AFVA 10-2510, US Air Force Emergency Notification Signals.
       3.2.2. AFVA 10-2511, USAF Standardized Attack Warning Signals for NBCC Medium
       and High Threat Areas.
       3.2.3. AFVA 10-2512, Mission Oriented Protective Postures (MOPP).
       3.2.4. 934AWVA10-2502, EM Program Unit Representative and Shelter Information, 8
       September 2009.
4. Specialized Training and Teams.
    4.1. CBRNE/SST
       4.1.1. Every member (reservist) of 934th AW must accomplish both the CBRNE
       Awareness Course and IED CBTs on ADLS.
       4.1.2. CBTs must be completed before, and within four (4) UTAs, of attending Survivor
       Skills Training (SST).
       4.1.3. Only personnel assigned to a mobility position are required to accomplish the
       hands-on portion (SST).
       4.1.4. Each individual assigned a mask must bring their mask and training C-1 bag to
       SST.
       4.1.5. Personnel will wear Battle Dress Uniform (BDU), Airman Battle Uniform (ABU)
       or flight suit to class. Skirts are unacceptable.
       4.1.6. Contact lenses will not be worn with the ground crew ensemble or masks.
       (Charcoal particles are very fine and may get underneath the lens).
       4.1.7. SST classes will start on time according to the published CBRNE training
       schedule. Students late for class will be allowed in class space permitting. (Do not
       schedule students for class when there is a known conflict)
934AWI10-2501 23 MARCH 2010                                                                 7


      4.1.8. SST classroom space is limited to 35 students. Personnel, who arrive for training
      without prior scheduling will be trained, space permitting.
5. Unit Control Centers.
   5.1. Unit Control Center members will be appointed by the Unit Commander.
   5.2. The Control Centers will maintain the following equipment, supplies, and
   documentation:
      5.2.1. Base grid map and overlay for the map.
      5.2.2. Road maps for the states of Minnesota and Wisconsin.
      5.2.3. Recall roster for unit personnel and Readiness Specialized team members.
      5.2.4. Emergency response action checklist.
      5.2.5. Log of events to include documenting emergency information received through
      the emergency notification phone system or by radio message, if applicable.
6. Readiness Support Team (RST).
   6.1. The RST will be comprised of non-mobility civilian personnel and will be trained just-
   in-time IAW AFI 10-2501, as supplemented, to augment the Readiness Emergency
   Management Office during disaster operations, exercises, and training. Personnel will be
   assigned from the following organizations:
   6.2. 934 BOS: Transportation, Supply and Civil Engineering – six (6) members total.
   6.3. RST members are appointed by Unit Commander or Division Chief. See Attachment 2
   for sample appointment letter.
   6.4. Training will be conducted by CEX and consist of 16 hours of classroom or
   demonstration-performance, or just-in-time training, whichever comes first.
7. Contamination Control Team (CCT).
   7.1. The following units are required to have a CCT:
      7.1.1. Civil Engineering Squadron (CES)
      7.1.2. Logistics Readiness Squadron (LRS) - Transportation
      7.1.3. Maintenance Squadron (MXS/AMXS) - Note: At least one from Munitions
      7.1.4. Aerial Port Squadron (27 APS)
      7.1.5. CCT members are appointed by the Unit Commander
   7.2. CCT members will be educated and trained only when threat posture increases (or prior
   to deployment, exercise, etc.)
8. Equipment.
   8.1. Chemical over garments must be marked (TRNG ONLY) to include suits, gloves and
   protective boots.
   8.2. Pants and coat: Letters will be approximately three (3) inches high. Coat will be
   labeled on back, several inches below the collar and parallel to the floor. Pants will be
8                                                         934AWI10-2501 23 MARCH 2010


    labeled on right leg, length-wise between front pocket and calf area with a visible color,
    preferably white.
    8.3. Gloves and boots: Letters will be approximately ¾‖ high. Both gloves will be labeled
    about one (1) inch from the opening and across the top. Boots will be labeled approximately
    one (1) inch from the top of each boot. NOTE: A latex paint pen works well for labeling
    gloves and boots.
    8.4. JSLIST jacket will have rank, name, and last four of the social security number (SSN)
    on front and back of hood and right breast (masking or duct tape).
    8.5. T.O.s (electronic or hard copy) are required for all operational chemical warfare
    equipment stored within the unit.
