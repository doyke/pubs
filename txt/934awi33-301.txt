This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 33-3, Air Force Information
Management, and extends the guidance of Air Force Qualification Training Package (AFQTP)
3A0X1-225E, Knowledge Operations Management Functional Manager’s Handbook and AFI
36-2201, V3, Air Force Training Program On the Job Training Administration. It defines
additional procedures and responsibilities for the position of Wing KOM Functional Manager
and Wing/Group points of contact (POCs) for training record documentation and managing the
Knowledge Operations Management (KOM) proficiency and Season Training Program (STP)
programs. It ensures Knowledge Operators (KO) are trained and tracked in the Training
Business Area (TBA) program to serve their organization’s mission and are qualified to fulfill
readiness requirements. It applies to all 934th Airlift Wing units. Refer recommended changes
and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route the AF Form 847 to 934
CF/SCOK, 760 Military Hwy, Minneapolis ARS MN 55450-2100. Ensure that all records
created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with
AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force
Records     Disposition     Schedule     (RDS)      located    at    https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/.

1. General. The KOM Functional Manager (FM) is a full-time position that requires the
integral support of each commander, supervisor, trainer, and base training expert to ensure
effectiveness. Each organization plays an important role in the KOM utilization and training
process. It is incumbent upon each commander and supervisor to be involved in the effective use
of their assets – both in accomplishing the mission as well as the proper use and career
development of assigned KOM personnel. The FM is the central control point to assist with
assignment allocation, training oversight, readiness and career guidance for all KOM personnel
assigned to an organization.
2. Responsibilities:
     2.1. Wing Commander will:
        2.1.1. Actively support the KOM training program by ensuring the FM has the necessary
        support and tools to maintain an effective training program.
        2.1.2. Appoint a fully qualified 7-level KO POC from the Wing Staff to oversee
        documentation in TBA and serve as FM liaison for the KOs assigned to the Wing Staff.
     2.2. Group commanders will:
        2.2.1. Appoint a fully qualified 7-level KOM POC from each Group to oversee training
        record documentation in TBA and serve as liaison to the FM for the Kos assigned to each
        respective Group.
        2.2.2. Support the FM's efforts to assign qualified KOs as subject matter experts for
        KOM training activities.
        2.2.3. Ensure FM coordinates on all KO deployment taskings.
        2.2.4. Ensure assigned KOs attend KOM workshop, when scheduled.
        2.2.5. Ensure Group POCs coordinate with FM on Career Development Course (CDC)
        proficiency training and upgrade requirements for all KOM personnel assigned.
     2.3. Squadron commanders will:
        2.3.1. Ensure that FM is notified of all KO manning changes, e.g., permanent change of
        station, permanent change of assignment, retraining requests, etc.
        2.3.2. Ensure supervisors coordinate any training issues with the KOM FM.
        2.3.3. Make a concerted effort to utilize KOs in the KOM career field, capitalizing on
        their primary responsibilities of knowledge, data and information management, to include
        SharePoint, Communities of Practice, and Air Force Portal.
           2.3.3.1. As a vital and integral part of the unit, the KO’s knowledge and skills should
           be used to enhance mission effectiveness in accordance with the CFETP.
           2.3.3.2. Additional duties will not interfere with the primary function and continued
           training of assigned KOs.

  2.4. The KOM FM will:
     2.4.1. Develop annual KOM training schedule, maintain training class attendance, and
     status of training for all KOs.
     2.4.2. Notify CF commander of training no-shows.
     2.4.3. Coordinate on all first and second time CDC failures.
     2.4.4. Assist the Group KOM POCs in monitoring unit training programs to ensure
     qualifications and skill level upgrade requirements meet core knowledge priorities on all
     skill level training issues or job rotations.
     2.4.5. Provide the Group KOM POCs the yearly training schedule NLT the month of
     November to insure sufficient preparation time .
     2.4.6. Ensure all KOM personnel, MSgt and below, have an active Individual Training
     Plan (ITP) in TBA and every KO has a TBA account established.
         2.4.6.1. Initiate a TBA account and assign role for all non-KOM supervisors who
         directly supervise KOs.
     2.4.7. Ensure KOM POCs are notified of individuals in their organizations who have
     requested STP and the dates of training.
  2.5. Wing/Group KOM POC will:
     2.5.1. Be a fully qualified 3D071, appointed in writing by the Wing/Group Commander,
     and function as the POC for all KOM-related unit level training issues.
     2.5.2. Responsible for conducting a minimum of two UTA training classes per year.
     This training may be delegated to a qualified KO within the wing/group.
         2.5.2.1. Provide instructor name and training material to FM one month prior to
         class.
         2.5.2.2. If instructor is unavailable, the training will be accomplished by the
         Wing/Group KOM.
     2.5.3. Coordinate with supervisors who are not in the 3D0X1 career filed to ensure
     qualified trainers are provided to each KO in upgrade training (UGT).
         2.5.3.1. If the unit does not have a qualified trainer, the KOM POC will assume all
         duties of trainer.
     2.5.4. Provide oversight of all KOM upgrade/proficiency training for personnel within
     the organization, to include STP personnel.
         2.5.4.1. Review ITP and TBA journal entries for personnel enrolled in STP.
             2.5.4.2. Journal entries must be made at least every unit training assembly
             (UTA).
     2.5.5. Notify KOM FM when 3D0X1 supervisors change so TBA will accurately reflect
     current supervisor.
  2.6. Supervisor will:

        2.6.2. Accomplish a trainee work center and initial evaluation within 120 days of

3. Methods of Training. Training is achieved through a combination of in-residence courses,
supervised hands-on training, Air Force provided interactive computer based training (CBT), and
locally provided training.
     3.1. A standardized time block for training will be established every UTA and designated as
     Air Force Specialty Code (AFSC) specific training for all KOM personnel.
        3.1.1. UTA KOM training is mandatory and excusals must be coordinated with direct
        supervisor to KOM FM prior to training. Commanders will be notified of no-shows.
4. Seasoning Training Program. STP has been established to provide hands on training for
newly assigned Airmen upon completion of technical training. 3D031 personnel are offered a
maximum of 99 days to train on tasks in order to become mission qualified. This is defined as
being qualified to perform the tasks required at the current duty position. The Wing has the
capabilities to accomplish STP at home station. No off-station STP is authorized.
     4.1. All newly assigned 3-level personnel should be detailed to 934 CF/SCOK for 50% of
     their STP to ensure focus on KOM core areas.
     4.2. STP will include, but is not limited to, items in attachment 2, KOM Seasoning Training
     Plan.

  4.3. Completion times may vary and are at the discretion and agreement of trainer/trainee.
