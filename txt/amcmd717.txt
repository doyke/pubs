This mission directive implements policy guidance in AFPD 10-1, Mission Directives, AFI 10-101, For-
mat and Content of Mission Directives, and AFI 38-101, Air Force Organization. The mission directive
defines the mission, command relationship, responsibilities, and organization of the Aerial Port Squadron.
It also applies to the Air National Guard and Air Force Reserve units, effective upon the date of unit acti-
vation.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
This document has been completely revised and must be reviewed in its entirety.

1. Mission. The Aerial Port Squadron operates air terminal facilities at Air Mobility Command (AMC)
aerial ports to move passengers, mail, personal property, unaccompanied baggage and cargo by military or
military contract aircraft. Operates a rapid, reliable, global cargo and passenger movement system in sup-
port of AMC’s Global Reach Mission.

2. Command. The Aerial Port Squadron is assigned to the Mission Support Group.

3. Responsibilities.
   3.1. Formulates plans, establishes procedures for, and directs the providing of aerial port support for
   assigned and AMC en route aircraft, passengers, and cargo.
   3.2. Deploys personnel and equipment to provide sustained support of airlift operations at established
   fixed and or nonfixed terminals.
   3.3. Provides movement of personal property and cargo to/from overseas and Continental United
   States (CONUS) locations.
   3.4. Coordinates all aerial port functions to ensure cargo and passenger moves at the appropriate time.
   3.5. Loading and downloading of military and commercial cargo aircraft.
