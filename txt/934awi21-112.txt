This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive 21-1, Air and Space Maintenance. This
instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101; AFRC Sup 1 Aircraft
and Equipment Maintenance Management; and Air Force Consolidated Occupational Safety
Instruction (AFI) 91-203. It provides safety guidelines and designates responsibility for training,
qualification, and documentation for personnel who operate hangar doors. It applies to all
personnel within the 934th Airlift Wing (AW) Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport, Air
Reserve Station, Minnesota. Ensure that all records created as a result of a process prescribed in
this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847.
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from their field through the
appropriate functional chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed in its entirety.
Office symbols have been updated along with clarification of procedures.
1. Responsibilities:

     1.1. Commanders and Supervisors will ensure only properly trained and qualified personnel
     operate hangar doors.
     1.2. MSG/CE
        1.2.1. MSG/CE will be responsible for the installation and repair of signage, floor safety
        markings, and repair of hangar doors including electrical, mechanical, and all warning
        devices.
        1.2.2. The MSG/CE facility maintenance section will conduct annual inspections to
        ensure all signage, markings, and warning devices meet prescribed directives and will
        maintain inspection documentation.
        1.2.3. For door maintenance issues, building custodians will call the MSG/CE trouble
        desk at extension 713-1918.
2. General.
     2.1. This AWI applies to the following hangar doors:
        2.1.1. Building                               Office(s) of Primary Responsibility
        2.1.2. 821 North/South                       Building Manager (Primary/Alternate)
        2.1.3. 870 South                             Building Manager (Primary/Alternate)
     2.2. All operators must be trained by a qualified trainer. Training must include a physical
     operation of the hangar doors prior to certification of all powered hangar doors.
     2.3. Hangar door trainers are personnel who are qualified to operate powered doors. They
     must be thoroughly familiar with all Air Force Instructions, Operating Instructions, and all
     precautions pertaining to safe hangar door operations.
     2.4. Written door operating instructions will be posted next to all door control switches.
3. Training.
     3.1. Annual hangar door awareness refresher training is required for all MXG personnel
     qualified as door operators.
        3.1.1. Annual hangar door awareness refresher training will consist of a MS PowerPoint
        presentation, located on the F Drive: 934th Maintenance Group\MOF\MXOT\42 -
        Lesson / Hangar Door Awareness.
        3.1.2. This training will consist of highlighting the danger areas and precautions taken
        during door operation. This training will be conducted for personnel working in, or who
        have frequent duties inside hangars with powered doors.
        3.1.3. Training will be tracked in G081.
     3.2. Initial hangar door operator training will consist of hands-on training for each specific
     door. Point of contact for all hangar door training will be the appropriate building manager.
     3.3. Annual hangar door operator training will consist of review of this AWI and review of
     training slides and AFI instruction for any and all changes.

      3.3.1. Training will be in G081 course code MARB000821 for annual and refresher
      training for hangar 821 north and south doors and MARB000870 for hangar 870 south
      doors.
   3.4. Non-MXG Personnel.
      3.4.1. Designated personnel will arrange initial hangar door training with MXS/MXMT
      office.
          3.4.1.1. Trainee will bring an AF form 2426 to the training session and after
          receiving training retain one copy for their records and one copy will be retained by
          MXMT as proof of training.
          3.4.1.2. Once training is completed the respective squadron commander will sign the
          attached letter (Attachment 4) to authorize non-MXG personnel to operate hangar
          doors. This letter will be retained on file for one year in the MXG Quality Assurance
          office.
      3.4.2. Refresher training will be done annually and consist of reviewing this AWI and
      lesson plan from MXG training office and appropriate AFI.
          3.4.2.1. Squadron commanders will generate a new letter (Attachment 4) annually
          after ensuring refresher training has been accomplished.
4. Procedures:
   4.1. All operators will refer to and comply with detailed checklists posted on the applicable
   hangar door (See attachment 2 for building 821 and attachment 3 for building 870). Each
   building door listed has specific operating characteristics and has a different course code
   assigned in G081.
      4.1.1. If automatic power door opener fails to operate main hangar doors, trained MXG
      personnel will disengage clutch and move doors manually.

                                         Attachment 2
                       HANGAR DOOR OPERATIONS CHECKLIST
                       HANGAR 821 NORTH AND SOUTH DOORS

ALL PERSONNEL WILL BE QUALIFIED/TRAINED PRIOR TO HANGAR DOOR
OPERATION
NOTE: For door malfunctions, contact the building manager and /or MSG/CE Trouble desk at
713-1918.

CAUTION: Never use vehicles to open or close hangar doors unless the clutch to the drive
motor is disengaged and overhead tail door is in the Closed position

CAUTION: During winter months both North and South doors shall not be opened
simultaneously. Doors must not remain open longer than necessary to move aircraft to prevent
the alarm system from going off and freezing of water lines.

Pre-Operations Checklist
1. Locate and inspect pinch points and crush point areas for obstructions.
2. Inspect hangar door tracks for debris and both doors and tracks for damage.
3. Ensure area is clear of equipment, aircraft, and personnel both inside and out.
4. Ensure all personnel entry doors are closed and latched.
5. Ensure Tail door is down/closed prior to operation of main doors

Opening Hangar Doors Building 821

CAUTION: Hazardous Noise Area, Hearing Protection Required During Operation.

1. At main door center section push appropriate control button indentified by arrows
NOTE: Doors will begin to move approximately 5 seconds after alarm sounds if main door
alarm does not sound, do not continue and contact MSG/CE
2. To ensure safety of personnel and equipment, all hangar doors must be opened a minimum of
10 feet and brought to a complete stop prior to entering or exiting through doors
3. Open main horizontal doors fully to wing tip clearance line before aircraft movement through
door area

CAUTION: While doors are moving, continually monitor to ensure doors are clear of
obstructions, personnel, equipment, and aircraft

4. Overhead tail door controls are on the east wall of hangar 821 south facing doors and on west
wall for north facing doors.
NOTE: Main Doors must be fully open before opening Overhead tail door
 6                                                       934AWI21-112 24 SEPTEMBER 2013


5. Open overhead tail door fully before aircraft movement through door area, ensure red
alignments marks line match up on outside of tail door

CLOSING HANGAR DOORS BUILDING 821

CAUTION: Hazardous Noise Area Hearing Protection Required During Operation.

1. Overhead tail door controls are on the east wall of hangar 821 south facing doors and on west
wall for north facing doors
2. Close overhead tail door fully before closing main hangar doors.
3. At main door center section push appropriate control button indentified by arrows
NOTE: Doors will begin to move approximately 5 seconds after alarm sounds if main door
alarm does not sound, do not continue and contact MSG/CE
4. Close main horizontal doors a minimum of 10 feet and brought to a complete stop prior to
movement through door area

CAUTION: While doors are moving, continually monitor to ensure doors are clear of
obstructions, personnel, equipment, and aircraft

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES FOR OPERATING DOORS IN HANGAR 821
In the event of electrical failure, hangar doors can be operated manually by:
Main door emergency procedures:
1. Open panels located on each center door section.
2. Pull clutch disengage handle located on each side of the center door.
3. Position doors open or closed by using vehicle or manpower.
Overhead door can be manually operated by:
1. Gain access to the hangar roof, move to a position above the overhead door.
2. Pull cable to engage chain drive.
3. Pull chain until overhead door is in the fully open position.

                       HANGAR DOOR OPERATIONS CHECKLIST
                               HANGAR 870 DOORS

ALL PERSONNEL WILL BE QUALIFIED/TRAINED PRIOR TO HANGAR DOOR
OPERATION
NOTE: For door malfunctions, contact the building manager and /or MSG/CE Trouble desk at
713-1918

CAUTION: Never use vehicles to open or close hangar doors unless the clutch to the drive
motor is disengaged and overhead tail door is in the Open position

CAUTION: During winter months doors must not remain open longer than necessary to move
aircraft to prevent the alarm system from going off and freezing of water lines.

Pre-Operations Checklist
1. Locate and inspect pinch points and crush point areas for obstructions.
2. Inspect hangar door tracks for debris and both doors and tracks for damage.
3. Ensure area is clear of equipment, aircraft, and personnel both inside and out.
4. Ensure all personnel entry doors are closed and latched.
5. Ensure alcoves are clear of equipment and personnel.
6. Ensure tail door is up/open prior to operation of main doors

Opening Hangar Doors Building 870

CAUTION: Hazardous Noise Area Hearing Protection Required During Operation.

CAUTION: During winter months hangar 870 has a low temperature alarm for water deluge
system. Doors must not remain open longer than necessary to move aircraft to prevent the alarm
system from going off.

1. Overhead tail door controls are on the east wall of hangar 870 push appropriate control
button indentified by arrows
2. Open overhead tail door a minimum one foot prior to opening main horizontal doors
3. Open overhead tail door to full open position, before aircraft movement through door area
4. Open horizontal doors fully before aircraft movement through door area
5. At main horizontal doors center section push appropriate control button indentified by arrows
NOTE: Doors will begin to move approximately 5 seconds after alarm sounds. If main door
alarm does not sound, do not continue and contact MSG/CE
6. To ensure safety of personnel and equipment, all hangar doors must be opened a minimum of
10 feet and brought to a complete stop prior to movement through doors
 8                                                      934AWI21-112 24 SEPTEMBER 2013


7. Open main horizontal doors fully to wing tip clearance line before aircraft movement through
door area

CAUTION: While doors are moving, continually monitor to ensure doors are clear of
obstructions, personnel, equipment, and aircraft

CLOSING HANGAR DOORS BUILDING 870

CAUTION: Hazardous Noise Area Hearing Protection Required During Operation.

1. Close main horizontal doors a minimum of 10 feet and brought to a complete stop prior to
movement through door area
2. At main door center section push appropriate control button indentified by arrows
NOTE: Doors will begin to move approximately 5 seconds after alarm sounds if main door
alarm does not sound, do not continue and contact MSG/CE

CAUTION: While doors are moving, continually monitor to ensure doors are clear of
obstructions, personnel, equipment, and aircraft

3. Overhead tail door controls are on the east wall of hangar 870 push appropriate control
button indentified by arrows
4. Close overhead tail door fully after closing main hangar doors.

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES FOR OPERATING DOORS IN HANGAR 870

In the event of electrical failure, main hangar doors can be operated manually by:
Main door emergency procedures:
1. Open panels located on each center door.
2. Pull clutch disengage handle located on each side of the center door.
3. Position doors open or closed by using vehicle or manpower. (Only in an emergency
situation where tail door chains cannot be reached will the main doors be opened or closed
while the overhead door is closed.)
Overhead tail door can be manually operated by:
1. Gain access to bottom of inside of tail door.
2. Pull cable to engage chain drive.
3. Pull chain until overhead door is in the fully open position. (Door must be open at least one
foot before main doors can be opened.)
