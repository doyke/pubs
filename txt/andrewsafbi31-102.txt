This instruction implements AFPD 31-1, Integrated Defense. It applies to and establishes
procedures for curfew hours on Andrews Air Force Base (AAFB) for children unaccompanied
by a parent, guardian, or any other adult 18 years of age or older authorized by a parent or
guardian. This instruction also applies to all civilian and military personnel on Andrews AFB,
including the Navy, Air Force Reserve and Air National Guard (ANG) units, except where noted
otherwise. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained IAW AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW with the
Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Fm 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.

1. Authority:
   1.1. The installation Commander has the responsibility to protect government and private
   property, as provided for the safety of juveniles and others residing on AAFB.
2. Terms Explained:
   2.1. Juvenile – All persons under the age of 18 years.
   2.2. Parent – Any natural or adoptive parent.
   2.3. Guardian – Any adult person having temporary or permanent control of a juvenile,
   whether or not such person has been appointed by court.

    2.4. Sponsor – Any military member (or adult dependent) who is responsible for juvenile
    dependents and/or guests under the age of 18 on AAFB.
3. Responsibilities:
    3.1. Sponsors will ensure their juvenile dependents and guests know and comply with this
    instruction.
    3.2. Commanders will ensure that military personnel in their units are aware of the
    provisions of this instruction.
4. Objective Rules:
    4.1. The base-wide curfew is in effect for all juveniles who reside on or visit AAFB. The
    times of the curfew are as follows:
       Sunday through Thursday – 2200 to 0500.
       Friday and Saturday – 2400 to 0500.
       Federal Holidays – 2400 to 0500.
    4.2. During curfew hours, juveniles are restricted to their quarters, yard and the area
    surrounding their quarters not to exceed 75 feet.
    4.3. Exceptions:
       4.3.1. Juveniles under the direct supervision of a parent or guardian are exempt from the
       curfew requirements.
       4.3.2. Juveniles en route home from off-base during curfew hours will be allowed to
       proceed directly to their quarters.
       4.3.3. Juveniles attending an organized social function sponsored by a base resident or
       base organization (e.g., youth center, community activity center or school) have 30
       minutes to reach their quarters after they leave the function.
       4.3.4. Base residents and organizations sponsoring events where juveniles will attend
       and which will conclude during curfew hours, will notify the NCOIC of Police Services,
       (301) 981-5079 or 8575 of the event’s location and conclusion time. Make this
       notification at least two duty days before the event. Late notifications made outside
       normal duty periods must go directly to the Security Forces Control Center (SFCC),
       (301) 981-2001 or 2002.
5. Enforcement:
    5.1. Security Forces may stop juveniles who appear to be breaking curfew requirements to
    inquire as to their intended destination. When applicable, Security Forces will require
    juveniles to produce a DD Form 1173, Uniformed Services Identification and Privilege Card.
    Security Forces may transport a juvenile who is in violation of this instruction to the SFCC to
    complete an AF Form 3907, Field Interview Card and/or AAFB Form 7, Field Contact Card.
    Sponsors will be contacted and are expected to respond to the SFCC to accept custody of
    their juvenile dependent or guest. Security Forces may complete any applicable paperwork
    on a repeat violator, and then notify the sponsor to report to the SFCC and take custody of
    the juvenile. Family Advocacy will also be notified on repeat offenders.

  5.2. Sponsors are responsible for the enforcement of these rules over their juvenile
  dependents and guests. In cases where the sponsor refuses to respond to the SFCC or take
  custody of their dependent(s), the Security Forces will notify the sponsor’s first sergeant or
  unit and request active intervention. Should a guest’s sponsor refuse to respond to the SFCC
  or take custody of the guest juvenile, the SFCC will contact the guest juvenile’s parents or
  guardians.
  5.3. For cases involving evidence of alcohol use or intoxication on the part of the juvenile,
  the sponsor’s first sergeant or unit commander will be notified, as well as Family Advocacy.
  At no time will intoxicated juveniles be released on their own recognizance. In cases where
  the guest sponsors cannot be reached, local civilian authorities will be contacted for
  assistance.
