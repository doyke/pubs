This supplement implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 33-106,
Managing High Frequency Radios, Personal Wireless Communication Systems, and the Military
Affiliate Radio System, 9 January 2002. This publication applies to all personnel assigned,
attached, or under contract to the 86th Medical Group (86 MDG). Users of this supplement
should read it in conjunction with AFI 33-106, Managing High Frequency Radios, Personal
Wireless Communication Systems, and the Military Affiliate Radio System. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) available at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rds/rds_series.cfm.

4.8.3.1.1. (Added) The 86th Medical Support Squadron, Systems Flight, Personal Wireless
Communications Systems (PWCS) Custodians are authorized to distribute and fund land mobile
radio (LMR) devices, on a case-by-case basis, per the Technical Solution Determining factors
listed in AFI 33-106, Figure A2.1, Determining a Technical Solution.
4.9.2.1. (Added) The 86th Medical Support Squadron Systems Flight (86 MDSS/SGSI) will be
responsible for the funding the maintenance of all cellular telephone devices and are authorized
to distribute and fund cellular telephone (CT) devices with data capabilities to the following
positions only:

4.9.2.2. (Added) In addition to the positions above, cellular telephones (CT) devices without
data capability will be distributed and funded for the following positions:
4.9.2.2.1. (Added) On-call medical personnel. Squadron Commanders will document their
requirements and submit to MDSS/SGSI.
4.9.2.2.2. (Added) Flight Commanders (reference: Flight Path). Squadron Commanders will
document their requirements and submit to MDSS/SGSI.
4.9.2.3. (Added) If a CT is needed for a position not on the authorized list, the requester must
submit a justification letter (See Attachment 10) signed by their unit commander for 86 MDG
Administrator consideration (approval/disapproval.) In addition, their unit will be responsible
for purchasing the device and service. Note: Alternative communications options may include a
desktop PC, laptop, etc with virtual private Network (VPN) capabilities.
4.9.2.4. (Added) All Personal Digital Assistant (PDA) and CT devices are assigned to the
positions listed above, not the individual. If the member goes on an extended temporary duty
assignment (TDY) or deployment, that device must remain at the 86 MDG and be turned over to
the member filling that position during the interim.
4.9.2.5. (Added) During a TDY outside of Germany, the member should limit CT usage to
emergency or mission critical communications only.
