This instruction implements AFPD 36-28, Awards and Decorations. It establishes a program to
award AFRC Wing First Sergeant Councils for outstanding achievement and contributions to the
Air Force mission. It describes the criteria for the award, establishes eligibility, and explains
nomination, selection, and presentation procedures. This publication does not apply to the Air
National Guard (ANG) or Regular Air Force (RegAF) units. Attachment 1 contains a glossary of
references and support information. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force (AF) Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication, route AF Forms 847 from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm


1. Purpose of the Chief Master Sergeant Carol Smits AFRC First Sergeant Council of the
Year Program. The award is named after the first AFRC Command Chief who wore the first
sergeant diamond in support of our Airmen prior to obtaining this prestigious appointment. The
purpose of this program is to recognize First Sergeant Councils for outstanding performance,
stimulate MAJCOM level competition, increase mission effectiveness, improve morale, and
emphasize a teamwork award program to sustain AFRC’s commitment to excellence.

2. Description. A strong and successful council contributes greatly to the success of the
mission while caring for Airmen and their families. The councils work with host and tenant
CCMs to ensure the needs of the base community and all assigned Airmen are met.
Consideration is based on total performance for the preceding year documented on an AF Form
1206 (Attachment 2). The award program will consist of a traveling trophy, handed off annually
to the winning council, enhancing competition and esprit de corps across the command.
3. Eligibility. Any Air Force Reserve Command First Sergeant Council.
4. Frequency of Award and Period Covered. Annually, 1 January to 31 December.
5. Responsibilities:
     5.1. HQ AFRC/CCC. Final approval authority for Award Winner.
     5.2. HQ AFRC/CCCF. Manages and monitors the program.
     5.3. Emails nomination suspense due date to the field 90 days prior to the suspense date.
     5.4. Acts as board president and solicits board members for the award.
     5.5. Monitors and processes nomination packages to the board.
     5.6. Submits board findings to the AFRC/CCC for final approval.
     5.7. Prepares the staff package for AFRC/CC and CCC for signature on congratulatory
     letters.
     5.8. Tracks “Traveling Trophy” to ensure proper handoff during award ceremony.
     5.9. Processes additions, changes, and deletions to this instruction.
     5.10. NAF/CCC. As the Functional Managers for First Sergeants at the Numbered Air Force
     Level, all nominations must be processed through the corresponding NAF/CCC prior to final
     submission to HQ/AFRC.
     5.11. WG/CCC. Responsible for nomination packages for their first sergeant council,
     utilizing AF Form 1206. Packages will be submitted through the corresponding NAF/CCC to
     the AFRC/CCCF, in the Office of the AFRC Command Chief.
6. Suspense.
     6.1. Due to HQ AFRC/CCCF 01 April.
