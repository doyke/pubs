This supplement implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 91-224,
Air Force Ground Safety Investigation and Reports, 1 August 2004. The AFMAN is published
word-for-word without editorial review. This supplement describes AFGSC's procedures for use
in conjunction with the basic AFMAN. This instruction applies to HQ AFGSC, subordinate
units, and all organizations receiving support from AFGSC assets. This supplement applies to
those Air Force Reserve and/or Air National Guard units participating in Classic Association
with the Active Air Force. Users of this instruction must notify Ground Safety Division (HQ
AFGSC/SEG) of conflicts between this instruction and other directives, instructions, or technical
orders. Forward unit supplements to HQ AFGSC/SEG, 245 Davis Avenue East, Suite 200,
Barksdale AFB, LA 71110.
Ensure that all records created as a result of the processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. The use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial
product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

6.1.3. (Added) Send a Quick Look (QL) report with digital pictures (less than 1MB) to HQ
AFGSC/SEG for all ground Class A and B mishaps and selected on-duty Class C mishaps. This
report will follow no later than 24 hours after the mishap. Do not delay this report while waiting
for outside agency input. See paragraph 6.10. (Added) for further guidance.
6.6.4.1. (Added) Command mishap prevention records will be managed by fiscal year versus
calendar year. Report records will be created, maintained, and disposed of in accordance with the
RDS. Contact servicing records manager as required.
6.8. (Added) Local Mishap Reporting. AFGSC Mishap Data Worksheet, may be used to
document a mishap to the Wing Safety office (Ref: AFI91-204_AFGSCSUP, Attachment 7).
AFGSC host/base Ground Safety Managers (GSM) will review the mishap data to determine
reportability of lost-time injuries and logging of certain on-duty mishaps.
6.9. (Added) Class A Off-Duty Fatality Mishap Video Teleconference (VTC). The squadron
commander experiencing the loss will present the ground fatality investigation results via VTC
to the HQ AFGSC Vice Commander (HQ AFGSC/CV). The briefing will focus on causes,
recommendations, and lessons learned to prevent similar mishaps.
6.9.1. (Added) HQ AFGSC/SEG will provide a fatality briefing template to base/wing safety
offices. The safety office will ensure the commander experiencing the loss is provided the
briefing template.
6.9.2. (Added) The squadron commander will have the briefing approved by their wing
commander prior to briefing HQ AFGSC/CV. Briefing will be provided to HQ AFGSC/SEG two
weeks prior to VTC.
6.9.3. (Added) Class A Fatality VTC briefings will be scheduled by HQ AFGSC/SE on an as
needed basis.
6.9.4. (Added) AFGSC units are encouraged to have commanders, supervisors, junior officers
and first-term Airmen attend the VTCs.
6.10. (Added) Quick Look (QL) Reports.
6.10.1. (Added) Ground Safety Offices will submit a QL report on all (Ground) Class A or B
mishaps, and on any requested Class C mishap. Also, submit QL reports for any mishap
involving a GMV rollover regardless of class or when specifically requested by HQ AFGSC/SE
or NAF/SE. Vehicles designated as gravel road trainers are exempt from this requirement when
performing their training mission. Forward QL reports through the appropriate NAF/SE to HQ
AFGSC/SEG. QL reports are designated “For Official Use Only” and must be appropriately
marked. Do not include privileged information in QL reports. The QL report template is found in
Attachment 2.
