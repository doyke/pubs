This operating instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-1, General Civilian
Personnel Provisions and Authorities, Public Law 101-510, Title XII, Defense Acquisition
Workforce Improvement Act, Title 10, Chapter 87, section 1701-1764 of the United States Code,
DoDD 5000.1, The Defense Acquisition System, DoDI 5000.2, Operation of the Defense
Acquisition System, DoDD 5000.52, Defense Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics Workforce
Education, Training, and Career Development Program, DoDI 5000.66, Operation of the
Defense Acquisition, Technology and Logistics Workforce Education, Training, and Career
Development Program, and DoD AT&L, Workforce Desk Guide, Federal Register Notice,
November 27, 1996, Volume 61, Number 230, page 60400, and AFRL Manual 36-104,
Laboratory Personnel Demonstration Project. This instruction establishes policy for the AFRL
Laboratory Personnel Demonstration Project broadband moves for employees on acquisition
coded positions. It applies to all AFRL organizations. This publication may not be
supplemented. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847 through the appropriate functional chain of command. Requests
for waivers must be process through command channels to the publication OPR for
consideration. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS).

1. Overview: To be eligible for a broadband move, candidates must be qualified for their
current position, which includes Position Certification, Continuous Learning Points (CLPs), and
 2                                                         AFRLI36-108 14 NOVEMBER 2013


if applicable, Acquisition Corps Membership. Candidates who do not meet requirements or have
an approved waiver on file by 31 October will be ineligible to broadband move.
     1.1. Position Certification: Candidates must meet all current Acquisition Professional
     Development Program (APDP) certification requirements and must be certified or have
     applied for their certification. All candidates must be certified or be on an approved waiver
     (if they exceed the 24 month grace period) by 31 October. If candidates are within the 24
     month grace period and not certified or on a waiver, their record will be reviewed to ensure
     work toward certification requirements is being accomplished. If no progress is shown,
     candidate will be ineligible to broadband move. APDP requirements can be found in the
     Defense Acquisition University catalog: http://icatalog.dau.mil/.
     1.2. Continuous Learning Points: Each candidate’s record must indicate the individual is
     current and on track. To be considered on track, an employee must have 10 CLP’s for each
     quarter that they are in their 24 month requirement (8 quarters x 10 points = 80 CLP’s).
     Candidates must achieve 80 CLPs every 2 years, with a goal to gain 40 points each year. If
     past the Continuous Learning (CL) suspense date without the required 80 CLPs, then
     candidates must gain 80 CLPs by 31 October in order to be considered for a broadband
     move.                           For                       CL                         info:
     https://www.atrrs.army.mil/channels/acqnowcl/default.asp?page=main.asp
     1.3. Acquisition Corps Membership: Required when candidates will be broadband moving to
     a Critical Acquisition Position (CAP) or are currently on a CAP. Candidates must meet all
     Acquisition Corps requirements or have submitted a waiver to AFRL/DPDW by 31 October
     and must attain Acquisition Corps membership at the time of the broadband move.
     Candidates currently assigned to a CAP position must already be an Acquisition Corps
     Member or on a waiver, otherwise will be ineligible to broadband move. Acquisition Corps
     membership requirements and information can be found at this link:
     https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
     af/USAF/ep/browse.do?programId=1426270&channelPageId=-
     2061051&parentCategoryId=-2061067
2. Tenure Requirement: Candidates who broadband move to a CAP are required to remain on
the position for three years and must sign a tenure agreement (DD Form 2888). The Senior
Personnel Advisor is responsible for initiating the DD Form 2888 and providing it to the
servicing Civilian Personnel Office staffing specialist for the individual’s signature and filing in
the Official Personnel File.
