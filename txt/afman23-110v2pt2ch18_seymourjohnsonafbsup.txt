AFMAN 23-110, Volume 2, Part 2, Chapter 18, is supplemented as follows: This supplement
implements procedures and guidance in AFMAN 23-110, Volume 2, Part 2, Chapter 18,
Document Control. This supplement establishes additional requirements not otherwise specified
in this publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Contact supporting records managers as
required. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF IMT 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF IMT 847s from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command to 4 LRS/LGLOC, 1280 Humphreys St., Seymour Johnson AFB, NC 27531.

18.15.6.Document Control will ensure all documents are cleared and update the 1AQ and 1DQ

18.15.6.4.Document Control will ensure that all documents in their possession are lined off the
delinquent listing. This will be accomplished prior to the delinquent document listing being
emailed to the delinquent document monitors. Flight chiefs will appoint a delinquent document
monitor in writing to Document Control. Document Control will be notified in writing, of any
changes as they occur. The Document Control supervisor will provide individual or group
training when requested. The delinquent document monitor(s) or alternate(s) will receive the
delinquent document listing with the exception of the maintenance functional areas. The cut-off
time for the turn-in of delinquent documents will be 1500 1400 hrs Monday - Friday, unless
otherwise specified by the Document Control supervisor.

18.15.7.2.2.Warehouse personnel will request the special inventory.

18.15.7.3.When replacement documents are created, the certificate of lost document will be
stamped or typed on the Document Control copy and signed by the applicable OIC, Flight Chief/
Superintendent. Document Control will ensure certification is accomplished and the statement
of thorough search has been made is on the face of the source document before accepting the
source document from the originator. A replacement document is defined as any handscribed,
typed or DUPER copy.

18.21.2.2.The initiating activity organizational commander will review and annotate required
changes to the authorization receipt listing (ARL) every June and December. Unit security
managers are responsible for reviewing the ARL listing. The listing will be returned to the
Document Control Section (4 LRS/LGLOC). The Document Control supervisor will provide
Customer Support Section a copy for validation of SJ Form 2, Delegate/Delete Property
Custodian. Customer Support will ensure SJ Form 2s authorizing receipt for classified property
are forwarded to Document Control for addition or deletion from the ARL listing. Document
Control will forward all ARL updates to Customer Support Section for validation of SATS
database.

18.21.3.All letters identifying personnel in the Logistics Readiness Squadron authorized to
receipt for classified property will be initiated at flight level, addressed to 4 LRS/LGLOC. The
security manager will review all letters, and will indicate by signing a review statement in the
text of the letter (i.e. paragraph 2. I certify that a check was accomplished using the ASCAS
roster, and all personnel on this letter have required clearances to receipt for COS classified
assets, Security Managers name, Unit Security Manager). Upon review by the security manager,
forward to commander for signature.

18.30.3.2.When personnel who are responsible for clearing delinquents are TDY, LGL Flight
Chief/Superintendent will approve entry of “TDY” in the DCR record.

18.30.3.4.Upon determination by Document Control (through research) that a delinquent source
document (DSD) has not been posted, the appropriate section will prepare and process the
transaction. However, the responsible activity will confirm that the DSD is valid prior to
preparing and processing the document. DSD images will be prepared and placed in the
delinquent document file by Document Control personnel no later than 3 workdays from the "as
of date" of ISU/DOR or other TRIC action.

18.30.3.7.The applicable warehouse will process their own reverse post (RVP). IAW AFMAN
23-110, Vol 2, Part 2, Chapter 16, Reverse-Post Procedures, para 16.4.1 SJSUP.

daily to be worked.
