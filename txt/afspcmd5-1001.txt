(AFPD) 10-1, Mission Directives, Air Force Instruction (AFI) 10-101, Format and Content of
Mission Directives, AFI 38-101, Air Force Organization. Upon mobilization, this mission
directive also applies to AFSPC-gained Air National Guard (ANG) units and members under
USC Title 10 status. In addition, it applies to all AFSPC-gained Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC) units and members.           This publication may not be supplemented or further

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

The new Air Force Forces command relationship changes have been included. The mission
statement, command, and responsibilities sections have also been updated. Lastly, an
abbreviations and acronymns section has been included.
1. Mission. As the Air Force's Numbered Air Force for space, the operational mission of the
14th Air Force includes space launch from the east and west coasts, satellite command and
control, missile warning, space surveillance and command and control of assigned forces. The
overall mission is to control and exploit space for global and theater operations, thereby ensuring

warfighters are supported by the best space capabilities available. The 14th Air Force consists of
two launch wings (the 30th Space Wing at Vandenberg AFB, California and the 45th Space
Wing at Patrick AFB, Florida); a space control and missile warning wing (the 21st Space Wing
at Peterson AFB, Colorado); a satellite command and control wing (the 50th Space Wing at
Schriever AFB, Colorado) and a missile warning wing (the 460th Space Wing at Buckley AFB,
Colorado).
2. Command. The Commander, 14 AF:
     2.1. Provides, plans for and employs Air Force space forces as the USAF component to Joint
     Functional Component Command Space (JFCC SPACE).
     2.2. Is assigned as one of the Component Numbered Air Force (C-NAF) commanders to
     United States Strategic Command (USSTRATCOM).
3. Responsibilities. The Commander, 14 AF:
     3.1. Plans, tasks, directs and synchronizes space operations to support global and theater
     missions in support of Commander (CDR) JFCC SPACE military objectives.
     3.2. Provides surveillance, tracking and intelligence or orbiting artificial objects, as well as
     space environment assessments.
     3.3. Provides space control capability, including offensive and defensive counterspace
     operations, and expertise in support of joint operations globally on behalf of AFSPC/CC.
     3.4. Provides global and theater ballistic missile warning, missile defense, nuclear detonation
     detection, Space Situational Awareness (SSA) and space object tracking capabilities to the
     United States and allied nations.
     3.5. Provides satellite weather, communications, navigation, and space warning capabilities
     and operates a global network of satellite control centers and satellite tracking stations
     supporting DoD and civil users.
     3.6. Provides assured access to space and conducts launch operations to support military,
     civil and commercial users. Additionally, operates ranges to develop, test and evaluate
     space, air and missile systems.
     3.7. Ensures effective employment of Air Force space forces worldwide under the direction
     of AFSPC/CC.
     3.8. Manages and coordinates with other commands involved with space operations to
     enable interoperability of space forces on behalf of AFSPC/CC.
     3.9. Ensures AFSPC/CC’s gained AFRC and ANG space units are interoperable and
     operationally ready.
     3.10. Directs day-to-day operations of the 614th AOC in support of CDR JFCC SPACE and
     CDR AFSPC.
