This publication establishes responsibilities, procedures and requirements for the
accomplishment of civil engineering self-help work which meets the criteria established in Air
Force Pamphlet (AFPAM) 32-1098, Base Civil Engineer Self-Help Guide, and AFI 32-6001,
Family Housing Management. All base units and family housing (FH) occupants will comply
with this guidance to expedite self-help work and requests. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Contact supporting records
managers as required. Refer recommended changes and questions regarding this publication to
the office of primary responsibility using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication, route AF Forms 847 through the base publications and forms manager.

1. Responsibilities:
   1.1. Requester. The organization or individual identified as being responsible for the
   accomplishment and quality workmanship of the finished product in the self-help work
   request.

       1.1.1. Requester will prepare an Air Force (AF) Form 332, Base Civil Engineer Work
       Request, for self-help projects. The AF Form 332 is electronic and is available on the Air
       Force e-Publications and Forms web site. The Self-Help Store (also referred to as the
       base U-Fix-It Service Store) is located in building 1208, and is open from 0730-1630 on
       Monday through Friday and closed weekends and holidays.
    1.2. Base Facility Occupants will obtain approval from the facility manager, the 366th
    Civil Engineer Squadron, Fire Emergency & Services Flight (366 CES/CEF), 366
    CES/CEAN (Environmental Element), the 366th Aerospace Medicine Squadron,
    Bioenvironmental Engineering Flight (366 AMDS/SGPB), the 366th Fighter Wing Ground
    Safety (366 FW/SEG), in block 14, and the organizational commander’s signature in block
    13 of the AF Form 332.
    1.3. Family Housing Occupants will obtain approval from 366 CES/CEACH (Family
    Housing Management Office) in blocks 14 and 28 of the AF Form 332. The occupant signs
    block 13 of the AF Form 332. Family housing occupant will:
       1.3.1. Establish and coordinate estimated start and finish dates for work.
       1.3.2. Obtain approval from CES/CEACH before starting work.
       1.3.3. Accompany 366 CES/CEACH on all in-progress and final inspections.
       1.3.4. Account for all materials and tools issued and return tools and unused materials
       after completing job.
       1.3.5. Follow proper safety precautions.
    1.4. 366 CES/CC (Commander) will support self-help requests to the maximum extent
    possible, consistent with the priority of the work programmed. 366 CES will encourage self-
    help requests requiring material, but no direct labor support. Requests requiring 366 CES
    direct labor support will be considered for approval based on the scope of the project, amount
    of support required, availability of material and BCE manpower, and the priority of work
    backlogged in the affected 366 CES shops. 366 CES will support the Self-Help Program as
    follows:
    1.5. 366 CES/CEO (Operations Flight Commander) will oversee management of the Self-
    Help Store.
    1.6. 366 CES/CEOSM/2 (Self-Help Planner) will:
       1.6.1. Brief requester on their duties and responsibilities in accomplishing self-help
       projects.
       1.6.2. Plan and estimate self-help work orders and provide planning service on an "as
       needed" basis.
       1.6.3. Determine if detailed planning is required.
       1.6.4. Exercise approval authority within delegated approval limits.
       1.6.5. Forward requests requiring higher approval authority to 366 CES/CEO for
       approval.
       1.6.6. Provide sample materials, if available, and display "how to" pamphlets and films.

       1.6.7. Identify material requirements and prepare associated acquisition documentation.
       (Maintain tools for loans and control issues and turn-ins).
       1.6.8. Red Stake will process Work Clearance Request if required for excavation, 828-
       3293.
       1.6.9. Receive requests and ensure they are administratively correct by making any
       minor corrections on AF Form 332.
       1.6.10. Ensure requester signs the AF Form 332, if required.
       1.6.11. Serve as the point of contact (POC) between self-help customers and 366 CES
       for all phases of the project.
       1.6.12. Coordinate work with in-service work programmer, scheduler and requester
       when CES shop support is required, and when dedicated shop personnel are not available.
       1.6.13. Visit the job site for "in progress" inspections and to check for materials
       containing asbestos.
       1.6.14. Ensure internal procedures comply with MHAFBPAM 32-1098.
       1.6.15. Ensure proper accounting of labor and materials for each proper work order.
       1.6.16. Maintain visual charts on uncompleted work.
       1.6.17. Conduct final inspection upon completion of work.
       1.6.18. Remind requester to return unused materials.
       1.6.19. Supply the real property section with completed work order, drawings of each
       self-help project within 30 work days of completion.
    1.7. 366 CES/CEOSM (Material Acquisition) will:
       1.7.1. Research and use residual materials whenever possible.
       1.7.2. Maintain records of the status of ordered materials and materials on hand.
       1.7.3. Notify Self-Help Manager when materials are complete and ready for pick-up.
       1.7.4. Receive and warehouse materials.
       1.7.5. Ensure and account for transfer materials.
    1.8. 366 CES/CEF will:
       1.8.1. Ensure requests for construction, removal or addition include a detailed layout
       sketch or drawing.
       1.8.2. Ensure doors, walls and areas requiring specific fire ratings meet the requirements
       of Life Safety Code 101.
       1.8.3. Ensure the construction of or removal of doors does not interfere with exit
       requirements based on Life Safety Code 101.
       1.8.4. Ensure the construction or demolition does not interfere with fire suppression or
       detection systems. Requester must coordinate with 366 CES/CEF for the removal of or
       work in the general location of fire suppression or detection systems.

        1.8.5. Ensure electrical work is accomplished by 366 CES or contractors and meets the

        1.8.6. Physically evaluate the requests for and areas which are potentially hazardous, or
        which may present extreme life safety hazards, or ensure that the authority having

2. Procedures:
     2.1. Self-Help Requests:
        2.1.1. Submit requests for self-help work on an AF Form 332. Requester must provide
        sketches, site plans, a detailed description of work required and justification. Requester
        must complete the coordination steps specified in paragraphs 1.1-1.3, and obtain
        appropriate approvals prior to submitting the AF Form 332 to Customer Service.
        2.1.2. Only if a requester has the experience, they can perform their own minor repairs
        (e.g., painting, landscaping, etc.) to upkeep their facility.
        2.1.3. Modifications to existing plumbing, electrical wiring, heating and air conditioning
        are not authorized via self-help.
     2.2. Review/Approval/Processing. Upon receipt of an AF Form 332, the Self-Help
     Manager will review the request for administrative errors and make corrections on-the-spot,
     if possible.
        2.2.1. 366 CES/CEOS (Operations Support) will:
            2.2.1.1. Check for duplications of requests.
            2.2.1.2. Determine the ability of the requester to perform the requested work.
            2.2.1.3. Determine the shop support required.
            2.2.1.4. Provide detailed planning.
            2.2.1.5. Provide requester with a work order number.
            2.2.1.6. Send the request to the planning and maintenance technicians/inspectors .
               2.2.1.6.1. 366 CES/CEOSM/2 will visit the job site to review the work plan with
               the requester, determine the impact of the project on the environmental
               maintenance control system, air conditioning and heating systems, fire protection
               systems, etc.. They will also list the materials needed from BCE, estimate the
               man-hours for shop support, inspect for asbestos and any related hazards.
               2.2.1.6.2. If the request requires no 366 CES support (requests for materials only,
               is small in scope, and requires no detailed planning), then the Self-Help Manager
               may approve or disapprove the request without delay.
               2.2.1.6.3. 366 CES/CEO will approve requests when support is required from one
               or more shops.
               2.2.1.6.4. Once the requested materials are delivered to 366 CES/CEOSM
               (Material Acquisition), 366 CES/CEOSM/2 will notify requester to pick up the
               materials and commence work. At the time the materials are picked up, 366
               CES/CEOSM/2 will brief requester on:

                   2.2.1.6.4.1. The nature and scope of approved work.
                   2.2.1.6.4.2. Normal safety precautions.
                   2.2.1.6.4.3. Asbestos hazards and precautions.
                   2.2.1.6.4.4. 366 CES material and shop technician support.
                   2.2.1.6.4.5. In-progress and final inspection procedures.
                   2.2.1.6.4.6. Estimated work start and completion dates and procedures.

3. Coordination and Control:
     3.1. The Self- Help Manager will instruct customers that the Self-Help Manager is the POC
     within 366 CES for all phases of the project.
     3.2. If an organization or occupant accomplishes unauthorized, excessive, unsatisfactory or
     incomplete work, they will return the facility to its original condition. 366 CES/CC, through
     the 366th Mission Support Group Commander (366 MSG/CC), may send a facility abuse
     letter.
