This instruction assigns responsibilities and establishes procedures for control of visitors and
photography in the flightline areas. It applies to all units attached or assigned to Luke AFB. It
implements AFI 35-101, Public Affairs Policies and Procedures. Ensure that all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/rds_series.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication has been revised for minor formatting updates, OPR change, publication date
update and releasability statement change.

1. Procedures:

1.1. The following action will be taken to support and control visitors and photography and
videography on the flightline.

1.1.1. Public Affairs (PA) will coordinate and escort all news media representatives visits to
base and flightline areas. Groups requesting tours should be referred to 56 FW/PA or
944th Fighter Wing Public Affairs (944 FW/PA) for scheduling of a tour.

1.1.2. Personnel desiring to take individual tours and photographs in or of flightline areas will
coordinate their request with the agency having control of the desired area and aircraft. In the
event the controlling agency is not on duty, visitor activities will be coordinated through
56th Maintenance Operations Squadron/Maintenance Operations Center (56 MOS/MXOOM), or

944 FW/PA. If the 56 MOS is closed, all visitor activity will be coordinated through the
Security Forces desk.

1.1.3. For purposes of this instruction, areas of responsibility are designated as follows:

1.1.3.1. The 56th Fighter Wing area is any facility or flightline area utilized, assigned to, or
controlled by the 56 FW or subordinate units.

1.1.3.2. The 944th Fighter Wing area is any facility or flightline area utilized, assigned to, or
controlled by 944 FW.

1.1.4. Visitor activities will be coordinated through 56 MOS/MXOOM, ext 6-5469 or
944 FW/PA, ext 6-5388.

1.1.5. The requesting agency will provide escort service when required. Civilians will have a
military sponsor or escort for restricted flightline areas. Visitations and photographs are limited
to approved areas. Security Forces will be notified through 56 MOS or 944 FW/PA of what and
when areas will be visited.

1.1.5.1. All escorts operating vehicles on the flightline must possess AF Form 483, Certificate of
Competency.

1.1.6. Personnel will be prepared to show a valid AF Form 1199A (Restricted Area Badge) and
military identification to the Security Forces.

1.1.7. Content of the attached Public Affairs Briefing for Visitors and Photography on the
Flightline will be briefed to the visitor by the Public Affairs Office, 56 MOS, or the escort.

1.2. 56th Communications Squadron personnel perform official photography duties will
coordinate their requirements with designated OPRs.

                                           Attachment 1

                                    Public Affairs Briefing for
                           Visitors and Photography on the Flightline

This briefing implements the necessary security and safety procedures for control of visitors and
photography on the flightline areas as required by Luke AFB Instruction 35-101. A copy of this
briefing will be available for all LAFB units, and will also be provided to escorts and visitors as
applicable by the 56th Maintenance Operations Squadron, 56 FW Public Affairs Office or
944 FW Public Affairs Office.

Photography or Videography

1. Filming or photographing aircraft on the runway, taxi-ways or aircraft parking ramp must be
coordinated with the Maintenance Operations Center.

2. Visitors must either have a line badge or an escort in all restricted areas.

3. Photography of open panels on the aircraft and inside the cockpit is prohibited.

4. In the event of an emergency or aircraft accident involving the exposure of classified
information, any exposed film may be confiscated to check for inadvertent classified exposures.

Safety

1. Visitors will remain with their escort at all times.

2. Visitors will access the parking areas through approved or posted entry control points only.

3. Visitors will not smoke around the aircraft or maintenance areas.

4. Visitors will not discard trash or drop objects on the parking areas, and will be aware of
potential for Foreign Object Damage.

5. Visitors will remove and secure hats on the parking ramp and near taxi-ways and runways.

6. Visitors must remain 30 feet from active taxi-ways and runways at all times to ensure their
safety.

7. Visitors will wear appropriate hearing protection while in high-noise areas. Escort will
provide hearing protection, as necessary.

(Visitors are considered personnel who are non-military, or who are military but without a Luke
AFB line badge, or badged personnel who are unfamiliar with LAFB flightline operations.)
