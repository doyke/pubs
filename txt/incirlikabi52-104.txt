This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 52-1, Chaplain Service, it
supports policy and guidance of Department of Defense Instruction (DODI) 1402.5, Criminal
History Background Checks on Individauls in Child Care Services, and Air Force Instruction
(AFI) 31-501, USAFE Supplement, Personnel Security Program Management. This instruction
provides guidance and procedure for implementation of Child Care National Agency Check and
Inquires (CNACI) and Installation Records Check (IRC) to all contractors and volunteers, and
CNACI to Local Nationals, involved in chapel-sponsored programs that require interaction with
children under the age of 18. This instruction applies to all 39 Air Base Wing personnel. This
publication does not apply to the Air National Guard (ANG) and the Air Force Reserve
Command (AFRC) and their units. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to 39 ABW/HC, Unit 7065, Box 35, APO AE 09824, using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management
System(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS). is publication requires the collection
and or maintenance of information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by [set forth
the legal authority such as the federal statute, executive order, or regulation (i.e. 10 U.S.C. 8013
and E.O. 9397)]. The applicable Privacy Act SORN(s) [number and title] is available at

1. Overview.
     1.1. To provide procedures for the safeguarding of children and youth participating in the
     chapel program and/or chapel events/functions.
2. Roles and Responsibilities.
     2.1. The Wing Chaplain shall:
        2.1.1. Develop procedures to ensure compliance with the requirements of DoDI 1402.5.
        2.1.2. Provide oversight of process and procedures to conduct criminal history
        background checks.
        2.1.3. Facilitate cooperation between specific organizations around Incirlik Air Base and
        the Base Chapel to include: Family Housing, Mental Health, Family Advocacy, and
        Security Forces. Note: Coordination with these base agencies are the minimum
        requirement for the Incirlik Chapel Background Check Process.
        2.1.4. Reserves the right to deny any applicant the right to work with children if the
        situation warrants. Denials can be made on any background check that has favorable or
        unfavorable findings if the Wing Chaplain determines upon further investigation the
        individual is not suited for the requested position.
     2.2. The Senior Faith Group Chaplains shall:
        2.2.1. Ensure every Lay Leader, Contractor, and Volunteer within his or her program has
        a background check initiated prior to working with children.
        2.2.2. Works with the Superintendent/Noncommisioned Officer in Charge (NCOIC),
        Chapel Operations to monitor the background check process of each applicant/volunteer
        to ensure timely and accurate records.
     2.3. The Superintendent/NCOIC, Chapel Operations shall:
        2.3.1. Provide technical support and resources as required.
        2.3.2. Oversee coordination between Incirlik Base Chapel and specific organizations
        around Incirlik Air Base to include: Family Housing, Mental Health, Family Advocacy,
        and Security Forces.
        2.3.3. Ensure the records of volunteers, all individuals hired, certified, and/or employed
        by Incirlik Chapel Programs are maintained in the chapel file plan.
     2.4. The Faith Group Religious Education and Community Coordinators shall:
        2.4.1. Initiate the Background Check process by issuing each volunteer a 39 ABW
        Installstion Records Check.
        2.4.2. Monitor each volunteer and advises the Wing Chaplain of suspicious actions made
        by a volunteer.
     2.5. Government Contractors, Chapel Tithes and Offering (CTOF) Contractors, Temporary
     Hires and Volunteers shall:
        2.5.1. Sign a background check requirement and authorization memorandum, signed by
        the Wing Chaplain to initiate the background check process. The individual must supply
INCIRLIKABI52-104 25 APRIL 2014                                                                 3


      the following information: Name (to include any alias), Social Security Number and
      Signature.
      2.5.2. All individuals who wish to work with children must answer the following
      questions on the Background Check Requirement and Authorization Memorandum:
          2.5.2.1. Have you ever been arrested for or charged with a crime involving a child?
          2.5.2.2. Have you ever been asked to resign because of or been decertified for a
          sexual offense?
          2.5.2.3. If the above questions are answered in the positive, the individual must
          provide a description of the case disposition.
      2.5.3. Volunteers must complete an Installation Records Check (IRC).
          2.5.3.1. If the volunteer is a Local National then the individual(s) must coordinate a
          National Agency Check Local National (NACLN) with the chapel’s security
          manager.
      2.5.4. Civilians paid with appropriated or CTOF funds must have an IRC and the Child
      Care National Agency Check and Inquires (CNACI).
      2.5.5. Military members with a valid security clearance assigned to the chapel must have
      a favorable IRC and have obtained a favorable personnel security investigation. An IRC
      is required for all Chaplain Corps staff members.
3. Routing Procedures
   3.1. Applicability
      3.1.1. Any member desiring to work with children in the Incirlik Chapel Community will
      sign the 39 ABW/HC Installation Records Check for Chapel Volunteers
   3.2. Process
      3.2.1. The completed/signed 39 ABW/HC Installation Records Check for Chapel
      Volunteers will be forwarded to the following agencies:
      3.2.2. Security Forces: Security Forces will complete an IRC for all volunteers and
      contractors. For contractors, a Child Care National Agency Check and Inquires
      (CNACI), Local Files Check, Criminal Justice Information Network Check (CJIN), State
      Criminal History Repository (SCHR), and finger prints will also be completed.
      3.2.3. Mental Health: Mental Health will verify the member’s Mental Health Records.
      3.2.4. Family Advocacy: Family Advocacy will check to ensure the member does not
      have derogatory information on record.
      3.2.5. Drug and Alcohol Program: Drug and Alcohol Program will check to ensure the
      member does not have any information on record.
      3.2.6. Family Housing: Family Housing will check the members records to ensure the
      member does not have any derogatory information on record.
   3.3. Disqualifications
4                                                       INCIRLIKABI52-104 25 APRIL 2014


       3.3.1. The Wing Chaplain will analyze the findings and make a determination of
       acceptance based on the results of the background check.
       3.3.2. Failure to answer questions 2.5.2.1. and 2.5.2.2. above will automatically
       disqualify an individual and make them ineligible to work in any position that allows
       access to children. In addition, the individual will not have the option to work under
       supervision.
       3.3.3. Any negative IRC and/or CNACI will result in immediate termination of volunteer
       and/or contractual service.
    3.4. Statements of Works
       3.4.1. Will have the following information for Contractors working with children under
       18.
       3.4.2. Notification that a background check is required for the position.
           3.4.2.1. Details the type of investigation(s) needed to qualify for the position,
           outlines the routing process and identifies what paperwork/forms are required to
           complete the background check.
           3.4.2.2. Any Negative IRC will result in immediate termination of volunteer and/or
           contractual service. This statement must be included in all contracts and SOWs.
           3.4.2.3. Any negative CNACI will result in immediate termination of volunteer
           and/or contractual service. This statmement must be included in all contracts and
           SOWs.
    3.5. Re-certification/Records Review
       3.5.1. A local background check will be accomplished every 5 years for individuals
       under contract with the Incirlik Base Chapel and that works with children under the age
       of 18. However, the Wing Chaplain has the right to request an investigation at any time.
       3.5.2. All employees, volunteers, temporary employees, summer hires, etc., should have
       their Installation Record’s Check reviewed annually, but not more than 36 months from
       the previous completion date.
