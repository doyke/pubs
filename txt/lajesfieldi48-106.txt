This publication is a companion document to Air Force Instruction (AFI) 48-101, Aerospace
Medicine Enterprise, ,AFI 36-2905, Fitness Program, AFI 44-102, Medical Care Management
and AFI 48-123, Medical Examinations and Standards. It provides guidance for managing
pregnant employees at Lajes Field, Azores, Portugal and outlines responsibilities for protecting
the mother and fetus from workplace hazards. Members of other services stationed at Lajes
Field, Azores, Portugal (e.g. Army and Navy) must comply with this instruction, with the
exceptions of paragraph 2.6. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication
to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the Lajes Field
publications/forms manager. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in
this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. This instruction requires colleting and
maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974, authorized by 10 U.S.C. 8013 and
E.O. 9397.

1. Responsibilities:
     1.1. 65th Air Base Wing Commander will:
        1.1.1. Ensure a written Fetal Protection Program is established to eliminate or minimize
        potential exposure of pregnant employees and their fetuses to chemical, biological,
        radiological and physical hazards in the workplace.
        1.1.2. Ensure the active duty military member’s assignment is immediately curtailed if
        local medical personnel are not capable of managing the complications of pregnancy.
     1.2. 65th Medical Group Commander will:
        1.2.1. Implement this instruction and ensure compliance of personnel under his/her
        authority.
     1.3. Women’s Health Nurse Practitioner (WHNP) will:
        1.3.1. Verify pregnancy of all active duty members and, when requested, for United
        States civilian employees.
     1.3.2. Make a duty recommendation (via Force Health Management section of Public
     Health) to the employee and her supervisor.
     1.4. Bioenvironmental Engineering (BE) will:
        1.4.1. Evaluate workplace risks in conjunction with Public Health and a Flight Surgeon.
        1.4.2. Perform a process-based evaluation to identify specific hazards applicable to
        pregnancy evaluations such as, but not limited to, chemical, ionizing radiation, hazardous
        noise, non-ionizing radiation, heat stress and ergonomic hazards. Inform provider of
        identified hazards in the workplace.
        1.4.3. Advise the employee of any identified risks and report the risks with any
        recommended techniques for avoiding the risks to the patient and supervisor.
     1.5. Public Health (PH) Element will:
        1.5.1. Interview all identified pregnant active duty members and civilian personnel who
        are enrolled in TRICARE Europe and work on base. Consult with BE and WHNP on
        potential occupational and environmental health threats.
        1.5.2. Identify pregnant workers' specific duties, workplace hazards and concerns
        through conversations with patient, patient’s supervisor and/or workplace site visit.
        1.5.3. Provide educational materials on general hazards to pregnant women and their
        fetuses and information on any specific hazards that exist in the involved workplace.
        1.5.4. Provide information on the wear of Chemical Warfare Defense Ensemble
        (CWDE) during pregnancy.
        1.5.5. Prepare an Air Force Form 469, Duty Limiting Conditions Report, specific to the
        individual's workplace, duties, and exposures.
     1.6. Unit Commander will:

      1.6.1. Ensure female workers understand the necessity of reporting suspected and/or
      confirmed pregnancy to their supervisor immediately, confirming pregnancy at the
      earliest possible time, and notifying supervisor upon confirmation
      1.6.2. Ensure active duty employees make an immediate appointment at the health care
      facility and permit civilian employees to see their health care provider immediately to
      confirm a suspected pregnancy.
      1.6.3. Not remove employees from normal duties without a medical recommendation in
      the form of an AF Form 469.
      1.6.4. Provide PH a description of the pregnant worker's specific duties and
      environmental work conditions.
      1.6.5. Refer Air Force employed civilians with confirmed pregnancy to PH.
   1.7. Active Duty Military will:
      1.7.1. Immediately notify supervisor of suspected pregnancy.
   1.7.2. Immediately seek a medical appointment for confirmation of suspected pregnancy.
   1.7.3. Immediately notify supervisor of confirmed pregnancy.
      1.7.4. Provide PH a complete description of specific duties and work conditions.
      1.7.5. Comply with medical recommendations noted on the completed AF Form 469.
      1.7.6. Comply with medical instructions regarding prenatal visits and care.
   1.7.7. Notify PH of any change in pregnancy status.
2. Civilian Employees, Dependents, Beneficiaries should:
   2.1. Immediately seek a medical appointment for confirmation of suspected pregnancy and
   follow conditions as stated in section 1.7.
