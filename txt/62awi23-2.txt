This instruction establishes procedures for the use of the Quick Reference Listing (QRL). This instruction
applies to all 62d Air Wing and 446th Air Wing personnel. This instruction does not apply to Western
Area Defense Sector (WADS) or Air National Guard (ANG) units. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-123 (will
convert to AFMAN 33-363), Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/. Refer recommended changes
and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF IMT 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF IMT 847s from the field through the appropriate
functional’s chain of command.


1. Daily Operations. The QRL will be used by the 62d Aircraft Maintenance Squadron (62 AMXS), 62d
Logistics Readiness Squadron (62 LRS) Aircraft Parts Store, and by 62 AMXS supervision to request
commonly used aircraft assets. Use of the QRL number will alleviate the need for researching and provid-
ing a part number, Technical Order (TO) figure, index, noun, etc.

2. Aircraft Parts Store Responsibilities:
   2.1. The QRL will be maintained by the Aircraft Parts Store.
   2.2. When a written request for items to be added to the QRL is submitted, a QRL number will be
   assigned based on the part number and work unit code and loaded in the G081 Core Automated Main-
   tenance System for Mobility database.
   2.3. Aircraft Parts Store personnel will print and distribute 10copies to 62 AMXS/MXA Maintenance
   Supervision, who will review the list and send five copies to AMXS SILVER 10 and AMXS BLUE
   10. Two copies will be kept in the Aircraft Parts Store.

    2.4. Semi-annually the QRL will be validated. A cover letter and three copies of the QRL will be sent
    to 62 AMXS supervision. When the validation is completed, the listing will be updated with changes,
    additions and/or deletions.
    2.5. The updated QRL will be printed and distributed within 10 working days of receiving recom-
    mended changes.

3. Aircraft Maintenance Squadron Responsibilities:
    3.1. Maintain a current copy of the QRL and utilize the listing whenever possible.
    3.2. Provide in writing, all data required to load/update the QRL and the G081 database. Include the
    part number, national stock number, work unit code, TO figure/index, and nomenclature.
    3.3. The commander will ensure personnel perform a semiannual validation.
    3.4. Upon receipt of the listing from the LRS Aircraft Parts Store, review and make changes, addi-
    tions, and deletions as required. Sign and date one copy and return with annotations back to the Air-
    craft Parts Store within 15 work days.
