This publication implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 23-1, Materiel Management,
and establishes local policy and procedures IAW AFMAN 23-220, Air Force Reports of Survey
Program, for the 22 ARW and applicable tenant units. Ensure that all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained In Accordance With (IAW) Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW with the Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR).

1. Initiation of a Reports of Survey.
   1.1. The unit commander (also referred to as the Responsible Officer or RO or Agency
   Chief) must appoint an Investigating Officer (IO) for each ROS. Appointment letters must
   be a digitally signed or a scanned wet-signed document. Forward the letter via E-mail to the
   ROS Program Manager (PM).
       1.1.1. The IO will be an officer, non-commissioned officer (E-7 or above), or civilian
       employee in grades GS-7, WG-9, WL-5 or WS-1 or above.
       1.1.2. If feasible, the IO will be from a different work center and senior in rank to the
       individual(s) being investigated. When the IO is not senior in rank or grade to the
       individual being investigated, the person who appointed the IO will advise the appointing

        authority, in writing, as to why this was necessary. This document will be made a part of
        the ROS file as an exhibit.
        1.1.3. The IO must be a disinterested, impartial individual who has no interest or
        involvement in the custodianship, care, accountability or safe keeping of the property in
        question.
        1.1.4. The IO, when appointed, must not be scheduled for deployment, retirement or
        separation within 60 calendar days of appointment. This should allow ample time for the
        IO to complete the report and be available for any follow-up requests if needed.
        1.1.5. Once appointed, the IO must contact the ROS PM to receive a briefing prior to
        conducting the investigation. The PM will assign a control number and provide an
        electronic version of the DD Form 200 and AF IMT 1168, Statement of
        Suspect/Witness/Complainant. If an IO has conducted an investigation within the last 12
        months, a briefing is not required; simply contact the PM to receive a control number,
        DD Form 200 and AF IMT 1168.
     1.2. When a member is being considered for separation under the provision of AFI 36-3208,
     Administrative Separation for Airmen, the RO/Agency Chief must initiate actions to ensure
     proper accountability of government property in the individual’s possession. The action
     must be completed while the 22 ARW/JA is conducting legal review of the basis for AFI 36-
     3208 discharge.
        1.2.1. Other than instances mentioned in paragraph 1.2. above, clearance through
        applicable units must be accomplished no less than 45 days prior to final out-processing
        date to allow ample time to initiate and complete an ROS investigation/reimburse the
        government for any lost or damaged property.
2. Documentation Requirements.
     2.1. ROSs are accomplished using an electronic version of the DD Form 200, Financial
     Liability Investigation of Property Loss. This form is digitally signed and routed via
     SharePoint accompanied by an electronic version of an AF IMT 1768, Staff Summary Sheet
     (E-SSS).
        2.1.1. An AF IMT 1768 is not required when submitting the completed DD Form 200
        back to the ROS PM. When the DD Form 200 is received from the IO, the PM will
        create and submit the required E-SSS.
     2.2. The DD Form 200 must be utilized, regardless of dollar value, to authorize adjustments
     to accountable records for ADPE, LMR and PWCS assets. Reference: AFI 33-112,
     Information Technology Hardware Asset Management, paragraph 10.2.9.
     2.3. All supporting documentation must be created as/converted into an electronic file (E-
     File) that can be attached to the DD Form 200.
     2.3.1. Click on the “paperclip” icon located on the left-hand margin of the DD Form 200 to
     attach E-Files. All E-Files must be attached prior to the IO digitally signing the form. Once
     signed, attachments can neither be added nor deleted.
        2.3.2. Supporting documentation must include, at minimum, an IO appointment letter
        and an AF IMT Form 1168, Statement of Suspect/Witness/Complainant. In addition,

3. DD Form 200, Financial Liability Investigation of Property Loss.
   3.1. Block 1. Date the IO was briefed and provided a control number. Filled out by ROS
   PM.
   3.2. Block 2. ROS control number. Filled out by the ROS PM.
   3.3. Block 3. Filled out by the IO.
   3.4. Blocks 4-8. If more than one asset with differing NSNs or serial numbers has been lost,
   damaged or destroyed, ensure the statement, “Refer to ‘Continuation of Blocks 4-8’
   Document” is entered in Block 5, click on “Add More Items” and list each item as described
   on the continuation document.
   3.5. Block 4. If a single asset or more than one asset with the same National Stock Number
   (NSN) has been lost, damaged or destroyed, enter the NSN. If the asset has no NSN, enter
   the serial number.
   3.6. Blocks 5 and 6. Self explanatory.
   3.7. Block 7. For 22 ARW ADPE, LMR and PWCS assets, the appointed IO must contact
   the 22 CS/SCOS, Information Technology Asset Manager, to receive the purchase costs for
   each item.
   3.8. Block 7. For 931 ARG ADPE, LMR and PWCS assets, the appointed IO must contact
   the 931 FSS/SCOI, ADPE Manager, to receive the purchase costs for each item.
   3.9. Block 7. For equipment items maintained on an R-14 (CA/CRL), the appointed IO must
   contact the 22 LRS/ LGRMCE, Equipment Accountability Element, to receive the purchase
   costs for each item.
   3.10. Block 8. If a single asset or more than one asset with the same National Stock Number
   (NSN) is entered in Block 4 and the quantity is entered in Block 6, Block 8 will auto-fill with
   the total cost.
   3.11. Block 9. Self-explanatory.
       3.11.1. If financial liability is recommended, the IO must contact the ROS PM to assist
       in computing the depreciated/useful life and disposal value. This is the maximum dollar
       amount that may be considered if financial liability is recommended. Ensure dollar
       amount is included in the Block 9 recommendation.
       3.11.2. For assets identified with an NSN, the ROS PM uses the Useful Life and
       Disposal Value table to determine the asset’s current value. This value is entered by the
       IO in Block 9 of the DD Form 200.
       3.11.3. For non-NSN assets, the ROS PM uses the Air Force Claims Center depreciation
       chart to determine an asset’s current value. This value is entered by the IO in Block 9 of
       the DD Form 200.

        3.19.1. The Accountable Officer for supply system stocks (warehouse parts/supplies,

        3.19.6. The Accountable Officer for assets not mention above and maintained by the unit

5. Responsibilities.
   5.1. The 22 ARW/CV is designated as the Approving Authority. During the absence of the
   22 ARW/CV, the 22 ARW group commanders will act as Approving Authorities. To avoid
   conflict of interests, group commanders will not act as Approving Authorities on ROSs
   initiated within their respective groups. The ROS PM will ensure these reports are routed
   accordingly.
   5.2. Group Commanders and their deputies, including the 931 ARG, are designated as
   Appointing Authorities for their respective groups.
       5.2.1. The 22 MSG/CC and deputy will be the Appointing Authorities for wing staff
       agencies and all tenant units, with exception of the Kansas Air National Guard.
       5.2.2. The Appointing Authority is authorized to take action on ROSs if the dollar
       amount is below $2,000 and there is no negligence, willful misconduct or deliberate
       unauthorized use.
       5.2.3. The dollar amount mentioned in paragraph 5.2.2. above is based on the total
       purchase cost, not the depreciated/useful life and disposal value.
   5.3. If required, the RO/Agency Chief must ensure all necessary documentation is completed
   for voluntary payment in lieu of an ROS.
   5.4. After the unit initiates the voluntary pay document, the individual incurring the financial
   liability will hand carry the document to the ROS PM to receive a control number. After the
   control number is assigned and applied to the document, the individual will immediately
   process the document through the Accounting and Finance Office Cashier and provide a
   copy of the completed document to the ROS PM. The ROS PM will file the document and
   close out the control number.
   5.5. If, as the result of an ROS investigation, a member volunteers to pay, the ROS Program
   Manger will contact the member and have them read and sign a letter of acknowledgment
   explaining the process, fill out a the applicable collection form and attach a copy of the DD
   Form 200. These documents will be forwarded to the 22 CPTS/FMFC office for processing.
   The member must return a copy of the completed collection document to the ROS PM. The
   ROS PM will file the collection document and close out the control number.
   5.6. The RO/Agency Chief must provide a copy of the completed ROS to the applicable
   custodian, when applicable.
   5.7. The ROS PM will apprise the Appointing and Approving Authorities of the status of the
   ROS program. Status will be provided by the end of each month following each fiscal
   quarter i.e., Jan, Apr, Jul and Oct.

6. Appeals.
     6.1. Appeals will be forwarded to: 18 AF/JA, Scott AFB, IL 62225 (DSN 779-0806). A
     forwarding memo must signed by the 22 ARW/CC and accompany the package.
7. Vehicle Accidents.
     7.1. The RO/Agency Chief that had possession of the vehicle will appoint an individual to
     conducted the accident investigation.
     7.2. When notified, the ROS PM will forward a request to the unit commander to submit a
     letter to the applicable Appointing Authority with a recommendation on whether or not an
     ROS should be initiated. A sample copy of the letter will be provided. The letter must be
     signed by the investigator, the unit commander (RO/Agency Chief) and the Appointing
     Authority.
     7.3. The unit commander (RO/Agency Chief) will have 14 calendar days to turn in the
     completed letter to the ROS PM.
     7.4. If, due to gross negligence, willful misconduct or deliberate unauthorized use, an ROS is
     recommended and the Appointing Authority concurs, the ROS PM will coordinate with the
     unit commander (RO/Agency Chief) to initiate the ROS. If an ROS is not recommended and
     the Appointing Authority concurs, the ROS PM files the letter and closes out the control
     number.
