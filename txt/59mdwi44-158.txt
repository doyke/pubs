This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive 44-1, Medical Operations. This
instruction describes the proper completion and submission of 59th Medical Wing (MDW) Form
3323, Report of Hearing Evaluation, for documentation of audiologic care provided at Wilford
Hall Ambulatory Surgical Center. This instruction only applies to personnel assigned, attached
or under contract to the Audiology Clinic at Wilford Hall Ambulatory Surgical Center. This
instruction does not apply to the Air National Guard, Air Force Reserve or personnel providing
audiologic care at the San Antonio Military Medical Center. This publication requires the
collection and or maintenance of information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by
10 U.S.C. 55, Medical and Dental Care, and E.O. 9397 (SSN). The applicable SORN F044 AF
SG D, and            Automated Medical/Dental          Record     System       is   available    at:
http://dpclo.defense.gov/privacy/SORNs/SORNs.htm. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication. The authority to waive requirements is the
publication approval authority. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management
System Records Disposition Schedule.

1. Overview. The purpose of 59 MDW Form 3323, Report of Hearing Evaluation, is to
document the audiometric results obtained in routine audiologic evaluation. The form
documents the following:
     1.1. Audiologic/otologic symptoms.
     1.2. Pure tone audiometry to include:
        1.2.1. Air conduction thresholds.
        1.2.2. Bone conduction thresholds.
     1.3. Transducer type.
     1.4. Speech audiometry results to include:
        1.4.1. Word recognition scores.
        1.4.2. Speech reception thresholds.
     1.5. Immittance results to include:
        1.5.1. Tympanometry.
        1.5.2. Acoustic reflex thresholds.
     1.6. Otoacoustic emissions results.
     1.7. Stenger results.
     1.8. Evaluation reliability.
     1.9. Otoscopic examination.
     1.10. Audiogram legend.
2. Personnel Responsibilities. The 59 MDW Form 3323 is utilized by audiologists and
technicians. Audiologists document clinical encounters and make changes when required, and
technicians document periodic data and changes as they occur.
3. Form Submission. The out-patient documents are scanned and loaded as notes into Armed
Forces Health Longitudinal Technology Application. Once all paper documents are scanned,
they are shredded.
