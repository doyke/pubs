This instruction establishes procedures for the management, use, and maintenance of wireless devices for
the following initiatives: Point of Maintenance (POMx) and Supply Asset Tracking System (SATS). All
hardware and software configurations should support those objectives. All local commanders and super-
visors are responsible for ensuring compliance with the provisions of this instruction. This instruction
applies to all members on Davis-Monthan AFB. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-123 (will convert to
AFMAN 33-363), Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/. The use of the name or mark of any
specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply
endorsement by the Air Force.


1. Purpose. The primary objective of the wireless program is to achieve maximum utilization of person-
nel and equipment with the economic operation of the support system and equipment without jeopardiz-
ing flight or operational safety.

2. Responsibilities. Commanders and supervisors in maintenance areas are responsible for compliance
with the procedures outlined in this publication.
   2.1. Contractor
       2.1.1. Monitors and analyzes performance of the entire Radio Frequency (RF) LAN system,
       including RF infrastructure, security products, and client devices.
       2.1.2. Troubleshoots problems and resolves maintenance issues of the RF LAN system as out-
       lined in the current Statement of Work.
       2.1.3. Changes Wired Equivalent Privacy (WEP) keys monthly on all access points. Schedules
       and coordinates monthly WEP key changes with all units.

       2.1.4. Formally trains CSAs on maintenance and troubleshooting of wireless data input device
       operation.
    2.2. Quality Assurance Evaluator (QAE)
       2.2.1. Monitors and analyzes LAN system (RF) performance, including RF infrastructure, secu-
       rity products, and client devices, analyze this performance.
       2.2.2. Troubleshoots problems and resolves maintenance issues as outlined in the current State-
       ment of Work.
       2.2.3. Ensures network uptime is met.
       2.2.4. Ensures outages and outage sources are identified, documented, and corrective actions
       taken.
       2.2.5. Accounts for contractor’s time.
       2.2.6. Troubleshoots wireless devices if needed.
    2.3. 355 CS
       2.3.1. Ensures backbone network connectivity configuration.
       2.3.2. Ensures properly configured network port/LANs.
       2.3.3. Advises contractor and QAE of planned and unplanned network outages.
       2.3.4. Performs biannual Information System Security (ISS) scans.
       2.3.5. Performs annual review of wireless accreditation package.
    2.4. 355 LRS
       2.4.1. Issues, maintains, configures, troubleshoots, and inventories SATS devices.
    2.5. Client Support Administrator
       2.5.1. Accounts for laptops through the Davis-Monthan Automated Data Processing Equipment
       office.
       2.5.2. Maintains laptops within current ACC network standards.
       2.5.3. Changes WEP keys monthly in accordance with contractor scheduling as required.
       2.5.4. Supplies an updated list of mission-critical users, systems, and websites quarterly.
       2.5.5. Ensures the most updated information is held within the Certification and Accreditation
       package and is resubmitted every 3 years.
       2.5.6. Properly executes the Telecommunications Monitoring Assessment Program.
    2.6. Support Section
       2.6.1. Controls wireless devices, batteries, and accessories through the Tool Accountability Sys-
       tem (TAS).
       2.6.2. Ensures devices are logged off and left on prior to returning to safes.
       2.6.3. Ensures wireless devices are connected to the LAN daily.

     2.6.4. Secures stylus pen to wireless laptops with Dacron lacing.
     2.6.5. Calibrates batteries once a month following manufacturer’s instructions.
