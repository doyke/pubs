AFMAN 23-220, 1 July 1996, is supplemented as follows. It establishes policies and procedures for
reports of surveys and applies to all individuals assigned to the 27th Fighter Wing, Cannon Air Force
Base, NM. It does not apply to Air National Guard or Air Force Reserve personnel. Send comments and
suggested improvements to this publication on AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publica-
tion, through channels, to 27CPTS/FMA, Cannon AFB, NM.

8.3.6.1. (Added) Notify their commanders that an ROS is required and Initial Investigator must be

8.3.6.4. (Added) Ensure the DD Form 200 is completed accurately and includes required statements and
exhibits.
8.3.6.5. (Added) Hand carry the ROS to the Base ROS Program Manager, building 600, room 1123 after
all required signatures are obtained.
8.3.6.6. (Added) Assist the Base ROS Program Manager as required to ensure the prompt completion of
the ROS, to include acquiring needed coordination with other base organizations.

9.1.1. (Added) The 27 CPTS/FM is designated as the appointing authority and is given the authority to
become approving authority when the amount of the loss or damages is less than $2,000, and there is no
evidence of negligence, willful misconduct, or deliberate unauthorized use.
9.1.2. (Added) Each group commander is the ROS approving authority, when damages exceed $2,000.00
or liability is assessed.
10.2.2.5.1. (Added) The 27 CPTS/FMA, located in building 600, room 1123, is designated the ROS Pro-
gram Manager.
11.8.1. (Added) The ROS package must include the unit of assignment, current rank, and DEROS of the
individual(s) who may have caused the loss or damage to government property. If the individual(s)
responsible for the loss/damage to government property is scheduled for separation the package must be
expedited.
14.1.7.1. (Added) The depreciated value, if determined, will be in block 8 of the DD 200 as described in
AFMAN 23-220, paragraph 15.2.
14.3.1.1. (Added) A completed copy of the DD Form 200 will be sent to the group/squadron Report of
Survey monitor to be kept on file for 6 years and 3 months.
15.2.3.7. (Added) The computation statement that will be included can be obtained from the Base ROS
Program Manager. The useful life and salvage value for equipment can be obtained by contacting the
Base ROS Manager at 4-6817.
16.1.6.4.1. (Added) Individuals, who choose to make a voluntary payment, must bring a signed memo-

18.4.3.1.1. (Added) The requirement to process a ROS requires the standard of gross negligence for the
vehicle operator. Once the investigating officer has made his/her recommendation regarding negligence
and signs his/her report, the unit commander will concur/non-concur with the investigating officer and
forward it to the Group Commander who will concur/non-concur and make the final decision as to
whether or not a ROS will be processed.
18.4.3.3.1. (Added) The ROS package will include an accident or incident report and/or statements from
those involved and the estimate of repair.
18.4.3.5.3. (Added) The ROS package will be given to the squadron ROS monitor who will then forward
the package to the Base ROS Program Manager.
