AFMAN 23-110, Volume 2, Part 2, Chapter 13, is supplemented as follows. This publication desig-
nates responsibilities and outlines procedures to be used for turning in equipment assets to the 366th
Logistics Readiness Squadron (366 LRS). Submit recommended changes or additions to the 366 LRS
Procedures and Accountability (366 LRS/LGRSP). Ensure all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in AFMAN 37-123 (to be replaced by AFMAN 33-363) are maintained in accordance with this
manual, and are disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil. Contact supporting records managers as required.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
Title, office symbols and paragraph numbers were changed to fall in line with basic publication. Added
local procedures for Consumable Materiel Recovery Program.
13.2.2.3.1. (Added) ECs will request turn-in (AF Form 2005, Issue/Turn-In Request, or AF Form 601,
Equipment Action Request, in 5 copies) of equipment items through the Equipment Liaison Office (ELO)
in 366 LRS.
13.2.2.3.2. (Added) ELO will request disposition instructions from item managers for each equipment
item turn-in request.
13.2.2.3.3. (Added) Upon receipt of disposition instructions, ELO will notify EC to deliver equipment
items for turn-in. ELO will also inform EC they have 7 days to turn in the items.
13.2.2.3.4. (Added) After notifying EC, property may be delivered for turn-in. ELO will stamp the turn-
in documents "SEVEN DAYS," write the turn-in due by date below the stamp, and forward turn-in docu-
ments (AF Form 2005 in three copies) to 366 LRS/LGMR (Receiving). Upon receipt of AF Form 2005,
Receiving will suspense file them by "end date" and await property. In addition, ELO will stamp one

copy of AF Form 2005 turn-in document "SUSPENSE," and forward to 366 LRS/LGRSP (Procedures
and Accountability) for creation of Delinquent Source Documents (DSD) the same day.
13.2.2.3.5. (Added) ECs will deliver equipment items for turn-in to 366 LRS/LGRMR.
13.2.2.3.6. (Added) ECs will ensure equipment is cleaned/purged, properly packaged and contains the
proper tags prior to turn-in. Equipment items not in compliance for turn-in will be rejected back to the
EC.
13.2.2.3.7. (Added) If EC fails to deliver equipment items to 366 LRS/LGRMR within 7 calendar days,
Receiving will forward turn-in documents back to ELO. ELO will close out the request and have 366
LRS/LGRSP (Procedures and Accountability) destroy DSDs.
13.2.2.3.8. (Added) After Document Control has destroyed DSD, ELO will contact the EC via e-mail or
phone call to inform them their TIN document will not be processed because they did not meet the 7 day
criteria. Furthermore, ELO will return the TIN documentation with the attached copy of the e-mail
through Base Information Transfer Center (BITC) to the EC or phone the EC to request pick-up of the
TIN documentation.
13.2.2.3.9. (Added) ECs who are unable to secure a vehicle to transport equipment items to 366 LRS/
LGRMR for turn in, may call Vehicle Operations (366 LRS/LGRVOD) for vehicle assistance.
13.2.2.3.10. (Added) Receiving will pull the suspense copy of the turn-in AF Form 2005 and verify the
following:
13.2.2.3.10.1. (Added) NSN, Quantity, Unit of Issue, Condition Code and Serial Number (if applicable)
match the tags and property.
13.2.2.3.10.2. (Added) Asset is in the proper Reusable Container or SPI or is accompanied by an AF
Form 451, Request for Packaging Service, signed by Outbound Freight personnel.
13.2.2.3.10.3. (Added) MSDS is present and is for the correct NSN (if required).
13.2.2.3.11. (Added) If all is correct, Receiving will accept property from the custodian by providing a
signed copy of the TIN to the EC.
13.2.2.3.12. (Added) Receiving will:
13.2.2.3.12.1. (Added) Process the turn-in as it appears on the AF Form 2005. Once processed:
13.2.2.3.12.1.1. (Added) Stamp or write "Processed" on both copies of the AF Form 2005 and "Docu-
ment Control" on one copy of the AF Form 2005.
13.2.2.3.12.1.2. (Added) Sign block "B" on both copies of the AF Form 2005.
13.2.2.3.12.1.3. (Added) Forward one copy to Document Control to clear the source document on file.
13.2.2.3.12.1.4. (Added) File one copy in Receiving for future reference.
13.2.2.3.13. (Added) For all turned-in equipment assets, Receiving will sign and forward all copies of
shipping documents along with property to the applicable Movement Team hold area. Receiving must
ensure assets are moved and scanned to appropriate locations via SATS when not in a post-post situation.
13.2.2.3.14. (Added) Movement Team actions:
13.2.2.3.14.1. (Added) The delivery times for routine shipments going to Outbound Cargo are Monday
through Friday at 1000 and 1500.

13A1.3.2. Critical (condemned waived) assets will be processed by Flight Service Center (FSC).
13B3. FSC is the designated location for processing due-in-from-maintenance (DIFM) turn-ins.
13B3.2.2.1. Customers will deliver DIFM returns directly to FSC IAW ACCI 23-251, Para 2.2.7.
13B3.2.5. The applicable section will perform inspection processes.
13B5.1.1. Base and off-base activities will use the following procedures for turning in consumable mate-
riel:
13B5.1.1.1. (Added) Generating activities will establish collection and pick up points for XB3 materiel in
their areas. Each collection point will have three segregated bins/areas; one for serviceable-identified
materiel, one for serviceable-unidentified materiel and one for unserviceable/scrap materiel.
13B5.1.1.2. (Added) Organizational personnel will place materiel in appropriate collection bin with a DD
Form 1574, Serviceable Tag - Materiel, attached and appropriate organization and shop code clearly
annotated on form.
13B5.1.1.2.1. (Added) Serviceable-identified and serviceable-unidentified materiel will be returned to
the LRS/supply activity. Unserviceable/scrap materiel will be turned in to DRMO directly by generator.
13B5.2. Vehicle Operations will pick up serviceable XB3 items from designated collection or pick up
points and deliver to Receiving Section for processing. Receiving personnel will follow procedures in
Para 13B5.5.
13B7.2.1. Serviceable excess material will be returned to LRS/supply activity with a DD Form 1574
attached. Organization and shop code will be clearly annotated on form along with a POC and telephone
number.
13B7.2.3. Unserviceable/condemned material will be transferred to DRMO by the using activity.
13B7.3. Receiving Section will perform inspection processes and process consumable turn-ins. If
Receiving determines more information or action is required, they will contact POC for resolution. If
property needs to be returned to originator for more actions, originator will pick up their own property
from Receiving.
13B9.3.2.2. The using activity will move their equipment. If the unit does not have the capability to
move an item, they will coordinate movement of assets with Vehicle Operations (extension 8-2215) to
arrange turn-in/delivery of serviceable equipment directly to Receiving for processing.
13B10.2. The Equipment Liaison Office (ELO) within Customer Service is responsible for processing
the Activity Code P document number turn-ins.
