AFI11-415, Weapons and Tactics Programs, 20 March 2007, is supplemented as follows. This
supplement provides additional guidance for the establishment of the weapons and tactics
program for Air Force Global Strike Command (AFGSC). This supplement applies to all
AFGSC units.
Ensure that all records created as a result of the processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. The use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial
product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

A3.8. (Added). WG level and below weapons shops:
A3.8.1. (Added). Participate in all Operations Review Panels/Boards.
A3.8.2. (Added). Establish a debrief process for operations and training activities. An effective
debriefing process plays a central role in the rigorous and ongoing critical analysis of all unit
operational processes. Self-analysis provides an unparalleled opportunity for a crewmember to
critically review, understand, and learn from his/her performance in a given situation.
Therefore, self-analysis should play a crucial role in the debrief process.
A3.8.2.1. (Added). As part of performance self-analysis, the debrief process should encompass
a review of events, identification of problems, determination of root causes, and development
of lessons learned.
A3.8.3. (Added). Weapons shops will develop processes for the collection and dissemination of
any lessons learned that can provide benefit beyond the specific crewmembers involved.
A3.8.4. (Added). Support the development of adequate procedures for the planning and
execution of the unit level mission, including mitigation actions when faced with an anomalous
