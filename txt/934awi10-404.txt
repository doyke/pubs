This instruction implements Air Force Reserve Command Instruction (AFRCI) 10-404, Unit
Recall. This instruction outlines procedures for the notification and assembly of personnel under
actual, alert, and test conditions. This instruction applies to all members assigned to 934th Airlift
Wing units. This instruction addresses information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974, under
Title 10 United States Code (U.S.C.), Section 8013. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; send it directly to the OPR. Ensure that all records
created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with
Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance
with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. This revision
provides additional information on the 934th Air Wing recall and personnel accountability
procedures; standardizes functional unit recall management responsibilities across the entire
Wing; re-designates unit recall monitors operations function and responsibilities; deletes section
5, Personnel Recall Reporting Procedures. Adds Attachment 3, Pyramid Notification System.
1. Overview. The ability of a commander to recall his or her unit members is one of the basic
premises for a unit to mobilize and meet its wartime commitments, and disseminate information
to unit members. To accomplish this, the commander must have a plan that ensures he or she will

be able to contact and assemble all assigned personnel within the required response time as
designated in the mobilization order. The response time could be as early as the time stipulated
in the Designed Operational Capability (DOC) statement for units which have a DOC statement.
2. Responsibilities.
     2.1. Wing Commander Responsibilities:
        2.1.1. Ensure each unit has a recall plan in place to meet wartime and peacetime recall
        requirements.
        2.1.2. Identify in writing a primary and alternate Wing Recall Program Manager, and
        provide the Command Post a copy of appointment letter.
        2.1.3. Ensure accurate and timely reporting of recall results as defined in AFRCI 10-404,
        Unit Recall.
        2.1.4. Ensure the recall plan includes accountability procedures to be used during a
        natural disaster or national emergency.
        2.1.5. Ensure the Wing conducts a no-notice communications test through the Command
        Post at least once annually to demonstrate the ability to contact all assigned Wing
        personnel.
     2.2. Group/Squadron Commander Responsibilities:
        2.2.1. Identify in writing a primary and alternate program monitor for the group or
        squadron using the prescribed appointment letter format in attachment 2.
        2.2.2. Provide the Command Post a copy of the unit program monitor appointment letter
        on a quarterly basis.
        2.2.3. Ensure unit members are familiar with the recall process as well as the importance
        of each member to maintain updated contact information
        2.2.4. Initiate a unit level communications test through the unit’s respective recall
        monitor at a minimum of once annually to identify any flaws in the unit recall process.
     2.3. Wing Command Post (CP) Responsibilities:
        2.3.1. Function as the Wing Commander’s representative for initiating and monitoring
        Wing recall progression.
        2.3.2. The Command Post will not be designated a key alerter for groups/squadrons,
        outside of CP personnel notifications.
        2.3.3. Maintain a current recall roster and appointment letter for each assigned unit.
        2.3.4. Maintain recall and communications test results for the wing.
        2.3.5. Upon initiation of an actual recall or mobilization order, submit reports in
        accordance with applicable guidance and procedures.
        2.3.6. Controllers will maintain proficiency with the primary emergency notification
        system (ENS), AtHoc.
     2.4. Unit Recall Program Monitors Responsibilities:
934AWI10-404 23 JUNE 2014                                                                      3


       2.4.1. Ensure recall programs fall into the guidelines of this instruction, and AFRCI 10-
       404.
       2.4.2. Create a pyramid notification system (recall roster) using the prescribed Pyramid
       Notification System in Attachment 3.
       2.4.3. Consider operational security (OPSEC) in all actions.
       2.4.4. Develop a positive validation system for monthly verification of individual home
       address, email, and telephone information. Ensure unit members verify and update their
       personal information in the AtHoc system (the purple globe), and through the virtual
       military personnel flight online (vMPF).
       2.4.5. Monitors will need to ensure they are properly trained on how to use the AtHoc
       system. Training can be requested by contacting the Command Post.
       2.4.6. Ensure measures are in place for in/out processing to add/delete (or enable/disable
       for deployments) unit members from the AtHoc database before they are gained or
       separated from the unit.
       2.4.7. Establish procedures for recall or mobilization under degraded communications
       conditions (comm-out).
       2.4.8. Provide the Command Post with a monthly update of recall rosters, no later than
       1500 local on Sunday of the primary Unit Training Assembly (UTA) on the attached
       template.
   2.5. Unit Member Responsibilities:
       2.5.1. Update the Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Installation Warning System
       (IWS) and recall monitor with the following mandatory information: ALL phone
       numbers to include home, work, cell, and email addresses. This information can be
       updated by right clicking on the AFRC IWS Alerts (purple globe) icon in the system tray,
       clicking on “access self-service”, and clicking on the “devices” tab.
       2.5.2. Update all personal contact information in the virtual military personnel flight
       online (VMPF).
       2.5.3. Notify unit recall program monitor with changes to their personal contact
       information, and update the AFRC IWS database.
3. Recall Rosters.
   3.1. Comply with provisions of the Privacy Act of 1974 (5 U.S.C. 552a).
   3.2. Rosters must be formatted as prescribed in AFRCI 10-404, Attachment 4, “Sample
   Recall Roster”, or at the discretion of the 934th Airlift Wing Recall Manager.
   3.3. Ensure the Headquarters Air Force Personnel Center/Personnel Recovery Center
   (AFPC/PRC) toll free number, the AFRC Personnel Accountability and Assessment Systems
   website https://afpaas.af.mil/ (AFPAAS), the unit recall monitors work phone number, and
   the units UCC is printed on all recall rosters, in accordance with
   3.4. Specify alternate procedures in the event of the loss of primary communications.
4. Notification Systems.
4                                                               934AWI10-404 23 JUNE 2014


    4.1. AtHoc.
       4.1.1. AtHoc will be the 934th Airlift Wings primary ENS. Recalls will be initiated for
       each unit by the Unit Recall Monitors as directed by the Wing Commander.
    4.2. Pyramid Recall.
       4.2.1. The pyramid recall system will be a secondary means of notifying unit members,
       used in situations that AtHoc is not functional or feasible. All Units must have a pyramid
       recall system established in accordance with AFRCI 10-404, paragraph 3, Recall
       Rosters.
       4.2.2. Monitors will relay accountability information to the Command Post.
    4.3. Comm-Out.
       4.3.1. Comm-out recalls will be initiated as a last resort for contacting unit members only
       once it is established that all lines of communications are unavailable or if unit members
       cannot be reached through primary or secondary means.
       4.3.2. Personnel will be contacted in person by a runner. All units will have established
       procedures for comm-out recalls.
