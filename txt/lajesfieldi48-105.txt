This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 48-1, Aerospace Medicine
Enterprise. Its purpose is to establish procedures for the administrative and medical
management of animal bite cases at Lajes Field, Azores, Portugal. It applies to all U.S.
personnel (military, civilian, dependent) assigned to Lajes Field. Refer recommended changes
and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field
through the Lajes Field publications/forms manager. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual
(AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.          This instruction requires
collecting and maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974, authorized by 10
U.S.C. 8013 and E.O. 9397.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

The format of this instruction has changed to comply with Air Force and 65 Air Base Wing
(ABW) requirements. Section 2.1, 65th Air Base Wing Commander’s Responsibilities, has been
added. No other changes noted.

1. General
     1.1. Rabies has never been reported on Terceira or anywhere else in the Azores Archipelago.
     1.2. Department of Defense Form 2341, Report of Animal Bite, will be accomplished on all
     mammal bites and scratches.
     1.3. The Rabies Advisory Board (RAB) will review all mammal bite incidents for treatment
     recommendations when an animal cannot be located or the situation warrants concern, (i.e.,
     when bitten by wild carnivores or when animal showed unusual behavior or prior illness).
2. Responsibilities.
     2.1. 65th Air Base Wing Commander will:
        2.1.1. Ensure a written Animal Bite Control Program is established to eliminate or
        minimize exposure of active duty military members, dependents and civilian personnel to
        rabies.
     2.2. 65th Medical Group Commander will:
        2.2.1. Implement this instruction and ensure compliance of personnel under his/her
        authority.
     2.3. Primary Care Manager (PCM) Team will:
        2.3.1. Initiate treatment of all bite wounds and scratches by following World Health
        Organization and Centers for Disease Control (CDC) and Prevention guidelines.
        2.3.2. Evaluate patient and provide education on risk of rabies (Capnocytophaga
        canimorsus) infection.
        2.3.3. Complete DD Form 2341, parts I and II in duplicate within 24 hours of incident
        and forward to Public Health. Notify Public Health immediately if the rabies risk is
        deemed high enough to warrant a Rabies Advisory Board (RAB).
        2.3.4. Provide treatment for bites which occur outside the Azores according to the RAB
        and current CDC guidelines.
     2.4. Outpatient Records Section will:
        2.4.1. Place the completed original DD Form 2341 in section 3 of the patient’s medical
        record.
     2.5. U.S. Army Veterinary Services will:
        2.5.1. Maintain close liaison with local veterinarians concerning the introduction of
        rabies to the island.
        2.5.2. Provide rabies vaccination services for dogs and cats owned by US personnel
        residing on the island of Terceira. Will advise the Chief of Aerospace Medicine (SGP) in
        the event of suspected rabies introduction to the island
        2.5.3. Follow applicable procedures to submit animal specimens to an approved
        laboratory.
        2.5.4. Attend RAB and/or Aerospace Medicine Council meetings to provide current data
        on the prevalence of rabies in the local area.

     2.5.5. Obtain bite information from Public Health and investigate the bite incident.
     Establish ownership, vaccination status, and general health of the animal and collect other
     pertinent data, such as amount of time on island to assess the risk of rabies transmission.
     2.5.6. Contact owner, if any, and determine appropriate quarantine procedures.
     2.5.7. Promptly notify Public Health and the SGP, if circumstances warrant additional
     follow-up (i.e., unusual animal behavior or other health concerns).
     2.5.8. At the end of the quarantine period or upon receipt of laboratory results, complete
     DD Form 2341, Part III and correct entries in blocks 9 and 10 of Part I, if necessary, and
     return all copies and original to Public Health.
  2.6. Security Forces will:
     2.6.1. Attempt to capture tame animals and, with the assistance of the US Army
     Veterinary Services, check for microchip identification. Refer to Lajes Field Instruction
     48-131, Control of Animals, for additional responsibilities.
  2.7. Public Health Element will:
     2.7.1. Notify chairperson and members of the RAB of all cases which warrant committee
     consideration.
     2.7.2. Monitor and control distribution of DD Forms 2341.
     2.7.3. Review all DD Forms 2341 to ensure proper documentation prior to final
     disposition.
     2.7.4. Forward completed original of DD Form 2341 to Outpatient Records for filing in
     patient’s medical record.
     2.7.5. Ensure animal bite patient is entered into the Patient Management Module in
     Preventive Health Assessment and Individual Medical Readiness (PIMR).
