This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 91-2, Safety Programs. It establishes
and defines responsibilities and procedures for operational safety of hangar doors within maintenance
activities of the 944th Fighter Wing (944 FW), as prescribed by Air Force Occupational Safety and
Health Standard (AFOSHSTD) 91-501, Air Force Consolidated Occupational Safety Standard; Air
Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101_Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Supplement (SUP), Aircraft and
Equipment Maintenance Management and AFOSHSTD 91-100, Aircraft Flight Line – Ground
Operations and Activities. This instruction applies to all 944 FW personnel. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the
Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 through
unit publications/forms manager. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in
this publication are maintained according to Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of according to the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. This instruction revises
program responsibility from the Maintenance Group to the Fighter Wing Commander and adds
attachments 2 through 5, adds paragraph 2.6., and updates forms and references.
1. Responsibilities:
   1.1. The 944th Fighter Wing (944 FW) Commander will authorize qualified personnel for the
   operation of hangar doors. Note: Only properly trained and qualified personnel are authorized to
   operate hangar doors.
   1.2. The 944th Safety office (944 FW/SEG) is responsible to the 944 FW commander for:
       1.2.1. Providing a list of qualified personnel to operate hangar doors.
 2                                                                       944FWI91-201 15 APRIL 2009


        1.2.2. Ensuring initial and annual hangar door training is accomplished for all personnel who are
        operating hangar doors.
     1.3. Supervisors are responsible to the 944th Fighter Wing Commander for:
        1.3.1. Ensuring all personnel who do not operate hangar doors but work in facilities that
        incorporate hangar doors receive hangar door awareness training for their designated facility.
        Documentation of hangar door awareness training will be recorded either in the individual’s AF
        Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record, or an AF Form 797, Job Qualification Standard
        Continuation/Command JQS, whichever may be applicable.
        1.3.2. Notifying building custodian of any hangar door malfunctions.
     1.4. Building custodians are responsible for:
        1.4.1. When hangar door maintenance problems occur, building custodians will check door
        operations to determine operational safety as prescribed by AFOSHSTD 91-100.
        1.4.2. In the event maintenance is required, building custodians will notify the 56th Civil
        Engineering Trouble Desk, coordinate repair actions, and document the facility maintenance log.
2. Operator Procedures:
     2.1. Comply with hangar door operating checklist (Attachment 2) located adjacent to hangar door
     controls.
     2.2. A visual check of the clear zone will be performed prior to moving hangar doors for clearance
     of personnel, equipment, and materials; also ensure that door tracks are free from obstructions.
     2.3. Notify all personnel working around hangar doors to stay clear (outside clear zone) of doors
     prior to door opening and closing procedures.
     2.4. Ensure personnel entry doors are closed.
     2.5. Under no circumstances will the hangar doors be left in the open position to a width of less than
     10 feet as prescribed by AFOSHSTD 91-100.
     2.6. Verify that applicable hangar doors, tracks, warning buzzers, and switches are operational.
3. Door Malfunction Procedures         :
     3.1. Turn off electrical switch and circuit breakers.
     3.2. If required, open access door and manually disengage drive components. Slowly open/close the
     hangar doors.
     3.3. Notify the building custodian listed on the hangar doors.
4. Adopted Forms:
AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication
AF Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record
AF Form 797, Job Qualification Standard Continuation/Command JQS

             HANGAR DOOR OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS FOR 999 AND 1022

A2.1. Opening Procedures:
   A2.1.1. Warning: ENSURE ALL PERSONNEL AND OBJECTS ARE CLEAR OF THE
   DOORS PATH BEFORE OPERATION.
   A2.1.2. Make sure there are no obstructions in or around door tracks.
   A2.1.3. Make sure that personnel doors are fully closed.
      A2.1.3.1. Note: Not fully closing personnel doors will render hangar doors inoperable.
   A2.1.4. Press the ―
                     OPEN‖ button on the hangar door to be opened.
      A2.1.4.1. Buzzer will sound.
      A2.1.4.2. 5 – Second delay will activate before door movement.
      A2.1.4.3. In some cases doors may accelerate several seconds after they begin moving.
      A2.1.4.4. Walk parallel to door movement ensuring safe operation.
   A2.1.5. Caution: Hangar doors must be opened a minimum of 10 feet for safety requirements
A2.2. Closing Procedures:
   A2.2.1. Warning: ENSURE ALL PERSONNEL AND OBJECTS ARE CLEAR OF THE
   DOORS PATH BEFORE OPERATION
   A2.2.2. Ensure there are no obstructions in or around door tracks.
   A2.2.3. Ensure that personnel doors are fully closed.
      A2.2.3.1. Note: Not fully closing personnel doors will render hangar doors inoperable.
   A2.2.4. . Press the ―
                       CLOSE‖ button on the hangar door to be closed.
      A2.2.4.1. . The buzzer will sound.
      A2.2.4.2. 5 – Second delay will activate before door movement. In some cases doors may
      accelerate several seconds after they begin moving.
      A2.2.4.3. Walk parallel to door movement ensuring safe operation.

                    HANGAR DOOR EMERGENCY OPERATION HANGAR 999

A3.1. Warning: ENSURE ALL PERSONNEL AND OBJECTS ARE CLEAR OF THE DOORS
PATH BEFORE OPERATION.
A3.2. Caution: Do not manually move doors faster than they would move under electrical power!
Serious injury or damage may result if the doors strike an object at unusually fast speeds. Expect the
doors to coast after pushing has stopped. Under electrical power the doors are stopped with a brake.
The brake is ―disengaged‖ when using manual operation.
A3.3. Note: If required, these doors can be pushed utilizing manpower or ―
                                                                         carefully” towed by vehicle.

Figure A3.1. Disconnect Handle for Hangar 999.




A3.4. Located on Outside Door Controls.
     A3.4.1. To operate, manually open the operator access door and locate the disconnect handle
     activating handle to release position and close door. Reverse this procedure to return to electrical
     operation.
     A3.4.2. If a holding brake is desired (example: to hold doors closed) return the lever to the electrical
     operation position.
     A3.4.3. If doors fail to operate in either mode contact the Building Custodian.

                                             Attachment 5
       HANGAR DOOR ON-THE-JOB-TRAINING (OJT) AND AWARENESS TRAINING

A5.1. Academic Training:
     A5.1.1. Review AFOSHSTD 91-100 and this instruction prior to operating hangar doors.
A5.2. General Precautions:
     A5.2.1. If any unsafe condition is noted discontinue use immediately and notify the Building
     Custodian.
     A5.2.2. Before operating the hangar door check that no personnel or objects are in the ―
                                                                                            clear zone‖
     or obstructing the door tracks.
     A5.2.3. Always walk parallel with door movement. NEVER get in the path of door travel when
     operating the hangar doors. The door weighs several tons and has the potential to inflect severe
     injury if not operated properly and in accordance with safety directives.
     A5.2.4. Hangar doors must be opened a minimum of 10 Feet!
     A5.2.5. The primary safety system is YOU the operator!
