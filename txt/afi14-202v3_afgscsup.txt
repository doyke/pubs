This supplement implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 14-202
Volume 3, General Intelligence Rules, 10 March 2008. The supplement outlines general
intelligence rules and procedures for Air Force Global Strike Command (AFGSC) units and
organizations. This AFI applies to applicable Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and Air
National Guard (ANG) units. This instruction may not address all missions or responsibilities of
AFGSC Intelligence units that perform specialized missions.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to headquarters (HQ)
AFGSC/A2X, 245 Davis Ave East, Suite 316, Barksdale AFB Louisiana 71110 using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847s from the field
through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims.

3.2.2.1. (Added) AFGSC/A2Q is the designated OPR for the A2 SAV program. AFGSC/A2Q
performs SAVs for active duty and ANG intelligence units. Although Air Force Reserve units
fall under AFRC, it is highly recommended that AFGSC be involved in any assistance program
for AFGSC-gained units. SAVs provide an overall objective look at the administrative
management, operational mission activities, and other unit functions to ensure intelligence units
and personnel can successfully perform their home station and expeditionary force missions.
3.2.2.2. (Added) Request for SAVs should be submitted via e-mail to HQ AFGSC/A2Q not later
than (NLT) 60 days prior to the desired visit date. Requesting units are required to inform
appropriate Numbered Air Force (NAF) SIO and the requestor’s chain of command.
3.2.3.1. (Added) AFGSC/A2 Chief Enlisted Manager (CEM) and AFGSC/A2X are the OPRs for
all queries concerning enlisted/officer force management issues. Units will identify critical
manpower requirements or shortfalls through Defense Readiness Reporting System (DRRS) and
through functional channels to AFGSC/A2. Note: AFRC units will identify matters to AFRC/A2
through approved channels.
3.2.4.1. (Added) AFGSC/A2 will designate a primary and alternate Command Dissemination
Manager (CDM/ACDM). The CDM ensures a current Statement of Intelligence Interest (SII) is
on file for all subordinate intelligence organizations through an annual data call. The CDM is
assigned to HQ AFGSC/A2X.
3.2.4.2. (Added) The CDM will create, maintain and make available a web-based Standard
Intelligence Document List (SIDL) to be updated at least annually. The SIDL identifies the
baseline documents required for intelligence work centers. AFGSC/A2O will provide a Standard
Intelligence Document List (SIDL) for all units. The AFGSC SIDL will be posted on
AFGSC/A2 web pages, linked to national-level producer home pages and updated, as necessary,
to ensure a living document.

include name, rank, duty title, position number, date of rank (DOR), completion date of last
contingency temporary duty (TDY), AEF posturing, phone number, fax number, date eligible for
return from overseas (DEROS) and projected personnel gains.

5.1.1.8.1.1. (Added) Provide weekly briefings to the commander and staff on subjects relating to
political, scientific and military developments which may have a bearing on enemy capabilities.
Additional topics could include briefing changes to ICBM targeting during affected week.
5.1.1.8.1.2. (Added) Provide weekly intelligence updates to missile combat crews at pre-

enemies. These briefings will be conducted during monthly EWO classes. Additionally, the
briefings may be incorporated into an intelligence supplement to the monthly EWO self-study
package.
5.1.1.8.1.4. (Added) Provide intelligence input to wing plans.
5.1.1.8.1.5. (Added) Provide intelligence support to the unit battle staff.
5.1.1.8.1.6. (Added) Establish and maintain an intelligence dissemination program and ensure
missile combat crews and staff are fully aware of sources and products available to them.

5.1.4.7.1. (Added) Appointed GI&S (Geospatial Information and Services) custodians will
coordinate with weapons/tactics and wing plans personnel to develop unit-specific GI&S,
imagery and/or target material requirements. Establish procedures to ensure materials are
current. Ensure crisis/emergency ordering procedures are available and communicated to
intelligence personnel.
5.1.4.7.2. (Added) AFGSC units are instructed to use the AFGSC supplement to AFI 14-205 in
developing their GI&S programs.
5.1.4.7.1. (Added) Unit Target Material Development and Maintenance. Unit levels Geospatial
Intelligence (GEOINT) and target materials will be based upon Combatant Commander
(COCOM) and/or Joint Force Command J2 core target material. If no target materials exist
and/or nuclear related materials are not available submit a request for production to
AFGSC/A2O.
5.1.5.2.1. (Added) Establish an effective system of communication between work centers that
ensures timely dissemination of intelligence information, accountability of personnel and current
operational status at all locations. Analyze all incoming information for impact on the unit
mission. Rapidly disseminate significant and critical intelligence to appropriate work centers.
5.1.6.3.1. (Added) Refer to applicable intelligence reporting guidance for specific requirements.
5.1.7.1.1. (Added) The SIO will ensure all intelligence personnel are capable of using all
available automated intelligence systems.

responsible for daily management and maintenance of assigned intelligence automated systems,
applications, and intelligence tools. A copy of the appointment letter will be provided to
AFGSC/A2O.
5.1.7.3.1. (Added) Ensure intelligence data systems (i.e., JWICS, SIPRNET) are functional and
operational. Report any limiting factors to NAF and AFGSC/A2 immediately and report
expected get-well dates.
