This instruction establishes the 82d Training Wing (82 TRW) Training Operations (TO) as a
Wing Staff Agency (WSA). This instruction applies to all personnel assigned to TO and
Training Groups (TRG) aligned under the 82 TRW to include Geographically Separated Units
(GSU). Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule located
in the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS). Any waivers to this
supplement will be sent through the 82 TRW/TO for approval. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847 through the group Director, Training and Education to
82 TRW/Training Operations Oversight. Training groups/squadrons will not supplement or
develop operating instructions based on this instruction. The use of the name or mark of any
specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication does not
imply endorsement by the Air Force. See Attachment 1 for a Glossary of References and
Supporting Information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes
include edited disposition schedule link in opening paragraph; edited office symbols, separated
Standardization and Evaluation; added foreign disclosure officer responsibilities, added Training
Contract Quality Assurance Management, Program Management, Non-recurrent Special
Projects/Programs Management, and Section E.

                                          Chapter 1

                                PHILOSOPHY AND SCOPE

1.1. The 82 TRW is comprised of multiple training groups. The 82 TRW/CC requires a
comprehensive understanding and compilation of training operations. Training Operations
provides this assistance through close coordination and communication with training groups,
Second Air Force (2 AF), and Air Education and Training Command (AETC) as described
within this instruction.
1.2. This instruction establishes 82 TRW/TO as the wing office of primary responsibility
(OPR) for all technical and instructor training related administrative staffing. It also
establishes the TO Director, in coordination with all Group Training and Education Directors, as
the training advisor to the 82 TRW/CC on training issues.

contracts and training technology computer support. TO provides international military program
management.

                                          Chapter 3

                               STAFFING PROCEDCURES

3.1. All training groups and wing staff agencies will ensure external technical/military
training taskings and suspenses are sent to 82 TRW/TO Workflow for staffing. 82
TRW/TO Workflow will solicit input/data from all affected parties, consolidate the data, and
draft the wing response.
3.2. To draft and coordinate wing technical training/military instructions, supplements,
and training policy, the TO Oversight Flight Policy POC and/or Military Training Support
Flight POC will:
   3.2.1. Form a Policy Working Group composed of appropriate personnel
   submitted/approved by each individual Group Training Director. The Policy Working Group
   will meet or conduct business via e-mail as often as required to write initial draft training
   related wing instructions and supplements as well as revisions/interim changes.
   3.2.2. Appoint the facilitator for Policy Working Groups.
   3.2.3. Coordinate draft instructions, supplements, revisions/interim changes, and annual
   reviews through training groups and TO.

                                          Chapter 4

                                WING POLICY COUNCIL

4.1. Policy plays a critical role in ensuring the short-term and long-term operational health
of the 82 TRW. Group commanders, group training directors, and the TO director, are
responsible for, and directly in touch with, the training processes critical to the mission
accomplishment of the training wing. This section establishes the Wing Policy Council as a
mechanism to recommend review for approval of SAFB training policy.
4.2. The Wing Policy Council is composed of group-designated representatives from each
training group and chaired by the Director, TO (or designated representative). The TO
Oversight Flight policy POC/Military Training Support Flight POC will serve as the Wing
Policy Council facilitator. Subject-matter experts from the wing are invited as advisors. The
Wing Policy Council will review and recommend approval of SAFB training. The edited
document will be staffed to the group commanders for their coordination prior to being
submitted to the 82 TRW/CC for signature. The consolidated comments, including all dissenting
comments and responses, will be provided to the Approving Authority during the approval
process.
   4.2.1. Wing Policy Council members will:
       4.2.1.1. Coordinate on all draft policy documents via Task Management Tool (TMT)
       and/or attend Wing Policy Council meetings when notified.
       4.2.1.2. Review and recommend approval of wing policy documents based on what is
       best for the wing, taking into consideration group-unique requirements, limitations and
       restrictions.
       4.2.1.3. Send a fully empowered-to-vote substitute to scheduled Wing Policy Council
       meetings when designated council member is unable to attend, in order to avoid delays in
       timely policy coordination and publication.
   4.2.2. The TO Oversight Flight Policy POC/Military Training Support Flight POC will:
       4.2.2.1. Coordinate Wing Policy Council meetings.
       4.2.2.2. Record changes, and complete subsequent final edits to the Wing Policy Council
       policy documents.
       4.2.2.3. Accomplish final coordination of approved policy documents through the group
       commanders, and then submit to wing leadership for signature. Forward approved
       documents to Base Publications IAW established policies.
       4.2.2.4. Inform groups when a SAFB technical training policy document has been
       published, changed, and rescinded.
