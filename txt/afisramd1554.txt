This mission directive implements Air Force Mission Directive 15. The Air Force Cryptologic
Office (AFCO) is an extension of the AF ISR Agency Headquarters. Maintain records created as
a result of prescribed processes IAW AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and dispose of
them IAW the AF Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) as found on the AF Portal at the AF
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) link. Contact supporting records
managers as required.

1. Mission. The AFCO is designated as the AF ISR Agency's principal cryptologic
representative to the National Security Agency (NSA) and also functions as adjunct cryptologic
staff to the Director of Air Force Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (HAF/A2). In
these capacities, AFCO collaborates with the national Intelligence Community (IC), Department
of Defense (DoD) agencies, as well as Service Cryptologic Component (SCC) and Service
Cryptologic Partner (SCP) staffs to actively support the Air Forceâ€™s cryptologic mission at all
levels on behalf of the AF ISR Agency Commander. As an integral part of the AF ISR Agency,
AFCO develops, leverages, and integrates Air Force cryptologic capabilities and resources
(national and tactical) to satisfy Total Force, Joint and Combatant Commander requirements.
2. Command. The AFCO Director:
   2.1. Reports to the Commander, AF ISR Agency.
   2.2. Exercises administrative and operational control over assigned personnel.

3. Responsibilities:
    3.1. AFCO is designated by the AF ISR Agency Commander (also the AF Service
    Cryptologic Component Commander (AF SCC/CC)) as the principal cryptologic staff in the
    NCR for the AF ISR Agency and serves as the adjunct cryptologic staff for the Director of
    Air Force Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (HAF/A2). In this capacity, AFCO
    serves as the sole Air Force entry point into the National Security Agency/Central Security
    Service (NSA/CSS) and as the AF ISR Agency focal point for IC cryptologic issues in the
    NCR.
    3.2. The AFCO, in coordination with AF ISR Agency, guides the development of Air Force
    cryptologic plans and programs, cryptologic missions, and technology integration for the AF
    ISR Agency Commander.
    3.3. As directed by the AF ISR Agency/CC and the HAF/A2, AFCO represents the Air
    Force both within the NSA/CSS, and to the larger national cryptologic IC. AFCO
    coordinates Air Force cryptologic policies, plans, and programs within the United States
    Cryptologic System (USCS) and the United States SIGINT System (USSS).
    3.4. Advises AF ISR Agency and HAF/A2 in validating and prioritizing Air Force
    cryptologic requirements and advocates these positions within NSA/CSS and to the IC.
    3.5. AFCO, in coordination with other AF ISR Agency staff elements, guides the
    development and assists AF ISR Agency in implementing Air Force cryptologic plans,
    policy, doctrine, and programs.
