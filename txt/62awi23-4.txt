This instruction provides procedures for the Wheel and Tire Supply Point which is managed by the Wheel
and Tire Shop (62 MXS/MXGMTA). The Wheel and Tire Shop, Receiving Section (62 LRS/LGRMR)
and the Flight Service Center (62 LRS/LGRMF) will follow these procedures to ensure accountability is
maintained for C-17A wheels and tires. This instruction does not apply to Air Reserve or Air National
Guard personnel attached to McChord Air Force Base. Ensure that all records created as a result of pro-
cesses prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-123 (will convert
AFMAN 33-363), Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/. The use of the name or mark of any
specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply
endorsement by the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
Office symbols and titles were updated to reflect current alignment within the 62d Logistics Readiness
Squadron (LRS). Processing procedures for receipts, turn-ins and shipment of unserviceable tires were
clarified. Attachment 1, Glossary of References and Supporting Documentation was added.


1. Wheel and Tire Shop responsibilities:
   1.1. Storage space will be provided to stock complete peacetime inventory of aircraft wheels and
   tires. Incoming shipments of tires will be made weekly from the Boeing Tire Contractor to the Wheel
   and Tire Shop.
   1.2. The Receiving Section (62 LRS/LGRMR), Wheel and Tire Shop (62 MXS/MXGMTA) and Boe-
   ing will provide personnel to load/off-load tires.

2. Receipt Processing:
    2.1. Receiving Section personnel will process receipts from Boeing produced documentation.
    2.2. Property will due-out release (DOR) to Supply Point organization code 005, shop code 08. Wheel
    and Tire Shop personnel will electronically sign for property in the Supply Asset Tracker System
    (SATS).
    2.3. If receipts are processed, which result in an excess to supply point authorizations, the Flight Ser-
    vice Center (62 LRS/LGRMF) will increase the authorization on appropriate supply point detail.
    2.4. As excess wheels/tires are used, no replenishment will take place until the level drops below
    original approved authorization.

3. Turn-in (TIN) Processing:
    3.1. Flight Service Center personnel will process TIN of wheels and tires. Unserviceable tires will be
    held at the Wheel and Tire Shop for weekly Defense Reutilization and Marketing Office (DRMO)
    and/or Michelin pick-up.
    3.2. Upon receiving shipment for unserviceable tires going to Stock Record Account Number
    (SRAN) EY8388, Flight Service Center personnel will annotate the document(s) with serial number
    of tire and give documentation to Boeing Repair of Reparables (ROR).
    3.3. Vehicle Dispatch (62 LRS/LGRO) will make weekly pickups of unserviceable tires for transfer
    to DRMO. The Wheel and Tire Shop will contact the Flight Service Center if more frequent pick ups
    are required.
