MEMORANDUM FOR ALL ASSIGNED/ATTACHED PERSONNEL
FROM: 6 AMW/CC
SUBJECT: MacDill AFB Guidance Memorandum (GM) for Rogue Systems Requirements
1. By order of the Commander, MacDill AFB this guidance memorandum immediately implements
changes to AFMAN 33-282, Computer Security (COMPUSEC), and CJCSI 6510.01, Information
Assurance (IA) and Support to Computer Network Defense (CND), referred to paragraph 2. Part of these
controls require the implementation of comprehensive network management guidance to ensure that all
systems on the network are updated on a regular basis and dormant systems can be suspended, disabled,
or terminated in a prompt manner. To the extent its directions are inconsistent with other Air Force
publications, the information herein prevails, in accordance with AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms
Management. This GM applies to anyone with access to the Area 52 domain. There are no releasability
restrictions on this publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS), or any updated statement provided by the AF Records Management office (SAF/CIO
A6P). Compliance with this memorandum is mandatory.
2. Pursuant to AFMAN 33-282, Computer Security (COMPUSEC), and CJCSI 6510.01, Information
Assurance (IA) and Support to Computer Network Defense (CND), the following requirements for
disabling and deleting network systems are immediately in effect:
   a. Any NIPRNet or SIPRNet system connected to the 6 AMW infrastructure that has not been
connected to the network and powered on within the past 30 days will be disabled.
   b. Any NIPRNet or SIPRNet system connected to the 6 AMW infrastructure that has not been
connected to the network and powered on within the past 45 days will be removed.
   c. Unit IAOs are responsible for submitting waivers to the 6 Air Mobility Wing Information
Assurance Office for systems that require an extension to the aforementioned 30 and 45 day dormancy
periods. Waiver requests must incorporate a plan to update the systems to attain the current level of
network security before it will be allowed back on the network.
    d. Systems that have been disabled or removed from the network will require users to bring the system
to the 6th Communications Focal Point (CFP) for evaluation, updating, and return to the network.
