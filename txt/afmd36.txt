1. Mission. Air Force Medical Support Agency (AFMSA) oversees the development of AF/SG
policies supporting Air Force expeditionary capabilities and national security strategy. The
Agency provides expert consultative leadership ensuring a cost-effective, modern, and
prevention-based healthcare continuum for 2.6 million beneficiaries worldwide.
2. Command. The AFMSA Commander:
   2.1. Is responsible to the Deputy Surgeon General, Office of the Air Force Surgeon General.
   2.2. Commands all personnel assigned to AFMSA.             Specifically, operational and
   administrative control for SGR, SG5, and SG6 directorates; administrative control only for
   all other directorates.
   2.3. Works directly with directorates in the Air Force Surgeon General’s office and other Air
   Staff, DoD, and major command offices.
   2.4. Partners with Assistant Secretary of Defense for Health Affairs, Secretary of the Air
   Force, Chief of Staff of the Air Force, Department of Veterans Affairs, and numerous
   professional societies and associations, civil organizations, education, industry, press, and
   non-governmental organizations.
3. Responsibilities. The AFMSA Commander:
   3.1. Executes medical modernization efforts in support of the strategic charter of the Air
   Force Medical Service (AFMS).

    3.2. Exercises direction, guidance, and technical management of entire AFMS to capitalize
    on the opportunities to improve processes at all levels.
    3.3. Develops policy, plans, resources, and decisions impacting operational readiness.
