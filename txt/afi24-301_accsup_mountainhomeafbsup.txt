AFI 24-301, 1 November 2008, and ACC Supplement, 14 August 2009, is supplemented as
follows. This publication specifies Mountain Home AFB's (MHAFB) government motor vehicle
(GMV) usage policy. It establishes 366 Fighter Wing (FW) policy on the use of GMVs and is
applicable to all 366 FW and tenant units. Ensure all records (e.g., Command Directed
Investigation (CDI); Report Of Survey (ROS); AF Form 2293, US Government Motor Vehicle
Identification Card; Optional Form (OF) 345, Physical Fitness Inquiry for Motor Vehicle
Operators) created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.
Contact supporting records managers as required. Refer recommended changes and questions
regarding this publication to the office of primary responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication, route AF Forms 847 through the base publications
and forms manager.

1.6.2.1.1. (Added) Individual Travel. Individuals or groups of less than seven passengers will
use privately-owned conveyance or commercial taxi for official transportation to commercial
terminals on a reimbursable basis. Transportation by GMV may be considered when service is
necessary due to emergency situations or to meet security requirements.
1.6.2.1.2. (Added) Group Travel. Vehicle Operations will provide transportation for groups of
seven or more traveling on official orders.
1.6.2.1.3. (Added) Weapons Transport. Personnel required to transport or accompany weapons
are authorized to use GMVs.
1.6.2.2. (Added) When approved, units may use assigned GMVs, Logistics Readiness Squadron
(LRS) U-Drive-It fleet, or request LRS transportation support. Commercial terminal service will
not be provided to or from private quarters. Pick up and drop off must be at an official duty
location on the installation. Visiting temporary duty (TDY) members may be picked up from
assigned lodging when authorized terminal support by LRS when manpower and resources are
available without detriment to mission. In the event manpower is not available, customers will
be requested to use a U-Drive-It GMV, unit assigned GMV, privately owned vehicle or local taxi
support.
1.6.2.3. (Added) Airport support will not be granted based on rank, position or prestige of
member. All members will adhere to the requirements outlined in this supplement and DoD
Regulation 4500.36-R, Management, Acquisition and Use of Motor Vehicles.

1.7.6.1. (Added) Maximum driving time for military and civilian drivers operating GMVs over
public roads/highways are limited to the following:
1.7.6.1.1. Cargo-carrying: No more than 11 hours following 10 consecutive hours off-duty.
1.7.6.1.2. Passenger-carrying: No more than 10 hours following 8 consecutive hours off-duty.
1.7.6.2. (Added) Any on-duty time, regardless of operating a GMV or not, is counted in the
overall driving time. This includes any co-drivers riding “shotgun.”
1.7.6.3. (Added) These measures will ensure safety of passengers, cargo, equipment and drivers
from incidents due to fatigue. Additionally, this policy brings drivers into compliance with 49
Code of Federal Regulation (CFR), Parts 395.3, Maximum driving time for property-carrying
vehicles and CFR 395.5, Maximum driving time for passenger-carrying vehicles.
1.8.4. . (Added) UDI support is generally limited to 72 hours.
3.3.6. (Added) Vehicle misuse is defined as operating or utilizing a government-owned, leased
or rented vehicle and/or equipment for other than "Official Use Only." The determination as to
whether a particular use is for official use shall be done consistent with the guidance outlined in
AFI 24-301, Chapter 3.
3.6.3.1. (Added) If used off base, restrict TDY personnel use of these vehicles to reputable
eating establishments in reasonable proximity to the installation, no further than Mountain
Home, Idaho. This does not include bars, taverns, sports lounges, night clubs or any other
establishment whose primary purpose is providing alcohol.
3.7.2. (Added) Restrict permanent party use of these vehicles to reputable eating establishments
not to include bars, taverns, sports lounges or night clubs. No other variances are extended.
