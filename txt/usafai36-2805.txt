This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards and Decorations
Programs, by establishing procedures to recognize significant contributions and deeds, which
exemplify good character. It governs all responsibilities and procedures to this special award as
defined in Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-2805, Special Trophies and Awards, and lists criteria
for selecting award winners. It applies to all personnel who are or have been assigned to the
United States Air Force Academy (USAFA). This publication does not apply to Air Force
Reserve Command (AFRC) units and the Air National Guard (ANG). Refer recommended
changes and conflicts between this and other publications to USAFA/CWCI, 2354 Fairchild
Drive, Suite 5A12, USAF Academy CO 80840, on Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation
for Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in
this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records,
and are disposed of in accordance with the AFRIMS Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This revision makes minor updates to the OPR, certifying, approving, title in paragraph 2.1., and
administrative changes to the opening paragraph.
1. Purpose.
   1.1. The USAFA Malham M. Wakin Character and Leadership Development Award offers
   the institution an official means of recognizing significant acts that are consistent with the
   Academy definition of character and the Air Force Core Values.

     1.2. The award honors Brigadier General Malham M. Wakin, USAF, Retired, who served as
     Vice-Dean and as head of the Philosophy and Fine Arts Department at USAFA from 1959 –
     1995.
2. Nominating and Selection Procedures.
     2.1. In the fall semester, the Assistant Director, Cadet Character Development, Center for
     Character and Leadership Development (USAFA/CWCD), will solicit nominees from the
     USAFA community at large via a Distribution P e-mail, a base newspaper advertisement, and
     an external USAFA web announcement.
     2.2. In early January, the Director, Center for Character and Leadership Development
     (USAFA/CWC) will preside over an awards board to rank order the nominations. The
     awards board will allow one representative from each of the following mission elements—
     CW, DF, AD, DRU, 10 ABW, PL, 306 FTG. As a minimum, the awards board will be
     comprised of three field grade officers, a civilian equivalent, and one senior non-
     commissioned officer.
     2.3. The Director, Center for Character and Leadership Development (USAFA/CWC) will
     submit to USAFA/CW and USAFA/CC in turn the top three nomination write-ups with his or
     her award selection recommendation.
     2.4. Nomination write-ups will be limited to one page using AF Form 1206, Nomination for
     Award. As a minimum, nominations must include significant contributions to Cadet
     Character and Leadership Development efforts. Nominations must be for an individual or
     team currently or previously assigned to the United States Air Force Academy. A squadron
     commander, department head, or directorate/division chief must indorse nominations.
     Nominations are open to all USAFA assigned personnel, both past and present, to include
     military and civilian individuals or teams. Cadets are not eligible for this award (refer to the
     Outstanding Cadet in Character Award, USAFAI 36-2809, USAF Academy Cadet Awards
     Program).
     2.5. Additionally, the following areas may be included for consideration:
        2.5.1. Conspicuous moral courage.
        2.5.2. Great personal sacrifice.
        2.5.3. Conduct above and beyond the requirements of duty.
        2.5.4. Leadership and professionalism in keeping with the highest ideals of Air Force
        tradition.
     2.6. USAFA/CC makes the final selection of the recipient of the award. Selection of the
     award recipient will be made by 31 January each year.
3. Funds.
     3.1. This award has been endowed by the USAFA Class of 1974 with approximately $1,000
     per year authorized in gift funds for reasonable associated costs such as trophies, plaques,
     travel expenses for award recipient and spouse, meals for all those associated with the
     presentation of the award and any cadets participating in the presentation ceremony.

   3.2. These costs include trophies, plaques, emblems, certificates, and similar items that are
   designed for display purposes. Refer to AFI 65-601, Volume 1, Budget Guidance and
   Procedures, for further guidance.
4. Travel.
   4.1. Military members or Department of the Air Force civilians will travel to the
   presentation ceremonies at government expense if no longer assigned to USAFA.
   4.2. Funded travel of an award recipient’s spouse, nonmilitary member, or non-Department
   of the Air Force civilian must be authorized in accordance with Joint Travel Regulation
   (JTR, Vol 2, Sections C6000-C6002).
5. Presentation. Award presentation will normally be made by the Superintendent
(USAFA/CC) or a representative during the annual National Character and Leadership
Symposium.
