This publication implements Department of Defense Instruction (DODI) 6060.4, Youth Program
(23 Aug 04) and Air Force Instruction (AFI) 34-249 Youth Programs (1 Jun 00). These
requirements and procedures are for personnel residing in military housing on Lajes Field,
Azores, Portugal regarding supervision of their children. Refer recommend changes and
questions about this publication to the OPR using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publications; route AF Form 847 from the field through the publications/forms
manager. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in
accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Revisions were made to add paragraph 4.8. Authorizing the 65 ABW/CC to set a curfew if
needed. This version clarifies updates and streamlines previous guidance and brings it into
compliance with AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management.

                                          Chapter 1

                                     RESPONSIBILITY

1.1. Sponsors will be responsible for the actions, supervision, and whereabouts of their youth
and family members.
1.2. Commanders are responsible for ensuring that all unit members adhere to this instruction.
LAJESFIELDI34-249 20 NOVEMBER 2013                                                                3


                                            Chapter 2

                                   GENERAL GUIDELINES

2.1. Parents are ultimately responsible for their children and no Lajes Field Instruction will
remove that responsibility.
2.2. Children 6 and under should NEVER be left alone in a vehicle.
2.3. Children 7-10 years old may be left unattended in a car for less than 10 minutes, providing
that an adult is within sight, the keys are removed, the engine is off, the handbrake is in use, and
the weather is below 80 degrees Fahrenheit.

                                              Chapter 3

                                           DEFINITIONS

3.1. A child is considered attended when in the presence and under the protection of an adult or
baby-sitter.
3.2. Children who baby-sit for other families must be at least 12 years old, have attended either
the Red Cross or American Heart Association Baby-Sitting Course and be Infant/Child Cardio
Pulmonary Resurrection certified.
     3.2.1. Children 12 years and older can baby-sit, but not overnight.
     3.2.2. Siblings can baby-sit for less than 2 hours if they are 11 years old or in the 6th grade.
3.3. Overnight is between the hours of 0100 to 0530.
3.4. The Lajes Youth Center is considered an approved child-play area for children less than 9
years of age only if the child is enrolled in a direct supervised program.

                                            Chapter 4

                                         SUPERVISION

4.1. Birth – 5 years old
   4.1.1. Will be attended at all times by a an adult or baby-sitter.
4.2. Age 6-9 years old
   4.2.1. Will be attended at all times except as follows:
   4.2.2. While traveling to and from school.
   4.2.3. While playing on approved playgrounds during daylight hours or within child-play
   areas that are within sight of residence.
   4.2.4. An adult or baby-sitter must physically check on their children at least hourly when
   they are unattended.
4.3. Age 9 years old
   4.3.1. May walk to/from Lajes Youth Center for open recreation/supervised programs
4.4. Age 10- 11 years old
   4.4.1. May be left unattended for periods not to exceed 2 hours between the hours of 0700-2200.
   4.4.2. May travel, with parent’s permission, on errands to on-base facilities (e.g. library,
   shoppette, etc.).
   4.4.3. May travel to/from Lajes Youth Center for open recreation/supervised program.
4.5. Age 12 – 15 years old
   4.5.1. May be left unattended, but not overnight.
   4.5.2. Must be checked on by an adult every 6 hours.
4.6. Age 16 - 17 years old
   4.6.1. May be left unattended while sponsor is on short temporary duty or leave not to
   exceed 7 consecutive days.
   4.6.2. Adult supervision/guardian must be available to make periodic checks.
   4.6.3. Must be provided with emergency telephone numbers, and knowledge of how to
   contact parents.
   4.6.4. Adult supervisor/guardian must have Power of Attorney for all medical care, etc.

4.7. Parents and guardians will ensure dependents under 18 years old maintain quiet hours
on-base and Lajes Field Housing from 2200 to 0730 Sunday through Thursday, 2300 to 0900 on
holidays and weekends.
4.8. 65 ABW/CC has the authorization to set a curfew for all Lajes Field youth when in the
best interest of the base and Air Force.
