This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Air and Space
Maintenance. This instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 63-1001,
Aircraft Structural Integrity Program, AFI 21-101 Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance
Management, AFI 21-101, Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) SUP 1, Aerospace Equipment
Maintenance Management and Technical Order (TO) 1C-130-101, Implementation of C-130
Series Aircraft Usage Report. It provides guidance and procedures on monitoring actual aircraft
usage and for documenting crack and corrosion findings, to obtain data for updating inspection
and maintenance technical orders, is in place and that the program is properly monitored. It
applies to all members assigned to the 440th Airlift Wing. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained In Accordance With (IAW) Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication, directly to 440 MXG/CC, 2541 Surveyor Street, Room 101, Pope AFB, NC 28308-
2409.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This instruction has been extensively revised and must be completely reviewed.
1. Maintenance Reporting Requirements:
 2                                                             440AWI21-106 8 AUGUST 2013


     1.1. Maintenance actions that require reporting will be identified in the specific work cards,
     TO and Time Compliance Technical Order (TCTO). For example, 1C-130A-6, 1C-130A-
     6WC-15, Isochronal Inspection (ISO) work cards, 1C-130A-3, and 1C-130A-36. In addition
     to inspections, if a defect is found that requires a repair against the following systems,
     11XXX primary structure and skin and 13XXX landing gear, the repair action must also be
     reported in Inspection, Corrosion and Repair Reporting (ICARR).
     1.2. After completion of inspection or repair, Aircraft Structural Integrity Program (ASIP)
     and ICARR inputs will be submitted no later than the end of the next duty day.
     1.3. At deployed locations, data may be entered on an Air Force approved network laptop or
     notebook computer with AIRCAT/ICARR loaded on them. Data may be uploaded to ICARR
     at deployed locations if connectivity is available, or upon return to home station.
2. Responsibilities:
     2.1. The ASIP project officer will reside in the Fabrication Flight Office and will insure
     accurate and timely inspection and reporting by the ASIP monitors in accordance with the
     applicable Technical Orders mentioned in paragraph 1.1.
        2.1.1. The ASIP project officer will recommend appointment of ASIP monitors to the
        MXG/CC from the applicable work centers (Aircraft Structural Maintenance, Non-
        Destructive Inspection (NDI) and Isochronal Inspection Section.
        2.1.2. The ASIP project officer will maintain a continuity binder to include the 440
        MXG appointment letter of both project officer and ASIP monitors and training
        information. Training on the use of ICARR software will be conducted by on-the-job
        training and documented on the users AF IMT 797, Job Qualification Standard (JQS)
        Continuation/Command. A training syllabus will be developed by the project officer so
        that consistent training can be conducted by user sections.
        2.1.3. The ASIP project officer will work with the Client Support Administrator to
        assure the most current software is downloaded for the ASIP program.
        2.1.4. The ASIP project officer will coordinate with the mission design series major
        command office of primary responsibility and ASIP manager to obtain feedback on data
        capture rates and to implement corrective actions as needed to achieve the required rates.
        2.1.5. The ASIP project officer or ASIP monitors for maintenance will report completion
        of ICARR inputs to the inspection section Flight Chief or representative during the post
        dock briefing commensurate with the conclusion of each aircraft Isochronal (ISO)
        inspection.
     2.2. The Plans, Scheduling & Documentation office will review and schedule in the ISO
     package all NDI requirements and will identify them as ASIP requirements from the 1C-
     130A-6, “Aircraft Scheduled Inspection and Aircraft Maintenance Instructions” and
     associated ISO work cards in accordance with AFI 21-101.
     2.3. Quality Assurance will monitor the ASIP program and perform quarterly inspections to
     ensure compliance and operational inputs into the AIRCAT system.
