This publication supplements USAF Standard Base Supply Customer Procedures Logistics
Readiness Squadron's/Supply Activity Customer's Procedures. It applies to all Air Force,
contractor operated, Air Force Reserve, and Air Force Guard organizations supported by Tyndall
Base Supply unless otherwise noted in contract or support agreement terms or conditions.
Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the     Air      Force      Records        Disposition     Schedule       (RDS)       located     at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm . Send recommended changes and
questions about this publication to 325 MSG/LRD using the AF IMT 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication. This publication requires collecting and maintaining information
protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by 10 U.S.C. 9832 and E.O. 9397. System of
records notice F023 AF IL C applies

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

AFMAN 23-110, Volume 2, Part 13, Chapter 1, is supplemented as follows:

Summary of changes includes procedures for items not managed or stocked in base supply, the
process of issue requests with CAMS and SBSS, the purpose of added TAFB forms, property
withdraw authorizations, storage location of deficiency reports, the Flight Service Center
processes of deficiency reports, purchases and equipment requests through GPC, certification
and proficiency training for AFSC 2S0XX, training requirements for new custodians, and re-
appointed equipment custodians will receive briefs about the supplements, local policy, and local
requirements.
1.3.5.2. Base Service Store and Tool Issue items are no longer managed or stocked in Base
Supply. Obtain these items by using GPC or AF Form 9, Request for Purchase, procedures.
Individual Equipment Function (IE) items are stocked in Base Supply. IE is operated by the
contractor IAW Performance Work Statement.
1.3.5.3. Customers with CAMS process their own issue requests through CAMS. Organizations
with remote SBSS terminals and personnel assigned with SBSS User-ID’s process their issue
requests direct in SBSS. Customers with CAMS will not process issue requests for weapons
system parts using Activity Code “X”, all issue requests must be Activity Code “J”.
1.3.5.4. The 325th Maintenance Squadron manages munitions commodities.
1.7. Logistics Readiness Squadron/Supply Activity Forms. The following forms are needed
to obtain service and support from the Logistics Readiness Squadron/supply activity.

Table 1.5. (Added) TYNDALLAFB Forms.

     Number        Title
     14            Organization Record Load/Change/Delete/Request
     17            Delegate/Delete Property Custodians
     18            Request for Issue of Individual Equipment/Clothing
     22            Reserve Location Cross Reference for Bench Stock
1.9. Use TAFB Form 14 to request a new organization code, or to change or delete an existing
organization code.
1.9.4.1. (Added) The Vehicle Operations, pick-up and delivery function operates on a sweep
basis refer to AFMAN 23-110, Vol 2, Pt 2, Ch 11, TAFB Sup 1 for further guidance.
1.10.3.1. Use TAFB Form 17 to appoint or change primary or alternate equipment custodians.
1.10.5.1. Individuals authorized by their organizational commander to withdraw property will
have access to the Base Supply last look staging area located in Building 266. Property will be
identified to a contracted employee for organizational reuse. Customers may browse the Defense
Logistics Agency Disposition Services worldwide inventory by accessing internet website:
http://www.dispositionservices.dla.mil/. See AFMAN 23-110, Vol 2, Part 2, Chap 9,
TAFBSUP 1 for AFREP second look procedures.
1.12.1. Deficiency reports (formerly MDR/QDR exhibits) are stored in Flight Service Center,
pending final disposition.
1.14.1. Local purchase supplies and equipment requests under $2,500 are purchased with a
Government Purchase credit card (GPC). When purchasing multiple quantities of the same item
through a pre-priced, approved government contract, GPC can be used to buy up to an extended
AFMAN23-110V2PT13CH1_TYNDALLAFBSUP 2 AUGUST 2012                                           3


value limit of $25,000. For all other items with either an extended or unit cost over $2,500,
prepare and submit AF Form 9, Request for Purchase, through electronic means. Regardless of
the procurement method used (GPC or AF Form 9), all equipment requests must be coordinated
through Customer Support, Equipment Management Function to ensure proper accountability
requirements are established. GPC purchased hazardous materials must be pre-approved and
authorized in AF-ESOHMIS. Contact CEV, environmental (3-8442), for assistance when the
item is not approved in AF-ESOHMIS. Also, use GPC to purchase non-weapon system GSA
items. GSA weapon system item requests are accepted and processed in Base Supply. Weapon
System parts are identified in USAF Technical Orders, Parts Manuals, or Illustrated Parts
Breakdown.
1.17.2.2. Certification and proficiency training for AFSC 2S0XX, Supply Specialty is
accomplished at the unit level.
1.17.3.4. All new custodians will complete Block III custodian training CBT on AFIT website
(https://online.afit.edu/msg). Custodians must pass with 85% and provide Customer Support,
Equipment Management Function with the Block III training certificate issued from the AFIT
provided Block III CBT.
1.17.3.5.4. Customer Support, Equipment Management Function briefs command supplements,
local policy, and local requirements to re-appointed equipment custodians.
