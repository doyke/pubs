This instruction implements Air Force Publishing Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Manage Aerospace
Equipment Maintenance. It extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101, Aircraft
and Equipment Maintenance Management. It outlines the procedures and responsibilities for the
Dropped Object Prevention and Inspection Program (DOPP) and applies to all 916th Air
Refueling Wing (ARW) personnel who perform their duties on or near the flight line. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the 916 AMDF/SGAS using the
Air Force AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Route AF 847s through
the appropriate chain of command. Ensure all records created as prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rds

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This interim change introduces the 4th Air Force Dropped Object Report as Attachment 2.
Additionally, it includes inspection requirements for personnel performing pre-flight, post-flight,
thru-flight and/or hourly post-flight inspections. The OG/CC is no longer identified as a co-chair
during quarterly DOPP committee meetings. The MXG/CC or a designated representative will
chair. In addition to the content change, the certifier of this instruction has changed; the approval
authority has changed, and referenced publication/instruction dates have changed. A margin bar
(|) indicates newly revised material.
1. Responsibilities.

     1.1. The 916th Air Refueling Wing Vice Commander (ARW/CV) will designate a Wing
     Dropped Object Prevention and Inspection Program (DOPP) monitor in writing. The DOPP
     monitor will report a summary at the quarterly DOPP meeting in accordance with AFI 21-
     101, Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance Management.
     1.2. The 916th Maintenance Group Commander (MXG/CC) will ensure that each
     organization assigned to the 916 ARW that removes, installs, or performs maintenance on
     aircraft panels and doors complies with this program.
     1.3. The 916th Maintenance Operations Flight-Training will ensure that the DOPP training
     video is available for all personnel required to remove, install or perform maintenance on
     aircraft panels and doors.
     1.4. The 916th Maintenance Group Quality Assurance will investigate each DOPP incident
     and submit reports as required in accordance with AFI 21-101. DOP reports submitted to 4
     AF/A4MY will use the format in Attachment 2.
2. Procedures.
     2.1. The following procedures apply to 916 ARW personnel that remove, install, or perform
     maintenance on aircraft panels and doors.
     2.2. Personnel must inspect all aircraft panels and doors that are removed, for serviceability
     of fasteners, latches and receptacles. Enter all discrepancies pertaining to defective fastening
     devices, panels and doors in the appropriate sections of the AFTO Form 781A, Aircraft
     Maintenance Discrepancy and Work Document.
     2.3. Enter a Red X in the AFTO 781A whenever panels and doors are opened. This Red X
     requires both “Corrected By” and “Inspected By” signatures. Panels and doors opened
     during Technical Order 1C-135-6WC-1 inspection need not be placed on Red X as long as
     they are closed immediately after the inspection item is completed.
     2.4. Personnel performing pre-flight/post-flight/thru-flight/hourly post-flight inspections will
     follow DOPP inspection requirements in accordance with 1C-135-6WC-1.
3. Dropped Object.
     3.1. In the event of a dropped object incident the following will apply:
        3.1.1. The affected flight must notify the Maintenance Operations Center (MOC) and
        squadron supervision immediately upon discovery.
        3.1.2. Maintenance Operation Control will notify the 916th Maintenance Group
        Commander, 916th Operations Group Commander, 916 ARW DOPP monitor, 916 Air
        Refueling Wing Safety Office, and the Command Post. The appropriate documentation
        will be entered in the appropriate sections of the AFTO Form 781A.
        3.1.3. MOC will report the dropped object incident in accordance with AFI 21-101.
4. Quarterly DOPP Committee Meeting.
     4.1. The DOPP committee will meet during the primary Unit Training Assembly (UTA)
     during the months of October, January, April, and July. This meeting will be chaired by the
     MXG/CC or designated representative. This meeting will be conducted in conjunction with
     the quarterly Foreign Object Damage Prevention Program Meeting.
