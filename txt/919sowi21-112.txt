This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Air and Space
Maintenance. This instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101,
Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance Management Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC)
Supplement (SUP), and Air Force Occupational Safety and Health Standard (AFOSHSTD) 91-
100, Aircraft Flight Line - Ground Operations and Activities. It provides guidelines in support
of the maintenance safety program. It applies to all 919th Special Operations Wing (919 SOW)
personnel and implements AFOSH standards and Technical Order requirements. Supervisors at
all levels will ensure strict compliance with this operating instruction. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the Air Force Form (AF Form) 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF
Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://afrims.amc.af.mil/.

1. Responsibilities: Commanders, Superintendents, Functional Managers and Supervisors are
responsible for ensuring personnel are familiar with and comply with this instruction. Only
properly trained and qualified personnel shall operate hangar doors.
2. Procedures:
2.1. Powered/Non-Powered hangar doors in the Fuel System Repair Hangar building 3087,
Flight Line Hangars buildings 3020, 3029, and 3025 will only be operated by authorized
personnel.

     2.2. Buildings 3087, 3029, and 3020 main hangar door controls (outside) will be covered
     and locked. The base Fire Department and building custodians will have a key to the locks.
     The base Fire Department will be the only authorized user of the outside controls.
3. Training:
     3.1. Operator training is accomplished using a lesson plan. As a minimum, qualification
     training will include hangar door hazards, emergency procedures, and hangar door
     operations.
     3.2. Hangar door awareness training will be conducted for all personnel. This training will
     be conducted as part of the annual work center AFOSH briefing in accordance with IAW
     AFI 91-301, Air Force Occupational and Environmental Safety, Fire Prevention and Health
     (AFOSH) Program.
     3.3. Operators will be identified utilizing Integrated Maintenance Data System
     (IMDS)/Graphical User Interface (GUI), Aviation Resource Management System (ARMS) or
     any other approved electronic password controlled system. Document hangar door training
     as prescribed in AFI 91-301.
     3.4. Post detailed hangar door operating procedures at each hangar door control panel (non-
     powered doors included), to include step-by-step procedures (i.e. tail doors, etc.), list the
     name of the building custodian, and display list of authorized operators for the doors.
