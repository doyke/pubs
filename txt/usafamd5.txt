This mission directive prescribes the mission, organization, and responsibilities of the United
States Air Force (USAF) Academy Preparatory School, Headquarters United States Air Force
Academy, Colorado. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the AF Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Changes were made to
Roles and Responsibilities to more accurately reflect the direction of DoDD 1322.22, Service
Academies, and redundancies were removed.
1. Mission. To motivate, prepare, and evaluate selected candidates in an educational, military,
moral, and physical environment, to perform successfully, and enhance diversity at the United
States Air Force Academy (USAFA).
2. Command. The Commander, United States Air Force Academy Preparatory School:
   2.1. Reports directly to the Superintendent, United States Air Force Academy.
   2.2. Exercises administrative and operational control for assigned faculty, staff members,
   and preparatory school cadet candidates.
 2                                                             USAFAMD5 25 JANUARY 2012


     2.3. Is the supported commander for cadet candidate development.
3. Roles and Responsibilities. The Commander, United States Air Force Academy Preparatory
School:
     3.1. Prepares cadet candidates for academics at the Air Force Academy through instruction
     in Mathematics, English, Science, and other necessary subject matter.
     3.2. Prepares cadet candidates for the military environment at the Air Force Academy
     through instruction in military knowledge, skills, and leadership as well as the principles and
     precepts of the Honor Code.
     3.3. Prepares cadet candidates to develop and maintain an appropriate level of physical
     fitness, strength and skill through physical education, fitness testing, and athletic
     competition.
