This instruction implements AFPD 31-4, Information Security and AFI 31-401, Information
Security Program Management and interfaces with DoD 5105.21-M-1, Department of Defense
Sensitive Compartmented Information Administrative Security Manual and AFI 91-302, Air
Force Occupational Environmental Safety, Fire Protection, and Health (AFOSH) Standards. It
establishes responsibilities and procedures for operating the document destruction system for
classified waste generated on Goodfellow AFB. This instruction applies to all personnel in units
and staff offices on Goodfellow AFB who generate, store, or otherwise handle classified waste
material and those individuals assigned to destruct detail under the supervision of squadron
facilitator. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained according to AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records and disposed of in
accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Air Force
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS), located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847 from the field
through the appropriate functional chain of command. This publication may be supplemented at
any level, but all direct supplements must be routed to the OPR of this publication for
coordination prior to certification and approval.

1. Responsibilities.
   1.1. Special Security Office (SSO).

        1.1.1. The OPR for the disintegrator equipment to include physical security,
        maintenance, and periodic inspections for any possible safety hazards.
        1.1.2. Maintains all necessary equipment including ear and eye protection.
        1.1.3. Schedules disintegrator usage.
        1.1.4. Trains all Document Destruction System (DDS) operators on proper operating
        procedures.
     1.2. DDS Operators.
        1.2.1. Must be present at the Document Destruction Facility (DDF) during the entire
        destruction operation.
        1.2.2. Train workers on assigned duties.
        1.2.3. Ensure Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is serviceable.
        1.2.4. Ensure the use of proper safety equipment and adhere to the procedures outlined in
        this instruction.
        1.2.5. Report any equipment malfunctions immediately to SSO personnel.
        1.2.6. Ensure complete destruction of all classified material.
     1.3. DDS Workers.
        1.3.1. Comply with operator or supervisor and Occupational Safety and Health Act
        (OSHA) guidance.
        1.3.2. Comply with PPE requirements.
        1.3.3. Report safety, fire, and health deficiencies to DDS operator.
        1.3.4. Consider the safety of other workers while performing tasks.
2. PPE.
     2.1. The following protective equipment must be worn while operating the DDS:
        2.1.1. Ear plugs or noise suppression headsets.
        2.1.2. Safety goggles or face shield are optional.
     2.2. SSO maintains and provides PPE. All equipment will be inspected for serviceability
     before being issued to operators and workers.
     2.3. DDS operator and workers will ensure safety equipment is in serviceable condition prior
     to use. When classified destruction operation is completed, equipment will be thoroughly
     cleaned and returned to proper storage.
     2.4. DDS operator will maintain an operable and accessible source of communication prior
     to operation for use in event of emergency.
     2.5. DDS operator will conduct pre- and post-operational inspections of the entire system for
     potential mechanical hazards. Bolts and other fasteners that anchor the DDS machine to the
     floor will also be visually inspected for evidence of wear and/or movement.

   2.6. DDS operator will know the location and check the serviceability of fire extinguishers
   and instruct workers on proper usage.
3. Training and Documentation. Operators and workers will be trained per AFI 91-302
utilizing the AFOSH Job Indoctrination Training Guide, prior to being allowed to operate
equipment in destruction of material. Training will be documented and maintained on each
person operating the DDS.
4. Operating Procedures.
   4.1. General Safety Procedures.
      4.1.1. In case of emergency, initiate shut-down by pushing in any of the five red
      emergency shut-down tabs.
      4.1.2. Always stand to the left or right of the conveyor incline. Do not stand at the end
      of the conveyor belt.
      4.1.3. Do not attempt to clear any jammed part of the DDS machine while any
      component of the machine is running.
      4.1.4. In the event of a jam and/or erratic operation, immediately implement shutdown
      procedures.
      4.1.5. The DDS makes a lot of noise when in operation. Wear hearing protection at all
      times and remain alert.
      4.1.6. In the event the DDS requires major maintenance, Lock-out/Tag-out procedures
      will be initiated by the SSO.
   4.2. Startup Procedures.
      4.2.1. Ensure all five emergency tabs are pulled out. All emergency tabs will be labeled
      and personally identified to each operator during the initial training.
      4.2.2. Ensure the entry door, compactor doors, and main disintegrator doors of buildings
      are shut before operating the disintegrator.
      4.2.3. Insert key #1 into the compactor control box and turn to the “On” position.
      4.2.4. Press and hold the green “Forward” button for 5-7 seconds, until the compactor
      motor starts up.
      4.2.5. Insert key #2 into the master control box and turn to the “Auto” position.
      4.2.6. Turn the primary power switch to the master control box to the “On” position.
      4.2.7. Turn the exhaust fan on.
      4.2.8. Press the green “Auto Start” button on the master control box.
      4.2.9. To start the conveyor belt, press the green “Start Conveyor” button on the master
      control box. To turn the conveyor belt off, press the red “Stop Conveyor” button on the
      master control box. These functions can be preformed regularly throughout the
      disintegration process.
   4.3. Loading Procedures.

       4.3.1. Sort all materials prior to placing on conveyor belt for destruction on the sorting
       table.
       4.3.2. Approved materials include paper products as well as cardboard.
       4.3.3. Unapproved materials include hard metals, paper clips/clamps, laminates,
       magnetic tape, film, compact disks, staples, and ring binders. If the material is in
       question, contact the SSO.
    4.4. Shutdown Procedures.
       4.4.1. Clear all material from the conveyor belt 3-5 minutes prior to continuing shutdown
       procedures.
       4.4.2. Press the red “Shut Down” button on the master control box. Disintegrator will
       continue to run for an additional 3 minutes before complete shutdown.
       4.4.3. Turn key #2 to the “Off” position and remove from master control box.
       4.4.4. Turn key #1 to the “Auto” mode and depress the shaker button 7-10 times.
       4.4.5. Turn key #1 to the “Off” position and remove from the computer control box.
    4.5. Cleanup Procedures. Check to ensure there is no classified material on the floor or in
    baskets. Sweep the floors in bays 1 & 2; remove waste from shaker and all trash from all
    waste containers.
