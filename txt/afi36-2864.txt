This instruction implements AFPD 36-28, Awards and Decorations, for the O’Malley award. It
provides selection criteria and nomination procedures for the General Jerome F. O’Malley
award, honoring the best airborne reconnaissance crew in the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication was inactive from 12 September 2011 and has been reinstated 14 June 2012.

1. O’Malley Award. This Air Force Association (AFA) award, in honor of the late General
Jerome F. O’Malley, a reconnaissance pioneer and former commander of Tactical Air
Command, is given to the best overall airborne reconnaissance crew in the Air Force. HQ
ACC/DO administers the award and selects the finalist for AFA. One nomination submission is
authorized for each of the following platforms: RC-135S/U/V/W, U-2 and SENIOR SCOUT. For
platforms with aircrews from multiple wings/squadrons, there will be one package, fully
coordinated with all units. The 55 WG will be the lead for RC-135 submissions, and 67 IOW
will be lead for SENIOR SCOUT submissions. The 9 WG will be lead for U-2 submissions.
2. Award Timetables. Nominations are based on achievements occurring during the previous
calendar year and are submitted to HQ ACC/DOTR NLT 1 March of the current year.
Incomplete or late nominations will not be accepted for consideration.
3. Selection Criteria. Award selection is based on a significant achievement in a single aerial
reconnaissance event.
4. Nomination Packages:
     4.1. The WG/CC or designee should submit an unclassified narrative on an AF Form 1206.
     The package will be sent via email directly to the HQ ACC OPR.
     4.2. Include the following information in each package:
        4.2.1. Name of the reconnaissance aircraft and the associated units flying the particular
        mission described in the narrative.
        4.2.2. List the names of all mission crewmembers selected for the award described in the
        narrative. Crewmembers must have participated in the event described in the narrative.
        The total list of names will not exceed the number of eligible crew positions (students
        will not be included).
5. Selection Process:
     5.1. HQ ACC/DO will chair the selection board. The board is comprised of selected O-5s/O-
     6s within ACC with a background in reconnaissance.
     5.2. COMACC will announce the O’Malley award winner via message.
     5.3. After notification of the winning crew, the selected wing will submit a 50-word citation
     describing the event. A list attached to the citation will include the names of the
     crewmembers selected for the award.

6. Award Recognition:
   6.1. The O’Malley award will be presented at the annual AFA convention. A maximum of
   six individuals from the winning crew will be invited to attend the award presentation. The
   selected unit is responsible for all TDY expenses. The award will be presented to one pre-
   designated individual from the winning crew
   6.2. The winners of the O’Malley Award are authorized to wear the Air Force recognition
   ribbon.
