SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This updates the initial publication of AFI 36-2010, which streamlines the instructions for officials imple-
menting the AFJROTC program. Major changes in this instruction include the requirement for AFOATS/
JR to evaluate annually the operation, administration, and effectiveness of the overall AFJROTC program
and the individual units for contractual compliance, cost, and performance objectives. Additionally, adds
the requirement for AFRS/CC to en sure advanced placement for JROTC students en listing in the Air
Force as prescribed by DoDI 1205.13.


1. Program Description and Purpose. AFJROTC is a congressionally mandated program designed to
instill in students within secondary educational institutions the values of citizenship, service to the United
States, personal responsibility, and a sense of accomplishment. Overall mission is to “Develop citizens of
character dedicated to serving their nation and community.”

2. Program Funding and Staffing:
    2.1. The Air Force jointly funds and administers the AFJROTC program with the high schools, con-
    sistent with 10 U.S.C. 2031.
    2.2. AFJROTC instructors ar e retired active duty commissioned and noncommissioned Air Force
    officers. Instructors are hired by the individual high schools as school employees.

JROTC—The Junior Reserve Of ficer Training Corps program conducted at the se condary educational
institution school (high school).
JROTC UNIT—An organized group of JROTC students a nd faculty at one secondary educational
institution school (high school).
MILITARY INSTITUTE SCHOOLS—Military schools at the secondary level of instruction that:
      Require a 4-year course in military training.
      Organize their military students as a Corps of Cadets under constantly maintained military
      discipline.
      Require all members of the Corps, including those members enrolled in military training, to
      be in uniform when on campus.
      Have as their objective development of student character through military training,
      regulation of student conduct in accordance with the principles of military discipline, and
      meeting of military standards similar to those maintained at Military Service academies.
MILITARY JUNIOR COLLEGES—Military schools that provide high school and college instruction,
but do not confer baccalaureate degrees.
MULTIPLE JUNIOR ROTC UNITS—Any group of units of one Se rvice organized and sponsored
under one school system in which JROTC is conducted concurrently in more than one school.
