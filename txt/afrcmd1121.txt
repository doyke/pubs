This publication implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 10-1, Mission Directives. It
delineates the mission, command relationships, and responsibilities of the Air Force Reserve
Aeromedical Evacuation (AE) Formal Training Unit (FTU). It does not apply to Regular Air
Force (RegAF) or Air National Guard (ANG) units. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW AFMAN 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of IAW the Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS). Contact supporting records managers as
required. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain of
command. This publication may be supplemented at any level, but all direct supplements must be
routed to the OPR of this publication prior to certification and approval. The authorities to waive
wing/unit level requirements in this publication are identified with a Tier (“T-0, T-1, T-2, T-3”)
number following the compliance statement. See AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms
Management, for a description of the authorities associated with the Tier numbers. Submit
requests for waivers through the chain of command to the appropriate Tier waiver approval
authority, or alternately, to the Publication OPR for non-tiered compliance items.

1. Mission: To operate the Air Force Reserve Command’s (AFRC) Aeromedical Initial
Qualification (AEIQ) Program. Provide formal aeromedical operational training and
qualification, achieving standardization, efficiency and timely upgrade for AE crewmembers
(AECM) to attain basic aircrew qualification (BAQ) IAW AFI 11-2AEV1 and AFI 11-2AEV2.
Training conducted at the FTU is structured to develop initial AE crewmember capability to
execute global patient movement.
2. Command. The AFRC AE FTU is organized as Detachment 1, Headquarters 440th
Operations Group, located at Pope AFB, NC. The Det 1, HQ 440 OG Commander (CC):
     2.1. Reports to the 440th OG/CC.
     2.2. Exercises administrative control over assigned personnel.
3. Responsibilities.
     3.1. Execute commander’s responsibilities as outlined in AFI 1-2, Commanders
     Responsibilities.
     3.2. Provides oversight and execution of the AEIQ Program instructors and support staff.
     Additional part-time forces from AFRC AES’ or AFRC/A3 are periodically attached for the
     purpose of staff augmentation during course execution. Other forces may attach from the
     active and/or Air National Guard component AES’ to augment staffing.
     3.3. May communicate directly with the HQ AFRC/A3 functional area managers, Lead
     Command (AMC/A3TM/A3V), and AFRC NAFs or AES representatives, and other agencies
     as appropriate on matters relating to training, qualification, inspection, and safety.
4. Mobilization. AFRC forces are under the administrative control (ADCON) of AFRC/CC.
When activated or mobilized (e.g. under 10 U.S.C. §§ 12301(a), 12302, 12304, 12304a, or
12304b), combatant command authority (COCOM) transfers to the combatant commander to
which the forces are assigned/attached and operational control (OPCON) transfers to the
operational chain of command established by that commander.

                        AE FTU ORGANIZATION AND FUNCTIONS

A2.1. Functions by Directorate/Staff Office:
     A2.1.1. Commander. The Commander and Program Director is a field grade officer Flight
     Nurse (FN) possessing Flight Examiner certification. This individual is responsible for the
     overall FTU Program operations and course management supervision of Cadre Staff,
     approval of course materials, coordination with AFRC/A3T and AFRC/A3V staff and AMC
     (lead command), liaison with aeromedical evacuation squadrons, OG/OGV, NAF and
     external agencies/components regarding students and flight instructor/flight examiner (FI/FE)
     augmentation staff direction, planning, and course development and execution. Works
     directly with the host wing and operations group for all facility, Information Systems,
     Operations Support, First Sergeant for serious issues and Career Advisor needs. Maintains
     readiness, flying and RSVP currencies/qualifications.
     A2.1.2. Staff FN Examiner/BAQ Course Manager. Primary POC for AFRC FTU
     Program course execution for flight nurse (FN) trainees. Serves as FTU representative to host
     operations group Stan/Eval function as required. Reviews and integrates applicable
     publications to include 40-, 41-, Series, AFI 11-2AE V1, V2, V3, AFI 11-202 V1, V2, AFI
     11-401 and AFI 11-402 and applicable flight manuals/publications into FTU Program for
     initial upgrade basic aircrew qualification (BAQ) of FN trainees (AFSC X46F3). Highly
     experienced in the mission qualification training and flight evaluation. Requires AECM
     currency/qualification in Flight Examiner certification, government vehicles licensure and
     flight line access badge. IT systems experience w/Windows, SMS/GDSS 2/
     TRAC2ES/PMITS knowledge required. Patriot Excalibur (PEX) required. Oversees the FN
     BAQ Course, while also training and qualification issues relative to Stan/Eval Program
     requirements.
     A2.1.3. FTU Superintendent/Stan Eval Program Representative. Senior X4N071-
     Serves as FTU representative to host operations group Stan/Eval function as required as they
     relate to interface between AFRC FTU Program and host operations group Stan Eval
     Program. Incumbent is also designated as the liaison officer (NCO) at the squadron/
     Coordinates with FTU Program Director and FTU Cadre Staff, FI/FE FTU augmentation
     staff, and supported aeromedical evacuation units regarding AECM training/qualification
     documentation for FTU trainees. Conducts AET evaluations. Validates documentation
     submitted by trainees, students and FTU Staff for inclusion in official aircrew
     training/qualification records. Prepares reports, analyzes trends, and represents FTU with
     FTU Program Director to HHQ, GMAJCOM, components, SAV/ASEV representatives.
     Briefs host OG/OGV on status of Stan Eval issues as they relate to the FTU Program.
     Reviews applicable pubs, communications and guidance provided by HHQ and
     AMC/A3T/V.
     A2.1.4. Training Program Manager. Flight Examiner, X4N071; Primary POC for FTU
     Program course execution for Aeromedical Evacuation Technician (AET) trainees. Reviews
     and integrates applicable publications to include 40-, 41-. Series, AFI 11-2AE V1, V2, V3,
     AFI 11-202 V1, V2, AFI 11-401 and AFI 11-402 and applicable flight manuals/publications
     into FTU Program for initial upgrade to basic aircrew qualification (BAQ) of AET trainees

  A2.1.5. Squadron Aviation Resource Management (SARM). Management of FTU

  from HQ AFRC, NAF or AE squadrons from traditional reservists or air reserve technician
