The purpose of this instruction ensures compliance with Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 90-
9, Operational Risk Management (ORM) and allows the ORM Steering Committee to implement
ORM and VPP requirements throughout AEDC. Ensure that all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 36-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air
Force         Records         Disposition       Schedule        (RDS)         located      at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF IMT847, Recommendation for Changes of
Publication; route AF IMT 847 through the appropriate functional chain of command. This
instruction is applicable to all DoD employees, Military and Civilian, assigned to AEDC
organizations. Refer recommended changes and questions

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

The OSHA’s Volunteer Protection Program (VPP) material is included in this instruction. The
areas eliminated include the redundancy between this instruction and the AF and AFMC
Instructions. The changes included in this instruction align this instruction with the format
requirements in AEDCI 33-100.
1. Scope. This instruction applies to all AEDC DoD personnel.
2. Responsibility and Authority

     2.1. The AEDC Commander has overall responsibility for the Center’s ORM and VPP
     program, while the AEDC ORM/VPP Steering Committee is the Commander’s agent to
     provide leadership and management of the ORM & VPP programs. The AEDC ORM
     Steering Committee is chaired by the AEDC Executive Director (AEDC/CA). AEDC/SE is
     the AEDC ORM Advisor on the AEDC ORM/VPP Steering Committee.
2.2. AEDC ORM/VPP Steering Committee. This committee will develop, approve, and
integrate a comprehensive, mission supportive ORM & VPP program within AEDC. The
chairperson of the AEDC ORM/VPP Steering Committee will oversee the development,
integration, and sustainment of the AEDC ORM & VPP programs. The chairperson will also
represent the AEDC Commander (AEDC/CC) on the Steering Committee. The committee will
incorporate the responsibilities identified in AFMCI 90-902, paragraph 5.4 and:

        2.2.1. Include sufficient Group/Directorate/Office representation to assure all center
        personnel are included in the ORM & VPP program. As a minimum, the committee will
        include representatives from the 704 Test Group (704 TG), 704 Maintenance Group (704
        MXG), 704 Test Systems Group (704 TESG), 704 Mission Support Group (704 MSG),
        Personnel Division (704 DP), Plans and Programs (704 XP), Financial Management
        (FM), Contracting Director (PK), Safety Office (SE), and ATA representation (as
        appropriate). Other DoD directorate and offices will be included as required.
        2.2.2. Ensure ORM & VPP principles are integrated into all AEDC operations/activities,
        where applicable.
        2.2.3. Develop a specific ORM & VPP policy, requirements, and an overall strategy
        necessary to integrate and sustain ORM & VPP requirements at AEDC.
        2.2.4. Obtain Level 2 or 3 ORM training identified in AFMC 90-902.
2.2.5. Maintain competency in the OSHA, DoD, AF, and AFMC ORM guidance. (See
Attachment 2 as guidance.)

     2.3. The AEDC ORM Advisor will:
        2.3.1. Perform administrative functions and handle day-to-day activities of the AEDC
        ORM and VPP programs.
        2.3.2. Work with the AFMC ORM Program Manager to ensure compliance with the
        AFMC ORM program.
        2.3.3. Conduct periodic audits to ensure implementation of ORM & VPP requirements in
        all applicable AEDC operations.
        2.3.4. Annually audit the AEDC ORM & VPP programs to assure compliance with
        OSHA, DoD, AF, and AFMC guidance.
     2.4. AEDC Groups/Directorates/Offices will:
        2.4.1. Integrate ORM & VPP requirements throughout the organization.

      2.4.2. Inform the ORM/VPP Steering Committee of any program shortfalls within the
      organization and identify/nominate risk management processes warranting committee
      attention and/or possible working group action.
      2.4.3. Ensure organization policies for making risk decisions at the appropriate level are
      in place and that policy, guidance, and taskings support ORM & VPP processes.
      2.4.4. Ensure all personnel receive appropriate ORM & VPP training.
      2.4.5. Ensure all personnel understand and apply ORM & VPP processes to manage risk
      in daily activities, both on- and off-duty, as appropriate.
3. Procedures
   3.1. ORM Principles. The four ORM principles are identified in AFI 90-901, para 3.
   3.2. ORM Process. See Attachment 3.
   3.3. System Safety Working Group. The ORM Steering Committee fulfills the requirement
   of a System Safety Group (or System Safety Working Group) IAW AFI 91-202, The US Air
   Force Mishap Prevention Program, paragraph 9.4.
4. Metrics. N/A
5. Flowcharts. N/A
6. Records. ORM Certificates of Training and ORM/VPP Steering Committee meeting minutes
are located in the AEDC/SE office.

                                         GUIDANCE

Figure A2.1. Assessment of Organizational ORM Integration
1. Is there evidence that Group leadership supports the AEDC ORM program?

2. Has ORM, consistent with the Center plan, been integrated throughout the organization?

3. Have organizational program shortfalls been identified to the ORM Steering Committee?

4. Do any organizational processes warrant ORM Steering Committee attention and possible
action by a working group?

5. Are organizational policies in place to ensure risk management decisions are made at the
appropriate level?

6. Have all personnel completed appropriate ORM training?

7. Is there evidence that personnel apply ORM processes to manage daily activities?

8. Can personnel identify, in general terms, the six steps of the ORM process?

9. Has the organization assessed its ORM program in the past 12 months?

10. Where can the organization better integrate ORM?
