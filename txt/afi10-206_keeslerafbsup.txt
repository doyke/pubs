AFI 10-206, 30 May 2003, is supplemented as follows.

This supplement establishes the 81st Training Wing policy and procedures for use in conjunction
with the basic publication. This supplement applies to all assigned, attached and/or associate
units supported by the 81st Training Wing at Keesler AFB MS, including Air Force Reserve and
Air National Guard (ANG) units, except where noted otherwise. Ensure that all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functionalâ€™s chain of command. Attachment 1 is a glossary of
references and supporting information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. AFRES changed

1.3.2. The 81st Training Wing (TRW) Command Post (CP) prepares and submits operational
reports (OPREPS) for all units on Keesler AFB. Reports are prepared in accordance with
AFI 10-206 and AETC Sup 1 formats and directives. EXCEPTION: 403 WG (AFRC) will
prepare and submit their own reports; however, when a certified 403 WG reports controller is not
on duty, the 81 TRW Command Post will contact the 403 WG/CP senior air reserve technician
for guidance.
1.3.2.1. (Added) Tenant commanders will provide the 81 TRW Command Post a list of
appropriate message addressees to be used for any incidents involving their unit. Notify 81
TRW Command Post as changes occur, and as a minimum, review for accuracy and send
updates to 81 TRW Command Post NLT 15 December each year.
3.6.1. (Added) Ensure any incident meeting the criteria for reporting is relayed to the 81st
Command Post within 15 minutes of the incident. Ensure the 81 TRW Command Post receive
updates in a timely manner to submit follow-up reports as needed. Provide information
pertaining to the incident that answers the questions of who, what, when, why and where.
Include any other pertinent factors or information. The following agencies are identified as basic
sources of information and are specifically tasked to provide the Command Post with timely,
reportable information: SFS, MDG, PA, JA, and 81 TRG. This list is not all inclusive, but
provides a list of the basic sources of information in regards to event/incident reports. The
81 TRW Command Post will, after proper coordination and approval, relay the report to the
appropriate higher headquarters for action or additional up-channel reporting.
