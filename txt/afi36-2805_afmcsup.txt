SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes
include a listing of AFMC Center/Unit and award monitor responsibilities (paragraph 1.1.2),
award procedures (paragraph 1.1.3), updated new requirements and authorization for the
purchase of official coins (paragraph 1.4.4.1) and updated requirements for award of the AFMC
Fitness Coin.

This instruction implements Air Force Instruction 36-2805, Special Trophies and Awards
(paragraph 1.4.4.4.). These procedures are established to improve timeliness, accuracy, and
quality of Special Trophies and Awards sponsored and processed by various organizations and
staff agencies in AFMC and to provide guidance for organizational and fitness coins. This
publication does apply to the Air National Guard (ANG) and the Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC) and their units. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm

1.1.1. (Added) AFMC Center/Unit Responsibilities.
1.1.1.1. (Added) Establish procedures to consider and select the best nominee for applicable
functional awards.
1.1.1.2. (Added)     Make every effort to submit a nomination for each applicable special
trophy/award.
1.1.1.3. (Added) Ensure nomination packages are submitted on-time and completed IAW
with functional award solicitation messages.
1.1.1.4. (Added) AFMC Center/Unit leadership will ensure all individuals nominated for an
award have clearly demonstrated superior and sustained performance. AFMC nominees for AF-
level recognition should not only meet the spirit and intent of the award criteria, but should also
be of the highest caliber and meet all AF standards and expectations associated with their grade,
to include fitness, PME, and off-duty education. This list is not meant to be all inclusive. If an
individual departs from the standards mentioned above, unit leadership must submit a letter of
explanation with the nomination package.
1.1.1.5. (Added) Appoint a Center/Unit Award Monitor.
1.1.1.6. (Added) Center/Unit Award Monitor will:
1.1.1.6.1. (Added)   Receive, review, process and track all Center/Unit functional award
nomination packages.
1.1.1.6.2. (Added)      Submit Center/Unit nomination packages IAW with functional award
solicitation messages, ensuring all documents are included.
1.1.1.6.3. (Added)      Coordinate corrections/modifications of all nomination package
documents, as required.
1.1.2. (Added) Headquarters Functional Area Procedures.
1.1.2.1. (Added)      Establish a suspense control process (i.e. SOCCER) that meets
conditions/approval requirements of their sponsored award, the sponsoring organization and this
AFMC Supplement to ensure the external suspense date is met.
1.1.2.2. (Added) Make every effort to submit as many eligible and deserving nominees as
permitted--for most awards, each Center should have at least one nomination per category.
1.1.2.3. (Added)     Identify staffing requirements. Prepare an award solicitation message
establishing the required process for submitting award nomination packages to include the
following:
1.1.2.3.1. (Added) Specific criteria for selection of award.
1.1.2.3.2. (Added) An endorsement letter signed by the Center/Unit leadership or designated
representative will be required and will include the following statement, “A records check was
conducted on (date) and revealed no information that would bring discredit to the award or to the
United States Air Force.”
1.1.2.3.2.1. (Added) Endorsement letters for enlisted members must reflect coordination by
Center/Wing Command Chief Master Sergeant or ranking Senior Enlisted Leader (SEL).

1.1.2.3.3. (Added) A publicity statement will be mandatory for each nomination. It will be
signed and dated by the nominee for individual nominations, or by Center/Unit leadership for
unit/group nominations.
1.1.2.3.3.1. (Added) Publicity statements will read (circle one, line through the other): “I,
(rank/title/name), understand (my/this) nomination for the (trophy/award) may involve release of
information contained in (my/this) nomination package for the purpose of publicizing the
program. I (do/do not) authorize my personal information to be used.”
1.1.2.3.4. (Added) Correct headers (if applicable) and a complete alphabetized list of all
acronyms (if used) will be included in either the space remaining at the bottom of the first page
or entirely on the second page.
1.1.2.3.5. (Added) Nomination packages must include most recent Fitness Scoresheet and
SURF for review by leadership.
1.1.2.3.6. (Added) All negative submissions will be coordinated by Center/Unit leadership or
designated representative.
1.1.2.4. (Added)     Ensure solicitation messages are sent to Center and appropriate HQ
Directorate workflows, and functional award POCs in sufficient time to allow for nomination
packages to be prepared.
1.1.3. (Added) Boarding Process.
1.1.3.1. (Added)    Unless specified differently in functional award guidance instructions,
Center/Unit leadership will select their award winner/nominee by utilizing a board process
whereby each nomination package will be reviewed to ensure required items are included in the
correct format and the listed accomplishments are of such quality to warrant selection as
winner/nominee of the specified award.
1.1.3.2. (Added)     Award owning Headquarter Functional areas will select overall AFMC
winners/nominees by utilizing a board process consistent of a panel of three ranking individuals
from the Functional Directorate. The board will review each nomination package to ensure
required items are included and the listed accomplishments are of such quality to warrant
selection as winner/nominee of the specified award.
1.1.3.2.1. (Added) Owning Headquarter Functional award POCs will prepare board material
to include award criteria, nomination packages, and a ballot to be used for scoring by board
members.
1.1.3.2.2. (Added) Owning Headquarter Functional award monitors/executive officers will
solicit individuals in either the rank of Major through Colonel, GS-13 and above, or CMSgt or
ranking Senior Enlisted Leader (SEL). Functional areas may deviate from this requirement in
the event manning levels do not support; however, board members should not review peer
nomination packages. The board will be comprised of at least one enlisted member when there
is an enlisted nomination package.
1.1.3.2.3. (Added) Unless higher selection/approval authority is directed, owning Headquarter
functional areas will utilize the board member results to select AFMC winners/nominees.

allows for a one-time award (i.e. organizational coins) to individuals for achievement. AFI 65-
601 Vol 1, paragraph 4.29.1. authorizes organizational coins as a mission accomplishment
award. For all questions and concerns about organizational coins, contact      HQ AFMC/FM at:
afmc.fm.workflow@wpafb.af.mil
1.4.4.1.1. (Added) Organizational coins provide senior leadership with an informal means to
show special recognition of, and appreciation for, a job well done to military and appropriated
fund civilian personnel who, by their dedicated individual efforts, have facilitated the successful
accomplishment of a particular AFMC program, function or mission.

1.4.4.4. (Added) Controls.
1.4.4.4.1. (Added) Coins may not be presented to anyone as a gift, memento, souvenir or used
as tokens of appreciation, community relations, or “mission accomplishment” for non-
appropriated funded (NAF) personnel, government officials, foreign officials or non-US
Government employees. NOTE: See AFI 65-603 for the use of Official Representation Funds,
AFI 36-2818 for NAF, and AFI 34-201 for Special Morale and Welfare (SM&W).
1.4.4.4.2. (Added) Coins will not be presented to officials in an oversight capacity (in the
unit’s chain of command) or inspection role.
1.4.4.4.3. (Added) Coins will not be awarded for an action that has already been recognized or
will be recognized by another form of award or decoration.
1.4.4.4.4. (Added) Coins cannot be used for commemorative, morale building or to recognize
Permanent Change of Station (PCS), retirements, going away ceremonies or similar occasions.
1.4.4.4.5. . (Added) Coins for contractor personnel are not authorized under any type of
funding except privately funded coins.
1.4.4.4.6. (Added) Coins will not be presented with another award.
1.4.4.4.7. (Added) Coins will not be personalized, except for the HQ AFMC Commander’s
coin. Use only the organization’s name and the position of the presenter.
1.4.4.4.8. (Added) Purchasing coin holders or display racks or the like is not authorized.
1.4.4.4.9. (Added) The organization with a stock of coins purchased with appropriated funds
will implement an internal tracking mechanism to ensure such coins are responsibly managed. If
there are multiple sets (bought with different funds), the sets will be segregated and tracked
separately. Appropriate documentation must be maintained and available for Government
Purchase Card (GPC) review and audit.
1.4.4.4.9.1. (Added) The coins must be in a secured area and if not under the Commander’s
control, a delegation letter will be issued to the individual responsible for the coins.
1.4.4.4.9.2. (Added)    Appropriate documentation must be maintained and available for GPC
review and audit.
1.4.4.4.9.3. (Added) At a minimum, documentation will include purchases, recipient’s name
and organization, date and reason for the presentation of the coin.
1.4.4.4.10. (Added)       Old Stock will be exhausted before new stock is issued. For
reorganizations, if the old coin is completely unusable the coins will be turned over to Defense
Reutilization and Marketing Office (DRMO) for disposal.

1.4.4.4.11. (Added) Coins will be ordered when required and in a quantity to ensure sufficient

1.4.4.4.12. (Added) AFMC Fitness Coins. Department of Defense Instruction 1308.3, DoD
Physical Fitness and Body Fat Programs Procedures, authorizes recognition of personnel who
attain outstanding levels of physical fitness. AFMC Commanders/Directors may use
appropriated funds in accordance with AFI 65-601 V1, Budget Guidance and Procedures, to
reward individuals that exceed fitness standards with an appropriate award to include an
organizational-type fitness coin as a mission accomplishment award. Installation Commanders
or their designees may present AFMC Fitness Coins to individuals identified by their local
installation Health and Wellness Center who, at a minimum, have a composite score of at least
90, complete all components (no exemptions), present a professional military image, and are
tobacco-free. This is a one-time award and is not to be awarded for subsequent excellent scores.
