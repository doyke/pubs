This supplement further extends guidance of AFI 36-807, Weekly and Daily Scheduling of Work
and Holiday Observances and AFI 36-815, Absence and Leave. It provides supervisors and
managers with information, definition, instructions essential to implementation of an Alternative
Work Schedule for organizations where shift hours are essential for mission accomplishment. It
implements guidance from, 5 CFR Part610, 5 USC Chap 61, and the Handbook on Alternative
Work Schedules, published by the Office of Personnel Management. This instruction applies to all
civilian personnel serviced by the 94th Airlift Wing Civilian Personnel Office. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Form 847s through local publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.

2.2. Alternative Work Schedules (AWS). Participation in the Alternative Work Schedule
(AWS) is voluntary. Depending on mission considerations or constraints, the AWS may be
offered to an employee. This program may be discontinued organizationally at any time if there
is an adverse impact on the 94th Airlift Wing’s overall mission, in accordance with the current
negotiated independent labor agreement and the Office of Personnel Management (OPM)
Guidance and Management Rights under Title 5, U.S.C. Chap 71.

2.2.1. The organizational unit as a whole remains open for business five calendar days (Monday
through Friday) each work week, excluding federal holidays. Managers are responsible for
ensuring coverage for the various shift schedules within their organizations, maintaining and
documenting work schedules to ensure service as required through the work week. Under the
negotiated labor agreement, bargaining unit employees may not start AWS shifts earlier than
0630. Additionally, AWS day shifts for non-bargaining employees will start no earlier than 0600
without the written permission of the Wing Commander, documenting the workload need for
night shift differential.

2.2.2. An employee’s regularly scheduled administrative work week must correspond with the
employee’s actual work requirement. Management has the right to vary tours of duty and to
change an employee’s scheduled hours of duty in order to meet mission essential requirements to
include; temporary duty (TDY) and/or training attendance and will give the maximum notice
possible to affected employees as applicable and in accordance with the negotiated labor
agreement. A work schedule change request will accompany the employee's timecard.

2.2.3. Alternative Work Schedules enable managers and supervisors to meet their work center’s
mission goals while, at the same time, allowing employees to have more flexibility in scheduling
their personal activities.

2.2.4. Responsibilities.

2.2.4.1. Group commanders will ensure the AWS program is applied fairly and equitably by
managers in their organizations.

2.2.4.2. Supervisors will:

2.2.4.2.1. Provide employees as much flexibility as the individual work center mission allows in
approving work schedule requests and provide all offered work schedule options, where feasible.

2.2.4.2.2. Ensure that employees have access to a copy of this instruction.

2.2.4.2.3. Ensure a process for internal timecard audits.

2.2.4.2.4. Protect employee(s) privacy relative to work schedule issues and timecards.

2.2.4.2.5. Reschedule the employee’s administrative workweek to correspond with the specific
days and hours the employee is expected to work. Notify the employee of the change, initiate a
Work Schedule Change Form and document the employee’s timecard as applicable.

2.2.4.2.6. Ensure each organization, work center, functional area, and “one-deep positions” are
staffed Monday through Friday during normal customer service hours (normally 0730-1600).

2.2.4.2.7. Ensure the accuracy and legitimacy of all submitted work schedule forms and timecard
documents by signing or initialing, as appropriate.

2.2.4.3. Employees will:

2.2.4.3.1. Request a work schedule which will allow them to meet mission needs.

2.2.4.3.2. Ensure the accuracy and legitimacy of all submitted work schedule forms and timecard
documents by signing or initialing, as appropriate.

2.2.4.3.3. Ensure that all periods of military duty, if applicable, are annotated on the automated
timecard regardless of whether the military duty was performed after duty hours or on non-
civilian duty days.

2.2.4.3.4. Ensure a work schedule change is prepared when the tour of duty and/or duty hours
need to be changed due to TDY or training. The work schedule change is normally submitted to
the supervisor for approval no later than one week prior to the beginning of the next pay period.

2.2.5. The civilian personnel office, employee relations section retains oversight of the AWS
program.

2.2.6. The financial management office provides advice and assistance on matters relating to
timecard submissions relative to this instruction.
