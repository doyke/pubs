This instruction implements AFPD 23-1, Materiel Management Policy and Procedures and
establishes additional procedures for accountability of organizational equipment to be maintained
on unit’s Organizational Visibility List (R15). This accountability is in addition to normal
Custodian Authorization and Custody Receipt Listing (R14) Equipment Custodian Accounts.
This applies to 56th Fighter Wing and tenant units to include all Air Force, Air Force Reserve
Command (AFRC) and to Air National Guard (ANG) Units. Submit an AF Form 847,
Recommendation for change of Publication to the OPR to recommend changes to this
publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been revised and must be completely reviewed.
1. Procedures
   1.1. Unit Commanders will:
       1.1.1. Prudently manage, control, store, and maintain government property under their
       jurisdiction and accurately maintain property records to reflect current inventory and
       condition of property IAW AFI 23-111.
       1.1.2. Appoint a custodian to manage and account for all organizational equipment
       bought through supply that is pilferable or the unit cost is more than $2,500. The
       custodian must also account for any pilferable item purchased through the 56th

        Contracting Squadron (56 CONS/CC) or with the Government Purchase Card (GPC)
        costing more than $500. Excluded from this requirement are items such as furniture and
        clothing.
        1.1.3. Ensure each appointed custodian is trained. Contact the 56th Logistics Readiness
        Squadron (56 LRS), Equipment Accountability Element (EAE), to schedule appropriate
        training.
        1.1.4. Ensure custodians maintain a folder with a R15 listing or Commander approved
        locally devised spreadsheet and custodian appointment letter.
        1.1.5. Ensure inventories are conducted annually or upon change of custodian.
        1.1.6. Ensure the appointed custodian prints out a new R15 or updates locally devised
        spreadsheet upon changes.
        1.1.7. Maintain a list of appointed R15 and/or Organizational Equipment custodians and
        monitor the appointment and training of appointed custodians.
        1.1.8. Ensure unit resource advisors, billing officials, and GPC cardholders are cognizant
        of the requirements to screen GPC purchases and coordinate with appointed R15
        custodians when purchases meet the requirements for R15 accounting.
     1.2. The 56 LRS/CC will:
        1.2.1. Upon request, will provide training through the 56 LRS EAE office to each
        appointed R15, and or Organizational Equipment Custodian. This training will include
        all information necessary to identify, account for, and manage property purchased with
        local base funds.
        1.2.2. Provide an instructor to participate in GPC cardholder training to train cardholders
        on how to recognize and coordinate budget code 9 equipment with their organizational
        custodian for accountability purposes.
        1.2.3. 56 LRS EAE office will submit an AF Form 2011, Base Supply ADPE Work
        Request, in order to provide each organization a copy of the R15 listing as requested.

2. Adopted Forms:

AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication
AF Form 2011, Base Supply ADPE Work Request
