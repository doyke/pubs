This instruction implements AFI 34-239, Food Service Management Program, Attachment 3,
Use of Air Force APF Dining Facilities. It is established to clearly define who is authorized to
utilize the Ray V. Hensman dining facility at Luke Air Force Base. This publication applies to
all Air Force, Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and Air National Guard (ANG) Units.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

All references to “SVS” and “Services” have been changed to read “FSS” and “Force Support.”
Added paragraph 3.4. and Attachment 1, Glossary of References and Supporting Information.
1. General:
   1.1. In accordance with AFI 34-239, Food Service Management Program, Attachment 3, the
   56th Fighter Wing Commander (56 FW/CC) has determined that use of the dining facilities
   be primarily for Essential Station Messing (ESM) customers and Temporary Duty (TDY)
   personnel. Enlisted members receiving the Basic Allowance Subsistence (BAS) are
   discouraged from eating in the dining facilities. Food service management will monitor
   usage of the dining facilities and enforce this instruction as needed to meet the intent of the

     56 FW/CC. All requests for exceptions to this policy must be forwarded to the 56th Force
     Support Squadron Commander (56 FSS/CC) in writing, explaining the circumstances and
     must include a date and time requested for approval.
2. Authorized Use of Ray V. Hensman Dining Facility:
     2.1. ESM members, active duty/DoD civilian TDY personnel with government meals
     directed on their orders, Civilian Fire Fighters on alert on a Fire Engine, Security Forces
     Personnel on post (patrolling and/or guarding a gate) and members of any Reserve
     component performing drill requirements.
     2.2. Group functions such as 56 FW/CC meetings with various organizations are authorized.
     2.3. Commander’s meetings with Airmen are authorized.
     2.4. Functions hosted by 56 FSS, approved by the squadron commander.
     2.5. Food Service workers, military and contract, in performance of their duties.
     2.6. Dependents of E-4 and below that are in PCS status. No operating charge will be
     incurred by the member and/or dependents. Member must have copies of PCS orders.
     2.7. Dependents of deployed members on Tuesdays and Thursdays during dinner and all day
     Saturday. Dependents must have a copy of deployment orders.
     2.8. 56 FW/PA is authorized to use the dining facility for community relations programs
     with prior concurrence from 56 FSS/CC.
3. Unauthorized Use of Ray V. Hensman Dining Facility:
     3.1. Food Service military and contract employees not in performance of their duties.
     3.2. Private Organization, Club, Group or Council meetings with E-5s and above without
     approval of the 56 FSS/CC.
4. Foreign Military Members:
     4.1. Foreign military members are not authorized use of the Ray V. Hensman dining facility
     unless approved by the 56 FSS/CC, as established in Paragraph 1, Section 1.1.
5. Holiday Meals:
     5.1. Retirees and their families and all military personnel and their families may dine at the
     Ray V. Hensman dining facility on Thanksgiving and Christmas holidays only. Restrictions
     will remain in effect for all other holidays.
