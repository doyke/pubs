AFI 31-601, 29 June 2005, and USAFE Supplement 1, 20 April 2006, are supplemented as
follows: This supplement sets out policies and procedures relating to the Industrial Security
Program at Spangdahlem Air Base (SAB), geographically separated units (GSU) and applies to
all Air Force (AF) military, civilian, and contractor personnel under contract by the Department
of Defense (DoD) who participate in the 52d Fighter Wing (52 FW) Industrial Security Program.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, prescribed by
Air Force Instruction (AFI) 11-215, USAF Flight Manuals Program. Route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW)
AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at: https://afrims.amc.af.mil
1.6.1.6. (Added) The Installation Commander has designated the 52D Security Forces Squadron
Commander (52 SFS/CC) as the Industrial Security Program Manager to provide Industrial
Security oversight and administration to on-base contractor operations and personnel located at
SAB.
1.6.1.7. (Added) All on-base or GSU contractor operations that require access to classified
information are designated as Visitor Groups (VG).
1.6.2.3.1. (Added) Route all Department of Defense (DD) Forms 254s to 52 SFS/S5I for
coordination prior to forwarding to the Contracting Officer (CO). 52 SFS/S5I ensures
coordination with responsible security disciplines, as applicable.

1.6.2.6. (Added) The sponsoring unit commander for the DoD contract will designate a program
manager, project manager or designee, in writing, for each contract and forward a copy to 52
SFS/S5I. The designee is the focal point for contractual activities during the life of the contract.
1.6.2.7. (Added) The sponsoring unit’s PM writes the Visitors Group Security Agreement
(VGSA). Contact 52 SFS/S5I for a VGSA template and guidance.
1.6.2.8. (Added) The Security Manager for the sponsoring unit or agency serves as the focal
point for security matters once the contractor has been integrated into the installation’s Industrial
Security Program.
1.6.5.1.1. (Added) 52 SFS/S5I administers the Industrial Security Program for the Industrial
Security Program Manager.
1.6.5.2.1. (Added) All VGs will be integrated into SAB’s Industrial Security Program. Other
security organizations may have security review and oversight responsibility for contractor
operations working with Sensitive Compartmental Information (SCI), Special Access Programs
(SAP), and or other special areas located on SAB. Note: In these cases, those security offices
will process VGSAs and maintain all required documentation for each contract.
1.8.1.5. (Added) Unit commanders, Security Managers, or other individuals aware of adverse
information concerning contractor personnel will immediately notify 52 SFS/S5I.
1.8.2.4. (Added) Guidance for reporting security violations is contained in the VGSA for all
contractors performing classified efforts on SAB.
1.8.5.8. (Added) VGs or the unit releasing classified information to the contractor must
immediately notify 52 SFS/S5I of any security incidents involving contractors.
3.1.5. (Added) Security training requirements are contained in the VGSA.
3.2.4. (Added) Security Managers will perform one of the following items: invite all VG site
managers to Industrial Security Program Manager security manager meetings and or provide a
copy of minutes from the meetings to VG site managers.
4.1.4. (Added) PMs or activity Point of Contacts (POCs) must involve 52 SFS/S5I early in the
acquisition process when contracts require contractor access to classified or unclassified
sensitive information or enhanced physical security for products under the contractor's control.
4.2.4. (Added) Any changes made to the DD Form 254, after the initial 52 SFS/S5I review and
coordination, will require another review process by 52 SFS/S5I.
4.3.1.1. (Added) 52 SFS/S5I reviews the DD Form 254 for the Industrial Security Program
Manager.
4.3.2.1. (Added) 52 SFS/S5I monitors the 5-year review of DD Forms 254 via Information
Security Program Reviews. The Security Manager will be tasked to ensure the appropriate PM
completes the review, in writing, and notifies 52 SFS/S5I of action taken.
4.5.8. (Added) The VGSA will identify specific procedures for receiving, processing and
handling incoming VG visit requests. Each squadron will incorporate procedures for VG
requests within their squadron’s security operating instructing and outlined those procedures in
the VGSA prior to coordinating the VGSA. Joint Personnel Adjudication System (JPAS) will be

the method used for VG visit requests. If JPAS is not available, a Visitor Authorization Letter
may be substituted.
4.5.9. (Added) At a minimum, the following personnel will sign the SAB VGSA: Host activity
commander, Security Manager, unit PM or their designee, company representative (typically the
Facility Security Officer (FSO), Chief, (52 SFS/S5I) and the Industrial Security Program
Manager). A copy of the completely signed document is forwarded to all parties to include the
CO.
6.2.1.1. (Added) VG industrial security program review reports will be drafted independently
from the unit’s annual Industrial Security Program Manager and semi-annual self-inspection
program review reports.
6.2.4.6. (Spangdahlem) VG site manager’s and Security Managers will maintain copies of the
minutes from the last two Industrial Security Program Manager security meetings within their
file plan and ensure VG personnel are briefed on any security requirements and or changes
outlined within the minutes.
6.2.4.7. (Added) 52 SFS/S5I is not required to maintain a copy of the self-inspection reports for
integrated visitor groups.
7.3.1.1. (Added) Security Managers are to “service” VG personnel in JPAS to ensure immediate
notification of a change in an individual’s security clearance status eligibility. If the contract is
assigned to another unit, the unit where the VG employee is located will “service” that VG
employee in JPAS as well (e.g. Contract with 52 CS but VG employee works at 52 FW/
Installation Control Center (ICC).
7.3.1.2. (Added) Security Managers are to provide any updates of VG personnel changes to
52 SFS/S5I to ensure 52 SFS/S5I is “servicing all” VG personnel in JPAS as well. Security
Managers will incorporate procedures into the unit’s security operating instruction for this
requirement.
7.3.2. The establishment of contact with the FSO is provided for in the VGSA. The utilization of
a faxed or email copy of the signed Visitor Access Letter from the FSO addressed to the Security
Manager or Industrial Security Program Manager is an acceptable practice, if JPAS is not
available.
11.5. (Added) United States (U.S.) citizens working for or representing foreign owned
businesses must be considered foreign nationals regardless of the individuals' citizenship.
Consult 52 SFS/S5I if there are concerns regarding the status of these individuals before granting
access to U.S. government information.
