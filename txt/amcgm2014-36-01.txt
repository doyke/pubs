SUBJECT: AMC Guidance Memorandum (GM) for AMC Security Forces (SF) Home Station
         Training (HST) Plan

RELEASABILITY: There are no releasability restrictions on this publication.

        1. By Order of the Commander, Air Mobility Command, this Guidance Memorandum
immediately implements AMC’s SF Home Station Training initiative. Compliance with this
memorandum is mandatory. To the extent its direction is inconsistent with other Air Mobility
Command instructions or guidance, the information herein prevails, in accordance with (IAW)
AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management. This publication may not be supplemented
or further implemented/extended. This publication does not apply to Air National Guard (ANG)
and Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) units.

       2. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this Memorandum are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of IAW the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS)
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).

       3. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain of
command.

       4. Submit requests for waivers through the chain of command to the appropriate Tier
waiver approval authority, or alternately, to the Publication OPR for non-tiered compliance
items.

        5. AMC Training Obligation. Along with the HAF directed 108 hours of training from
the Foundational Skills Library (FSL), all Active Duty and DAF CP/SG 0083/0085 will
complete 64 hours of HQ AMC/A7S directed training. A remaining balance of 44 hours will be
directed by Defense Force Commanders (DFC). DFCs will also ensure the following HQ
AMC/A7S directed training tasks and hours are integrated into the unit’s annual training plan
(ATP):


              UNRIVALED GLOBAL REACH FOR AMERICA … ALWAYS!
   a. Law and Order Operations Library. 32 Hours
   b. AMC Weapons and Tactics Proficiency Shooting & Force on Force. 24 Hours
   c. Combatives. 8 Hours

        6. AMC Training Obligation (IMAs). Along with the HAF directed 108 hours of
training from the FSL, all IMAs will complete 32 hours of HQ AMC/A7S directed training. A
remaining balance of 11 hours will be directed by DFCs. DFCs will ensure the following HQ
AMC/A7S directed training tasks are integrated into the unit’s ATP.

   a. Law and Order Operations Library. 32 Hours

        7. The memorandum becomes void after one year has elapsed from the date of this
memorandum, or upon publication of AFI 36-2646_AMCSUP, Security Forces Training and
Standardization Evaluation Program, incorporating the guidance, whichever is earlier. Please
direct any questions to HQ AMC/A7SXT at DSN 779-7696 or 779-0997.
