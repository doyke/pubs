This mission directive implements policy guidance in AFPD 10-1, Mission Directives, AFPD 38-
1, Organization, and AFI 38-101, Air Force Organization. It applies to the United States Air
Force Expeditionary Center (USAF EC). Its purpose is to prescribe mission, organization, and
processes. It applies to Air Mobility Command (AMC) active duty units, the Air National
Guard, or Air Force Reserve units, effective upon the date of unit activation. Ensure all records
in this publication are maintained and disposed of in accordance with AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and the Air Force Records Disposition located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the
appropriate functional chain of command.

1. Mission. The United States Air Force Expeditionary Center’s (USAF EC) mission is to train,
exercise, and enable expeditionary combat support and mobility Airmen to conduct global en
route and contingency response operations, joint base management and installation support. The
USAF EC is the Air Force's Center of Excellence for expeditionary combat support and
advanced mobility training and education. The USAF EC provides command oversight to
multiple Air Mobility Command (AMC) units and two unique schools. Located on Joint Base
McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst N.J., the USAF EC partners with Air Staff, AMC, Air Education and
Training Command, and the U.S. Air Force Warfare Center to provide a disciplined training
process assuring the right skills are taught at the right time across the expeditionary enterprise.
2. Command. USAF EC is a direct reporting unit assigned to AMC.
     2.1. USAF EC/CC is under the command of AMC/CC, and normal command channels apply
     except when otherwise authorized. USAF EC/CC exercises ADCON of assigned forces to
     include command authority to organize, train, and equip.
     2.2. USAF/CC exercises General Court-Martial Convening Authority (GCMCA) over
     assigned and attached personnel belonging to the USAF EC, 43 AG, 87 ABW, 305 AMW,
     319 ABW, 437 AW, 621 CRW, 627 ABG, and 628 ABW. GCMCA for the 515 AMOW
     and 521 AMOW is exercised in accordance with current AF Instructions and
     AMC/USAFE/PACAF command to command agreements.
3. Responsibilities.
     3.1. Commands six wings, two groups and two schools focusing on the evolving mobility
     and expeditionary combat support missions to include: contingency response, building
     partnership capacity, global enroute operations, joint basing and installation support, pre-
     deployment contingency airmen’s skills training, security forces training, mobility training,
     contingency response force training, and exercises.
        3.1.1. Prepares airmen to conduct air mobility and expeditionary combat support with the
        correct tactics, techniques and procedures to advance expeditionary and global reach
        operations.
        3.1.2. Provides Joint Base (JB) command oversight as the Intermediate Command
        Summit Chair (ICS) for AMC led Joint Bases McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst and Charleston.
        Further, is the Senior AF commander providing command oversight on the Army chaired
        JB Lewis-McChord ICS.
        3.1.3. Provides installation management to Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, and
        Coast Guard mission partners on the command’s joint bases.
        3.1.4. Provides mission-ready, expeditionary airmen to support unified combatant
        commanders for on-going military operations.
        3.1.5. Provides rapidly deployable and expeditionary multi-skilled combat teams to
        quickly open forward contingency airbases and extend Global Reach Laydown support
        worldwide. Provides expertise and mobile equipment to execute initial airfield
        operations, and expediting the delivery of joint/combined follow-on forces.
