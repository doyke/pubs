MEMORANDUM FOR 26 COG AND 690 COG UNITS

FROM: 67 CW/CC

SUBJECT: 67th Cyberspace Wing Guidance Memorandum, Commander’s Guidance on
         Telecommunications Monitoring and Assessment Program (TMAP)

Releasable: There are no restrictions on this publication

1. By Order of the 67th Cyberspace Wing (67 CW) Commander, this Guidance Memorandum
immediately implements AFI 10-712, Telecommunications Monitoring and Assessment Program
(TMAP), and establishes guidance for the Quality Assurance Program Management
responsibilities for the 67 CW’s TMAP units. Compliance with this memorandum is mandatory.
To the extent its directions are inconsistent with other 67 CW publications, the information
herein prevails, IAW AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management.

2. The supported TMAP unit will ensure a Quality Assurance Representative (QAR) is assigned
to provide and perform necessary QA maintenance management activities and monthly
scheduled managerial evaluations. The supporting units will provide all necessary training
assistance required to complete required QA functions. Ensure that all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).

3. This memorandum becomes void after one-year has elapsed from the date of this
memorandum, or upon publication of an Interim Change or rewrite of the affected publication,
whichever is earlier. Questions concerning this memorandum should be directed to the 67
CW/CCV office, DSN 312-969-6994 or commercial 210-977-6994.




                                     WILLIAM J. POIRIER, Colonel, USAF

        Due to limited manpower and maintenance personnel assigned to the TMAP units,
overall Quality Assurance (QA) program management will be supported by other 67 CW units at
the same geographic location. The units impacted and involved with this are:

TMAP Unit:                                        Quality Assurance Supporting Unit:

68 NWS, Joint Base San Antonio-Lackland         33 NWS, Joint Base San Antonio-Lackland
352 NWS, Joint Base Pearl Harbor-Hickam         Det 1, 561 NOS, Joint Base Pearl Harbor-
Hickam                                          Hickam
Det 1, 352 NWS, Vogelweh Germany                Det 4, 83 NOS, Ramstein AB, Germany

        The supporting unit QA office, along with the supported unit QAR, will assess
cyberspace maintenance management activities and identify shortfalls to each commander.
These shortfalls will be indentified and acknowledged in the form of an official, signed report
and filed appropriately.

        Supported units must ensure Integrated Maintenance Data System (IMDS) accounts are
established and all core and ancillary equipment are uploaded into their respective IMDS
accounts. Additionally, they will be required to ensure Preventive Maintenance Inspection
schedules and required Equipment Evaluations are accomplished IAW Technical Order 00-33A-
1001. The supported unit must ensure all required programs are in place or a written agreement
between the supporting and supported unit is established. Programs applicable are:

Tools Management Program
Corrosion Control Program
Electrostatic Discharge Program
Technical Order Distribution Account Program.

     Applicable programs listed above maybe tailored with written approval from both
26 COG/CC and 690 COG/CC.
