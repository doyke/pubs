This instruction establishes the local processes and procedures for the oversight and operation of
the Consolidated Munitions Control Center (CMCC). The purpose of this publication is to
provide guidance and instruction to the munitions organizations operating within Hill Air Force
Base, (Hill AFB) Munitions Storage Area (MSA) (comprised of Missile and Munitions Storage
(MAMS) I and II areas) regarding Munitions Control functions. This publication is to be used in
conjunction with AFI 21-200, Munitions and Missile Maintenance Management, AFI 21-201,
Conventional Munitions Maintenance Management, AFI 21-202, Missile Maintenance
Management Volume 1, AFI 21-204, Nuclear Weapons Maintenance Procedures, AFI 31-101,
Integrated Defense, AFMAN 91-201, Explosives Safety Standards and Hill AFB Comprehensive
Emergency Management Plan (CEMP) 10-2. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the office of primary (OPR)
responsibility using Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This Instruction has been revised and should be read in its entirety.
1. Consolidated Munitions Control Center (CMCC) Chief. The area of responsibility for the
CMCC Chief covered in this instruction is limited to the operational oversight of Munitions
Control operations being performed in the Hill AFB MSA. The primary purpose for this position
is to verify individual unit compliance with all applicable Air Force instructions with regard to

Munitions Control activities and provide operational coordination between assigned units under
control of the CMCC. The CMCC Chief should not be tied directly to any workload in MSA,
therefore to ensure objectivity the position will be established under the Explosive Clear Zone
(ECZ) Program Management office (OO-ALC/XPL) within the Ogden Air Logistics Center,
Plans and Programs office (OO-ALC/XP).
2. CMCC Chief shall:
     2.1. Verify operational units are documenting facility inspections and annual earth covering
     depth checks in accordance with (IAW) AFI 32-1065 Grounding Systems, and AFMAN 91-
     201.
     2.2. Verify operational units have sufficient access to Secure Transmission Encrypted (STE),
     Secure Internet Protocol Network (SIPRNET) and Non-secure Internet Protocol Network
     (NIPRNET) and required communications capability within the CMCC.
     2.3. Verify Munitions Control personnel document inspection of facilities with intrusion
     detection systems (IDS) installed IAW DoD 5100.76-M, Physical Security of Sensitive
     Conventional Arms, Ammunition, and Explosives.
        2.3.1. Verify operational units are protecting munitions as outlined in AFI 31-101.
        2.3.2. IDS checks will be documented on the AF Form 2530 Alarm System Test Record.
     2.4. Conduct weekly munitions scheduling/production review meetings with operational unit
     CMCC senior controllers to identify and help resolve inter-unit operational conflicts as
     required.
     2.5. Review and coordinate on all local checklists.
     2.6. Verify Munitions Control personnel use prescribed technical data and locally developed
     checklists.
     2.7. Verify key and lock custodians are appointed IAW DoD 5100.76-M and AFI 21-200.
     2.8. Verify enforcement of procedures for Key and Lock Control for Munitions Facilities per
     AFI 21-200, AFI 21-204 and AFI 31-101.
     2.9. Verify Munitions Control personnel are controlling keys to munitions facilities, using
     procedures in DoD 5100.76-M, AFI 31-101, AFI 21-200, AFI 21-204 and lead MAJCOM
     supplements to these directives, as applicable.
     2.10. Verify applicable explosives site plan data and maps are available in the CMCC.
     Source data is maintained by the Wing Safety office.
     2.11. Maintain and verify Hill AFB MSA standardized emergency action procedures
     (checklists) governing munitions operations during severe weather or electrical storms,
     explosive incidents and accidents, increased security conditions, contingency support and
     Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (START) inspections are coordinated through applicable
     Wing Safety, applicable Quality Assurance and the Arms Control Office (as required).
        2.11.1. Plan, coordinate, schedule and oversee semi-annual MSA area fire drills and
        coordinate with HAFB Installation Fire Department Inspectors prior to their initiation.

  2.12. Verify Munitions Control personnel notify the local safety office, Explosives Ordnance
  Disposal (EOD), Fire and Emergency Services (FES) and Command Numbered Air Force
  (C-NAF) (or lead MAJCOM) munitions staff (or equivalent) in the event of an explosive or
  munitions mishap or incident.
  2.13. Conduct annual self inspection of Munitions Control functions IAW AFI 21-201, AFI
  21-204 and AFI 90-201_AFMCSUP_HILLAFBSUP_1 Inspector General Activities.
     2.13.1. Discrepancies noted during self inspections will be reported to the respective
     organizational Maintenance Supervision or Flight Chief.
  2.14. Verify assigned Munitions Control personnel are tracking/monitoring job status/delays,
  significant difficulties and vehicles using Munitions Control 2000 version 2 (MC2) and
  equipment in Integrated Maintenance Data System (IMDS).
  2.15. Verify assigned Munitions Control personnel are tracking/monitoring changes to fire
  symbol, hazard symbol, Controlled Item Identification Code (CIIC), or the storage of
  classified munitions to include weekly 100% fire symbol verification in Hill AFB GeoBase
  Fire Symbol Tracking System (FSTS) for all MSA operational and storage facilities.
  2.16. Develops and coordinates written entry control procedures and performs Controlled
  Area Monitor/Security duties IAW AFI 31-101 and DoD 5100.76-M.
     2.16.1. Coordinate on Entry Authorization Lists for visiting units needing access into the
     MAMS areas.
     2.16.2. Grant/authorize unescorted access into the MSA by signing the AF Form 2586,
     Unescorted Entry Authorization Certificate IAW AFI 31-101.
         2.16.2.1. Limit delegation of Controlled Area access authority for areas T (MAMS I)
         and U (MAMS II).
     2.16.3. Manage the MAMS I & II Vindicator system.
         2.16.3.1. The vindicator system will be used for access into the MSA.
         2.16.3.1.1. Conduct quarterly validations on the vindicator system with unit security
         managers to ensure MAMS access accountability.
  2.17. Deleted.
  2.18. Act as the single point-of-contact to facilitate resolution of MAJCOM-level
  instructions when MAJCOM instructions are in conflict. The CMCC Chief will work with
  affected flight chief and/or unit commander as required to resolve conflicts.
  2.19. Act as single point of contact for work orders pertaining to maintenance of facilities,
  roads and grounds within the MSA.
     2.19.1. Verify a snow removal plan for the MSA is in place, POC 75th Civil Engineer
     Squadron (CES/CEOH).
     2.19.2. Coordinate with the 75 CES/CEOS office to ensure MSA vegetation control
     contract is in place and reviewed annually and MSA vegetation is maintained IAW AFI
     31-101 and AF MAN 91-201.
  2.20. Deleted.

     2.21. Perform munitions controller duties to augment active/reservist during times of
     minimal manning (i.e., Commander’s Calls, exercises, deployments, down days, etc.). The
     CMCC Chief should be proficient on all controller tasks and skills; however, units should
     attempt to fulfill their own manning needs first. Second, they should seek lateral support
     from sharing organizations; finally, use the CMCC section chief infrequently to resolve a
     temporary shortage.
     2.22. Be appointed as the CMCC Security Manager and will:
        2.22.1. Maintain completed AF Form 1109s Visitor Register Logs for the CMCC and
        both MAMS Areas for one year.
        2.22.2. Maintain completed SF 701 Activity Security Checklist for the CMCC for one
        year.
        2.22.3. Ensure all CMCC assigned personnel complete Quarterly Security training as
        provided by TSS/ADLS. Completed certificates will be kept in the CMCC Security
        Manager’s Handbook.
            2.22.3.1. The CMCC Security Manager will not perform any personnel security
            actions or any other security manager functions other than those listed above.
     2.23. Be appointed as the CMCC and MAMS Resource Protection Program Monitor as
     outlined in AFI 31-101.
     2.24. Maintain and approve the unescorted entry authorization listing for the CMCC as
     delegated.
3. Unit Munitions Control Elements shall:
     3.1. Plan, coordinate, direct, and control all munitions activities for their respective unit as
     prescribed in AFI 21-201 and AFI 21-204.
     3.2. Manage unit keys and locks IAW AFI 21-200, AFI 21-204 and AFI 31-101.
     3.3. Maintain map(s) showing the entire munitions storage area(s), primary and alternate
     explosives routes, evacuation route(s), and sited explosives locations outside the MSA (e.g.,
     aircraft parking locations, hot cargo pads, railheads, munitions holding areas, etc.).
     3.4. Use Hill AFB standardized Emergency Action Checklist (EAC) to include:
     war/contingency plan execution notification, crash, fire, spill response, severe weather and
     explosive mishaps. Units will work with CMCC Chief and base agencies to develop local
     EAC (Fire Dept, Security Forces, etc.). Coordinate all checklists involving explosive
     operations through the 75th Air Base Wing Weapons Safety office (75 ABW/SEW), 388th
     Fighter Wing Weapons Safety office (388 FW/SEW), 309th Explosive Safety (309
     MXW/QPE) and Unit Quality Assurance sections as applicable to each unit within the
     CMCC.
     3.5. Facility Status: Use Combat Ammunition System (CAS) and Hill AFB GeoBase FSTS
     to validate fire/hazard symbol, Net Explosive Weight (NEW), Risk category and CIIC.
     3.6. Track NEW for operating locations, holding areas, and facilities storing assembled
     complete round munitions on trailers with bulk stored assets must be computed and tracked
     using Hill AFB GeoBase FSTS and CAS as applicable.
