This instruction establishes policy and procedures for handling Congressional and high-level
inquiries for the 17th Training Wing (17 TRW) and outlines guidelines to answer inquiries from
members of Congress and other high-level inquiries based on AFI 90-401, Air Force Relations
with Congress. These procedures are intended to ensure complete and accurate responses are
released in a timely manner. It applies to all 17 TRW organizations including geographically
separated units (GSUs). Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained according to AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records and disposed
of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Air
Force          Records         Disposition        Schedule     (RDS),        located         at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847 from the field
through the appropriate functional chain of command. This publication may be supplemented at
any level, but all direct supplements must be routed to the OPR of this publication for
coordination prior to certification and approval.

1. Responsibilities.
   1.1. 17 TRW Inspector General (17 TRW/IG) will:
       1.1.1. Act as focal point for all Congressionals and high-level inquiries tasked to the 17
       TRW.
       1.1.2. Obtain Privacy Act Release if necessary.
2                                               GOODFELLOWAFBI90-401 27 JUNE 2012


       1.1.3. Determine office of primary responsibility (OPR) and provide tasking. Any
       organization tasked to respond to a Congressional or high-level inquiry will provide a
       complete and accurate response to 17 TRW/IG.
       1.1.4. Notify OPR chain of command and 17 TRW/CV/CC of Congressional or high-
       level inquiry.
       1.1.5. Establish suspense for response and obtain extension, if necessary, to ensure
       responses are released in a timely manner through proper channels to avoid missing
       established suspenses.
       1.1.6. Assist OPRs in preparing draft questions and response.
       1.1.7. Ensure response is complete, accurate, and in proper format.
       1.1.8. Coordinate draft response through OPR, OPR chain of command, and JA.
       1.1.9. Coordinate all responses with 17 TRW/CV/CC for approval to release.
       1.1.10. Release approved response and ensure response is provided to tasking
       organization or individual.
       1.1.11. Ensure informational copies are provided to OPR, OPR chain of command, JA,
       HQ AETC/IGQ, and others as deemed necessary.
       1.1.12. Maintain a file copy of the inquiry and response.
    1.2. All 17 TRW Units (Including GSUs). Immediately notify 17 TRW/IG after receipt of
    any Congressional or high-level inquiry.
