SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This instruction is updated to reflect changes in the implementation of the 552 ACW self-
inspection and exercise programs. The major changes include clarification of roles and
responsibilities within each program; the utilization of SharePoint as a collaborative tool to
collect/disseminate information; the validation of self-inspection results; and Group EET leads
will provide exercise after action report inputs to 552 ACW/IGI within seven duty days.

1. General Guidelines.
     1.1. The purpose of this instruction is to outline unit self-inspection and Exercise Evaluation
     Team (EET) responsibilities in order to evaluate wing compliance, safety and combat
     capability.
2. Self-Inspection Program.
     2.1. The primary focus of the Self-Inspection Program (SIP) is to assess the wing’s
     compliance with public law, regulations, directives and safety guidelines. The SIP
     encourages continuous assessment and improvement within wing organizations.
     2.2. Roles and Responsibilities
        2.2.1. 552 ACW/IGI is the Wing Commander’s Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
        for oversight of the SIP. The 552 ACW Group Commanders and the 552 ACW Director
        of Staff (DS) are responsible for implementation.
        2.2.2. 552 ACW/IGI will:
            2.2.2.1. Provide guidance and oversight of the self-inspection process.
            2.2.2.2. Maintain an IGI SharePoint site containing:
                2.2.2.2.1. Inspection reports, Special Interest Items (SIIs).
                2.2.2.2.2. A master list of squadron applicable checklists.
                2.2.2.2.3. A Wing Deficiency Tracker.
            2.2.2.3. Provide the Wing Commander with a semiannual summary of SIP results
            NLT 30 April and 31 October.
            2.2.2.4. Validate SIP results.
            2.2.2.5. Brief the Wing Commander monthly on the status of open discrepancies.
            2.2.2.6. Highlight Best Practices to share information throughout the wing.
        2.2.3. Group Commanders and the DS will:
            2.2.3.1. Appoint primary and alternate SIP monitors and provide an updated
            appointment letter to 552 ACW/IGI IAW Attachment 3.
            2.2.3.2. Ensure SIP monitors post semiannual self-inspection results on the IGI
            SharePoint NLT 15 April and 15 October IAW Attachment 4. Geographically
            separated units will send reports to their respective Group SIP monitor for upload to
            the IGI SharePoint site.
            2.2.3.3. Review corrective actions and ensure monthly follow-ups on open
            discrepancies are conducted.
        2.2.4. Squadron Commanders and Wing Staff Agency Chiefs will:
            2.2.4.1. Appoint primary and alternate SIP monitors and provide an updated
            appointment letter to the respective group/DS as required IAW Attachment 3.
            2.2.4.2. Conduct semiannual unit self-inspections and provide a report to the Group
            Commanders and DS NLT 31 March and 30 September IAW Attachment 4.
552ACWI90-201 6 FEBRUARY 2012                                                                  3


         2.2.4.3. Ensure all applicable checklists are completed by the appropriate work
         centers.
         2.2.4.4. Conduct monthly follow-ups on all open discrepancies
         2.2.4.5. Ensure each new section chief reviews the most recent self-inspection of the
         duty section within 30 days of appointment.
     2.2.5. SIP Monitors will:
         2.2.5.1. Maintain a SharePoint Continuity book IAW Attachment 2.
         2.2.5.2. Ensure the most current checklists are in use.
         2.2.5.3. Conduct a quality review of unit checklists to ensure answers are complete,
         processes are thorough and documentation demonstrating compliance is adequate.
         2.2.5.4. Upload all completed checklists to the WG SharePoint site NLT 31 March
         and 30 September. If GSUs are unable to access the Tinker SharePoint site, the ACG
         SIP monitor will do this for them.
         2.2.5.5. Request any deviations/alterations to the master list of checklists based on
         changes to the underlying guidance.
         2.2.5.6. Forward local, supplemental checklists as approved at the squadron/group
         level to IGI for incorporation in the master list.
  2.3. Deficiency Tracking
  2.3.1. All deficiencies will be assigned a severity of Critical, Significant, or Minor as
  defined:
         2.3.1.1. CRITICAL DEFICIENCY: Any deficiency that results or could result in
         widespread mission impact or failure.
         2.3.1.2. SIGNIFICANT DEFICIENCY: A deficiency that has or could have
         significant mission impact.
         2.3.1.3. MINOR DEFICIENCY: A deficiency that does not meet the definition for
         a Critical or Significant Deficiency, but requires corrective action.
     2.3.2. Group SIP monitors will add any new deficiencies and update the status of all
     existing open items. All items must contain the following information:
         2.3.2.1. A tracking number assigned in the following format: Group (i.e.
         MXG/OG/ACG/WG), SQ (i.e. 964/963/AMXS/MXS), year (i.e. 11/12) and finding
         number (i.e. 01, 02, 03).
         2.3.2.2. Date identified.
         2.3.2.3. Severity (Critical, Significant, or Minor).
         2.3.2.4. Deficiency Code IAW AFI 90-201 Table 2.1.
         2.3.2.5. OPR responsible for corrective action and Office of Collateral Responsibility
         (OCR), if applicable.
         2.3.2.6. Applicable instruction, technical order, policy letter or other reference.
 4                                                         552ACWI90-201 6 FEBRUARY 2012


            2.3.2.7. Deficiency description.
            2.3.2.8. Impact of continued deviation or non-compliance for critical deficiencies.
            2.3.2.9. Action(s) taken/status of corrective action(s) in progress.
            2.3.2.10. Estimated/Actual Completion Dates (ECD/ACD).
        2.3.3. Authority to close items rests with the affected Squadron Commander or WSA
        Chief.
            2.3.3.1. Deficiencies will be closed using an E-SSS routed through the appropriate
            group commander/DS to 552 ACW/IGI. It must include the deficiency tracking
            number, severity, description, and resolution action(s) taken. More than one
            deficiency may be closed on the same E-SSS.
            2.3.3.2. If a deficiency affects two or more squadrons/agencies, the commander
            responsible for all of the affected organizations will close the deficiency.
        2.3.4. IGI will remove deficiencies from the tracker once they are captured in a historical
        document to allow for trend analysis.
     2.4. Reporting Requirements
        2.4.1. Squadrons will provide the results of their semiannual self-inspection to their
        Group Commander using the format specified in Attachment 4. Groups will consolidate
        their units’ semiannual SIP reports and forward them to the 552 ACW/IGI NLT 15 April
        and 15 October.
        2.4.2. Wing Staff agencies will provide reports to the DS using the format specified in
        Attachment 4. DS will consolidate Wing Staff reports and forward them to 552
        ACW/IGI NLT 15 April and 15 October.
        2.4.3. Waiver to Inspection Frequency. Waivers should be the last resort after all other
        avenues have been exhausted. WG/CC approval is required to exceed the inspection
        frequency.
     2.5. Self-Inspection Validation
        2.5.1. IGI will conduct Self-Inspection Validations (SIVs) on at least 10% of 552 ACW
        organizations each SIP period.
        2.5.2. IGI may use EET members and inspected unit expertise to conduct SIV
        inspections.
3. Exercise Program.
     3.1. The wing exercise program will include Emergency Management, Readiness and
     targeted exercises (e.g. NORAD). Robust, realistic scenarios and minimal simulation of
     processes encourages units to prepare for likely real-world taskings versus inspections.
        3.1.1. Roles and Responsibilities
            3.1.1.1. 552 ACW/IGI will:
                3.1.1.1.1. Develop an annual exercise program to meet the intent of AFI 10-2501,
                Air Force Emergency Management (EM) Program Planning and Operations and
552ACWI90-201 6 FEBRUARY 2012                                                                5


           prepare the wing for its unique mission.
           3.1.1.1.2. Emergency Management Exercises. IGI will attend / coordinate 552
           ACW participation in host wing exercises and attend EET meetings as required.
           3.1.1.1.3. Readiness Exercises. IGI will have overall responsibility for exercise
           scenario development, but will request inputs from Group Commanders via the
           Lead EETs to exercise unit specific functional areas.
           3.1.1.1.4. Brief all required EET members prior to exercise initiation.
           3.1.1.1.5. Control exercise information for realism and scenario integrity.
           3.1.1.1.6. Coordinate flight line exercise activity with Base Operations and
           Airfield Management.
           3.1.1.1.7. Conduct a ―Hotwash‖ within three duty days following the exercise
           with EET and unit leadership to consolidate all inputs.
           3.1.1.1.8. Release a draft AAR to EET, Squadron and Group Commanders within
           five duty days of exercise termination for validation.
           3.1.1.1.9. Publish the final report and out-brief 552 ACW/CC NLT 10 workdays
           after End of Exercise (ENDEX).
     3.1.2. Group Commanders will:
        3.1.2.1. Appoint a Group Lead EET member and provide an appointment letter to
        552 ACW/IGI IAW Attachment 5.
        3.1.2.2. Provide exercise inputs through their Lead EET to evaluate unit-specific
        functional areas.
        3.1.2.3. Validate observations and return draft AAR to 552 ACW/IGI no later than
        seven duty days after the exercise.
        3.1.2.4. The OPR of the listed deficiency will initiate corrective action and submit an
        electronic written reply through the respective Group Commander to 552 ACW/IGI
        no later than seven duty days after the exercise.
     3.1.3. Director of Staff will provide exercise inputs to evaluate wing staff agency
     processes.
     3.1.4. Squadron Commanders and Wing Staff Agency Chiefs will appoint highly
     qualified EET members. Nominated EETs should have a minimum of one-year
     retainability.
     3.1.5. Group Lead EET Members will:
        3.1.5.1. Act as a liaison between the 552 ACW/IGI and their respective groups and
        attend all exercise preparation meetings. Once an exercise begins, all team members
        will be under the control of the EET Chief until released back to their duty stations.
        3.1.5.2. Ensure all Group EET members remain current on training in accordance
        with AFI 10-2501, Air Force Emergency Management (EM) Program Planning and
        Operations, Table 6.1.
