The AFMAN 23-110, Volume 6, Chapter 4, 1 April 2009, Precious Metals Recovery Program,
is supplemented as follows. It applies to all personnel, military, and civilian assigned to Luke
AFB. This instruction applies to all Air Force military and civilian personnel including the Air
National Guard and Air Force Reserve. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information
Management         System       Records        Disposition        Schedule        located     at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been revised and must be completely reviewed. Added paragraph 4.2.1.,
delegating the appointment of Precious Metals Recovery Program Manager (PMRPM). Due to
the reorganization of the 56th Logistics Readiness Squadron, titles, work centers, and
responsibilities have changed. Refer to the HQ USAF PAD 08-01 for further guidance.
4.2.1. The 56th Fighter Wing Commander (56 FW/CC) designates the 56th Logistics Readiness
Squadron Commander as approving authority to delegate the appointment of PMRPM (and an
alternate when deemed appropriate) for management of the program for the installation and to
 2                                  AFMAN23-110V6CH4_LUKEAFBSUP1 2 AUGUST 2011


act as the focal point for all matters concerning the PMRP. The PMRPM will be assigned to the
56th Logistics Readiness Squadron, Materiel Management Flight.
4.2.5. Commanders of all squadron staff agencies and detachments that use or consume supply
items bearing precious metals will designate a primary and alternate PMRP monitor to be
accountable for the PMRP. Appointment letters are to be forwarded to the base PMRPM (56
LRS/LGRMSS).
4.2.5.1. (Added) Squadron monitors will conduct an annual visit to their respective
AMUs/shops 6 months after the base PMRPM visit. Copies of the visit will be forwarded to the
base PMRPM.
4.2.5.2. (Added) Squadron PMRP monitors will brief their respective commanders on the
effectiveness of their program after each inspection or visit. Discrepancies noted during the
inspection/visit will be forwarded to the base PMRPM.
4.2.9.1. (Added) Each PMRP monitor will develop a PMRP book containing the following:
     Tab 1            Letters of Appointment
     Tab 2            Publications or other governing directives
     Tab 3            Checklist
     Tab 4            Inspections
     Tab 5            Correspondence
     Tab 6            Training Program
     Tab 7            Listings/Reports
     Tab 8            Turn-In log and copies of DD Form 1348, DoD Single Line Item
                      Requisition System Document
4.2.9.2. (Added) Organizations with bench stock items that contain precious metals will
highlight the corresponding bench stock bin label(s) in blue, indicating the need for recovery of
precious metal items on a one for one basis.
4.2.9.3. (Added) Generating activities will be provided a monthly Precious Metals Listing (L67-
B) from the base PMRPM on the first of each month to identify and track PMRP items that are
not bench stock assets. Tracking these items will ensure PMRP items issued to all organizations
are recovered on a one for one basis.
4.2.9.4. (Added) Turn-in precious metal-bearing scrap and waste to the Defense Reutilization
and Marketing Service (DRMS) (56 LRS/LGRDDC). Organizations will ensure precious metal-
bearing scrap and waste are placed in a container marked as such until turned in. Turn-in
procedures for precious metal-bearing scrap and waste will be IAW AFMAN 23-110, Vol 6,
Chapter 4.
4.2.9.5.  (Added) Ensure auditable turn-in documentation, DD Forms 1348-1A, Issue
Release/Receipt Documents, are maintained in file for 2 years.
