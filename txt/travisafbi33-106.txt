This instruction implements AFPD 33-1, Information Resources Management . It establishes
responsibilities and procedures for requesting public address (PA) system support for official and
non-official military functions at Travis Air Force Base (TAFB). This instruction applies to all
units, host and/or tenant on or associated with Travis AFB. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field
through Major Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records
created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with
Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance
with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. The use of the
name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this
publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.

1. Responsibilities.
     1.1. The Commander, 60th Communications Squadron (60 CS/CC) will:
        1.1.1. Approve all PA support not meeting the 5 duty day notification requirement.
        1.1.2. Approve public address (PA) support for non-profit, civic and charitable
        organizations.
        1.1.3. Approve PA support for non-appropriated fund activities.
     1.2. Radio Transmission Systems (60 CS/SCOT) will:
        1.2.1. Provide and set up PA systems for 60 AMW official functions.
        1.2.2. Maintain portable PA systems for loan to base organizations for unofficial events.
        1.2.3. Train users on set up, operation and care of loaned PA equipment when requested.
        1.2.4. Provide technical guidance for both official and non-official PA events.
        1.2.5. Provide official ceremonial music for PA events.
        1.2.6. Provide assistance in locating vendors for organizations desiring to purchase
        portable or fixed PA systems.
        1.2.7. Not assume maintenance responsibilities for any PA systems.
        1.2.8. Not provide PA support to tenant wings with communications personnel assigned
        to their subordinate organizations. They must self operate either permanent or sign-out
        PA systems. Training will be provided when requested.
2. Requesting Organizations will:
     2.1. Submit formal PA request on the 60 CS SharePoint site NLT 5 DUTY DAYS prior to
     scheduled PA event, exceptions must be approved by 60 CS/CC.
     2.2. Provide and operate PA system during use of non-ceremonial music. Ceremonial music
     consists of Ruffles and Flourishes, National Anthem, To the Colors, Reveille, Retreat, and
     Air Force Song.
     2.3. Ensure AC power is available at the location (commercial or generator).
     2.4. Provide security for PA equipment remaining overnight. Hand receipts will be required.
     2.5. Never connect personal equipment to 60 CS equipment without permission.
     2.6. Appoint a primary and alternate point of contact to coordinate PA support requirements.
     2.7. Have a point of contact in place at the start time listed on the PA request form.
     2.8. Inform 60 CS about any changes to original PA request NLT 3 duty days prior to the
     event.
     2.9. Limit the use of wireless microphones. They are unreliable.
     2.10. Ensure 60 CS set up team has access to facility a minimum of 2 hours prior to event.
     2.11. Be fully responsible for any and all equipment damage during an event.

   2.12. Make every effort to hold the event at one of the permanent PA systems locations
   (paragraph 4.3.1).
3. Definition of Terms.
   3.1. Official Events (on base):       Parades, change-of-command ceremonies, regular
   commander’s calls only, distinguished visitor events/ceremonies, military award ceremonies,
   military retirement and promotion ceremonies (E-9, O-6 and above), events/ceremonies
   attended by Wing leadership (60 AMW/CC or CV) and open house/air shows.
   3.2. Unofficial Events: Organizational events including but not limited to: commander’s
   calls associated with an unofficial event (e.g. picnic), farewell events, parties, picnics, social
   gatherings, sporting events, booster club events and association events (any event off of
   TAFB is considered unofficial for the purpose of this instruction). Unofficial events may
   sign-out equipment only.
4. Procedures for Requesting Public Address Support.
   4.1. Official Events (on base):
      4.1.1. Request PA support using 60 CS SharePoint site.
      4.1.2. Complete form NLT 5 duty days prior to scheduled event. This will allow
      adequate time for equipment operational checks, planning and coordination and user
      training for official events, if required. Note: Strict adherence to the 5 duty day
      requirement will be enforced to allow for the work center's primary mission of Personal
      Wireless Communication Systems and Ground Radio Communications. 60 CS has no
      manpower allocations for public address; it is provided as a courtesy and mission
      permitting.
      4.1.3. Official PA events will take precedence over unofficial events.
      4.1.4. Same day events will be prioritized by scope and importance of event.
      4.1.5. Permanent and organizationally owned public address systems will be utilized
      where and when possible.
      4.1.6. 60 CS will not support events scheduled at the Delta Breeze Club or Travis
      Conference Center unless directed by 60 CS/CC because they have their own systems.
      4.1.7. Organizations must request access to the base theater through 60 FSS/CCS.
   4.2. Unofficial Events.
      4.2.1. Limited equipment is available for loan to support unofficial events (paragraph
      3.2).
      4.2.2. 60 CS/SCOTR will provide training if needed and if manning allows. Proper care
      and timely return of loaned equipment is essential to ensure quality customer service. A
      report of survey will be initiated for abused, damaged or missing equipment.
      4.2.3. Loaned PA equipment will be prioritized by scope and importance of event.
      4.2.4. Ceremonial music is available upon request. No other music support will be
      provided.

       4.2.5. 60 AMW/CC, 60 MSG/CC or 60 CS/CC may deem an unofficial event official per
       guidelines.
    4.3. Available Public Address Systems.
       4.3.1. Official Event PA Systems:
          4.3.1.1. Permanent PA systems are available for use through 60 CS/SCOT at the
          following locations:
              4.3.1.1.1. Base Theater
              4.3.1.1.2. Hanger P-14 (Wing function use only).
              4.3.1.1.3. Flightline Spot 251 (Wing function use only).
              4.3.1.1.4. Wing Flag Pole.
              4.3.1.1.5. Air Museum.
          4.3.1.2. The following are additional permanent PA setups on Travis AFB. Use of
          these PA systems may be coordinated through the owning agency:
              4.3.1.2.1. Building 381 Auditorium, 60 FSS.
              4.3.1.2.2. Building 31 MXG Atrium, 60 MXG/CCK.
              4.3.1.2.3. 6 ARS Auditorium.
              4.3.1.2.4. 9 ARS Auditorium.
              4.3.1.2.5. Travis Conference Center Ballroom/Daedalian Room.
              4.3.1.2.6. Building P1.
              4.3.1.2.7. DGMC Auditorium.
              4.3.1.2.8. Delta Breeze Club.
          4.3.1.3. Basic PA System: Best for small gatherings up to 100 people. Equipment
          will consist of one microphone, one CD player and two small speakers. This basic
          system is recommended for military award and promotion ceremonies.
          4.3.1.4. Intermediate PA System: Best for medium sized gatherings of 100 to 200
          people. Equipment will consist of up to two microphones, one CD player and two
          medium speakers. This configuration is recommended for change of commands,
          retirements and promotion ceremonies.
          4.3.1.5. Advanced PA System: Best for large sized gatherings over 200 people.
          Equipment will consist of up to four microphones, one CD player and four medium or
          large speakers. This configuration is recommended for base-wide and flight line
          events, change of commands and retirement ceremonies.
       4.3.2. Unofficial Event PA Systems.
          4.3.2.1. All permanent PA systems mentioned in paragraphs 4.3.1.1-4.3.1.2 are
          available for unofficial events with proper coordination through the owning agency.

        4.3.2.2. Basic Sign Out PA System: Best for small gatherings up to 50 people.
        Loaned equipment will consist of one microphone and a maximum of two small
        speakers. This configuration is recommended for squadron functions, sporting events
        and heritage functions.
        4.3.2.3. Intermediate Sign Out PA Systems: Best for medium sized gatherings of 50
        to 100 people. Loaned equipment will consist of one microphone, one CD player and
        two medium speakers. This configuration is recommended for commander calls,
        sporting events and squadron functions.
        4.3.2.4. Advanced Sign Out PA Systems: Best for large sized gatherings over 100
        people. Loaned equipment will consist of two microphones, one CD player and two
        large speakers. This configuration is recommended for large outdoor fund-raisers or
        squadron activities.
