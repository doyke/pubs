This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards and Decorations
Programs. It clarifies the processes for the Air Force Special Operations Command (AFSOC)
Company Grade Officer of the Year (CGOY) Award program and sets forth the eligibility
requirements used to identify and recognize the AFSOC CGOY. This instruction requires the
collection and maintenance of information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974, Privacy Act
System of Records F900 AF MPC, Military Decorations, applies. This instruction applies to all
active duty AFSOC company grade officers (CGOs). This instruction does not apply to the Air
National Guard (ANG) or Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC). Ensure that all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air
Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This revision updates criteria for submitting AFSOC Company Grade Officer of the Year
(CGOY) nominations and contains other minor administrative corrections.
1. General Information. The purpose of the AFSOC CGOY award program is to ensure CGOs
are recognized for their efforts to become effective professional military leaders. The AFSOC

CGOY award will be presented annually at the AFSOC Annual Awards Banquet. The period of
award is 1 January - 31 December. Nominations are due to Headquarters (HQ) AFSOC/A1KP
NLT 15 March each year unless otherwise specified in the award announcement message. This
is a MAJCOM-level award only.
2. Eligibility. All CGOs (second lieutenants, first lieutenants, and captains not selected for
promotion to major) assigned to AFSOC are eligible. Nominees for the annual award are not
limited to quarterly award winners.
     2.1. The following units may submit one nominee to HQ AFSOC/A1 for consideration by
     the MAJCOM board: 23 Air Force (AF), 1 Special Operations Wing (SOW), 27 SOW, 352
     Special Operations Group (SOG), 353 SOG, 720 Special Tactics Group (STG), 724 STG, Air
     Force Special Operations Training Center (AFSOTC), 66 Air Operations Squadron (AOS),
     and HQ AFSOC/Command Support Staff (CSS), as shown in Attachment 2.
        2.1.1. HQ AFSOC/CSS solicits and receives nominations from HQ AFSOC directorates.
        Nomination packages are boarded under authority of the CSS and one nominee will be
        chosen to represent the headquarters staff at the MAJCOM board.
3. Nominations. Nominations will be submitted electronically and will consist of the following:
     3.1. Write-up. AF Form 1206, Nomination for Award, limited to 30 lines to include the
     following mandatory headings:
        3.1.1. LEADERSHIP AND JOB PERFORMANCE:
        3.1.2. SIGNIFICANT SELF-IMPROVEMENT:
        3.1.3. BASE OR COMMUNITY INVOLVEMENT/OTHER
        ACCOMPLISHMENTS:
     3.2. Biography. Biography is limited to one single-spaced, typewritten page, 10- or 12-font
     only. See sample biography at Attachment 2.
4. Selection Procedure. A board consisting of three colonels will review all nominations and
will recommend a winner. Final selection/approval lies with AFSOC/CC.
5. Recognition. The winner will be recognized by the AFSOC/CC during the AFSOC Annual
Awards Banquet.

                                   SAMPLE BIOGRAPHY

                                 Captain Michael L. Allen
                        AFSC: C38F3Q, Squadron Section Commander

Captain Michael L. Allen is a squadron section commander assigned to the 16th Operations
Support Squadron, Hurlburt Field, Florida. He is 28 years old. Captain Allen was born in
Jefferson City, Missouri, on 15 August 1967. He attended Central High School and excelled
across the entire spectrum of school activities. As a 3-year track letterman, his exceptional
performance earned him his team's coveted Scott's Award for spirit, dedication and leadership on
and off the track. After graduation from high school in 1985, Captain Allen was locally
employed while attending St. Louis University in St. Louis, Missouri. He graduated with honors
in the top third of his class. Upon completion of Officer Training School at Lackland Air Force
Base, Texas, in 1989, Captain Allen attended technical training school at Keesler Air Force Base,
Mississippi, where he was an honor graduate. He was then assigned to Randolph Air Force
Base, Texas, where he was the executive support officer to the 12 FTW Commander. He moved
to his present position in 1992. Captain Allen is married to the former Patricia MacHenry of San
Antonio, Texas. They have two children, Sara and Marina. Captain Allen actively participates
in community activities and coaches an area little league team. He was selected as the
Outstanding Company Grade Officer of the Quarter, Jan-Mar 95 and is the recipient of the Air
Force Commendation and Air Force Achievement Medals and has received many civilian awards
for his civic involvement.
