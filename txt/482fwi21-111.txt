This Fighter Wing Instruction (FWI) assigns responsibilities, directs action and prescribes
procedures for notifying and identifying agencies and the products they are responsible for
providing the 482d Maintenance Group (482 MXG), 482d Maintenance Operations Flight/Plans
Scheduling and Documentation (PS&D (482 MOF/MXOOC) office to include in the
Weekly/Monthly/Quarterly schedules. It incorporates procedures from Air Force Instruction
(AFI) 21-101, Air Force Reserve Command Supplement 1 (AFRC Sup 1), Aircraft and
Equipment Maintenance Management. Commanders and supervisors are responsible for
ensuring personnel comply with the provisions of this instruction. Refer recommended changes
and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air
Force Form 847 (AF Form 847), Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form
847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records and disposed of
in accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition      Schedule       (RDS)       located        at      https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. General:
   1.1. The following agencies are required to provide a monthly schedule of maintenance,
   inspections and /or other requirements or request for the month, IAW AFI 21-101, chapter 7:
   1.2. Avionics Flight: Off-equipment maintenance, scheduled Time Compliance Technical
   Orders and other required inspections.
   1.3. Munitions Flight: Loading or configuration requirements and ammunition changes.

     1.4. Propulsion Flight: 6 month engine removal schedule and in-shop inspections.
     1.5. Aerospace Ground Equipment (AGE) Flight: On and Off-equipment maintenance.
     1.6. Petroleum Oil and Lubrication, (POL) requirements.
     1.7. Supply requirements.
     1.8. Food service requirements.
     1.9. Fire department requirements.
     1.10. Security requirements.
     1.11. Civil Engineer requirements.
     1.12. Airfield operations.
     1.13. Random Anti-Terrorism Measure (RAM) requirements.
     1.14. Weapons Load Training, (WLT) schedule.
     1.15. Shift change letter.
     1.16. Monthly calendar from Operations.
     1.17. Monthly aircraft inspections.
     1.18. AF Form 2406, Maintenance Preplan.
     1.19. AF Form 2402, Weekly Equipment Utilization and Maintenance Schedule.
     1.20. Other special requirements.
2. Operations Squadron (OS).
     2.1. OfficerNo later than two workdays before the weekly scheduling meeting, the
     Operations Squadron (OS) operations officer shall give maintenance supervision the
     following information (as required):
        2.1.1. Aircraft takeoff and landing times.
        2.1.2. Configuration requirements.
        2.1.3. Munitions requirements.
        2.1.4. Fuel loads.
        2.1.5. Special or peculiar mission support requirements
        2.1.6. Alert requirements.
        2.1.7. Exercise vulnerability.
        2.1.8. Deployments.
        2.1.9. Off-base sorties.
        2.1.10. On equipment training requirements.
        2.1.11. Other special requirements.
3. Procedures:

  3.1. PS&D shall publish the listed information in the monthly schedule which shall be
  distributed five days prior to the 1st of the month. All agencies are required to provide the
  requested information for the following month no later than the 15th of each month.
