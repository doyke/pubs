AFI 33-332, 29 January 2004, is supplemented as follows:
This publication applies to all Air Education and Training Command (AETC) units and members. It also
applies to Air Force Reserve Command units and only to the Air National Guard (ANG) in United States
Title 10 status assigned to AETC. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-123, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://
webrims.amc.af.mil/rds/index.cfm. Attachment 1 contains a glossary of references and supporting
information.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
This revision incorporates interim change (IC) 2005-1, which deletes guidance on the use of AFVA
33-276, Air Force Privacy Act Label (paragraph 10.2.2.). A bar (|) in the left margin indicates revision in
the left margin.
1.1.1. DoD and Air Force Privacy Act System of Records Notices are listed on the Department of
Defense Privacy Act Web site; DoD notices at: http://www.defenselink.mil/privacy/notices/; Air Force
Notices at: http://www.defenselink.mil/privacy/notices/usaf.
1.3. Privacy Act Complaints. Packages referred to the AETC PA officer will include a copy of the com-
plaint and/or violation, a detailed investigation report, the legal review, as well as all other pertinent infor-
mation regarding the case.
1.5.1. The systems manager for F036 AETC A, Lead Management System (LMS) will review the LMS
contract during the annual systems review to ensure contract is in compliance. Submit the annual review,
IN TURN, to the base PA officer and AETC PA officer no later than 31 December of each year.
1.6.9.1. The FARM is the designated PA monitor (PAM) for their area of responsibility.

1.6.10. The base records manager is also the base PA officer.
1.6.10.1. Provide initial and annual refresher training to unit PAMs and system managers, to include
awareness of PA violation penalties.
1.6.10.4. Use AETC IMT 155, Privacy Act Notice Certification, to validate AETC systems.
1.6.10.7. (Added) Review installation public web sites (see paragraph 12.8 of the basic instruction and

1.6.11.2. Provide and document initial and annual refresher training to personnel using or maintaining PA

1.6.13. (Added) PAMs:
1.6.13.1. (Added) Are the focal points in their functional areas.
1.6.13.2. (Added) Maintain a list of all applicable PA systems of records and the systems managers.
1.6.13.3. (Added) Act as liaison with the base PA officer.
1.6.13.4. (Added) Provide annual training to required personnel.
4.2. Processing a Request for Access. Coordinate all releases responding to a PA written request with
the base legal office. Process requests for records retrieved from a PA system of records with a general or
specific exemption under both the PA and FOIA. See Attachment 3 of the basic for a complete listing of
exempt systems of records. An example of a system of record with an exemption is F031 AF SF B, Secu-
rity Forces Management Information System (SFMIS), exemption (j)(2).
4.2.4. (Added) First party requests for records retrieved from a nonexempt PA system of record will be
processed under the PA, and records will be released by the base PA officer to the requester after all third
party information has been removed. Advise the requester that the releasable documents contain all infor-
mation about them and all third party information was removed. If the requester is a third party requester,
process the request under the FOIA. Additionally, if the retrieved records contain documents not covered
by the system’s categories of records, remove those documents and process them under the FOIA.
4.4. Denying or Limiting Access. Refer full or partial denial packages to the AETC PA Office. Ensure
the cover letter contains the PA system of record number and title, how the record was retrieved, and the
following statement: “These documents are exempt from release in accordance with PA exemption (put
specific exemption here), and if applicable, FOIA exemption (put specific exemption here).” The denial
package will include the cover letter, written request, OPR recommendations, legal review, other admin-
istrative documentation, and one single-sided copy of the records. Highlight in green all information rec-
ommended for denial, and write the appropriate exemptions in the upper right hand corner of each
highlighted document.
4.5. Denial Authorities. The denial authority for AETC is the director or deputy director of communica-
tions and information, HQ AETC/SC.
5.3. Approving or Denying a Record Amendment. Process a denial of a record amendment the same
as described in paragraph 4.4.
