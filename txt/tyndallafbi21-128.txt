This instruction implements AFPD 21-1, Managing Aerospace Equipment Maintenance. This
instruction establishes procedures to reduce flight line and airfield noise levels during late night
periods. This instruction applies to all flying and maintenance units assigned to the 325th Fighter
Wing, associate units, and units deployed to Tyndall AFB. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

The OPR and Certifier have been changed, Removed Attachment #1 Job Qualification Standard
Continuation/Command Job.
1. RESPONSIBILITIES: Supervisors at all levels will ensure strict compliance with this
instruction.
2. PROCEDURES.
   2.1. There will be no take offs or landings between 2200 and 0600 local time without the
   approval of 325th Operations Group (OG)/CC. Squadron operations officers or commanders
   will coordinate the request through the 325 OG/CC/CD. Blanket waivers during scheduled
   night flying, hurricane evacuations or contingency operations may be granted at the
   discretion of the 325 OG/CC.

    2.2. There will be no flight line/trim pad ground engine runs above idle between 2200 and
    0600 local time without the approval of 325th Maintenance Group (MXG)/CC/CD. This
    policy does not apply to Hush House operations. Requirements for run clearance outside of
    established quiet hours will be identified to the 325th Aircraft Maintenance Squadron/325th
    Maintenance Squadron supervision (or deployed maintenance supervisor, for TDY units) for
    evaluation. Maintenance supervision will coordinate the request with MOC who will call the
    325 MXG/CC/CD for approval. Blanket waivers during scheduled night flying, hurricane
    evacuations or contingency operations may be granted at the discretion of the 325 MXG/CC.
    2.3. For approved ground engine runs after quiet hours, MOC will log the following:
       2.3.1. Type of aircraft.
       2.3.2. Time of run.
       2.3.3. Power setting used.
       2.3.4. Discrepancy requiring run.
       2.3.5. Why the run could not be delayed until after 0600.
