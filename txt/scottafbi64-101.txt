This instruction implements AFI 64-101, Multi-channel Video Programming Distribution
(Broadcast Cable), AFI 65-106, Appropriated Fund Support of Morale, Welfare, and Recreation
(MWR) and Nonappropriated Fund Instrumentalities (NAFIS), and AFI 65-601, Volume 1,
Budget Guidance and Procedures. This instruction establishes guidelines for requisition,
validation, funding and installation of Cable Television Services (CATV) in government offices
on Scott AFB. This guidance is applicable to 375th Air Mobility Wing and tenant units residing
on Scott AFB. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/ . Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to 375th Communications Group Plans and Program (CG/SCX) using the
AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. This publication may not be
supplemented.

1. Background. As of June 2007, Air Mobility Command has a recurring requirement to allow
Internet Protocol Television Distribution System (IPTV), a technology that provides the war-
fighter with up-to-date information direct to the desktop, to replace our current reliance on local
dedicated CATV transmission. IPTV technology, when combined with contracted multimedia
access, enables access to specific nationally broadcast television channels (CNN, MSNBC, Fox
News and the Weather Channel), access to military channels, and the capability to support
training via the desktop. IPTV reduces costs associated with CATV. CATV funding is now

2. Responsibilities.
     2.1. Unit Commanders will:
        2.1.1. Appoint a unit CATV/IPTV Monitor.           The appointee may be the Unit
        Requirements Officer with access to CIPS or the Resource Advisor of the requesting unit.
        2.1.2. Ensure all requests for CATV or IPTV are submitted through the unit CATV or
        IPTV Monitor.
        2.1.3. Consider utilizing IPTV, which is provided by 375 CG, prior to procuring
        commercial cable television services from an authorized local provider. Thoroughly
        review the requirement for CATV to ensure it is For Official Use Only.
        2.1.4. Budget, fund and validate CATV installation cost and monthly service fees.
        Premium channels are unauthorized For Official Use Only. Non-375th Air Mobility
        Wing (AMW) tenants must fund all of their CATV services.
     2.2. Unit CATV/IPTV Monitor will:
        2.2.1. Serve as the unit CATV focal point for installation and operational management of
        CATV connections.
        2.2.2. Ensure all requests are submitted through CIPS and attach a letter, endorsed by
        their commander that clearly identifies and justifies CATV.
        2.2.3. Ensure requestors prepare a single-line drawing of the proposed CATV installation
        area and attach it to CIPS.
        2.2.4. Directly notify the vendor or designated representative within the customer’s
        facility of any breakdown in service access. Notify 375 CG/SCX if vendor does not
        provide satisfactory service.
        2.2.5. Ensure CATV connections are not altered to allow unauthorized hookups.
        Maintain records on all cable drop locations within their area of responsibility.
        2.2.6. Assist in the periodic physical inventory of CATV connections within the assigned
        area of responsibility.
     2.3. Base Communications Computer Systems Officer (CSO) will:
        2.3.1. Serve as the approval authority of all For Official Use Only CATV requests.
        2.3.2. Appoint a Base CATV Program Manager (PM).
        2.3.3. Appoint a Base CATV Quality Assurance Evaluator (QAE).
     2.4. Base CATV Program Manager will:
        2.4.1. Assist the contracting officer with the negotiation and administration of CATV
        Franchise Agreement and related contract actions for television services.
        2.4.2. Receive and review each CATV requirement submitted for service availability.

     2.4.3. Present the requirement to the base communications-computer system officer
     (CSO) for approval/disapproval.
     2.4.4. Assist customers with defining their requirements for television services and, with
     support organizations, prepare the television services system description, including the
     Performance Work Statement (PWS).
     2.4.5. Work with the QAE to track customer or unit complaints.
  2.5. Base CATV Quality Assurance Evaluator will:
     2.5.1. Help prepare the PWS, evaluate proposals, and verify that services meet minimum
     Air Force requirements.
     2.5.2. Survey potential installation sites with base civil engineer to assess interference
     and potential communication issues.
     2.5.3. Ensure periodic re-validation and inventory are conducted and documented for all
     CATV connections.
     2.5.4. Maintain periodic revalidation of CATV connections.
     2.5.5. Work with customers to identify and remove existing CATV connections that are
     no longer needed.
     2.5.6. Report customer complaints to the PM and contracting officer.

For Official Use Only—Appropriated funds may be used to pay for CATV services authorized

mission needs, then it should be used instead of CATV services.

processing system, replaced the AF Form 3215.
