This instruction implements AFPD 44-1, Medical Operations. It establishes policy guidance and delin-
eates the responsibilities and procedures required to implement the United States Air Forces in Europe
(USAFE) Military Blood Program. The provisions of this instruction apply in peacetime and wartime,
unless otherwise directed, and pertain to all USAFE medical facilities. This instruction does not apply to
Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) or Air National Guard (ANG) units. Ensure that all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-123,
Management of Records and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at: https://afrims.amc.af.mil.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Deletes USAFE Blood
Donor Center requirements referenced in the previous edition.


1. Policy:
   1.1. The USAFE Military Blood Program Office (USAFE MBPO) is under the medical direction of
   the USAFE Surgeon and manned by personnel who are technically qualified and fully aware of blood
   program instructions, requirements, and capabilities.
   1.2. USAFE conducts a blood program to meet the following objectives:
       1.2.1. Meet the overall blood and blood component requirements of all patients receiving medical
       care in USAFE medical treatment facilities (MTF).
       1.2.2. Properly manage blood components.

       1.2.3. Offer state-of-the-art blood storage, and transfusion services according to Air Force Blood
       Program standard operating procedures. AFMAN(I) 41-111, Standards for Blood Banks and
       Transfusion Services establishes these standards. All USAFE MTFs will use forms designated by
       AFI 44-105, The Air Force Blood Program.

2. General:
    2.1. The United States European Command (USEUCOM) Joint Blood Program Office (JBPO) serves
    as the overall manager for blood and blood components within USEUCOM. The USAFEMBPO pro-
    vides a system for storage, and distribution of blood and blood components for USAFE MTFs during
    peacetime, wartime, and contingency operations. Wartime management of these resources, not other-
    wise assigned to USAFE, is provided by the USEUCOM JBPO.
    2.2. During peacetime and contingency operations, MTFs utilizing blood and blood components will
    establish internal procedures for the processing, storage, and use of blood resources according to AFI
    44-105, and AFMAN(I) 41-119, The Technical Manual of the American Association of Blood Banks
    as appropriate. Requirements that exceed local capabilities are forwarded to the USAFE MBPO or, in
    emergencies, directly to the Commander, United States Army in Europe (USAREUR) Blood Bank,
    Landstuhl, Germany.
    2.3. During wartime, USAFE MTFs will follow operational guidance according to USEUCOM
    Directive 67-4, (S), USEUCOM Joint Blood Program. USAFE will augment the USEUCOMJBPO
    by providing a deputy director, USEUCOM joint blood program and two USEUCOM area blood pro-
    gram officers. Designated USAFE Blood Transshipment Centers will become fully operational.
    2.4. The USAFE MBPO will maintain blood program operations, as feasible, as required by the rec-
    ommendations of the USEUCOM Joint Blood Program Office. Wartime planning will be according
    to USEUCOM JBPO directives.

3. Responsibilities:
    3.1. USAFE Surgeon:
       3.1.1. Establishes a military blood program to meet blood and blood component requirements of
       all patients receiving medical care in USAFE medical treatment facilities.
       3.1.2. Establishes the guidance and procedures for medical direction of the USAFE Military
       Blood Program.
       3.1.3. Appoints, in writing, primary and alternate USAFE blood program managers, and two area
       blood program managers as required by USEUCOM Directive 67-4 unless otherwise specified.
           3.1.3.1. Designates an officer who is qualified by experience or training to administer the
           command blood program as the primary blood program manager. The alternate blood pro-
           gram manager is the Area I Joint Blood Program Officer.
           3.1.3.2. Designates a laboratory officer qualified by training or experience in blood banking,
           at the 48 Medical Group (MDG) Lakenheath Royal Air Force (RAF), as the USEUCOM Area
           1 Joint Blood Program Office. (AJBPO1).
           3.1.3.3. Appoints the Chief, Laboratory Services, 39 MDG Incirlik, UNIT 7095 BOX 185,
           APO AE 09824-5185, as the USEUCOM Area Joint Blood Program Office IV. (AJBPO4).

        3.1.3.4. During training exercises, contingencies, and wartime operations, releases the pri-
        mary blood program manager to augment the USEUCOM JBPO.
        3.1.3.5. Provides mechanisms for travel and pay allowances for area joint blood program
        officers to perform blood program functions, to include annual inspection of blood transfusion
        services and blood transshipment centers, and participation in training exercises.
        3.1.3.6. Designates USAFE facilities required to establish and support a Blood Transship-
        ment Center TA893D (receipt, inspection, storage, re-ice, and transshipment capability).
  3.2. Chief, USAFE Blood Program:
     3.2.1. Determines USAFE Blood Program wartime readiness requirements.
     3.2.2. Monitors USAFE Blood Bank operations through reports and inspections.
        3.2.2.1. Provides the USEUCOM JBPO a quarterly consolidated component Armed Services
        Blood Program Blood Bank Operational Report (BBOR), DD Form 2555, Blood Bank Oper-
        ational Report, no later than 30 days after the end of each quarter.
     3.2.3. Maintains close liaison with other component blood program managers, donor center offic-
     ers, and medical treatment facilities supporting blood transshipment center and blood donor center
     operations.
     3.2.4. Ensures an adequate level of USAFE Blood Program readiness is maintained through exer-
     cises and inspections.
        3.2.4.1. Ensures that area joint blood program officers support exercises and contingency
        operations, and that they obtain appropriate security clearances and participate in medical
        planning functions.
     3.2.5. Provides technical and policy guidance to USAFE Surgeon and USAFE medical treatment
     facilities.
     3.2.6. Coordinates and ensures proper utilization and management of USEUCOM/USAFE blood
     products and resources.
     3.2.7. Ensures use of Armed Services Blood Program standardized forms and labels for blood
     program activities.
     3.2.8. On request, coordinates training requirement for Blood Donor Center and Blood Transship-
     ment Center personnel requiring additional training not available at their facility.
  3.3. USAFE Designated Area Joint Blood Program Officers:
     3.3.1. Establish lines of communication and develop and maintain information on the capability
     of all regional assets (medical facilities and Blood Transshipment Centers in the region) to store,
     and transship blood and blood components.
     3.3.2. Maintain close liaison with the Chief, USAFE Blood Program and component blood man-
     agers, including participation in training programs, exercises, and blood program planning.
  3.4. Designated Blood Transshipment Centers:
     3.4.1. Develop operational plans by USEUCOM Directive 67-4.
     3.4.2. Operate according to applicable portions of AFMAN (I)41-111.

       3.4.3. Conduct semiannual training exercises, involving assigned personnel, to ensure familiarity
       with administrative and operational procedures.
       3.4.4. Participate in exercises as directed by USAFE Surgeon.
    3.5. References. References in paragraph 1. outline wartime responsibilities for all USAFE Blood
    Program functions.

4. Reports. Use DD Form 2555, Blood Bank Operational Report, for the Air Force quarterly Blood
