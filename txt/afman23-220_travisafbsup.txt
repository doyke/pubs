AFMAN 23-220, Reports of Survey for Air Force Property, dated 1 July 1996 is supplemented
as indicated in the summary of changes. The provisions of this instruction apply to all Active
Duty, Reserve, other Armed Forces, DoD Civilians, and contractors who utilize Air Force
property on Travis AFB.

Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
from the field through Major Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms managers. Ensure that
all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. The
use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or
service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. The purpose of
this supplement is to delineate portions of AFMAN 23-220, Reports of Survey for Air Force
Property, as applicable to Travis AFB.

2.3.5. Units whose members are found to have abused property resulting in loss, damage, or
destruction will be responsible for reimbursing the accountable unit as established in local policy
(e.g., base instruction, policy letter, etc., or as determined by the ROS Approving Authority).
2.3.12.2. If a member is found financially liable for individual equipment items such as mobility
or flight gear intended for their personal use, they will be liable for the entire cost IAW AFMAN
23-220, Chapter 15. A ROS is required in all such cases unless an individual desires to pay the
amount up front.
2.3.18. Subject to the provisions of Chapter 3 and paragraph 8.4.4 of the basic manual, the
responsible commander may submit a waiver (Attachment 7) to bypass the requirements for a
ROS when the loss, damage, or destruction of property is $500.00 or less. The waiver request
must clearly establish that abuse, negligence, willful misconduct, deliberate unauthorized use,
fraud or theft was not involved. The waiver letter must be routed through the Base ROS
Manager for approval before the accountable unit adjusts required records.
5.2. In cases when ROS processing timelines cannot be met, the appointed ROS Investigating
Officer (IO) may submit an extension request approved by their unit commander to the Base
ROS Program Manager, 60 LRS/LGLO.
8.2.2. The 60th Mission Support Group (MSG) Commander is designated by the Base
Commander as the Senior ROS Approving Authority for Travis AFB. As such, the 60 MSG/CC
appoints the Base ROS Program Manager and has signature authority for all ROS cases except
those initiated within the 615th Contingency Response Wing (CRW). The 615th Contingency
Operations Support Group (COSG) Commander is designated Approving Authority for the 615
CRW and has signature authority for all ROS cases initiated within the 615 CRW.
9.1. The 60th Air Mobility Wing (AMW) Group Commanders are designated as the Appointing
Authority for units aligned under their command. For the 60 MSG, the Deputy Commander (60
MSG/CD) is designated as the Appointing Authority for all units within the 60 MSG and all
tenant units. For the 615 CRW the 615 COSG/CC or delegated representative is designated as
the Appointing Authority for all units.
11.2. The Base ROS Program is aligned under the 60th Logistics Readiness Squadron
Operational Compliance (60 LRS/LGLO) section; however, the appointed Base ROS Program
Manager works directly for the Approving Authority in matters concerning the ROS program.
11.6. To aid in meeting ROS processing timelines and other program objectives, each unit shall
appoint a primary and alternate unit-level ROS Monitor to serve as the focal point for all Reports
of Survey initiated within their unit. Once appointed, these individuals will provide the Base
ROS Program Manager with an appointment memorandum from their commander and contact
the Base ROS Program Manager for training associated with their assigned duties. Unit
Monitors are responsible for the coordination of all ROS originated within their unit and for
maintenance of historical files for 1 year.
14.1.1. IOs will submit the original DD Form 200, to the Base ROS Program Manager.
Attachments to the DD Form 200, including witness statements or depositions, must be originals.
Copies of police reports, vehicle accident/abuse reports, maintenance repair reports, equipment
logs, listings, or receipts must be legible.

14.4.2.4. When individuals make restitution the Base ROS manager will prepare a DD Form
