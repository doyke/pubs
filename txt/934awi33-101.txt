This instruction implements AFPD 33-1, Information Resources Management. It provides
information regarding audio/visual equipment and public address system usage and guidance to
934th Airlift Wing personnel on the capabilities and limitations of Audio/Visual (A/V) support
provided by the 934th Communications Flight (934 CF). Responsibilities and resources for both
the customer and the 934 CF are included. It establishes responsibilities and outlines procedures
for obtaining A/V support. Information is also provided on alternate means of obtaining support
and applies to all 934thAirlift Wing units. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the office of primary responsibility (OPR) (see Attachment 1) using the AF Form
847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Official records created as a result of this
publication shall be maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rds/rds_series.cfm.

1. Responsibilities:
   1.1. The 934 CF Radio Frequency Transmission (RF) (934 CF/SCOT):
       1.1.1. The primary mission is to maintain radio communications systems in support of
       the installation and national missions. Any outage affecting, or potentially affecting
       safety or the SCOT mission, has priority over Audio/Visual (A/V) support.
       1.1.2. Supported Events:
           1.1.2.1. Retirements:
              1.1.2.1.1. Set-up and operate A/V equipment for retirement ceremonies for the

              rank of Colonel and above and all CMSgt retirement ceremonies.
              1.1.2.1.2. For other retirement ceremonies, portable audio systems can be
              checked out and equipment operation will be conducted by the requesting unit.
          1.1.2.2. Change of Command Ceremonies.
          1.1.2.3. Set-up and operate A/V equipment for Change of Command ceremonies at
          Group level and above.
          1.1.2.4. Commander’s Calls:
              1.1.2.4.1. Set-up and operate A/V equipment for Wing Commander Calls and
              Wing level ceremonies.
              1.1.2.4.2. Provide portable sign out equipment for squadron/flight commander’s
              calls on a first come, first serve basis.
          1.1.2.5. Other Authorized Events:
              1.1.2.5.1. Any other official function authorized by 934 AW/CC, 934 AW/CV,
              934 MSG/CC or 934 CF/CC will be supported by 934 CF/SCOT personnel.
       1.1.3. 934 CF/SCOT personnel will provide official military music pertaining to all
       branches of military service.        This includes, but is not limited to, official
       cadence/marching music, Ruffles and Flourishes, the Star Spangled Banner, respective
       military branches songs, and so forth.
       1.1.4. Functions not approved for government A/V system use:
          1.1.4.1. Under no circumstances will an A/V system be provided for any profit
          making events in accordance with AFI 65-106, Appropriated Fund Support of
          Morale, Welfare, and Recreation (MWR) and Nonappropriated Fund
          Instrumentalities (NAFIS).
          1.1.4.2. Will not be tasked to operate fixed A/V systems belonging to another unit,
          unless approved by the 934 CF/CC.
          1.1.4.3. Will not be tasked to operate systems off-site of military installations unless
          approved by 934 AW/CC, 934 AW/CV, 934 MSG/CC or 934 CF/CC.
       1.1.5. May cancel A/V set-ups or remove A/V equipment from operation if personnel or
       equipment safety is threatened.
       1.1.6. Will not provide disc jockey services for any A/V set-up.
       1.1.7. 934 AW/PA will provide projector/screen support to 934 AW units.
    1.2. Scheduling:
       1.2.1. The 934 CF Communications Focal Point (CFP) is the section responsible for all
       scheduling of approved audio/visual requests.
          1.2.1.1. Will receive all requests for A/V support and assign them to 934 CF/SCOT,
          as required.

2. Procedures to Request On-Site Public Address Support:
   2.1. Customer will submit an AF Form 833 to the 934 CFP. The request form must be
   completed no less than 30 days prior to the day of the event. A draft copy of the event script
   is required to be attached to any requests. This is to provide adequate time for processing
   and scheduling. This also provides the requesting unit time to locate an alternate source in
   the event a request is denied or cannot be satisfied.
   2.2. The 934 CF/SCOT is not responsible for procurement, installation or maintenance of
   A/V systems for other installation units/squadrons. Permanently installed A/V systems are
   justified and procured by the using organization in accordance with Table of Allowance 006
   and AFMAN 23-110. The 934 CF/SCOT may be used to provide technical solutions on A/V
   system acquisition by properly submitting a 934 AW Form 30.
      2.2.1. For a short notice A/V request, the requesting organization will submit a Public
      Address Request Form as outlined in paragraph 2.1. The request form must be endorsed
      by the requesting organization’s Squadron Commander or above.
3. Procedures to Request Sign-Out A/V Support:
   3.1. The 934 CF/SCOT has portable audio systems for checkout. Contact the CFP for
   availability/suggestions on portable systems. Training is provided at the time of receipt to
   ensure the customer can operate the equipment. Audio equipment signed out can be picked
   up at Building 761 one duty day prior to the event and must be returned no later than 1600
   the first duty day after the event. Extensions are handled on a case by case basis.
      3.1.1. All A/V equipment is signed out on an AF Form 1297 Temporary Issue Receipt, at
      the time it is issued. Equipment must be returned in the condition it was loaned. OPRs
      will roll/secure all cables to prevent tangling prior to turn in. Identify broken parts,

        problems, or discrepancies when the equipment is returned. The OPR is responsible for
        any lost or damaged equipment.
4. Approved A/V Requests:
     4.1. The requesting unit POC will be contacted for confirmation and for any additional
     information that may be required. POCs will also be contacted the day before the event to
     coordinate any final changes.
     4.2. All agencies (that is, public affairs, local media, and so forth) requesting interface with
     an A/V system must connect to and test the system no less than 1 hour prior to the start of an
     event. POCs should annotate any planned interfaces on the A/V Request. Should this
     additional equipment cause interference with the A/V system, the 934 CF/SCOT technician
     is authorized to disconnect equipment and deny the interface.
     4.3. Practices:
        4.3.1. Official practices will be supported by the full complement of A/V equipment.
5. Audio Equipment:
5.1. The 934 CF/SCOT can provide the following audio equipment, on a sign-out basis, for use
at official functions.
        5.1.1. Small Venue: Designed for audiences up to 100 people with only one microphone
        needed and no music required. Consists of portable PA system with two speakers and
        one microphone.
        5.1.2. Medium Venue: Designed for audiences up to 150 people with one or two
        microphones and music required. Consists of portable PA system with two speakers, two
        microphones and a CD/MP3 player.
        5.1.3. Large Venue: Designed for audiences ranging from 200-300 people with multiple
        microphones and music required. Consists of portable mixer/amplifier, two to four
        speakers, microphones as needed, and a CD/MP3 player.
     5.2. The 934 CF/SCOT can provide and operate audio equipment for setups ranging from
     50-800 people, involving multiple microphones, speakers, and outputs to media personnel
     when directed by 934 CF/CC.

Terms
Fixed A/V System—A system permanently installed in a base facility.
Portable A/V System—A system set-up on a temporary non-repetitive basis, for any function of
short duration.
Portable Audio System for Sign Out—A system signed out by the requesting office, set-up on
a temporary, non-repetitive basis, for any function of short duration. System is returned to the
934 CF/SCOT Work Center upon event completion.
Official Function— A direct military related function such as a change of command, retreat,
retirement ceremony, dining-in, commander’s call, briefings, and so forth.
