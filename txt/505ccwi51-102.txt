This instruction describes the duties and responsibilities of staff judge advocates and defines
specific legal requirements unique to the 505 CCW for Commanders. The Judge Advocate
General’s Corps (TJAGC) provides professional legal services needed to accomplish the mission
of the US Air Force and maintain the highest degree of effectiveness and readiness.

This instruction requires the collection and maintenance of information protected by the Privacy
Act of 1974 authorized by title 10 U.S.C.and is subject to Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)
requirements. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command.

1. The Judge Advocate General’s Corps.
     1.1. Staff Judge Advocates. Unless otherwise specified by The Judge Advocate General, in
     units of the active duty force, Air Force Reserve and Air National Guard in Federal service,
     the senior judge advocate on the commander’s staff will serve as the commander’s staff
     judge advocate (SJA). A staff judge advocate must advise his or her convening authority of
     the statutory duty under 10 U.S.C. 806(b) to at all times communicate directly with the staff
     judge advocate in matters relating to administering military justice. An SJA:
        1.1.1. Provides legal services required by commanders and staff agencies.
        1.1.2. Advises commanders on disciplinary matters, prepares charge sheets and assists in
        preparing nonjudicial punishment actions; provides legal advice and assistance to
        Security Forces and Air Force Office of Special Investigations personnel; and provides
        advice to commanders and investigating officers on all investigations conducted under a
        commander’s inherent authority or under regulation.
        1.1.3. Provides legal advice and reviews actions for legal sufficiency.
        1.1.4. Acts as Air Force liaison with the US Attorney and other Federal, state, and local
        legal departments, administrative agencies and judicial bodies; represents Air Force
        interests in utility rate matters; supports command and represents Air Force interests in
        environmental and civilian labor matters..
        1.1.5. Provides briefings required by law, directive, instruction, or policy.
        1.1.6. Drafts and reviews operation and exercise plans for compliance with the law of
        armed conflict; provides advice to commanders and staffs on international law matters,
        including foreign criminal jurisdiction, host country law, civil litigation, negotiations, and
        treaty and agreement interpretation; and provides advice and counsel to participants in
        mobility and contingency operations.
2. 505 CCW SJA/Legal Advisor.
     2.1. 505 CCW.
        2.1.1. Most traditional legal services in paragraph 1.1 will be provided by the host
        installation (Hurlburt Field) SJA, pursuant to a host-tenant support agreement. This
        includes support for military justice actions, claims services, civilian labor law,
        environmental law, contracting, and any other legal matters outside the purview of the
        505 CCW legal advisor. Depending on the scope of the issue, the 505 CCW legal advisor
        will provide the appropriate support when available, time and resources permitting.
        2.1.2. Within the 505 CCW, the 505 CCW SJA/Legal Advisor will:
            2.1.2.1. Provide legal advice on contracting issues.
            2.1.2.2. Provide legal advice on fiscal law issues.
            2.1.2.3. Provide legal advice on ethics issues and manage the OGE Form 450
            program.
            2.1.2.4. Provide legal assistance, including notary services, to all eligible personnel.
            2.1.2.5. Provide legal advice on civil law issues.

          2.1.2.6. Provide any additional legal advice as required by the commander.
   2.2. 505 TRG.
       2.2.1. 505 CCW SJA/Legal Advisor will support the 505 TRG as follows:
          2.2.1.1. Provide platform instruction and develop and maintain lessons and ancillary
          materials for the following courses:
Familiarization Course
Air and Space Operations Center (AOC) Initial Qualification Training
Command and Control advanced training
Joint/Combined Senior Staff Course
Any other course designated by the commander
          2.2.1.2. Provide over-the-shoulder training for AOC personnel at all command-
          related or sponsored exercises.
          2.2.1.3. Develop and maintain JA Training Task Lists.
       2.2.2. SJA/Legal advisor will provide any additional legal support as required by the
       commander.
   2.3. 505 DWG.
       2.3.1. 505 CCW SJA/Legal Advisor will support the 505 DWG as follows:
          2.3.1.1. Develop and implement legal-based Mission Event Synchronization Lists
          (MSEL) for all command-related or sponsored exercises.
          2.3.1.2. Assist in the development of Rules of Engagement and other ancillary legal
          documents for all command-related or sponsored exercises.
          2.3.1.3. Provide legal professional control force for all command-related or
          sponsored exercises.
       2.3.2. SJA/Legal advisor will provide any additional legal support as required by the
       commander.
   2.4. 505 OG.
       2.4.1. 505 CCW SJA/Legal Advisor will support the 505 OG as follows:
          2.4.1.1. Develop and maintain JA portions of all AOC-related documents, including,
          but not limited to, AFIs, AFOTTP, and DASH-1.
          2.4.1.2. Develop and maintain JA-specific TTP for AOC systems used by AOC legal
          personnel.
