This mission directive defines the mission of AFNWC and implements Air Force Policy
Directive 10-1, Mission Directives. It does not apply to the Air National Guard or the US Air
Force Reserve.

Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of IAW the Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).

Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication;
route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes
include: Paragraph 1, Mission, including list of components.
1. Mission. The Air Force Nuclear Weapons Center (AFNWC) is responsible for delivery of
quality solutions to sustain, recapitalize, and modernize Nuclear Warfighting Capabilities and to
ensure safe, secure, and effective nuclear weapons and related nuclear certified systems in
support of the President, as well as supporting warfighter operational objectives. Additionally,
the AFNWC provides a mission critical base for Chemical and Biological threat agent

neutralization technology, including leading the development and testing of weapons effect
modeling for operational environments. The AFNWC is responsible for all safety, security, and
configuration aspects of nuclear weapons management and Chemical and Biological agent
neutralization technology including sustainment, modernization, and storage for the Departments
of Defense and Energy. AFNWC accomplishes its mission through three components: the 377th
Air Base Wing (Kirtland AFB), the ICBM Systems Directorate (Hill AFB), and the Nuclear
Capabilities Directorate (Kirtland AFB).
2. Command. AFNWC, Kirtland AFB, New Mexico, is a primary subordinate unit of Air
Force Materiel Command (AFMC). The 377th ABW Commander is the Installation
Commander.
3. Organization Structure. The organization structures of AFNWC and its subordinate units
are in accordance with the Air Force Blueprint, AFI 38-101, Air Force Organization, and HQ
USAF/HQ AFMC - approved variances to the structures. The approved blueprint organizational
structure                 for                AFNWC                    is                 at:
https://org.eis.afmc.af.mil/sites/hqafmca1/A1M/Organization/AFMC%20Organization%20
Blueprint%20Charts/Forms/AllItems.aspx.
     3.1. Organization Changes. Requests to change the organization structure of AFNWC and
     its subordinate organizations and units must be made in accordance with AFI 38-101 and
     HQ AFMC/A1 policy guidance. Requests requiring HQ AFMC/A1 approval must be
     submitted through the AFNWC/DPB and approved by the AFNWC/CC.
