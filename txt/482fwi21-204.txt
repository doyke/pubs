This instruction incorporates procedures from Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101, Air Force
Reserve Command Supplement (AFRC SUP I), Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance
Management. It establishes Transient Alert (TA) procedures for all 482d Maintenance Group
(482 MXG) personnel and Satellite Services Incorporated (SSI) and Environmental Management
Incorporated (EMI) maintenance personnel. Commanders and supervisors are responsible for
ensuring personnel comply with the provisions of this instruction. Refer recommended changes
and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air
Force Form 847 (AF Form 847), Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form
847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records and disposed of
in accordance with (IAW) the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS)
Records     Disposition    Schedule    (RDS)     located     at    https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. 482 MXG Responsibilities:
   1.1. In addition to the TA requirements prescribed in AFI 21-101, the 482 MXG shall
   support the following:
       1.1.1. Upon request from the Service Provider (SP), Satellite Services Incorporated
       (SSI), the 482d Maintenance Operations Center, (MOC) shall:
       1.1.2. Contact 482d Aircraft Maintenance Squadron (482 AMXS) and 482d Maintenance
       Squadron (482 MXS) for maintenance support on F-16C/D, Block 30 aircraft only.
2                                                         482FWI21-204 9 FEBRUARY 2012


       1.1.3. In the event of a transient aircraft In Flight Emergency (IFE), contact 482 AMXS
       Weapons Loading Section for arming or de-arming, unloading/loading actions, impulse
       cart storage and weapons safety pins.
           1.1.3.1. The Weapons Loading section will store transient aircraft impulse carts as
           prescribed in 482d Maintenance Group Operating Instruction (482 MXGOI) 21-111,
           Aircraft Impulse Cartridge Storage and Transportation Cartridges for transient
           aircraft shall be separated from local cartridges while in the locker.
           1.1.3.2. The responsible supervisor for impulse cartridge storage will ensure that
           quantity, compatibility, and explosive class/division limits are not exceeded for the
           licensed location. The Munitions Storage Area (MSA) will segregate and courtesy
           store munitions items for reload or further disposition.
           1.1.3.3. In the event transient aircraft require munitions downloading for
           maintenance purposes, the MSA will provide necessary munitions trailers and
           provide necessary storage of munitions until the aircraft has been repaired and
           returned to Fully Mission Capable (FMC) status.
    1.2. 482 MXS Non Destructive Inspection Section performs oil sample inspections during
    normal duty hours.
    1.3. 482 MXS Egress Section shall safe F-16C/D, Block 30 aircraft only, if maintenance is
    required. All other aircraft explosive egress systems shall be rendered safe by the transient
    aircrew.
    1.4. 482 MXG/Quality Assurance (482 MXGMXQ) shall perform inspections on TA
    personnel and equipment. All TA inspections shall be updated in the Quality Assurance
    Trending and Tracking Analysis System (QANTTAS).
       1.4.1. 482 MXG/MXQ will email the 482d Mission Support Group, Performance
       Management Office (482 MSG/PMO) the QANTTAS report for review and action, if
       required. If an inspection is rated a fail, the 482 MSG/PMO shall ensure SSI provides
       corrective action and will e-mail the QANTTAS report to 482 MXG/MXQ.
