This Luke Air Force Base instruction (LAFBI) establishes guidance for shipping, receiving, and
providing a secure holding area or refuge for explosive shipments at 56th Fighter Wing (56
FW). It implements DoD 4500.9-R, Defense Transportation Regulation, Parts I-VI, and AFPD
24-2, Preparation and Movement of Air Force Materiel, and applies to 56 FW/SEW, Weapons
Safety; 56 CES/CED, Explosive Ordnance Disposal; 56 CES/CEF, Fire Emergency Services;

56 SFS/SFO, Security Forces; 56 EMS/LGMWOC, Munitions Control; 56 OSS/OSAA, Airfield
Management; and 56 LRS/LGRD, Deployment and Distribution Flight. This publication does
not apply to Air Force Reserve Command or Air National Guard Units. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from
the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at

https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. Safeguarding: Handling munitions constitutes a continuous potential hazard to personnel
and property. It is mandatory that safety regulations be followed to the letter. Receipt
inspections will be conducted to determine if any damage or tampering occurred during transit.
The 56 LRS/LGRD, Distribution Section, will periodically check procedures and operations
involving the receipt of explosives and/or sensitive materials. The term “explosives” as used
herein refers to conventional explosive material.
2. Safety Precautions:
     2.1. All personnel engaged directly or indirectly with the operations involving explosives
     items must be thoroughly briefed on safety operations including emergencies, firefighting
     procedures, and evacuation distances. Drivers will not assist with loading or unloading.
     2.2. In accordance with (IAW) AFMAN 91-201, Explosives Safety Standards, each
     explosives vehicle used for transportation will be equipped with at least two portable fire
     extinguishers, each rated at least 2A:10BC. All purpose dry chemical extinguishers of five
     pounds or greater are acceptable.
     2.3. All vehicles will be properly placarded prior to movement when required by CFR 49,
     Transportation.
     2.4. Smoking within the Munitions Storage Area (MSA) will only be permitted within
     designated areas. Outside the MSA; smoking is permitted 50 feet away from loaded
     vehicles.
     2.5. All explosives must be stable and secure before movement.
     2.6. Security Forces will immediately notify Fire Emergency Services when a shipment of
     explosives arrives at Luke AFB by motor vehicle. Notification of incoming shipments will
     aid in pre-incident planning and prevent possible delays in receipt of property.
3. Emergency Procedures:
     3.1. Prompt action will be taken to control any hazard. If an immediate danger is
     encountered, all operations in the vicinity will be stopped and personnel evacuated to a safe
     location IAW AFMAN 91-201. Explosives Ordnance Disposal and Fire Emergency Services
     personnel will be called to eliminate the hazard. Operations will not be resumed until the
     hazard has been eliminated.
     3.2. When an abnormal condition is noted and pertinent procedures do not specifically relate
     to the noted irregularity, work will be stopped. Technically qualified guidance will obtained
     before the operations continue.
     3.3. Wing Weapons Safety will be notified as soon as possible following any explosives
     mishap.
4. Responsibilities:
     4.1. Commander, 56th Equipment Maintenance Squadron (EMS) will:
        4.1.1. Ensure no commercial vehicles are loaded with munitions items (explosive,
        chemical or inert) without notifying Distribution Section.
        4.1.2. Maintain and provide a DoD-approved secure lighted and paved holding area for
        vehicles to be parked for shipments requiring secure holding.

     4.1.3. With a Distribution Section representative, have shipping documents examined to
     prevent entry of unauthorized shipments into the MSA.
  4.2. Commander, 56th Logistics Readiness Squadron (LRS) will:
     4.2.1. Ensure Distribution Section has Restricted Area Badges to permit unescorted entry
     into the MSA.
     4.2.2. Ensure Distribution Section personnel inspect all motor carriers’ equipment on all
     inbound/outbound munitions shipments and equipment using DD Form 626, Motor
     Vehicle Inspection (Transporting Hazardous Materials).
     4.2.3. Provide briefings to military drivers using DD Form 836, Dangerous Goods
     Shipping Paper/Declaration and Emergency Response Information for Hazardous
     Materials Transported by Government Vehicles.
     4.2.4. Ensure motor vehicles carrying explosives have proper placards IAW the
     Department of Transportation Regulations and CFR 49.
     4.2.5. Ensure Air Terminal Operations (ATO) coordinates with Airfield Management on
     parking aircraft IAW LAFBI 13-203, Para 2.19., Aircraft Operations and Base Flying
     Procedures. Aircraft will be parked in a location properly sited for the munitions
     operation being performed.
     4.2.6. Ensure ATO personnel are available for any cargo aircraft arriving for the on or
     off loading of munitions shipments.
  4.3. Commander, 56th Security Forces Squadron (SFS) will:
     4.3.1. Assist commercial carriers requesting secure holdings. Carriers may approach
     security gate personnel seeking secure holding during emergencies or other
     circumstances beyond the carrier’s control, for delivery, waiting for shipment loading, or
     while in transit to another DoD location. Drivers must remain with tractor and trailer at
     all times.
     4.3.2. Notify Distribution Section personnel of secure holding requests (call6-
     7715/7716/7717, after hours contact the Command Post for standby personnel).
     4.3.3. If a commercial courier representative requesting secure holding or refuge is
     incapacitated, provide temporary security until the commercial transportation company
     involved can provide another representative.
     4.3.4. When notified of an inbound shipment, ensure personnel are available at the south
     gate, to assist vehicles transporting explosives and/or sensitive shipments.
     4.3.5. When shipment arrives at the south gate, Distribution Section will be notified of
     shipment. The Distribution Section will then verify shipping documents at the security
     inspection station and accompany Secuity Forces personnel and the vehicle to be escorted
     to the MSA Gate 5. Once driver is inside gated area, Distribution personnel will placard
     area gates.
     4.3.6. Provide a radio-equipped vehicle with one armed guard to precede all commercial
     and government vehicles transporting explosives on base IAW 56 FW OPLAN 31.
     Vehicles will be escorted to and from Gate 5, MSA.

        4.3.7. Notify Fire Emergency Services any time explosives shipments by motor vehicle
        have arrived at Luke AFB.
     4.4. Commander, 56th Civil Engineer Squadron (CES) will:
        4.4.1. Provide firefighting support when required.
        4.4.2. Provide explosives ordnance disposal support of munitions when necessary.
        4.4.3. Be exempted from the provisions of these instructions for movements between
        Luke AFB and Gila Bend.
     4.5. The 56 FW Weapons Safety will provide safety guidance and technical advice
     concerning explosives safety.
5. Location of Operation:
     5.1. Aircraft: Sited hazardous cargo areas used for loading/unloading aircraft will be
     determined by Airfield Management IAW LAFBI 13-203.
     5.2. Commercial and/or military vehicles: The MSA Gate 5 will be used as the primary
     secure holding site.
     5.3. Vehicle holding point: The vehicle holding area is located next to Gate 5 at the east end
     of Tiger Street, as indicated in OPLAN 10-1, Installation Antiterrorism/Force Protection
     Plan. Coordination will be made with Distribution Section, Munitions Control, and Weapons
     Safety anytime vehicles laden with explosives arrive at the base. The vehicle holding point is
     reflected on base map Tab D-8 as the munitions holding area, map Legend 2D (Gate 5).
     5.4. Secure holding for Non-Explosive Sensitive/Classified shipments: The secure holding
     area for vehicles carrying Non-Explosive Sensitive/Classified items that arrive after hours or
     need intransit secure holding will be the 56 SFS supply area located at 7011 North El Mirage
     Rd, Glendale AZ 85307.
6. Sequence of Operations:
     6.1. Receipt of explosives by commercial and/or military truck:
        6.1.1. Commercial or military vehicles carrying explosives will be directed to the south
        gate by Security Forces personnel.
        6.1.2. The Security Forces gate guards will notify the Security Forces desk sergeant and
        Distribution Section. After-hours, Distribution Section support will be provided by
        requesting the Command Post to notify the standby representative. Standby munitions
        personnel will not be called in after duty hours. Shipping and receiving hours are Mon-
        Thurs 0730-1500, Fri 0730-1200. There will be no shipping and receipt operations
        performed after these hours or on weekends, holidays, and down days. Vehicles arriving
        during these times will be processed the next duty day.
        6.1.3. During duty hours, when Distribution Section is notified of the arrival of a truck
        containing explosives, Munitions Control will be notified. The Distribution Section will
        verify inbound documents arecorrect, and check seals for tampering of the vehicle at the
        vehicle holding point at south gate. Further explosives shipment inspections will be
        conducted at Munitions Gate 5 for verification of quantities with documentation;

     inspected for condition, securing method, and any other shipment discrepancies by the
     Distribution Section or Munitions Section.
     6.1.4. If any damage is found or the safety of the load is in question, the following are
     notified: Fire Emergency Services, Weapons Safety, and Explosives Ordnance Disposal.
     The vehicle must then be relocated to “Suspect Holding Area”, IAW AFMAN 91-201.
     The suspect vehicle holding area is reflected on base map Tab D-8 under Legend 2D.
     6.1.5. The Distribution Section will be responsible for accepting advance notice secure
     holding requests for munitions shipments, coordinate requirements with 56 EMS and
     SFS, and updating the Terminal Facility Guide as required in DoDR 4500.9-R, Part II.
  6.2. Receipt of explosives by air:
     6.2.1. The ATO will coordinate with Airfield Management on the sited aircraft parking
     location to be used for munitions on- or off-loading operations.
     6.2.2. The loading or unloading of explosive laden cargo aircraft is the responsibility of
     ATO. The ATO will notify Airfield Management prior to vehicles utilizing the airfield.
     6.2.3. The ATO will coordinate with Munitions Control to receipt for explosives into the
     MSA.
  6.3. Outbound explosives shipments by commercial or military truck:
     6.3.1. The Distribution Section will prepare all required documentation and affix labels
     for all items IAW DoDR 4500.9-R, Part II, AFI 24-203, CFR 49, and AFI 21-204,
     Nuclear Weapons Maintenance Procedures.
     6.3.2. The Distribution Section will accompany the drivers to the MSA on all outbound
     shipments and perform blocking and bracing when required. The Distribution Section
     will compare assets being transported to shipment documentations prior to departing the
     installation.
     6.3.3. The DD Form 626 will be processed by Distribution Section personnel.
     Deficiencies on vehicles delivering shipments will be reported as required in DoDR
     4500.9-R, Part II, Chapter 204, Paragraph G. Photographs of rejected equipment will be
     attached to the completed DD Form 626 in order to clearly indicate the nature of any
     unsatisfactory elements.
     6.3.4. The Distribution Section will instruct drivers to provide notification when a
     shipment is involved in an accident or delayed enroute for a period exceeding 12 hours.
  6.4. Outbound explosive shipments by air:
     6.4.1. The ATO will coordinate with Airfield Management on the sited aircraft parking
     location to be used for loading operations and provide them with notification of travel
     route prior to vehicles utilizing the airfield.
     6.4.2. The ATO will coordinate with Munitions Control on releasing explosive
     shipments from the MSA.
     6.4.3. The loading of explosive laden cargo aircraft is the responsibility of ATO.

    6.4.4. The ATO will ensure that units deploying as a contingency with munitions
    requirement have trained personnel to perform Hazardous Declaration (HAZDEC)
    certification and pallet build-up. These two functions must be accomplished by the unit
    providing the fund cite for the deployment and is their sole responsibility.
    6.4.5. The Distribution Section will perform HAZDEC certification on all
    noncontingency outbound shipments by coordination with Munitions Inspection to verify
    assets and packaging.
