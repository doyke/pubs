AFI91-103 dated 16 September 2005 and USAFE Supplement dated 15 June 2006 are supplemented as
follows:
This supplement establishes RAF Lakenheath specific requirements for the monitoring and reporting of
Nuclear Certified Equipment (NCE) damage and deficiencies. It implements the Dull Sword reporting
requirements of AFI 91-204, Safety Investigations and Reports and AFMAN 91-221, Weapons Safety
Investigations and Reports, and the objectives of AFI 91-101, Air Force Nuclear Weapons Surety Pro-
gram and AFI 91-103, Air Force Nuclear Safety Certification Program. This supplement applies to all
units permanently assigned to RAF Lakenheath. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using AF IMT 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication; route AF IMT 847s from the field through publications/forms managers. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance
with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 37-123, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil.
This instruction requires collecting and maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974
authorized by Title 10, USC Section 8013. System of Records notice F033 AF B, Privacy Act Request
File applies.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This supplement has been revised to supplement the latest AFI 91-103 USAFE Supplement 1 Attachment
5 (Added)(USAFE). It incorporates paragraphs A5.2. and A5.3. and adds paragraph A5.8. (Added) It also
adds Attachment 6 (Added), Sample Memorandum - Unit Nuclear Certified Equipment Monitor and
Attachment 7 (Added), Sample Memorandum – Unit NCE Monitor Disclosure Statement.

A5.2.5.8. (Added) Training of all squadron primary and alternate NCE monitors to include:
A5.2.5.8.1. (Added) Inspection requirements.
A5.2.5.8.2. (Added) NCE tracking requirements.
A5.2.5.8.3. (Added) Procedures for routing 48th Fighter Wing Weapons Safety Office (48 FW/SEW)
Dull Sword worksheets.
A5.2.5.8.4. (Added) Requirements for locally developed checklists, Operating Instructions (OIs), Opera-
tions Plans (OPLANs), and Lesson Plans. Wing Weapons Safety managers will review all locally devel-
oped checklists, OIs, OPLANS, and Lesson Plans that pertain to surety prior to their implementation and
annually thereafter.
A5.3.2.1. (Added) Unit NCE Monitors must posses as a minimum a 5 skill level.
A5.3.2.2. (Added) Appointment letters will contain the unit, name, rank, office symbol, date appointed,
duty phone number, and DEROS. The appointment letter will be in the standardized format (outlined in
Attachment 2). This instruction requires collecting and maintaining information protected by the Privacy
Act of 1974 authorized by Title 10, USC, Section 8013. System of Records notice F033 AF B, Privacy
Act Request File, applies.
A5.3.3.2.9. (Added) Tab I: Spot Inspections Log/Report.
A5.3.3.2.9.1. (Added) Monitors will conduct a monthly program review. Minimum documentation will
include: date, time, POC, activity inspected, discrepancies noted, and any follow-up activities required.
A5.3.3.2.10. (Added) Tab J: FY Dull Sword Listing/Open Squadron Formal Dull Swords.
A5.3.3.5.1. (Added) Ensure NCE is not modified in any way except as authorized in appropriate techni-
cal orders or by the item manager.
A5.3.3.9. (Added) Squadron NCE monitors will establish points of contact (POC) in each flight that uses
and/or maintains NCE. The POC will be trained on the unique reporting procedures for NCE. The squad-
ron monitor can provide this training or training can be coordinated through the Weapons Safety Office.
A5.3.3.10. (Added) Monitors will send monthly updates on open formal Dull Swords or when any signif-
icant changes are made.
A5.4.8. (Added) NCE monitors will use a standardized format for the NCE listings. NCE listing will be
sent to the Weapons Safety Office no later than the first Friday of each quarter or when there is a change
to the listing. A disclosure statement will be attached to the listing (Attachment 3)
A5.8. (Added) Dull Sword Reporting Procedures. All Dull Sword worksheets must be transmitted by
e-mail unless they are classified, in which case they must be hand-carried or forwarded using SIPRNET.
If SIPRNET is used, notify personnel by e-mail to let them know.
A5.8.1. (Added) Personnel discovering any deficiency with NCE items will report the Dull Sword as fol-
lows:
A5.8.1.1. (Added) Complete the standardized Dull Sword worksheet available from 48FW/SEW
webpage. Give a complete description of the circumstances that led to the discovery of the deficiency.
(e.g. what happened, the activity equipment was involved in, etc.).
A5.8.1.2. (Added) Attach the initial worksheet to the piece of equipment when it goes to a repairing orga-
nization. Place an entry in the AFTO Form 244, Industrial/Support Equipment Record, prescribed by TO
4                                       AFI91-103_USAFESUP_LAKENHEATHSUP 7 JUNE 2007


00-20-1, Aerospace Equipment Maintenance Inspection, Documentation, Policies and Procedures, or AF
Form 1800, Operator’s Inspection Guide and Trouble Report (General Purpose Vehicles), prescribed by
AFI 24-301, Vehicle Operations, if applicable.
A5.8.1.3. (Added) Send an electronic copy of the initial worksheet to your squadron NCE monitor. Cour-
tesy copy (cc) the Weapons Safety Office and the Wing Weapons Manager.
A5.8.2. (Added) Repairing organizations that receive NCE with a deficiency will do the following:
A5.8.2.1. (Added) Forward an electronic copy of the initial worksheet that came in with the item to the
repairing organization’s squadron NCE monitor.
A5.8.2.2. (Added) When the item is repaired, properly disposed of, or turned in to supply, complete the
initial Dull Sword worksheet with the repair action.
A5.8.2.2.1. (Added) Forward an electronic copy of the updated/completed worksheet to your squadron
NCE monitor.
A5.8.2.2.2. (Added) Return the updated worksheet to the using organization with the repaired item. If the
item is turned in to supply, the completed worksheet still needs to be returned to the using organization.
Using organization will forward closed-out worksheets to their squadron monitor.
A5.8.3. (Added) Squadron NCE monitors will forward all Dull Sword worksheets received to the Weap-
ons Safety Office and the Wing Weapons Manager. This information must be gathered and forwarded
within 24 hours so that reporting time limits in AFMAN 91-221 are met.
