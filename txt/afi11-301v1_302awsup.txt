Air Force Instruction (AFI) 11-301, Volume 1, Aircrew Life Support (ALS) Program, 19
July 2002, is supplemented as follows: This supplement provides guidance on 302 AW
specific ALS issues. It applies to all 302d AW personnel. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air
Force Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847 directly to
the OPR at the 302AW/DOL, 45 W. Hamilton Ave. Ste 171, Peterson AFB, CO 80914. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/.

3.21. (Added) ALS Quality Council:
3.21.1. This council is designed to review and take courses of action on problems associated
with ALS operations and introduction of new or modified LSE. Also, form the council to
identify customer and supplier requirements.
3.21.2. A council meeting may be convened at any level and may task appropriate agencies to
correct deficiencies effecting the ALS program. Regularly scheduled meetings are highly
encouraged to maintain a positive cross-flow of information and take action on customer support
issues associated with LSE inspection, modification, or control procedures. Conduct meetings as
required.
3.21.3. Council membership will meet at a minimum of every six months and will consist of
Life Support, Survival Equipment, Fabrication, Medical Supply, Supply, Flight Safety,
Loadmaster, Engineer, Flight Line Chief, Quality Assurance (QA), Air Force Cost Analysis
Improvement Group (AFCAIG) Representative, and other outside supporting agencies as
deemed necessary. An agenda will be prepared by life support prior to the meeting and e-mailed
out to each member. Meeting minutes will be generated and sent to all parties involved to ensure
everyone is on the same level of understanding of what took place during the course of the
meeting.
3.21.4. The wing/group life support officer (LSO) or ALS superintendent will chair the meeting.
Minutes will reflect the problem (observation), impact (if not resolved), recommendation
(solution), and establish an office of primary responsibility (OPR) for action or suspense/follow-
up. If required, forward minutes to the appropriate levels for final resolution/coordination.
3.21.5. Customer support issues that cannot be resolved at unit-level should be elevated to the
appropriate MAJCOM for resolution. Air National Guard (ANG) units will direct their issues
through the White Sands Test Center (WSTC) to ANGRC/ XOOSF. AFRC units will direct their
issues through the appropriate numbered air force (NAF) to HQ AFRC/DOTSL.

3.5.1. (Added) The life support supervisor (LSS) or non-commissioned officer in charge
(NCOIC) will e-mail or transmit a message to all Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC)
superintendents with an information copy to 22 AF/DOTL and HQ AFRC/DOTSL. Include
equipment type, national stock number (NSN), and excess quantity. Request action addresses
respond within 15 days.
3.5.1.2. (Added) After all units have responded, transmit a message through 22 AF/DOTL to
HQ AFRC/DOTSL requesting redistribution of excess assets. Units will receive redistribution
instructions not later than 30 days after HQ AFRC/DOTSL receives final redistribution request.
HQ AFRC/DOTSL will work with other MAJCOMs to redistribute assets.
6.2.7. (Added) Cold weather clothing requirements apply from November through March. The
winter flying jacket or summer flying jacket, nomex long underwear (top and bottom), and
leather gloves with wool inserts are required for all training sorties outside the local flying area
(within 250 miles). When flying North of 49.59 degrees North (in addition to the previous
items) include Mukluks with wool socks, hood for winter jacket and N-4B mittens (Artic) are
required. See supply for these items, they will issue a cold weather bag.
2.15.3.1. (Added) Aircrew will preflight and sign for all Aircrew Chemical Defense Ensemble
(ACDE) equipment in Building 207.
