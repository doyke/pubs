This instruction implements, procedures and management responsibilities for managing accountable
organizational budget code 9 (BC-9) items in accordance with AFMAN 23-110, USAF Supply Manual,
Volume 2, Part 13, Chapter 8. This instruction applies to all US Air Forces in Europe (USAFE) units
assigned to Lajes Field. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the OPR
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publications; route AF Form 847s from the field
through the Lajes Field publications/forms manager. Ensure that all records created as a result of pro-
cesses prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located in Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS): https://
www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.


1. General. The intent of this instruction is to ensure proper use and disposition of government property.
To ensure compliance with the intent and spirit of this instruction, effective immediately, organizational
commanders will establish local procedures for managing and controlling accountability of BC-9 items.
   1.1. Policy. DoD 7000.14-R, Financial Management Regulation, Accounting Policy and Procedures,
   Volume 4, establishes policy requirements for Department of Defense agencies regarding government
   owned assets whenever public funds are invested. The Department of Defense has an obligation to
   safeguard property from theft, abuse, waste and unauthorized use.
   1.2. Headquarters USAF directed that organizational commanders be responsible for the control and
   accountability of all Budget Code 9 items that meet the established criteria that is issued to or backo-
   rdered by their organization.
   1.3. Asset tracking and custodial responsibilities for these items are the responsibility of the organiza-
   tional commander or designated representative.

    1.4. The category of property includes locally funded items valued at $2,500 or more and all locally
    funded pilferable assets, regardless of cost, acquired through 65 LRS, a local purchase transaction
    through 65 CONS or from the use of the Government Purchase Card (GPC).
    1.5. 65 LRS personnel have no responsibility to manage, inventory or monitor these items unless they
    are the property of LRS.

2. Responsibilities. Unit commanders will appoint a primary and an alternate BC-9 monitor and provide
a courtesy copy letter to 65 LRS/LGRSC.
    2.1. Monitors will physically inventory these types of assets and maintain custodial responsibility.
    2.2. The Organizational Visibility List (R15) is an as-required product created specifically for this
    program. Organizations requiring this product should submit an AF Form 2011, Base Supply ADPE
    Work Request, to request the product on a recurring basis.
       2.2.1. BC-9 assets purchased through 65 LRS meeting the accountability criteria appear on the
       Organizational Visibility List (R15).
       2.2.2. Units will add GPC acquisitions and local purchase transactions meeting the criteria to the
       R15 within 10 days of receipt.
       2.2.3. 65 LRS personnel have no responsibility to manage, inventory or monitor these items
       unless they are the property of LRS, but can provide assistance updating and obtaining the R15
       through the 65 LRS Customer Service Equipment Liaison at extension 2-7005.
    2.3. BC-9 monitors will develop local procedures to identify individuals pending permanent change
    of station assignment, separation or retirement actions.
    2.4. BC-9 items issued to these individuals must have custody transferred prior to the member’s
    departure.
    2.5. BC-9 monitors will perform an annual inventory and reconcile results with the organization’s
    R15.
    2.6. Adopted Forms: AF Form 2011, Base Supply ADPE Work Request
