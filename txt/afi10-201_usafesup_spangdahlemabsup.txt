AFI 10-201, Status of Resources and Training System (SORTS), United States Air Forces in
Europe (USAFE) Supplement, 12 June 2007, is supplemented as follows: This publication
incorporates reporting guidance from AFI 10-201, Status of Resources and Training System,
dated 13 April 2006 and clarifies reporting responsibilities for 52nd Fighter Wing (FW).
This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 10-2, Air Force Readiness. This
document provides Air Force procedures for those areas listed in the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff Manual (CJCSM) 3150.01, Joint Reporting Structure General Instructions, and
Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Instruction (CJCSI) 3401.02, Global Status of Resources
and Training System, as requiring Service direction. This instruction applies to all Spangdahlem
22 Fighter Squadron (FS), 23 FS, 81 FS, 606 Air Control Squadron (ACS), 52 Operation Support
Squadron (OSS), 52 Civil Engineering Squadron (CES), 52 Comptroller Squadron (CPTS), 52
Communication Squadron (CS), 52 Force Support Squadron (FSS), 52 Logistic Readiness
Squadron (LRS), 52 Medical Group (MDG), 52 Security Forces Squadron (SFS) and 52
Maintenance Group (MXG) Reporting Units. The reporting requirements under SORTS are
exempt from licensing according to Air Force Instruction (AFI) 33-324, The Information
Collections and Reports Management Program: Controlling Internal, Public, and Interagency
Air Force Information Collections. Submit any recommended change or clarification request,
and supplements to this instruction to the Headquarters United States Air Force Operational
Readiness (HQ USAF/XOOA) office, 1480 Air Force Pentagon, Washington, DC 20330-1480.
Waiver authority is HQ USAF/XOOA. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management

1.11.10.2.13. Subordinate Reporting Organization (SRO) will provide a monthly turn-in
schedule to all units for calendar year and provide changes as needed. Schedule will be posted
on 52 FW/SORTS calendar accessible by all Monitors.
1.11.10.2.15. SRO will perform Staff Assistance Visits (SAV) for Measured Units and maintain
a record of the most recent unit staff assistance visit. Results from each SAV will be signed by
the SRO and results provided to the Squadron Commander within 2 weeks of the SAV. Wing
SAVs will be performed 30 days prior to United States Air Forces in Europe (USAFE) SAVs
and/or any other inspection and as requested by SROs (minimal semi-annually).
1.11.10.2.15.1. SRO will provide templates and guidance on the construction and maintenance
of monitor continuity books including Spangdahlem SAV checklist, maintained on the SORTS
PORTAL in the AF Portal under Command Post (CP).
1.11.10.2.15.2. Monitors will complete USAFE and Wing self inspection checklists and ensure
their SORTS Program is ready for inspection prior to SRO SAV Appointment.
1.11.11.1.6. Each time a new report is processed, commanders will signify their review of their
current database by signing a SRO provided SORTS “EASY Read (EZ Read) Readiness
Assessment System Input Tool (RASIT) report that reflects the current data in the Global Status
of Resources and Training System (GSORTS) database.
1.11.11.1.6.1. (Added) Monitors and Commanders or Releasing Officials review and sign EZ
Reads to ensure database accuracy and return to SRO at monthly scheduled appointment. Out of
cycle EZ Reads will be returned to SRO within 24 hours or when designated.
1.11.11.1.7. Commanders and Releasing Officials will ensure Monitors are receiving their
information in a timely manner to complete reporting.
1.11.11.1.8. Commanders and Releasing Officials will ensure Monitors have adequate time to
complete reports.
1.11.11.1.9. Commanders and Releasing Officials will ensure they are available for reviewing
and signing reports and documents for Monitors as needed.
1.11.11.1.10 Commanders will ensure Monitors have full, unlimited and lone access to all
SORTS material and equipment.

1.11.11.2.4.1.5. (Added) A checklist for finalizing reports prior to turn-in will be accomplished

                                   Attachment 11 (Added)
                                 TURN-IN PROCEDURES


A11.1. Monitors will complete worksheets using Worksheet Turn – In Checklist. Worksheets
will be briefed to Unit Commander or Releasing Official.
A11.2. Unit Commander or Releasing Official will review the Worksheets, assign the overall C-
level and add any remarks deemed necessary. Unit Commander or Releasing Official will
forward worksheets to the SRO for submission on day designated by SRO.
A11.3. SRO will review for any errors and input information into RASIT. Any corrections that
need to be made to worksheets will be E-mailed to the monitor for correction prior to printing.
C-level changes will need to be resubmitted thru the Commander or Releasing Official’s E-mail.
A11.4. Monitor will make changes to worksheets. Both worksheets and EZ Read will be
reviewed by both monitor and Commander or Releasing Official to ensure accuracy prior to
signature. Commander or Releasing Official will sign worksheets when review is complete.
Turn – In Checklist will be used again.
A11.5. Monitor will bring signed documents to SRO appointment for final check to SRO
scheduled turn – in appointment.
