This Instruction implements Air Force Instruction (AFI) 38-101, Air Force Organization, and
defines responsibilities and procedures for processing Unit Manpower Document (UMD)
Authorization Change Requests (ACR) within the Headquarters Air Force Weather Agency
(AFWA), 1st Weather Group (1 WXG), and 2d Weather Group (2 WXG). It applies to all
AFWA personnel. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s
chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS)
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This revision updates office symbols as a result of organizational structure changes.
1. General. This instruction describes procedures, policies, and responsibilities for initiating
and processing a modification to the attributes of a manpower authorization on the UMD. The
UMD is a preformatted computer product that shows manpower authorization requirements for
AFWA. All changes will be submitted in the form of an ACR.
2. Responsibilities. This section describes the responsibilities for initiating and processing an
ACR.

    2.1. Requester. The requester or action officer is responsible for submitting change requests
    in the proper format and routing it through the appropriate levels of coordination and
    approval.
    2.2. AFWA Commander (CC) or Vice Commander (CV). The CC or CV is the final
    approval authority for all actions involving:
       2.2.1. Changes to a civilian authorization grades and/or series.
       2.2.2. Changes to a military grade.
       2.2.3. Changes affecting more than one directorate or group. Example: A3 determines
       workload is more in line with 2 WXG mission, and initiates a transfer of one
       authorization to 2 WXG to cover the workload.
    2.3. Directors, Group Commanders. Director, Group Commander will approve/sign all
    change requests impacting authorizations in his/her area and is the final approval authority
    for such changes as:
       2.3.1. Realignment of authorizations exclusive to that directorate or group, with the
       exception of those changes listed in 2.2.
       2.3.2. Changes to funding of an authorization (swapping cuts) within the same Air Force
       Specialty Code within that directorate or group.
       2.3.3. Changes to an authorization’s Personnel Accounting Symbol (PAS).
       2.3.4. Changes to an authorization’s security code.
       2.3.5. Changes to an authorization’s Inherently Governmental Commercial Activity
       (IGCA) code.
    2.4. AFWA Deputy Director (DD) will review changes to civilian authorizations which
    require CC/CV approval.
    2.5. AFWA Director, Manpower and Personnel (DP) will coordinate on all ACRs.
    2.6. AFWA Manpower and Organization (DPB) action officer is responsible for:
       2.6.1. Determining the validity of the ACR by using manpower analysis tools. This will
       require a manpower review of the impacted area(s).
       2.6.2. Recommending approval or disapproval of the change request.
       2.6.3. Managing MPES which contains all attributes applied to authorizations and
       requirements, and inputting any changes in order to generate an updated UMD.
       2.6.4. Maintaining documentation on any changes to MPES.
       2.6.5. Coordinating with appropriate functional managers at Air Staff, when required.
       2.6.6. Coordinating with appropriate functional managers at AFPC for authorizations
       requiring advanced academic degrees.
    2.7. AFWA Special Security Office (A6W). All ACRs will be coordinated through the
    security office to determine Security Access Requirement (SAR) code impacts on billeted
    Sensitive Compartment Information positions.

   2.8. AFWA Security Manager (DSS). All ACRs with SAR code changes will be
   coordinated through the security manager to determine SAR code impacts on the
   organization, as well as update information in the Joint Personnel Adjudication System
   (JPAS) database.
   2.9. AFWA Human Resources (DPC) will review all ACRs related to changes to civilian
   authorizations.
   2.10. AFWA Comptroller (FM) will review all ACRs affecting civilian authorizations for
   funding impacts.
   2.11. The Senior Enlisted Advisor (CCC) will review all ACRs related to military enlisted
   changes.
   2.12. AFWA Military Personnel (DPP) will review all ACRs affecting changes to military
   authorizations.
3. ACR Procedures. Requesters are responsible for submitting an ACR on a signed E-SSS or
AF IMT 1768, which becomes the ACR (see Atch 2), and coordinating changes within their
directorate or group before following the routing instructions in section 3.4.
   3.1. The ACR can be initiated by any work center supervisor; however, it must be
   coordinated through the appropriate chain of command to the director or group commander
   before following the routing instructions in section 3.4.
   3.2. If more than one directorate, division, branch, group, or squadron is affected by the
   change, then all parties must coordinate respectively.
   3.3. ACR Content. Use E-SSS or AF IMT 1768 and include the following headers to
   complete the ACR.
      3.3.1. First paragraph--PROPOSAL. Paragraph should contain a description/explanation
      and should be limited to one paragraph. Be specific: realignment of positions, changes in
      funding, modifications to PAS, security, or IGCA codes; include position numbers.
      3.3.2. Second paragraph--JUSTIFICATION. This paragraph should consist of: Why is
      the action needed? What are the expected benefits? (Identify factors driving the need for
      the action, e.g., changes to mission or shift in workload.)
      3.3.3. Third paragraph--POC. This paragraph should reflect the POC’s information. The
      POC may be the same as the action officer.
      3.3.4. Last paragraph--RECOMMENDATION. This paragraph should clearly describe
      the action(s) you want to take place.
      3.3.5. Attach the completed Manpower/Authorization Change Request Worksheet (see
      Atch 3) which details the breakout of the exact position(s) to be changed on the ACR.
      List the position(s) in alpha-numeric order.
   3.4. ACR Routing. The ACR must be coordinated appropriately before DPB can make
   changes to the UMD.
      3.4.1. A majority of ACRs will require at least the following routing: FM, A6W Special
      Security Office, DSS, DP, and DPB.

       3.4.2. If the ACR affects civilian authorizations which require CC/CV approval use the
       following routing: FM, A6W Special Security Office, DSS, DPC, DP, DS, CCE, DD,
       CV/CC, and DPB.
       3.4.3. If the ACR affects military grades, and requires CC/CV approval, use the
       following routing: FM, A6W Special Security Office, DSS, DPP, DP, DS, CCE, CCC,
       CV/CC, and DPB.
       3.4.4. Full coordination and approval must be obtained prior to completion of the
       requested action(s).
    3.5. Projects are built in MPES by DPB and forwarded (through MPES) to SAF/AARX for
    approval. After SAF/AARX approval, the UMD is updated.
