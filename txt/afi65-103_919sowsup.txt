This supplements implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 65 -103,
Temporary Duty Orders, dated 5 August 2005. This supplement applies to all units that are
funded by or publish orders through the 919th Special Operations Wing Financial Management
(919 SOW/FM). Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office
of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication; route the AF Form 847s from the wing through your publications/forms
manager. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and dispose of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.
SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed.
1.2.5. Each unit commander will forward to 919 SOW/Financial Management Orders Office
(FMFR), a letter designating the following: orders specialist, supervisor, resource advisor and
commander roles in Air Force Reserve Order Writing System (AROWS-R). Personnel assigned
to the role of supervisor or commander will not approve orders in AROWS-R if he or she is the
traveler.
1.2.14. Verbal orders are routed through the unit commanders for review and approval. Units
must establish in-house procedures to minimize verbal orders.

1.2.15. To prevent unreasonable delays in canceling or modifying travel orders, unit supervisors,
resource advisor or commander will submit cancellation or modification request to the orders
specialist, within two (2) workdays after the end of tour date on the order.
1.2.15.1. Order specialist will process the cancellation or modification request within two (2)
workdays of receipt from the supervisor, resource advisor or commander.
1.2.15.2. 919 SOW/FMFR will process cancellation action within two (2) workdays after
request is entered into AROWS-R and submit to Eglin Financial Service Office (FSO), for de-
obligation of unit funds.
1.2.15.3. 919 SOW/FMFR will provide unit Resource Advisors (RAs) with Reserve Personnel
Appropriation (RPA) unpaid listings weekly. This product will be distributed by RAs within
respective units and be used as a tool by unit approving officials and supervisors to track orders,
which need to be revoked or rescinded.
1.2.20. FM requires that all orders request be routed in AROWS-R at least (3) workdays prior to
the orders report date for normal publication of orders. Occasional rush orders may be
accomplished by notifying the 919 SOW/FMFR.
3.3.2. All Temporary Duty (TDY) Away from Home Station orders published by the 919 SOW
will show a specific location where the TDY will be performed; for example, unit and military
installation, air base, air station, site, test range, federal government agency, foreign government
agency, etc. Orders will not be published that list only a city, without any other specific
information about the TDY location.
3.3.2.1. (Added) TDY orders will be published to the nearest military installation, to the
maximum extent possible, with the following exceptions:
3.3.2.1.1. (Added) Conference, workshop or commercial training, seminars meeting at
approved non-military installations.
3.3.2.1.2. (Added) Group commanders approve TDY orders to non-military installations when
mission requirements dictate.
