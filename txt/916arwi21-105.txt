This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Air and Space
Maintenance. This instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101 Air
Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Supplement, Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance
Management, Air Force Occupational Safety Health Standard (AFOSHSTD) 91-100 Aircraft
Flight Line – Ground Operations and Activities, and AFOSHSTD 91-501, Air Force
Consolidated Occupational Safety Standard. This instruction establishes procedures and
responsibilities for operating hangar doors and applies to all 916th Air Refueling Wing (ARW)
personnel who operate hangar doors or enter through hangar doors. Refer recommended changes
and questions about this publication to the OPR using the Air Force AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication. Route AF 847s through the appropriate chain of
command. Ensure all records created as prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rds.

1. Responsibilities:
   1.1. Personnel assigned to the wing shall become familiar with 916ARWI21-105, Hangar
   Door Responsibilities. In order to ensure strict compliance and provide maximum safety of
   personnel and prevent damage to equipment, familiarization of all personnel who operate
   hangar doors is mandatory. All training and operation of hangar doors will be accomplished
   in accordance with AFI 21-101 AFRC Supplement and this instruction.
   1.2. Individuals are responsible for notifying the facility manager of any maintenance
   problems with hangar doors in each building.

     1.3. Facility Manager responsibilities for buildings with hangar doors.
        1.3.1. Notifies the base Civil Engineering (CE) trouble desk when a hangar door
        deficiency is discovered.
        1.3.2. Maintains a log of required maintenance and follow-up until fixed.
        1.3.3. Determines if hangar doors are safe to operate through the use of a locally
        developed checklist, attachments 2-4. If doors are deemed unsafe or if power is turned
        off, the fire department will be notified.
        1.3.4. Assures that all signage requirements on and around the hangar doors meet the
        criteria established in AFI 21-101 AFRC Supplement.
        1.3.5. Assures that the applicable operating checklist is displayed at each hangar door
        control panel with the building custodian(s) contact information.
            1.3.5.1. Assures training is provided to all contractor personnel who are required to
            transient through or operate hangar doors. Proof of training will remain on file with
            the facility manager until project completion.
2. Training:
     2.1. Hangar Door Awareness Training.
        2.1.1. 916 MOF/MXOT will provide/maintain annual training for personnel that do not
        operate hangar doors, but routinely work in hangar facilities regardless of AFSC.
        Training will include hazards associated with hangar doors and emergency procedures.
        Initial hangar door awareness training will be provided by a certified hangar door
        operator trainer.
     2.2. Hangar Door Operator Training.
        2.2.1. Certified individuals will act as hangar door operator trainers and will be identified
        on an appointment letter signed by the 916 MXG/CC. These individuals will provide
        individual hands on training in operation of doors using a locally developed training plan.
        The training plan will be maintained by the 916 MOF/MXOT. It is the responsibility of
        the local commanders to enforce the hangar door training requirements. Only qualified
        personnel authorized by their squadron commander may operate hangar doors.
        2.2.1. Hangar Door Operator Training Plan will consist of:
            2.2.1.1. Having each selected person read 916ARWI21-105, Hangar Door
            Operations, and becoming familiar with its contents.
            2.2.1.2. Having each person demonstrate an understanding of the dangers involved in
            operating hangar doors.
            2.2.1.3. Having each person demonstrate the ability to engage and disengage door
            clutch mechanisms.
            2.2.1.4. Having each person demonstrate the ability to operate doors safely.
            2.2.1.5. Discussing emergency procedures applicable to hangar door operation.
        2.2.2. Trainer will:

          2.2.2.1. Discuss all safety related items.
          2.2.2.2. Explain warning signs and their meanings.
          2.2.2.3. Explain the purpose and operation of door switches. CAUTION: For
          hangar 4909, individuals will trail each door segment during operation and open one
          door per side of centerline at a time.
          2.2.2.4. Demonstrate declutching and manual movement of the hangar doors in the
          event of power loss. If doors are inoperative a minimum five people are required to
          push the doors. CAUTION: Under no circumstances will hangar doors be moved
          using motorized vehicles.
          2.2.2.5. Show where minimum open door floor markings are painted and explains
          their purpose. NOTE: Doors will be opened a minimum of 10 feet.
          2.2.2.6. Explain that individuals will not pass through hangar doors opened less than
          10 feet.
          2.2.2.7. Explain clear zones and their function.
          2.2.2.8. Complete and submit an AF Form 2426, Training Request and Completion
          Notification with GO-81 Course Code SEYM 000225 for trained personnel.
3. Documentation:
   3.1. Hangar Door Awareness Training: GO-81 Course Code AFRC 001100, Hangar Door
   Awareness Training is maintained in GO-81 and is updated annually.
   3.2. Hangar Door Operator Training: GO-81 Course Code SEYM 000225 (Hangar 4909
   Door Operator Training), SEYM 000226 (Hangar 4822 Door Operator Training), and SEYM
   000227 (Hangar 4828 Door Operator Training) is maintained in GO-81.
   3.3. Hangar Door Awareness Training for personnel not tracked in GO-81 will be
   documented on AF Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record and updated annually.
   3.4. Hangar Door Operator Training for personnel not tracked in GO-81 will be documented
   on AF Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record and the applicable squadron
   commander will forward a letter to the 916 MXG/CC listing qualified and authorized door
   operators for each type of hangar door.

        POWERED HANGAR DOOR OPERATOR CHECKLIST (HANGAR 4909)

A2.1. Preparation for opening and closing hangar doors. CAUTION: Powered and
manually operated hangar doors pose a potential crushing hazard; all unnecessary personnel must
stand clear when doors are in motion.
     A2.1.1. Only qualified personnel authorized by their squadron commander may operate
     hangar doors.
     A2.1.2. Check doors and tracks inside and outside for obstructions prior to movement.
     A2.1.3. Check to ensure the personnel/emergency exit doors are fully closed prior to
     operating the hangar doors.
     A2.1.4. Check door-warning bell prior to operation by quickly pressing and releasing the
     control button in the direction the door will be traveling. NOTE: Hangar door warning bell
     will sound approximately five seconds prior to movement of door. When the warning bell or
     buzzers are temporarily out of order or awaiting repairs, spotters are used while opening or
     closing hangar doors.
     A2.1.5. If the warning bell malfunctions during any phase of the operation, the operator
     must cease operating the doors and notify the facility manager concerning malfunctions.
     A2.1.6. Prior to movement of the door(s) ensure that all personnel are outside of the five-
     foot clear zone.
     A2.1.7. Locate minimum door open marks on hangar floor when doors are not to be opened
     fully.
A2.2. Opening Doors. NOTE: Only operate one door per side of the centerline at a time. Doors
must be opened a minimum of 10 feet.
     A2.2.1. Locate switch control panel. NOTE: Switches should be marked with luminescent
     or reflective arrows indicating direction of door travel for corresponding switch.
     A2.2.2. Press and hold corresponding switch for opening the door.
     A2.2.3. Trail door while in motion if each door segment is operated separately (Hangar
     4909). Never place yourself in the doors path of travel.
     A2.2.4. Release switch once door has reached minimum opening mark of 10 feet or when
     door is fully open.
     A2.2.5. Move to next door repeat steps A2.2.1 thru A2.2.4.
A2.3. Closing Doors. CAUTION: Powered and manually operated hangar doors pose a
potential crushing hazard; all unnecessary personnel should stand clear when doors are in
motion. NOTE: Only operate one door per side of centerline at a time. If a door is not
completely closed it must remain open a minimum of 10 feet.
     A2.3.1. Locate switch control panel for the door that will be closed. NOTE: Switches
     should be marked with luminescent or reflective arrows indicating direction of door travel for
     corresponding switch.
916ARWI21-105 1 APRIL 2011                                                                  7


   A2.3.2. Press and hold corresponding switch for closing door.
   A2.3.3. Trail door while in motion if each door segment is operated separately. Never place
   yourself in the doors path of travel.
   A2.3.4. Release switch once door has reached minimum opening mark of 10 feet or when
   door is fully closed.
   A2.3.5. Move to next door repeat steps A2.3.1 thru A2.3.4.
A2.4. Manually Opening Doors. CAUTION: Powered and manually operated hangar doors
pose a potential crushing hazard; all unnecessary personnel must stand clear when doors are in
motion.
   A2.4.1. Locate door clutch.
   A2.4.2. Declutch the door to be opened. CAUTION: Under no circumstances will hangar
   doors be moved using motorized vehicles. NOTE: Minimum five people required when
   opening doors manually.
   A2.4.3. Push door open.
   A2.4.4. Move to next door.
   A2.4.5. Repeat steps A2.4.1 thru A2.4.4.

motion.
   A2.5.1. Locate door clutch.
   A2.5.2. Assure that door is declutched. CAUTION: Under no circumstances will hangar

   closing doors manually.
   A2.5.3. Push door closed.
   A2.5.4. Move to next door.

A3.1. POWERED HANGAR DOOR OPERATOR CHECKLIST (Hangar 4828)
PREPARATION FOR OPENING AND CLOSING HANGAR DOORS. CAUTION:
Powered hangar doors pose a potential crushing hazard; all unnecessary personnel must stand
clear when doors are in motion. NOTE: IF DOORS DO NOT OPEN/CLOSE UNDER THEIR
OWN POWER, AT NO TIME WILL THE DOORS BE OPENED MANUALLY WITH A TUG
OR VEHICLE.           NOTIFY BUILDING CUSTODIAN OR CE SERVICE DESK
IMMEDIATELY AT 722-5139.
     A3.1.1. Only qualified personnel authorized by their squadron commander may operate
     hangar doors.
     A3.1.2. Check doors and tracks inside and outside for obstructions prior to movement.
     A3.1.3. Check to ensure the personnel/emergency exit doors are fully closed prior to
     operating the hangar doors.
     A3.1.4. Check door-warning bell prior to operation by quickly pressing and releasing the
     control button in the direction the door will be traveling. NOTE: Hangar door warning bell
     will sound approximately five seconds prior to movement of door. When the warning bell or

     A3.1.5. If the warning bell malfunctions during any phase of the operation, the operator
     must cease operating the doors and notify the facility manager concerning malfunctions.
     A3.1.6. Prior to movement of the door(s) ensure that all personnel are outside of the five-
     foot clear zone.
     A3.1.7. Locate minimum door open marks on hangar floor when doors are not to be opened
     fully.
A3.2. OPENING DOORS.
     A3.2.1. Open tail doors first. Go outside and lift the micro-switch release bar out of its
     detent and raise until it fits into the upper detent and the micro-switch arm is released.
     A3.2.2. Locate OPEN switch on panel marked TAIL DOOR CLOSURE panel.
     A3.2.3. Press OPEN switch to open tail door.
     A3.2.4. Repeat steps 1 thru 3 for the other tail door, as applicable.
     A3.2.5. After tail doors are fully opened, go outside and lower the micro-switch release bar
     to the lower detent securing the micro-switch arm against the door.
     A3.2.6. Return to MAIN DOOR panel. Ensure the personnel/emergency exit doors are fully
     closed prior to operating the hangar doors.
     A3.2.7. Press and hold OPEN button on MAIN DOOR panel and walk with door while in
     motion until doors are opened to the desired amount. Release switch when opened 10ft or
     fully opened. If the doors are required to be fully opened to its furthest extent possible, press

   and hold OPEN button on the MAIN DOOR panel and walk with door until you reach the
   column that is in line with the doors. Once the MAIN DOOR panel gets close to the column
   the doors will automatically stop. Walk around the column, then press and hold the OPEN
   button on the COLUMN CLOSURE panel and walk with door until it opens fully. Repeat for
   opposite door.
A3.3. CLOSING DOORS. NOTE: Close MAIN doors first. If a door is not completely
closed it must remain open a minimum of 10 feet.
   A3.3.1. Locate switch control panels for the door that will be closed. NOTE: Switches
   should be marked with luminescent or reflective arrows indicating direction of door travel for
   corresponding switch.
   A3.3.2. If there is NO AIRCRAFT IN THE HANGAR proceed to steps A3.3.4 thru A3.3.10.
   A3.3.3. If there is an AIRCRAFT IN THE HANGER proceed to steps A.3.3.11 thru
   A.3.3.16.
   A3.3.4. NO AIRCRAFT IN THE HANGAR.
   A3.3.5. To close the MAIN doors if the door was opened fully, locate CLOSE button on
   COLUMN DOOR panel. Press and hold button and walk with door until the door is about
   two feet from the column, the door will automatically stop.
   A3.3.6. Walk around the column, then press and hold the CLOSE button on the MAIN
   DOOR panel and walk with the door until the door reaches the 10 foot open mark on the
   floor.
   A3.3.7. Go outside and lift the micro-switch release bar out of its detent and raise until it fits
   into the upper detent and the micro-switch arm is released.
   A3.3.8. Return to the TAIL DOOR CLOSURE panel, press and hold the CLOSE button to
   close the tail door.
   A3.3.9. After the tails doors are completely closed and the micro-switch bar is able to be
   fully seated, press the CLOSE button on the MAIN DOOR panel to fully close the door.
   A3.3.10. Repeat steps A3.3.4 thru A3.3.8 for the other hangar door.
   A3.3.11. AIRCRAFT IN THE HANGAR.
   A3.3.12. To close the MAIN doors if the door was opened fully, locate CLOSE button on
   COLUMN DOOR panel. Press and hold button and walk with door until the door is about
   two feet from the column, the door will automatically stop.
   A3.3.13. Walk around the column, then press and hold the CLOSE button on the MAIN
   DOOR panel and walk with the door until the door reaches the “Aperture Adjustment” mark
   on the floor.
   A3.3.14. Raise or lower the aperture door. Press and hold the RAISE or LOWER button on
   the APPETURE panel to align door to fit around the aircraft tail.
   A3.3.15. Locate the MAIN DOOR panel, press and hold the CLOSE button until the
   aperture door fits around the tail. Release the CLOSE button if at any time the aperture door
   looks like it is not aligned with the tail of the aircraft and make minor adjustments as needed.

A4.4. Manually Opening Doors. CAUTION: Powered and manually operated hangar doors
pose a potential crushing hazard; all unnecessary personnel must stand clear when doors are in

   required when closing doors manually.
   A4.5.3. Push doors closed starting with center door (by design, remaining doors will be
   pulled as center door closes). CAUTION: Never place yourself in the doors path of travel.
   A4.5.4. Repeat steps A4.5.1 thru A4.5.3 for other side if required.
