This instruction implements Department of Defense Directive (DoDD) 3100.10, Space Policy; DoDD
3200.11, Major Range and Test Facility Base; DoDD 3230.3, DoD Support for Commercial Space
Launch Activities; AFPD 91-1, Nuclear Weapons and System Surety; AFPD 91-2, Safety Programs;
AFPD 63-12, Assurance of Operational Safety, Suitability, and E ffectiveness ; AFI
91-202_AFSPCSUP1, The US Air Force Mishap Prevention Program; and the Memorandum of Agree-
ment between the Department of the Air Force and the Federal Aviation Administration on Safety for
Space Transportation and Range Activities. This instruction also establishes and implements require-
ments and procedures for publications in the 91-7XX range safety publication series by Headquarters Air
Force Space Command (HQ AFSPC) Safety (SE). It applies to all HQ AFSPC, subordinate, supporting
units, and all Range Users conducting or supporting operations on AFSPC ranges. Subordinate units may
supplement this instruction after approval by HQ AFSPC/SE. This publication applies to the Air National
Guard (ANG). Send correspondence pertaining to this instruction to HQ AFSPC/SEC, 150 Vandenberg
St., Ste 1105, Peterson AFB, CO 80914-4260.


1. General. This instruction allows HQ AFSPC/SE to:
   1.1. Develop, issue, and maintain range safety publications designated as the AFSPC 91-7XX series.
   The AFSPC 91-7XX series will contain policy, requirements and guidance related to the AFSPC
   range safety programs.
   1.2. Revise AFSPC publications as a result of directed changes and implement policy and guidance to
   subordinate safety elements in a timely and economical manner.

2. Publications. The publications covered by this publication series will be referenced in the Air Force
publication system. The publications will be numbered in the AFSPC 91-7XX series and will be reserved
solely for use by AFSPC range safety programs.

    2.1. Proposed changes to the range safety publications should be submitted via a cover letter/memo
    with the proposed changes on Change Request Forms as an attachment. These proposed changes
    should be submitted to HQ AFSPC/SE along with appropriate coordination to HQ AFSPC/SE for
    review. Once approved by HQ AFSPC/SE, interim changes or a publication revision, whichever is
    appropriate, will be forwarded to HQ AFSPC CSS/SCTI (Publishing) for publication. After publica-
    tion the documents will be posted to a controlled web site for access by approved agencies (see the
    responsibilities section).
    2.2. These range safety publications will not contain privileged, classified or proprietary information.
    2.3. The present Eastern and Western Range (EWR) 127-1, Range Safety Requirements, publication
    will be replaced by a HQ AFSPC developed 91-7XX series publication (AFSPCMAN 91-710, Range
    Safety User Requirements Manual). Range Safety approved program unique EWR 127-1 Tailored (T)
    documents shall remain in effect on approved contracts until rescinded or superceded.

3. Publication Responsibilities:
    3.1. HQ AFSPC/SE:
       3.1.1. Retains overall approval authority for the AFSPC range safety publication series.
       3.1.2. Issues final approval of AFSPC 91-7XX series publications.
       3.1.3. Reviews and approves all safety publications, as the approval authority in block 20 of AF
       Form 673, Request to Issue Publication.
       3.1.4. Ensures coordination with the HQ AFSPC staff, HQ AFSC and other external agencies.
    3.2. HQ AFSPC/SEC:
       3.2.1. Prepares, issues and maintains AFSPC 91-7XX series publications.
       3.2.2. Evaluates and approves all change requests from subordinate units to AFSPC 91-7XX pub-
       lications.
       3.2.3. Co-Chairs the Common Standards Working Group (CSWG). The CSWG is chartered to
       establish common Air Force/Federal Aviation Administration (AF/FAA) range safety require-
       ments.
       3.2.4. Performs coordination with the HQ AFSPC staff, HQ AFSC and other external agencies.
       3.2.5. Forwards all publications to AFSPC CSS/SCTI for publishing actions.
       3.2.6. Forwards requests for rescission to AFSPC CSS/SCTI.
       3.2.7. Maintains a copy of the official record set for all publications under the AFSPC 91-7XX
       series. The set includes:
           3.2.7.1. Copies of all required AF Form 673s.
           3.2.7.2. The most recent review conducted of each publication in this publication series.
           3.2.7.3. A copy of each publication.
       3.2.8. Maintains editorial control on all publications in this series.

     3.2.9. Maintains proposed changes to range safety publications on a controlled web site, for
     review by Range Users.
     3.2.10. Maintains the latest approved copies of range safety publications on a controlled web site
     for access by HQ AFSPC/SE approved Range Users and other HQ AFSPC/SE approved agencies.
     Approved publications will also be posted on the Air Force publications web site.
  3.3. Subordinate Safety Offices:
     3.3.1. Space and Missile Systems Center (SMC) Safety (SMC/SE):
        3.3.1.1. Reviews and coordinates (AFSPCMAN 91-710) change requests with the SMC Sys-
        tem Program Offices (Air Force Range User community) and provides a consolidated SMC
        recommendation to the 30 SW and 45 SW or HQ AFSPC/SE, as applicable.
        3.3.1.2. Participates in the CSWG.
     3.3.2. Fourteenth Air Force Safety (14 AF/SE):
        3.3.2.1. Reviews, verifies standardization and forwards SW coordinated change request rec-
        ommendations to HQ AFSPC/SEC for review and approval.
        3.3.2.2. Ensures standardization of the 30 SW and 45 SW range safety publications, pro-
        cesses, requirements and activities, to the maximum extent possible. Where standardized rec-
        ommendations between the wings are not possible, forwards each wingâ€™s rationale for their
        proposed recommendations to HQ AFSPC/SE for review and resolution.
        3.3.2.3. Participates in the CSWG.
     3.3.3. 30 Space Wing Safety (30 SW/SE):
        3.3.3.1. Implements AFSPC 91-7XX safety requirements publications for the range safety
        program at the Western Range (WR). Strives for maximum standardization of processes and
        requirements with the Eastern Range (ER).
            3.3.3.1.1. Coordinates all internal change requests to range safety publications with the 45
            SW and SMC/SE.
            3.3.3.1.2. Performs initial evaluations of all public and Range User comments to draft
            revisions of range safety publications and coordinates proposed responses with the 45 SW
            and SMC/SE.
        3.3.3.2. In conjunction with the 45 SW and HQ AFSPC/SE, provides the FAA Associate
        Administrator for Space Transportation (AST) a copy of proposed range safety changes that
        affect the FAA Launch Site Safety Assessment criteria and resolves any conflicts with the
        change requests.
        3.3.3.3. Reviews and forwards all coordinated changes to range safety publications to 14 AF/
        SE for review and concurrence. Provides the rationale for any WR non-standardized processes
        or requirement.
        3.3.3.4. Prepares, approves, and maintains copies of WR program unique tailored versions of
        range safety requirements publications.
        3.3.3.5. Participates in the CSWG.

      3.3.4. 45 Space Wing Safety (45 SW/SE):
         3.3.4.1. Implements 91-7XX safety requirement publications for the range safety program at
         the ER. Strives for maximum standardization of processes and requirements with the WR.
             3.3.4.1.1. Coordinates all internal change requests to range safety publications with the 30
             SW and SMC/SE.
             3.3.4.1.2. Performs initial evaluations of all public and Range User comments to draft
             revisions of range safety publications and coordinates proposed responses with the 30 SW
             and SMC/SE.
         3.3.4.2. In conjunction with the 30 SW and HQ AFSPC/SE, provides the FAA/AST a copy of
         proposed range safety changes that affect the FAA Launch Site Safety Assessment criteria and
         resolve any conflicts with the change requests.
         3.3.4.3. Reviews and forwards all 30 SW, 45 SW, SMC/SE and FAA/AST coordinated
         changes to range safety publications to 14 AF/SE for review and concurrence. Provides the
         rationale for any ER non-standardized processes or requirements.
         3.3.4.4. Prepares, approves and maintains ER program unique tailored versions of range
         safety requirements publications.
         3.3.4.5. Acts as the Office of Primary Responsibility for and maintains the master copy of
         EWR 127-1 and prior active and inactive safety documents. This includes versions of the
         range safety publications previously used or in use by Range Users; i.e., EWR 127-1 and or
         EWR 127-1 (T) tailored versions.
         3.3.4.6. Participates in the CSWG.
    3.4. AFSPC CSS/SCTI:
      3.4.1. Publishes all range safety publications governed by this program.
      3.4.2. Identifies all revised and newly distributed publications in the appropriate section of the
      publications web page.
      3.4.3. Maintains the official record set for all publications under the AFSPC 91-7XX series to
      include:
         3.4.3.1. All original required AF Form 673 Overprints.
         3.4.3.2. All original 2-Letter coordination and publications mandatory coordination, along
         with comment resolutions.
         3.4.3.3. The master copy of each publication.
         3.4.3.4. All AF Form 1382s (biennial reviews) for each publication
