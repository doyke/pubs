This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards and Decorations
Program. This instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-2803, The Air
Force Awards and Decorations Program. It provides for personal recognition and appreciation
by the nominating person of individuals in or associated with the 507th Air Refueling Wing
(ARW). The Wing Commander or Vice Wing Commander will present the 507 ARW
Commander’s Coin to military and civilian personnel, who, by their dedicated individual effort,
have facilitated the successful accomplishment of a particular program, function, or mission.
The purpose of this coin is to provide senior leadership with an informal means to show special
recognition of and appreciation for a job well done. It is intended to be “on the spot,” informal,
timely, and in the vicinity of the recipients work center. It applies to all 507 ARW members.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force Form (AF Form) 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. Responsibilities.
   1.1. The 507 ARW commander is responsible for the Wing Commander’s Coin Program on
   this installation.
   1.2. The Executive Officer (CCE) or delegated representative is responsible for the coin
   inventory.

     1.3. All actions concerning coin matters will be coordinate through the appropriate Group
     Commander and hence approved by the Wing Commander. In the case of an Air Refueling
     Wing direct report, the approving authority remains the Wing Commander through the
     Executive Officer.
2. Procedures.
     2.1. Anyone can nominate a deserving individual. Nominations should be forwarded
     through the Group Commander to the Executive Officer/Commander for approval.
     2.2. The Executive Officer will keep a physical inventory of all coins. CCE will also keep a
     list of coins to be presented and those presented and will forward updated lists to Historian
     and Public Affairs for further publication.
     2.3. Period coin counts will be accomplished to ensure accountability.
     2.4. Prescribed Forms:
     None.
     2.5. Adopted Forms:
     AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.
