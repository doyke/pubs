This instruction implements AFPD 33-1, Information Resource Management and AFI 33-106,
Managing High Frequency Radios, Personal Wireless Communication Systems (PWCS), and the
Military Affiliate Radio System. It outlines responsibilities for the Command, Control,
Communications, and Computer (C4) Systems Officer, Base PWCS Manager, Unit PWCS
Managers, user support agencies and others involved in the daily operation, maintenance,
acquisition, accountability and control of two-way radios. It is applicable to all units subordinate
to, or supported by the 39th Air Base Wing Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual
(AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This is a major Instruction revision and requires complete review. This instruction identifies
Unit PWCS roles in accounting for and securing payment for PWCS assets. All obsolete

1. PWCS Land Mobile Radio (LMR) Responsibilities:
     1.1. 39th Air Base Wing (ABW) C4 Systems Officer (CSO) will:
        1.1.1. Appoint a base PWCS Manager and forward a copy of the appointment letter to
        Headquarters, United States Air Forces in Europe (HQ USAFE) PWCS Manager.
        1.1.2. Approve/disapprove requests for establishment of new LMR nets/equipment.
        1.1.3. Approve/disapprove requests for new LMR equipment to be used on Major
        Command MAJCOM) approved nets.
     1.2. 39 ABW PWCS Managers will:
        1.2.1. Assist PWCS custodians, geographically separated units (GSU) site managers and
        users in managing and controlling their LMR equipment.
           1.2.1.1. Complete a Staff Assistance Visit (SAV) biannually on all unit PWCS
           Custodians on Incirlik AB and annually for GSUs.
        1.2.2. Review this instruction bi-annually and create a customer education guide to cover
        local conditions and procedures if necessary.
        1.2.3. Direct installation and removal of LMR equipment.
        1.2.4. Review all requests for new LMR nets and equipment to determine:
           1.2.4.1. Technical adequacy/validity.
           1.2.4.2. Frequency authorization and compatibility.
           1.2.4.3. Geographical coverage.
           1.2.4.4. Equipment compatibility with current systems.
           1.2.4.5. Alternative (cost effective) technical solutions.
        1.2.5. Budget for recurring and nonrecurring LMR maintenance.
        1.2.6. Function as the Contracting Officer Representative (COR) for LMR contract
        surveillance. Survey and report contractor performance to the contracting officer
        monthly. File a Contract Deficiency Report when the LMR maintenance contractor is not
        complying with the contract.
        1.2.7. Verify invoices monthly for services performed by contractors and prepare
        payment documentation.
        1.2.8. Coordinate preventative maintenance inspections (PMIs) biannually on all LMR
        equipment on Incirlik Air Base and GSUs.
           1.2.8.1. Ensure a 4 hour battery burn test (Uninterruptible Power Source) is
           completed every 180 days for surety related units and/or sections on all base stations
           and remote handsets.

        1.2.8.2. Complete PMIs on GSUs in correlation with completing the annual SAV.
     1.2.9. Maintain the (AIM) to include: Unit PWCS Custodians and LMR equipment
     assets for Incirlik AB and GSUs.
     1.2.10. Maintain system diagrams for each LMR net and provide copies to the Unit
     PWCS Custodians.
        1.2.10.1. System diagram will contain a listing of points of contact’s that may
        authorize other users on their respective nets (i.e. EM, EET, SFS 1, Crash, etc.).
        POC’s will be contacted prior to adding anyone to their respective nets.
     1.2.11. Maintain a list of all primary and alternate Unit PWCS Custodians. Appointment
     letters are to be re-accomplished annually and upon assignment of new and/or
     replacement PWCS custodians.
     1.2.12. Inspect LMR equipment after maintenance.
     1.2.13. Prepare documentation for defective items covered under warranty.
     1.2.14. Ensure all LMR radio communications are in the AES mode.
     1.2.15. Conduct SAVs bi-annually and upon request from equipment custodians (ECs).
     1.2.16. Train ECs within 60 days of appointment and annually for refresher training.
     1.2.17. Provide Intrinsically Safe LMRs (ISLMRs) to pertaining units. The following
     units require the use of ISLMRs:
        1.2.17.1. 39 ABW Safety
        1.2.17.2. 39th Civil Engineer Squadron (CES) Emergency Management
        1.2.17.3. 39 CES Environmental/Liquid Fuels
        1.2.17.4. 39 CES Explosive Ordinance Disposal (EOD)
        1.2.17.5. 39 CES Fire Department
        1.2.17.6. 39 CS Cable Maintenance
        1.2.17.7. 39 Logistics Readiness Squadron Petroleum, Oils and Lubricants/
        Cryogenics
        1.2.17.8. 39th Medical Operations Squadron/Bioenvironmental Engineering Element
        1.2.17.9. 39th Maintenance Squadron (MXS) Ammo
        1.2.17.10. 39 MXS Explosive Ops
        1.2.17.11. 39th Security Forces Squadron (SFS)
        1.2.17.12. 728th Aircraft Maintenance Squadron (AMS) All Flight line Ops
        1.2.17.13. 728th AMS Passenger Services
        1.2.17.14. Det 522, Air Force Office of Special Investigations (OSI)
        1.2.17.15. 39 ABW Wing Plans & Programs (XP)

       1.2.18. Perform a base-wide rekey of all LMR equipment in any of the following
       situations:
          1.2.18.1. Quarterly.
          1.2.18.2. If there has been a compromise of the current AES key.
          1.2.18.3. Disable or inhibit an LMR if a Report of Survey (ROS) is initiated and the
          asset is not accounted for (i.e. lost, stolen, etc.).
          1.2.18.4. If there has been any compromise not mentioned above that creates a
          vulnerability.
       1.2.19. Provide a point of contact for LMR trouble reporting after normal duty hours.
    1.3. Unit PWCS Equipment Custodians will:
       1.3.1. Provide the 39 ABW PWCS Manager with a current letter of appointment for
       primary and alternate ECs within 10 days of appointment.
          1.3.1.1. Primary and alternates will need to provide a new appointment letter to the
          PWCS Management Office no later than 60 days prior of the losing EC’s Date
          Eligible for Return From Overseas (DEROS), training completed by the new primary
          or alternate unit PWCS custodian and completion of a joint inventory prior to being
          signed off in Virtual Military Personnel Flight (vMPF) system.
       1.3.2. Maintain a Unit PWCS continuity folder, tabbed out. It will include the following:
          1.3.2.1. Tab A - Appointment Letter
          1.3.2.2. Tab B - Training Certificates
          1.3.2.3. Tab C - Equipment Inventory
          1.3.2.4. Tab D - Call Signs
          1.3.2.5. Tab E - Frequency Authorizations
              1.3.2.5.1. Memorandum for Record (MFR) provided by the Base PWCS Office
          1.3.2.6. Tab F - Reports of Survey (if any)
          1.3.2.7. Tab G - Hand Receipts AF Form 1297, Temporary Issue Receipt and a
          locally generated Maintenance Log
              1.3.2.7.1. Hand receipts are valid for 90 days.
          1.3.2.8. Tab H - Operating Instructions and Report of Survey Guide (Cross-reference
          sheet or hard copy will suffice).
          1.3.2.9. Tab I - User Training slides and documentation of user training annually.
          1.3.2.10. Ensure documentation of user training includes the notice and consent
          statement as outlined in AFI 10-712, Telecommunications Monitoring and
          Assessment Program (TMAP), para. 4.7.2. NOTE: User training is completed by the
          unit PWCS custodian annually and when new personnel arrive.
          1.3.2.11. Tab J - Miscellaneous

     1.3.3. Submit LMR maintenance requests to the 39 ABW PWCS Management Office
     according to the following guidelines:
        1.3.3.1. ECs must call the 39 ABW PWCS Management Office to submit a
        maintenance request.
        1.3.3.2. The Base PWCS managers will fill out a USAFE Form 23, Land Mobile
        Radio Service Record and create a maintenance work order (WO) in AIM.
        1.3.3.3. Unit PWCS custodians ensure the LMR has been annotated on their
        maintenance log prior to being sent in for repair.
        1.3.3.4. Once the WO is complete, the PWCS Management Office will contact the
        customer to schedule a time to bring the LMR assets in for maintenance.
        1.3.3.5. When maintenance is complete, the PWCS Management Office will contact
        the customer to arrange a time to pick up the LMR assets.
     1.3.4. Assist equipment users in preparing supply documentation to requisition or turn in
     LMR equipment.
     1.3.5. Maintain a minimum of 10% stock of ancillary items (batteries, antennas, etc.) to
     support mission requirements.
     1.3.6. As notified by users:
        1.3.6.1. Perform initial checks on malfunctioning LMR equipment before reporting
        problems to the 39 ABW PWCS Management Office.
     1.3.7. Make fixed equipment (repeaters, base stations, remote controls, etc.) available,
     without delay, to the contractor for maintenance and PMI. Sanitize the area and escort
     the LMR maintenance contractor in secure/restricted areas. NOTE: To avoid costly
     delays, ensure you have an escort available within 5 minutes of arrival.
     1.3.8. Report Electromagnetic Interference (EMI) incidents to the 39 ABW Spectrum
     Manager through the 39 Communications Squadron Communications Focal Point (CFP).
     1.3.9. Conduct Unit PWCS Equipment Custodian and user education training within the
     organization.
     1.3.10. Notify the Base PWCS manager of unsatisfactory maintenance contractor
     performance.
     1.3.11. Secure all Unit PWCS equipment assets when not in use.
     1.3.12. Ensure all PWCS equipment is listed on the LMR inventory and inform the Base
     PWCS Manager to add any previously undocumented items.
     1.3.13. Conduct an official inventory and provide results to the Base PWCS Office under
     any of the following circumstances:
        1.3.13.1. Annually.
        1.3.13.2. When directed by the Base PWCS Manager, Base CSO, or Squadron
        Commander.
     1.3.14. Conduct a joint inventory when there is any change in equipment custodians.

       1.3.15. Establish call signs on a signed memorandum from the unit Commander.
       1.3.16. Submit all new/replacement LMR requests through the 39 ABW PWCS
       Management Office.
       1.3.17. Prepare and process AF Form 9, Request for Purchase or International Merchant
       Purchase Authorization Card (IMPAC) authorization for required services that the
       existing LMR contract does not support.
       1.3.18. Maintain adequate spare (non-fixed) LMR equipment for use when primary
       equipment is in maintenance or loaned out for temporary requirements.
       1.3.19. Initiate a (ROS) for lost or damaged LMR equipment through the unit's ROS
       monitor and IAW AFMAN 23-220, Reports of Survey for Air Force Property. Provide
       the following information to the base PWCS Systems Manager within 48 hours of the
       incident:
          1.3.19.1. Nomenclature.
          1.3.19.2. Model Number.
          1.3.19.3. Serial number.
          1.3.19.4. Location (i.e., vehicle registration number, or building and room number).
       1.3.20. Train all users on what to do in the event of failure of the LMR trunking system.
          1.3.20.1. Radios will be programmed for three zones.
              1.3.20.1.1. Zone “A” will be for normal day-to-day operations and utilize the
              LMR trunking system to conduct communications.
              1.3.20.1.2. Zone “B” will have nets on backup repeaters.
                  1.3.20.1.2.1. (SFS) Net.
                  1.3.20.1.2.2. RAMP Net.
                  1.3.20.1.2.3. CRASH Net.
                  1.3.20.1.2.4. Commander’s Net.
                  1.3.20.1.2.5. Base Support.
                  1.3.20.1.2.6. OSI Net.
              1.3.20.1.3. Zone “C” will be utilized for line of site communications (simplex).
       1.3.21. Ensure all LMR equipment is affixed with the DD Form 2056, Telephone
       Monitoring Notification Decal
    1.4. LMR Users will:
       1.4.1. Report interference to the unit PWCS custodian for proper EMI reporting.
       1.4.2. Comply with COMSEC, Operations Security (OPSEC), operating procedures and
       immediately report lost or stolen LMR’s to the unit PWCS custodian, Base PWCS
       Manager and SFS Law Enforcement (LE) desk.
       1.4.3. Secure all LMR equipment assets.

      1.4.4. Comply with established guidelines for use in hazardous areas (e.g. Petrol, Oil and
      Lubricants (POL) refueling areas and near explosive ordnance).
          1.4.4.1. The use of (ISLMRs) is required to be used at any time in a hazardous
          environment.
          1.4.4.2. The recommended minimum safe operating distance for any LMR in a
          hazardous/explosive environment is 100 feet for handhelds and 300 feet for base
          stations and mobiles.
      1.4.5. Report outages directly to the Unit PWCS Equipment Custodian during normal
      duty hours.
      1.4.6. Report after-duty outages to the 39 CS CFP at 0533-924-8150.
      1.4.7. Contact the Unit PWCS Equipment Custodian for any maintenance requirements,
      to turn equipment into Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) or transferring equipment to
      another account.
      1.4.8. Perform immediate operational checks on LMR equipment returned from
      maintenance.
2. 39 ABW Spectrum Manager will:
   2.1. Process all requests for new LMR frequencies or requests for modifications to existing
   frequencies IAW AFI 33-118, Electromagnetic Spectrum Management.
   2.2. Track all frequency authorizations throughout Turkey.
   2.3. Assist unit PWCS custodians with EMI reporting.
