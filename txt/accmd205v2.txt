This mission directive implements AFPD 10-1, Mission Directives; and AFI 10-101, Format and Content
of Mission Directives. It prescribes the mission and establishes the responsibilities of the Air Combat
Command Training Support Squadron (ACC TRSS) at Langley AFB VA. It does not apply to Air
National Guard (ANG) or Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) units and members. Send comments and
suggesed improvements to this mission directive on AF IMT 847, Recommendation for Change of Pub-
lication, through channels, to ACC TRSS/TSF, 205 Dodd Blvd, Suite 121, Langley AFB VA 23665-2703.
Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFPD 37-1, Air Force Information Management; and AFMAN 37-123, Management of
Records; and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located
at https://webrims.amc.af.mil/.



1. Mission. Responsible for supporting training requirements for 23 weapon systems in support of all
ACC operational training and providing policy and guidance for fielded and emerging aircrew and main-
tenance training devices.

2. Command. The Commander, ACC TRSS, reports to the Air Combat Command Director of Aero-
space Operations (HQ ACC/DO) for Combat Air Forces (CAF) training issues.

3. Responsibilities:
   3.1. Directly oversees 13 operations training development (OTD) detachments supporting operations
   training throughout ACC for current and emerging weapon and defense systems training development
   to include OTD team manning and structural alignment.
   3.2. Validates training requests and works with ACC weapon system functional managers to build
   training proposals, review available AF operations training products, task appropriate development
   agencies, validate training prototypes, deliver completed courseware and required evaluation, review
   and revision of completed courseware.

    3.3. Manages HQ ACC/DO formal training course syllabi and graduate evaluation programs.
    3.4. Provides centralized management for HQ ACC/DO Contract Aircrew Training (CAT) and
    Courseware Development (CWD) contracts.
    3.5. Procures new and modifies existing CAT/CWD contracts in conjunction with ACC CONS to
    maximize training efficiency and effectiveness.
    3.6. Manages HQ ACC/DO Aircrew Training Support Plan (ATSP) outlining CAT/CWD indepen-
    dent contractors, the development, maintenance, and updating of all Formal Training Unit Learning
    Centers, and the execution of HQ ACC/DO's follow-on continuation training topics for warfighters.
    3.7. Oversees the development, implementation, and stability of the HQ ACC/DO Briefing Room
    Interactive (BRI) initiative supporting ACC Formal Training Units.
    3.8. HQ ACC/DO focal point for human performance issues affecting the warfighter. Identifies
    human factors training initiatives and safety issues impacting training and training systems.
    3.9. Provides aircrew and maintenance training systems cradle-to-grave advocacy, policy, guidance,
    contract management, and support for the CAF IAW AFI 36-2248, Operation and Management of
    Aircrew Training Devices. NOTE: ACC TRSS/CC has direct authority from HQ ACC/DO to task
    the 53rd Wing's 29th Training Systems Squadron through 53rd Wing/XP to provide management,
    technical, and operational assistance for CAF training system programs, projects, and events.
    3.10. Within ACC, acts as single POC for operations training development for tasking and staffing
    valid requests IAW ACCI 36-2250, ACC Operations Training Development Program. NOTE: ACC
    TRSS/CC has tasking authority from HQ ACC/DO to task the 436th Training Squadron through 7
    OG/CC for development, modification, or deletion of training programs, ACC-sponsored formal
    schools, multimedia products/presentations, and computer software applicable to CAF needs.
NOTE: The ACC TRSS/CC has direct authority from HQ ACC/DO to task the 29 TSS and 436 TS as
described in paragraphs 3.9. and 3.10.
