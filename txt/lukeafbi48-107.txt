This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 48-1, Aerospace Medicine and
Air Force Instruction 44-102, Community Health Management. It establishes responsibilities for
active duty Air Force, DOD civilian, Air National Guard (ANG), Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC), and other military service personnel by directing them to Public Health (PH) upon a
positive pregnancy test. It is PH's responsibility to evaluate the work environment of all
pregnant employees regardless of their duties. Refer recommended changes and questions about
this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR), using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. Responsibilities.
     1.1. Employees will:
        1.1.1. Report to their primary care manager (PCM) to confirm suspected pregnancy
        (after missing one menstrual cycle).
        1.1.2. Report to PH after positive pregnancy test.
     1.2. Supervisors will:
        1.2.1. Brief all newly assigned female personnel upon arrival on the elements of the Fetal
        Protection Program.
        1.2.2. Brief all personnel annually on the elements of the Fetal Protection Program.
        1.2.3. Incorporate Fetal Protection training into annual safety briefings and document the
        training on the AF Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record, Core Automated
        Maintenance System, (CAMS), AF ESOH-MIS, or other electronic equivalent. This
        information shall be accessible electronically or hard copy.
        1.2.4. Direct all female personnel, who suspect they are pregnant (after missing one
        menstrual cycle), to their PCM for pregnancy testing.
        1.2.5. Direct all personnel with a positive pregnancy test to PH.
     1.3. Public Health will:
        1.3.1. Assess patient's work environment for potential health and safety risks.
        1.3.2. Generate work restriction recommendations to PH physician/consultant.
        1.3.3. Initiate AF Form 469, “Duty Limiting Condition Report,” with approved work
        restrictions by PH physician/consultant. Original AF Form 469 is filed in patient's
        medical record; copies provided to patient, patient's supervisor, and patient's unit orderly
        room.
     1.4. Bioenvironmental Engineering (BEE) will:
        1.4.1. Review work environment and practices of pregnant employees assigned to
        industrial shops with potential health and safety risks.
        1.4.2. Provide PH and patient a description of job activities, potential exposure hazards,
        available protective measures, and current work practices within patient’s workplace.
        1.4.3. If necessary, visit the patient's shop or facility to evaluate potential hazardous
        exposures.
     1.5. Civilian DOD employees will be handled in the same manner with the exception that
     PH will initiate a duty restriction letter instead of an AF Form 469. Original duty restriction
     letter is filed in patient's medical record, copies provided to patient, patient's supervisor, and
     56 FSS/DPC.
     1.6. Pregnant Air Force Reserve members will be handled in the same manner by the 944th
     Reserve unit personnel.

   1.7. Pregnant Air National Guard members will be handled in the same manner and
   recommended duty restrictions will be forwarded to the appropriate ANG medical unit
   representative.
   1.8. Other military service personnel will be directed to contact and advise their units of their
   pregnancy. PH will support as deemed necessary.
   1.9. If the patient performs hazardous duties the following will happen:
      1.9.1. After initial PH pregnancy interview and BEE shop review, a Fetal Protection
      Working Group (FPWG) will convene as needed. The FPWG members are comprised of
      a PH representative, BEE representative, PH physician/consultant, patient, and patient’s
      supervisor.
      1.9.2. The FPWG will convene as soon as all workplace data is collected. Members of
      the FPWG will evaluate work environment information and discuss any additional
      restrictions to the standard pregnancy profile.
      1.9.3. AF Form 469 will be finalized, addressing new restriction(s) as mandated by
      FPWG.
   1.10. If patient performs administrative duties, a standard DLC profile will be issued.
2. Documentation. Profile completion time will be 5 working days for administrative
workplaces; BEE Industrial Hygiene (IH) workplace survey profiles are completed in a timely
manner.
