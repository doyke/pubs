The purpose of this wing instruction is to identify formal promotion expectations and outline the
promotion approval process for SMSgt/E-8 & CMSgt/E-9 unit vacancies. This instruction
implements Air Force Policy Directive AFPD 36-25, Military Promotion and Demotion.
Supervisors and commanders will ensure that all assigned personnel are briefed on the contents
of this wing instruction. This instruction requires the collection and maintenance of information
protected by the Privacy Act of 1974. The authorities to collect and maintain the records outlined
in this instruction are Title 10, United States code (U.S.C.), Section 8013 and Executive Order
9397. Ensure that all records created as a result of process prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS)
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcssaf61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility
(OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form
847s from their field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

934AWI36-2502, 27 August 2013
1. Scope: This wing instruction applies to enlisted personnel assigned the 934th Airlift Wing.
2. General Policy: Standardization of Air Force Reserve (AFR) SMSgt/E-8 and CMSgt/E-9
unit promotion requirements. Today’s enlisted leaders are required to obtain more than the basic
 2                                                        934AWI36-2502 29 OCTOBER 2014


requirements to compete and lead in today’s AFR. This instruction is designed to lay out the
promotion requirements for SMSgt/E-8 and CMSgt/E-9 positions in the 934th Airlift Wing.
3. Promotion Requirements for SMSgt (E-8):
     3.1. All requirements are mandatory for promotion.
        3.1.1. Current & Passing Fit to Fight Assessment.
        3.1.2. Community College of the Air Force (CCAF) Degree.
        3.1.3. Senior rater endorsement (SRE) on most recent Enlisted Performance Report
        (EPR).
        3.1.4. Waivers to this paragraph may be approved by the wing commander, with the
        exception of CCAF. Waivers should be submitted with the promotion package. The
        waiver request is normally a memo for record (MFR) and it must delineate the reason for
        the waiver and appropriate justification for approval.
4. Promotion Requirements for CMSgt (E-9):
     4.1. All requirements are mandatory for promotion.
        4.1.1. Current & Passing Fit to Fight Assessment.
        4.1.2. Community College of the Air Force (CCAF) Degree.
        4.1.3. Senior Enlisted Joint Professional Military Education (SEJPME).
        4.1.4. Senior rater endorsement (SRE) on most recent Enlisted Performance Report
        (EPR).
        4.1.5. Waivers to this paragraph may be approved by the wing commander, with the
        exception of CCAF. Waivers should be submitted with the promotion package. The
        waiver request is normally a memo for record (MFR) and it must delineate the reason for
        the waiver and appropriate justification for approval.
5. Promotion Procedures.
     5.1. Unit will fill out the AF Form 224 in accordance with established policy and guidance
     from FSS/FSMPD.
     5.2. Routing of AF Form 224 is as follows:
        5.2.1. For unit-level SMSgt/E-8 and CMSgt/E-9 promotions, unit commanders forward
        the completed wet-signed hard copy AF Form 224 to the group commander. ASTS will
        forward this directly to the FSS/FSMPD.
        5.2.2. Group commander will approve and then forward the wet-signed hard copy AF
        Form 224 to the Force Support Squadron Force Career Development office
        (FSS/FSMPD) for a compliance quality check. ASTS will send their AF Form 224
        directly to the FSS/FSMPD.
        5.2.3. FSS/FSMPD will forward the compliance-checked AF Form 224 to command
        chief for review.
        5.2.4. The command chief will then forward to the wing commander for final approval.
