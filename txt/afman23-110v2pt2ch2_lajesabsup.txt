AFMAN23-110V2PT2CH2, dated 1 October 2008 is supplemented as follows. This supplement
implements and extends the guidance Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 23-110, USAF Supply
Manual. These procedures apply to personnel assigned to the Materiel Management,
Management and Systems, and Vehicle Management Flights. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publications; rout AF Form 847s from the field
through the Lajes Field publications/forms manager. Ensure all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.
SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This supplement has been updated to reflect current operating procedures. The document has
substantially revised and must be reviewed entirely.
2.14.1. Forums will be conducted as required.
2.16.1. The Base presents a Right Start Program every other week for newly assigned
personnel.
2.19.11.4.1. During the Commander of Logistic Readiness Squadron (LRS/CC) absence the

Logistic Readiness Squadron Director of Operations (LRS/DO) will sign for documents
requiring the signature of the accountable officer.
2.27.4.2. The Procedures and Accountability section will act as the primary point for resolving
incoming and outgoing supply data transmission problems through Defense Data Network
(DDN).
2.28.4.4. A semi-annual analysis will be conducted on all incoming and outgoing Supply
Discrepancy Reports (SDR).
2.28.4.5. The M16 (Shipment Loss Analysis) analysis will be conducted semi-annually to
identify trends in shipment losses by source of supply.
2.28.4.6. A customer complaint/problem analysis will be conducted semi annually.
2.28.4.8.3. The Procedures and Accountability Section will conduct a semi-annual inventory
analysis.
2.29.4. The responsibility to maintain the Authorization Receipt Listing and documentation that
authorizes individuals to receipt for classified property will be maintained in the Document
Control Section.
2.31.1.7. A Weighted Airman Promotion System(WAPS) library will not be established in the
Training Section. Fuels Training will maintain a WAPS study reference library within the fuels
activity in accordance with AFI 23-201, Fuels Management.
2.31.1.10. The Training Section will manage the Squadron Ancillary Program.
2.32.3.5.1. (Added) Reappointed equipment custodian will be required to take a Computer
Based Training (CBT), if their prior training was more than one year old. Customer Service will
provide the training. Personnel will be briefed on local policies, local requirements, and
command supplements. Acknowledgement of this briefing will be kept in the custodian folder.
2.49.6. Receiving will contact Customer Section to obtain missing local purchase status for
local purchase receipts. Receiving will contact Base Contracting for missing DD Forms 1155,
Orders For Supplies or Services.
2.54. The Individual Equipment Element (IEE) is located in the Material Management Flight.
2.55.2.2. Customer Service will maintain one file of technical orders to include Time
Compliance Technical Order (TCTO) and stock lists to serve all supply activities.
2.97.10. (Added) Forms Adopted. DD Form 1155, Order for Supplies or Services, AF Form
847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.
