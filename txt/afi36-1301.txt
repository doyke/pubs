This instruction supplements the requirements of DoD 5000.52, Defense Acquisition,
Technology, and Logistics Workforce Education, Training and Career Development Program. It
provides instruction on how to manage, code and track KLPs as directed by the Assistant
Secretary of the Air Force for Acquisition (SAF/AQ). It applies to all personnel who manage,
administer, or support KLPs, all members assigned to KLPs and all agencies that select members
for KLPs. This publication does not apply to Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Units.
SAF/AQ is the waiver authority for this instruction. This AFI may be supplemented at any level,
but all supplements must be routed to SAF/AQXD for coordination prior to certification and
approval. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to SAF/AQXD
using the AF Information Management Tool (IMT) 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF IMT 847s from the field through Major Command (MAJCOM)
publications/forms managers. This instruction requires collecting and maintaining information
protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by Title 10 USC, Section 8013. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AF Manual 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance
with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/.

                                           Chapter 1

     GENERAL GUIDANCE FOR ACQUISITION KEY LEADERSHIP POSITIONS

1.1. Definition. In accordance with the DoDD 5000.52, Key Leadership Positions (KLPs) are a
subset of Critical Acquisition Positions (CAPs). KLPs include positions that require special
Office of the Under Secretary of Defense (Acquisition, Technology and Logistics)
(OUSD(AT&L)) and Service Acquisition Executive (SAE) attention with regard to
qualifications, accountability, and tenure. In general, KLP incumbents have direct responsibility
for, and direct influence on, the success of an acquisition program, acquisition system, or major
technical area of responsibility.
1.2. Purpose. The purpose of this AFI is to supplement DoDD 5000.52 and provide guidance
for managing KLPs. This instruction also describes the roles and responsibilities of the space
and non-space SAEs, Program Executive Officers, Designated Acquisition Officials, Major
Commands (MAJCOMs), Air Force Personnel Assignment/Employment Teams, and other
participants in the assignments to and management of KLPs. (Note: For the purpose of this
instruction, the term MAJCOM also refers to Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (JCS), Air Staff, and Field Operating Agencies (FOAs).)
1.3. KLP Position Designation. KLPs are designated by the responsible SAE and approved by
the OUSD(AT&L).
   1.3.1. The Air Force Director, Acquisition Career Management (DACM) assists the SAEs
   regarding KLP policy and management and submits a listing of KLPs, via the SAEs, to
   OUSD(AT&L).
   1.3.2. KLPs will include the following positions:
       1.3.2.1. Program Executive Officers (PEOs).
       1.3.2.2. Deputy Program Executive Officers (DPEOs).
       1.3.2.3. Designated Acquisition Officials (DAOs).
       1.3.2.4. System Program Managers           (SPMs)     for    Acquisition   Category   I/II
       (ACAT I/ACAT II) programs.
       1.3.2.5. Program Managers (PMs) for ACAT I/ACAT II programs.
       1.3.2.6. Deputy Program Managers (DPMs) for ACAT I programs.
       1.3.2.7. Senior Contracting Officials (SCOs) – (Air Staff/MAJCOM Level).
       1.3.2.8. Senior Center Contracting Officials (SCCOs).
       1.3.2.9. Acquisition-coded General Officer (GO) positions.
       1.3.2.10. Acquisition-coded Senior Executive Service (SES) positions.
       1.3.2.11. Product and Logistics Center Senior Engineer positions.
       1.3.2.12. Product and Logistics Center Senior Financial Manager positions.
       1.3.2.13. Other positions identified by the SAEs.

     1.3.3. MAJCOMS will ensure SAE approved KLPs are coded correctly in the appropriate
     manpower and personnel systems. In addition, owning units will identify the position as a
     KLP in the civilian position description (PD).
     1.3.4. Commands may request SAE designation of additional KLPs for leadership positions
     at the GS-15/NSPS PB III (or equivalent) and O-6 level.
1.4. Assignment Qualifications. To be assigned to a KLP, candidates must meet the position
qualification requirements (Attachment 2) or be able to attain them within the time frame
allowed by statute and DoD policy (Attachment 2). (See also 1.6 Waivers)
1.5. Tenure. Individuals assigned to KLPs are required to remain in the respective position for
the tenure period specified by the responsible SAE and must sign a tenure agreement reflecting
the tenure period (Attachment 3). (See also 1.6 Waivers)
     1.5.1. MAJCOM/CCs and PEOs/DAOs will recommend tenure periods for their KLP
     nominees as required in support of program requirements.
        1.5.1.1. Unless otherwise specified by the SAE, the tenure period for a KLP will be three
        years. For ACAT I PMs/DPMs the tenure should be through the program milestone
        closest to 4 years.
     1.5.2. For military personnel, the Active Duty Service Commitment (ADSC) will be
     adjusted to correspond with the tenure completion date.
     1.5.3. The gaining organization is responsible for executing the tenure agreement and
     forwarding the tenure agreement to the servicing personnel office for inclusion into the
     respective civilian or military personnel system and official personnel records consistent with
     paragraph 1.7.7.2. A selectee will not be eligible for appointment to, and an incumbent will
     not be eligible to retain employment in, a KLP if they decline to sign the tenure agreement.
     1.5.4. Tenure Agreements. DD Form 2889, Critical Acquisition Position Service Agreement
     Key Leadership Position (KLP) (Attachment 4) will be used to document the KLP tenure
     agreement. The tenure agreement for all other CAPs will be documented on DD Form 2888
     and will not be used for KLPs.
1.6. Waivers. If circumstances necessitate, the MAJCOM/CCs, PEOs or DAOs may request the
responsible SAE approve a Position Requirements Waiver or a Tenure Waiver.
     1.6.1. Position Requirements Waiver. If a KLP candidate does not meet the minimum
     position requirements (Attachment 2), a Position Requirements Waiver request will
     accompany the proposed selection. The gaining organization must specify the target date by
     which the individual is expected to meet the position requirements. Follow MAJCOM and/or
     Center coordination instructions to route via SAF/AQXD the request through the DACM to
     the responsible SAE for approval. Individuals who fail to meet the KLP requirements within
     the timeframe as stated in Attachment 2 or the training plan documented on their position
     requirements waiver, may be removed from the KLP by the SAE.
     1.6.2. Tenure Waiver. If warranted, the responsible SAE may approve a waiver of the
     tenure duration to release an individual from the tenure agreement. MAJCOM/CCs, PEOs
     and DAOs shall explain why release from statutory position tenure is in the best interests of
     the Air Force or DoD. Follow MAJCOM or Center coordination instructions to route the
     request via SAF/AQXD through the DACM to the responsible SAE for approval.

   1.6.3. Waivers must be formally requested on DD Form 2905, "AT&L Workforce Position
   Requirements or Tenure Waiver," which is generated using the ACQ Now AT&L Workforce
   Waiver System (Attachment 5).
   1.6.4. Waiver Disposition. Approved KLP waivers are recorded in ACQ Now AT&L
   Workforce Waiver System and personnel systems by SAF/AQXD for the responsible SAE.
1.7. Responsibilities and Authority.
   1.7.1. Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Acquisition (SAF/AQ).
       1.7.1.1. Develops Air Force policy for KLP management.
       1.7.1.2. Implements policy to manage and administer the KLP process.
   1.7.2. Responsible SAE. The Secretary of the Air Force appoints the SAE for Air Force
   space acquisition programs who is responsible for KLPs associated with space acquisition
   programs. The Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Acquisition is the SAE for non-space
   acquisition programs and is responsible for all other Air Force acquisition KLPs.
       1.7.2.1. Designates KLPs.
       1.7.2.2. Establishes tenure requirements and serves as the final selection approval
       authority for each KLP, unless delegated to the DACM.
       1.7.2.3. Acts as the approval authority for Position Requirements Waivers and Tenure
       Waivers, unless delegated to the DACM.
       1.7.2.4. When the Defense Acquisition Executive (DAE) is the Milestone Decision
       Authority (MDA), consults with DAE prior to assigning PEOs or PMs to KLPs.
   1.7.3. DACM.
       1.7.3.1. Assists the SAEs regarding KLP policy and management.
   1.7.4. SAF/AQXD (DACM Office).
       1.7.4.1. Manages the KLP designation process.
       1.7.4.2. Maintains and distributes on an as needed basis the approved list of KLPs to
       include information on incumbents, waivers and tenure.
       1.7.4.3. Staffs proposed KLP selections including position qualification and/or Tenure
       Waiver to the appropriate SAE for approval.
   1.7.5. PEO/DAO.
       1.7.5.1. Ensures that individuals waived into KLPs meet the qualification requirements
       of the positions within the specified timeframe.
       1.7.5.2. Recommends to the SAE candidates and tenure for ACAT I PMs and DPMs and
       ACAT II PMs, considering program factors.
   1.7.6. MAJCOM/CC.
       1.7.6.1. Establishes MAJCOM KLP management policies consistent with this AFI.
       1.7.6.2. Recommends tenure for MAJCOM HQ KLP positions and other KLP positions
       not under the auspices of a PEO/DAO.

    1.7.7. Gaining Organization.
       1.7.7.1. Advises members of KLP tenure requirement.
       1.7.7.2. Ensures that tenure agreements and waivers are filed in appropriate personnel
       record/system.
    1.7.8. Assignment/Employment Teams (e.g. AFPC/DPI, AFPC/DPA, AF/DPO, AF/DPG,
    AF/DPS).
       1.7.8.1. Notify centrally managed candidates of tenure and qualification requirements.
       1.7.8.2. Coordinate nominations through the DACM to the responsible SAE for approval

                                        Chapter 2

                      MILITARY KLP ASSIGNMENT PROCESS

2.1. General Officers. AF/DPG will coordinate General Officers nominated for KLPs through
the DACM to the responsible SAE before final selection and notification.
2.2. Colonels.
   2.2.1. Command Selection Board (CSB) process. Once candidates are matched to positions,
   and prior to public release, AF/DPO will produce a Command Selection List (CSL) and
   coordinate through the DACM to the responsible SAE for final approval.
   2.2.2. The Gameplan Process. Once a member is selected, AF/DPO will coordinate the final
   selection through the DACM to the responsible SAE for final approval.
2.3. Lieutenant Colonels. AFPC/DPA will coordinate final selections through the DACM to
the responsible SAE for final approval.

                                          Chapter 3

                        CIVILIAN KLP ASSIGNMENT PROCESS

3.1. Senior Executive Service and Equivalent. AF/DPS will coordinate candidates for
acquisition SES KLPs through the DACM to the responsible SAE prior to
consideration/approval by the Executive Review Board (ERB).
3.2. Civilian Strategic Leader Program (CSLP) Positions. MAJCOMs will provide AF/DPS
position qualification requirements for KLPs filled under the CSLP process. AF/DPS will
forward CSLP candidates selected for KLP positions to the DACM for responsible SAE
approval before final selection and notification to the employee.
3.3. All Other Civilian KLP Positions. PEOs, DAOs and leaders of other organizations will
ensure that the Request for Personnel Action (RPA) identifies the position as a KLP and provides
the qualification requirements. The responsible employment team will coordinate the selection
through DACM to the responsible SAE for approval before final selection and notification to the
employee.
