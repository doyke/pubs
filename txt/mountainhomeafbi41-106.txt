This instruction establishes Mountain Home Air Force Base’s (MHAFB) PAD program and
implements public law. The program provides for Automated External Defibrillators (AED) be
placed in strategic locations on the installation to allow access by trained individuals to use in
reviving personnel during certain cardiac crisis prior to arrival of medical personnel. This
instruction pertains to the 366th Fighter Wing (366 FW), the 366th Comptroller Squadron,
Financial Analysis (366 CPTS/FMA), the 366th Mission Support Group (366 MSG), the 366th
Force Support Squadron, Military Personnel Section (MPS) (366 FSS/FSMP), the 366th Civil
Engineer Squadron, Electrical Shop (366 CES/CEOA), 366 FSS/FSCC (Collocated Club),
Afterburner Lounge, 366 FSS/FSCP (Airman's Lounge and Community Center), Strike Dam
Marina, Lodging Office, 366 FSS/FSVS (Fitness and Sports ), the 366th Security Forces
Squadron (366 SFS) Grandview Gate Search Pit, the 366th Medical Group (366 MDG), the
366th Medical Support Squadron, Medical Maintenance (366 MDSS/SGSLE), the Army, Air
Force Exchange Service (AAFES), and the Defense Commissary Agency (DeCA). It identifies
responsibilities, maintenance, quality assurance, and documentation requirements. Ensure all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.
Contact supporting records managers as required. Refer recommended changes and questions
regarding this publication to the office of primary responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication, route AF Forms 847 through the base publications
and forms manager.

1. Responsibilities:
     1.1. 366 FW/CC has overall responsibility for the PAD program. 366 FW/CC directs the
     Director of Base Medical Services (DBMS) to ensure proper medical objectives are
     maintained for the PAD program.
     1.2. 366 MDG/CC (Commander) is responsible to 366 FW/CC for implementation of the
     PAD program. 366 MDG/CC will ensure all medical objectives are maintained and provide
     professional guidance on program administration. 366 MDG/CC will appoint through two
     separate letters: (1) a medical director; and (2) a program director for the PAD program.
     1.3. Medical Director. The medical director will be a physician proficient in emergency
     medical services protocols, cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) and the use of AEDs IAW
     Idaho State Code, 5-337. The medical director is responsible for providing oversight for
     training, emergency medical services coordination, protocols, standing orders and
     formulation of AED deployment strategies. Medical director will develop quality assurance
     and guidelines for use of the AED and will review or have a designated representative review
     Attachment 2, AED Event Summary Sheets, within 5 days of AED use.
     1.4. Program Director. The program director, as a minimum, will be a Basic Life Support
     (BLS) Training Site Faculty. Program director will oversee all training processes for Heart
     Saver (HS) AED, and is responsible for providing recommendations to squadron
     commanders for individuals to serve as site coordinators.
     1.5. Site Coordinators. Program director will notify squadron commanders, by letter, to
     appoint site coordinators. Site coordinators will, as a minimum, be trained in HS AED, and
     appointed in writing by their squadron commander, or in the case of AAFES and DeCA, by
     their respective store managers. Site coordinators are responsible for arranging installation
     of AEDs in their areas through either their facility managers or 366 CES. Site coordinators
     will also establish an account custodian for the AED in their area. Site coordinators will
     select an AED emergency response team and coordinate the team member’s training. Site
     coordinators will communicate with the program director and medical director of the PAD
     program on issues regarding emergency response. Maintain AEDs in the following
     locations:

2. Equipment Maintenance. Site coordinators will ensure all equipment and accessories
necessary for support of medical emergency are maintained in a state of readiness. Inspect AED
and accessories once a week and document on Attachment 4, Lifepack CR Plus Defibrillator
Checklist. Site coordinators are responsible for ensuring prompt replacement of parts. 366
MDSS/SGSLE will serve as the point of contact for site coordinators and the manufacturer
representatives concerning problems with AEDs. Additionally, 366 MDSS/SGSLE at 828-7777,
is responsible for inspections or maintenance beyond the site coordinator’s ability to accomplish
using the manufacturer owner’s manual. Inspections will take place every 12 months by 366
MDSS/SGSLE.
3. Quality Assurance:
   3.1. Post resuscitation event review. Immediately following an event, the site coordinator or
   designee will conduct a debriefing, and involve key participants in the emergency response.
   The debriefing will review the event and hold a discussion regarding what went well, as well
   as areas requiring improvement. The individual using an AED on a victim will complete and
   forward the AED Event Summary Sheet to the site coordinator and PAD program director
   within 48 hours. The medical director or designee will review all event summary sheets to

                                           Attachment 3
                  AED SITE PROTOCOL FOR EMERGENCY RESPONSE

A3.1. Life-Threatening Cardiac Emergency. In the event of any potentially life-threatening
cardiac emergency:
     A3.1.1. First person on the scene will:
        A3.1.1.1. Assess scene for safety.
        A3.1.1.2. Determine unresponsiveness of victim.
        A3.1.1.3. Activate local Emergency Medical Services (EMS) by calling 911, and
        activate site AED emergency response team.
            A3.1.1.3.1. Perform CPR until AED arrives on scene.
            A3.1.1.3.2. Send a co-worker or bystander to meet the emergency team at the main
            entrance and escort to site.
     A3.1.2. AED response team will:
        A3.1.2.1. Obtain AED and other emergency equipment and proceed directly to the scene.
        A3.1.2.2. AED trained personnel will initiate use of the AED.
     A3.1.3. First AED trained responder will:
        A3.1.3.1. Check for unresponsiveness.
        A3.1.3.2. Call for help/AED and instruct a bystander to call 911.
        A3.1.3.3. Open airway and check for breathing. If there is no breathing or breathing
        appears abnormal, give two slow breaths and start compressions.
        A3.1.3.4. First responder will continue compressions while the AED is turned on and
        pads are placed on victim’s bare chest.
        A3.1.3.5. To use AED, press the lid release/on-off button to open the lid, this turns the
        AED on.
            A3.1.3.5.1. Follow voice prompts to apply electrode pads.
            A3.1.3.5.2. Remove all clothing to expose bare chest.
            A3.1.3.5.3. Shave chest hair, if necessary.
            A3.1.3.5.4. If victim’s chest is wet, wipe dry.
            A3.1.3.5.5. Ensure good placement and contact of the electrodes. Once pads are
            appropriately attached, the AED will automatically analyze the heart rhythm.
        A3.1.3.6. If shock is advised:
            A3.1.3.6.1. Victim must be on a dry surface.
            A3.1.3.6.2. Call out shock is advised, I am clear, you are clear, everyone clear,
            shocking.
MOUNTAINHOMEAFBI41-106 13 JULY 2010                                                       9


         A3.1.3.6.3. Deliver shock by pressing "SHOCK" button when instructed by voice
         prompt.
         A3.1.3.6.4. After first shock is delivered, the AED will prompt you to begin CPR
         starting with chest compressions.
         A3.1.3.6.5. After two minutes, the device will reanalyze checking for shock-able
         rhythm. If shock is indicated follow voice prompts otherwise continue CPR until
         advanced help arrives.
     A3.1.3.7. If no shock advised:
         A3.1.3.7.1. Device will prompt to check victim responsiveness, breathing, and pulse
         again.
         A3.1.3.7.2. If no pulse or breathing, begin CPR for two minutes.
         A3.1.3.7.3. Device will repeat analysis of heart rhythm again after two minutes of
         CPR.
         A3.1.3.7.4. Continue cycles of heart rhythm evaluations, shocks (if advised), and
         CPR until advanced help arrives.
         A3.1.3.7.5. Do not disconnect AED from victim.
  A3.1.4. Additional AED responders shall assist with CPR, recording of data, notifications,
  crowd control, and escorting of EMS, as needed.
  A3.1.5. Site coordinator will:
     A3.1.5.1. Following AED use, the site coordinator or designee will hold a debriefing
     with team members and an event summary sheet will be completed and forwarded to the
     AED program coordinator within 48 hours.
     A3.1.5.2. Clean the AED according to the manufacturer’s instruction manual.
     A3.1.5.3. Re-supply the quick-pak electrode packet and charge-pak battery charger after
     every use.
     A3.1.5.4. Replace used accessory emergency medical supplies.
