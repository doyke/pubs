This supplement implements AFI 36-2903 and AFI 36-2903_AETCSUP. It establishes policies
and procedures specific to Maxwell AFB. Refer recommended changes and/or corrections to
this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication, through your chain of command. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS. This supplement applies to all Air Force personnel (Active Duty,
Guard and Reserve) assigned or attached to Maxwell AFB. Submit any waiver requests to
guidance contained in this supplement to the OPR through the appropriate chain of command.
The use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or
service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This rewrite updates office symbols, reflects unit re-designations, uniform standards to be in line
with higher headquarters guidance and includes renumbering due to AFI restructure.

                                     Attachment 8 (Added)
                         APPROVED NO-HAT/NO-SALUTE AREAS

A8.1. (Added) Designated no-hat/no-salute areas.
     A8.1.1. (Added) For safety considerations, the flight line, CATM range and skeet range are
     no-hat and no-salute areas.
     A8.1.2. (Added) To facilitate mission effectiveness and efficiency, the open-air quadrangles
     within the confines of ACSC, SOC, AFLOA/AFJAGS, and 42 ABW headquarters building
     are no-hat and no-salute areas.
     A8.1.3. (Added) The LeMay Center enclosed connector between Bldgs 1400 and 1400A
     and the Maxwell Clinic courtyard are authorized no-hat and no-salute areas.
     A8.1.4. (Added) The Blue Thunder/Leadership Reaction Course (Project X, Combatives
     area and Cultural Leadership area) facility is a no-salute area during warrior training
     events—hats will be worn.
     A8.1.5. (Added) At Gunter Annex, the patio behind Bldg 1143 is a no-hat and no-salute
     area.
     A8.1.6. (Added) Bldg 205, the area to include the AFLMA picnic area, adjacent to
     southeast wing of Bldg 205, enclosed parking area, and door-to-door building access is a no-
     hat and no-salute area.
     A8.1.7. (Added) The breezeway between Bldg 892 and Bldg 892 auditorium is a no-hat and
     no-salute area.
     A8.1.8. (Added) The Leadership Reaction Course area is a no-salute area during training
     events—hats will be worn.
     A8.1.9. (Added) The breezeway between Bldg 1487 and Bldg 1487A is a no-hat and no-
     salute area.
A8.1. 10 (Added) Bldg 501, Education Center courtyard is a no-hat and no-salute area.
     A8.1.11. (Added) 908 AW: The courtyard area and sidewalks transitioning between
     buildings 1050 and 1052. Aircrew transitioning to and loading into crew transportation
     adjacent to buildings 1050 and 1051.
     A8.1.12. (Added) Maxwell Fire Station #1, Bldg. 1092, the vehicle ramp facing Maxwell
     Blvd. and the picnic tables/patio area on the west side facing the refueling yard.
     A8.1.13. (Added) Maxwell Fire Station #2, Bldg.834 on Gunter Annex, the vehicle ramps
     facing Turner Blvd., and the backside ramp connecting to Computer Loop Road and near
     picnic tables and patio area.
A8.2. (Added) No-hat and/or no-salute areas will be visibly marked and unit commanders will
brief their personnel on the designated areas with particular emphasis on the limits of such an
area. Commandants and commanders seeking additional designated no-hat and/or no-salute
areas may seek approval—in writing—from the installation commander.
