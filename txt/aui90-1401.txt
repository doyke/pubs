This instruction assigns oversight responsibility for actions pertaining to Air University advisory
committees. It implements DODI 5105.4_AFI90-1401, Department of Defense Federal
Advisory Committee Management Program, and applies to all AU centers and schools (including
AFIT), 42 ABW, and Headquarters AU; does not apply to AF Reserve or ANG units. Refer
recommended changes and/or corrections to this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication,
through your chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document (formerly AUI 36-2307, Air University Board of Visitors Actions) has been
substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes are included in
Responsibilities (paragraph 1) and additional revisions are included throughout the instruction.
The number was changed to correctly align the publication with implemented guidance.
1. Definitions. See Attachment 1 for definitions of terms used in this publication.
2. Responsibilities.
   2.1. The Office of the Secretary of Defense, Director, Administration and Management
   (DA&M), is delegated authority (SECDEF letter, dated 22 August 2006) to establish, renew,

     and terminate DoD-supported Federal Advisory Committees.              Consequently, further
     delegation is not authorized at AF, AETC, or AU level.
     2.2. The Air University Commander and President is authorized to establish, renew, and
     terminate advisory committees.
     2.3. The Chief of AU Advisory Boards (HQ AU/CFAB) is responsible to the Air University
     Commander and President (AU/CC) through the Vice President for Academic Affairs
     (AU/CF) for oversight and management of all federal advisory committees (e.g. AU Board of
     Visitors) and for selected advisory committees (e.g. AU Command Board of Advisors).
     2.4. AU Centers, Schools and Organizations are responsible for complying with
     requirements outlined in this instruction.
3. Management and Support of Advisory Committees.
     3.1. HQ AU/CFAB (Chief, AU Advisory Boards):
        3.1.1. Serves as the Designated Federal Officer (DFO) for AU and is appointed by the
        Secretary of the Air Force as the DFO for federal advisory committees (e.g. AU Board of
        Visitors) subject to the provisions of DODI 5105.4_AFI90-1401, DoD Federal Advisory
        Committee Management Program.
            3.1.1.1. Select and obtain training for alternate DFOs, as needed.
        3.1.2. Functions as the primary liaison with the Secretary of the Air Force’s office for
        federal advisory committee matters.
        3.1.3. Interprets DoD issuances, AF publications, U.S. Code, Federal Register and other
        sources regarding advisory committees and provides policy and guidance to Air
        University.
        3.1.4. Ensures compliance of all advisory committees with DODI 5105.4_AFI90-1401,
        DoD Federal Advisory Committee Management Program.
        3.1.5. Responds, on behalf of AU, to all requests for advisory committee information by
        the Secretary of the Air Force office (SAF).
        3.1.6. Serves as AU’s database administrator for the Federal Advisory Committee Act
        (FACA) database and ensures all data is accurate and up-to-date.
        3.1.7. Serves as the security officer and ensures compliance with OSD memorandum,
        DOD Policy Changes – Handling National Security Information.
        3.1.8. Coordinates and prepares the annual outbrief to the Secretary of the Air Force and
        any other related meetings or outbriefs for federal advisory committees.
        3.1.9. Manages, coordinates, and executes the activities of other advisory committees
        determined not applicable to the provisions in DODI 5105.4_AFI90-1401, DoD Federal
        Advisory Committee Management Program, but reports directly to the AU Commander
        and President (e.g. AU Command Board of Advisors).
        3.1.10. Monitors activities of all other advisory committees that meet throughout AU.

  3.2. HQ AU/CCP (Director of Protocol):
     3.2.1. Assigns an experienced Protocol Officer responsible for, but not limited to, the
     following tasks to support advisory committee meetings at the AU Commander and
     President level:
         3.2.1.1. Lodging
         3.2.1.2. Meals and meeting refreshments
         3.2.1.3. Nametags and meeting place cards
         3.2.1.4. Welcome letters and baskets/bags
         3.2.1.5. AU mementoes
         3.2.1.6. Payment of meals and lodging
  3.3. HQ AU/PA (Director of Public Affairs): Prepares base newspaper articles related to
  advisory committee membership, meetings, and other committee-related activities.
  3.4. HQ AU/JA (Staff Judge Advocate):
     3.4.1. Provides appropriate notices and instructions related to the requirement of each
     federal advisory committee member to submit OGE Form 450, “Executive Branch
     Confidential Financial Disclosure Report” (prescribed by 5 CFR, “Administrative
     Personnel”, Part 2634, “Executive Branch Financial Disclosure, Qualified Trusts, and
     Certificates of Divestiture”, Subpart I, “Confidential Financial Disclosure Reports”).
     3.4.2. Reviews OGE Forms 450 and provides counseling and or interpretation, as
     necessary, to federal advisory committee members.
     3.4.3. Maintains completed OGE Forms 450.
     3.4.4. Provides initial and annual ethics training for federal advisory committee
     members.
  3.5. HQ AU/FM (Financial Management): Prepares and presents, when necessary, an
  annual financial report to the federal advisory committee.
  3.6. HQ AU/CCX (Commander’s Action Group): Ensures the setup and operation of the
  audiovisual requirements for the federal advisory committee meetings.
  3.7. AU Centers, Schools, and Organizations:
     3.7.1. Submit to AU/CFAB proposed meeting agenda topics, information, briefings, and
     other-related activities for the selected committee meetings (e.g. Board of Visitors and
     Command Board of Advisors).
     3.7.2. Ensure that no advisory committee shall meet or take any official action (to
     include expenditure of funds) until the committee’s charter and membership are reviewed
     by AU/CFAB. This only applies to advisory committees which include members (or
     regular attendees) not employed by The Air University or the federal government.

Terms
Federal Advisory Committee— A Federal Advisory Committee is defined to mean any
committee, subcommittee, board, commission, council, conference, panel, task force, or other
similar group which is established to obtain advice or recommendations and where at least one
non-federal member participates (e.g. AU Board of Visitors).

Advisory Committee— An advisory committee is defined to mean any committee,
subcommittee, board, commission, council, conference, panel, task force, or other similar group
which is composed wholly of full-time, or permanent part-time, officers or employees of the
Federal Government (e.g. AU Command Board of Advisors).
