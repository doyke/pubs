The purpose of this instruction is to put in place a mechanism for which occupational safety and
health directives or advisories can be issued from the Center Chief of Safety to the ALC Wings,
Directorates and Tenant organizations. Directives (Robins AFB Form 111) and Advisories
(Robins AFB Form 110) will be used when OIs, Process Orders, or tech data cannot be
accomplished in a timely manner to meet mission need. This publication applies to all
employees at Robins AFB including Tenant organizations. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force
(AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field
through the appropriate functional chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual
(AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. See Attachment 1 for a
glossary of references and supporting information.

                                           Chapter 1

                                     RESPONSIBILITIES

1.1. Center Chief of Safety (COS). Approval authority for implementation and cancellation of
directives and advisories.
1.2. Center Occupational Safety and Health Office (WR-ALC/SH). Review and or develop
directives and advisories that are necessary to provide a safe and healthy work environment for
base employees. Office with primary responsibility for initiating directives or advisories and for
initiating or cancelling directives and advisories. Assist COS in adjudication process for
resolving directive or advisory issues.
1.3. Center EN. Serves as steering advisor for safety and health directives and advisories.
Assist COS in adjudication process for resolving directive or advisory issues.
1.4. Wings. Responsible for reviewing and implementing directives and advisories or issuing a
reclama.
1.5. Directorates. Responsible for reviewing and implementing directives and advisories or
issuing a reclama.
1.6. Tenants. Responsible for reviewing and implementing directives and advisories or issuing
a reclama.

                                           Chapter 2

                                       INSTRUCTION

2.1. Draft directives (Robins AFB Form 111) and advisories (Robins AFB Form 110) will
be vetted through the WR-ALC/SH prior to initiating the coordination process through
EN, Wings, Directorates, and Tenant organizations. Draft directives (Robins AFB Form 111)
and advisories (Robins AFB Form 110) will be sent from WR-ALC/SH to EN for review and
coordination prior to COS approval. Upon COS approval, COS will initiate a seven (7) day
suspense to Wings, Directorates and Tenant organizations for coordination. At the end of seven
days, COS will arbitrate reclamas within a three (3) day adjudication period with assistance from
WR-ALC/SH and EN. Those issues that cannot be adjudicated will be boarded by WR-
ALC/CC for resolution. See attachment 1, Flow process.
2.2. Robins AFB Form 111, WR-ALC Safety and Health Directive, and Robins AFB Form
110, WR-ALC/Safety and Health Advisory, will be used when OIs, Process Orders, or tech data
cannot be accomplished in a timely manner to meet mission need.
