Air Force Instruction (AFI) 10-244, 12 September 2005, and AFI 10-244_Air Force Global
Strike Command (AFGSC) Supplement, 01 July 2010, is supplemented as follows. This
supplement provides policy, guidance and procedures to all Aerospace Expeditionary Forces
(AEF) tasked units to report status to execute their AEF taskings. It applies to all persons
reporting AEF status at Whiteman Air Force Base (AFB). Refer recommended changes
regarding this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR), using AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual
(AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.

3.9. (Added) Ensure unit AEF Reporting Tool (ART monitors have Secret Internet Protocol
Router Network (SIPRNET) access.
2.6.6. (Added) 509th Bomb Wing (509 BW) ART Point of Contact (POC) will:
2.6.6.1. (Added) Collect and compile unit ART status for the 509th Bomb Wing Commander
(509 BW/CC) and key staff. This information will be provided to the 509 BW/CC at least
monthly.
2.6.6.2. (Added) Monitor the Whiteman AFB ART database as well as the ART classified
email account daily for changes, additions and deletions.

2.6.6.3. (Added) Immediately inform unit ART POCs of any changes, additions and deletions.
Unit ART POCs will be notified monthly via email or when ART updates are due.
2.6.6.4. (Added)     Periodically review     the    AEF   SIPRNET     Web     site   located   at
https://aef.afpc.randolph.af.smil.mil.
2.6.6.5. (Added) The 509 BW ART POC will train all ART POCs on reporting requirements
located in AFI 10-244_AFGSCSUP and this supplement coupled with ART hands-on training.
ART training will be documented in the 509 BW ART POC continuity book.
2.6.7. (Added) Group/unit ART POCs will.
2.6.7.1. (Added) Brief the status of unit Unit Type Code (UTCs) to their commander every 30
days and when notified of any change in UTC status.
2.6.7.2. (Added) Ensure a current copy of the signed UTC or commander’s approval report is
on file in a secure area. Signed UTC reports will be kept on file in a secure area for 6 months.
2.6.7.3. (Added) Update the ART database with current information:
2.6.7.3.1. (Added) Within 28 days of the last update.
2.6.7.3.2. (Added) Upon notification by the wing ART POC.
2.6.7.3.3. (Added) When the status of a UTC has changed to avoid shortfalls/reclamas.
2.6.7.4. (Added) Respond to all tasks required by the 509 BW ART POC.
2.6.7.5. (Added) Resolve all UTC discrepancies with ART, the AEF Time-phased Forced
Deployment Date (TPFDD) library, UTC Availability, and DOC statements through their
command-level functional manager(s); keep 509 BW ART POCs informed of the status of their
discrepancies.
2.6.7.6. (Added)     Periodically review      the   AEF    SIPRNET       Website     located   at
https://aef.afpc.randolph.af.smil.mil.
2.6.7.7. (Added) Ensure the data in the remarks column clearly and accurately reflect the
deficient UTC status.
3.2.1.4.1. (Added) Distribution and Reporting Requirements. Individual unit ART printouts
may be used to collect and update data in ART. All complete printouts are considered classified
and must be handled accordingly.
3.8.4. (Added) The 509 BW ART POC will collect and compile ART status and present that
information to the 509 BW/CC and staff at least monthly.
