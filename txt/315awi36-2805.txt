This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive 36-28, Awards and Decorations. It
provides guidance for the 315th Airlift Wing Commander’s Coin of Excellence Program and
defines how coins are procured, awarded and tracked. This instruction applies to all members of
the 315 AW including civilians. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF IMT Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms IMT 847 from the field through
Major Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-
123 (will convert to AFMAN 33-363), Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance
with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.mil. The
use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or
service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.


1. Program Guidance:
   1.1. The purpose of the wing commander’s coin is to provide the 315th Airlift Wing
   Commander with an informal means to recognize the highest standards of excellence.
       1.1.1. The Coin of Excellence will be presented by the 315th Airlift Wing Commander
       or a designee to military and/or civilian personnel who have achieved the highest
       standards of mission excellence.

     1.2. The numbered Historical Coin is presented when recognizing achievements which have
     impacted the wing to such an extent as to be recorded in the wing history and may be
     presented to military and/or civilian personnel.
        1.2.1. Individuals can be nominated to receive the historical coin by anyone within the
        wing.
        1.2.2. Nominations can be sent via email or dropped off in the Public Affairs or Wing
        Staff office and will include nominee’s name and significant accomplishment(s) to be
        considered.
        1.2.3. A panel to include the Wing Commander, Vice Wing Commander, Command
        Chief, and member of the Wing Staff will vote on whether the achievement(s) should be
        included in the wing’s history and an historical coin presented to the nominee.
2. Procurement:
     2.1. Coins will be procured in accordance with standard contracting and funding guidance.
     2.2. Coins will be ordered when required and in a quantity to ensure sufficient availability.
3. Presentation:
     3.1. The 315th Airlift Wing Commander Coin of Excellence may be presented at any time
     by the 315th Airlift Wing Commander or Vice Commander on his behalf.
     3.2. The Historical Coin will be presented by the 315th Airlift Wing Commander or the Vice
     Commander or Command Chief on his behalf.
4. Documenting Coin Awards:
     4.1. The office of the 315th Airlift Wing Commander will secure, track and administer
     presentation and documentation of the Commander’s coin and the Historical coin.
     4.2. Coin inventory will be tracked by spreadsheet to include:
        4.2.1. Name and rank (or civilian grade) of individual.
        4.2.2. Unit of Assignment
        4.2.3. Date received.
        4.2.4. Name and rank of presenter.
        4.2.5. The coin number for numbered historical coin.
        4.2.6. For numbered historical coin, brief summary of accomplishment(s).
