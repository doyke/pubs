This publication implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 10-1, Mission Directives. It
delineates the mission, command relationships, and responsibilities of the Air Force Targeting
Center (AFTC), a field operating agency of the Headquarters, Air Combat Command, Director of
Intelligence (ACC/A2). It applies to ACC as well as Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and
Air National Guard (ANG) units. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of IAW the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS)
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS). Contact supporting records managers as required. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication;
route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. This
publication may be supplemented at any level, but all direct Supplements must be routed to the
OPR of this publication prior to certification and approval.

1. Mission. Provide cross-domain targeting and Geospatial Intelligence (GEOINT) products
and services, expertise, and advocacy to Air Force and Joint Warfighters.
2. Command. The Commander, AFTC:
   2.1. Is responsible to the ACC/A2 for administration, training, and readiness of assigned
   forces.
   2.2. Commands assigned and attached forces across the distributed AFTC enterprise.
3. Responsibilities. The Commander, AFTC:
   3.1. Organizes, trains, and equips assigned forces to accomplish the AFTC mission.

    3.2. Provides global reachback targeting and GEOINT products and services for the Air
    Force.
       3.2.1. Provides reachback support to Air Force operational elements via access to
       targeting and geospatial systems and databases.
       3.2.2. Provides immediate and dedicated targeting and GEOINT across the range of
       military operations to the supporting/supported air components and their air operations
       centers.
       3.2.3. Provides target development; target planning; target analysis; target materials
       production; weaponeering; battle damage assessment; collateral damage assessment;
       limited integration of kinetic and non-kinetic capabilities planning; and GEOINT
       production, imagery products, and foundational data.
       3.2.4. Ensures readiness of Combat Air Force unit intelligence through Unit
       Support/Staff Assistance Visit, Visual Recognition, Force Protection, and Threat
       Analysis programs.
    3.3. Communicates with Air Force organizations at all levels, other Services, combat support
    agencies, combatant commands, Joint Staff, and the Intelligence Community on operational
    matters of direct interest to the AFTC’s mission areas and responsibilities.
    3.4. Implements the Air Force Precise Point Positioning Program and Collateral Damage
    Estimation training and certification programs in accordance with Chairman Joint Chiefs of
    Staff and Headquarters Air Force guidance.
    3.5. Manages targeting and GEOINT services and production taskings for AFTC associated
    Air National Guard and Air Force Reserve Command intelligence squadrons.
    3.6. Maintains an appropriate staff to plan, program, budget, support, maintain, and execute
    FOA resources and requirements within the AFTC.
