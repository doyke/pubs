AFI 90-1301, 1 April 2008, is supplemented as follows:
This supplement implements AFPD 90-13, Military Flight Operations Quality Assurance, 28 Mar 2008,
and AFI 90-1301, Implementing Military Flight Operations Quality Assurance, 1 Apr 2008. It expands
on AFI 90-1301 by providing guidance and procedures for AFMC personnel who participate in the
MFOQA processes. This supplement applies to all aircraft/air vehicle program offices within AFMC.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Route AF
Form 847s from the field through the appropriate functionalâ€™s chain of command. Ensure that all

Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located in Air Force Records Information Management System

3.2.1.1.1. (Added) All aircraft/air vehicle developmental and sustainment program offices identify
Military Flight Operations Quality Assurance (MFOQA) and Aircraft Information Program (AIP) focal
points to AFMC/A3O/ENS in October on an annual basis. All program offices provide names of
MFOQA and AIP focal points within 30 days of this Supplement publication and focal point changes.
3.2.1.1.2. (Added) All aircraft/air vehicle program offices identify Aircraft Information Working Group
(AIWG) and Aircraft Information Management Plan (AIMP) status to AFMC/ENS in October on an
annual basis and establish an AIWG for every Air Force aircraft weapons system to include both fixed
wing and rotary wing systems; whether manned or unmanned. The AIWG is responsible to the program
manager via the AIWG chairperson. All program offices provide status of AIP activities to AFMC/ENS
within 60 days of this Supplement publication; this will provide a baseline to measure MFOQA and AIP
progress.

3.2.1.2.All aircraft/air vehicle program offices, in October on an annual basis, provide to AFMC/ENS a
schedule of aircraft modifications planned in the future that might impact existing MFOQA.
3.2.3.Risk mitigation measures shall be identified to the AIWG. Mitigation measures shall be tracked
by the AIWGs and/or System Safety Groups (SSGs).
3.2.4.Use MIL-STD-882D order of precedence. Uses of lower orders of precedence in MIL-STD-882
will be coordinated with the AIWGs, SSGs, and the Lead Command and documented in the AIMP.
Also, follow risk acceptance guidance in the AFI 91-202_AFMCSUP, The U.S. Air Force Mishap
Prevention Program.
3.4.2.HQ AFMC MFOQA focal point shall develop waivers using the cost benefit analysis (CBA) on
AFMC lead aircraft per AFPD 10-9.
3.4.12.AFMC MFOQA Monitoring Team (MMT) shall be comprised of members from operations,
training, maintenance, safety, the Aeronautical System Wings responsible for AFMC aircraft
development, aircraft in sustainment supported by Air Logistics Centers (ALCs), and others as deemed
necessary by AFMC. The goal is to meet annually via teleconference or video teleconference. If all
AFMC aircraft are waived, MMT will not meet.
3.4.12.1. (Added) AFMC MMT shall track status of waiver request(s), monitor status of identified risks
from AIWGs and/or SSGs, and communicate essential MFOQA identified trends/hazards to AFMC
leadership and field personnel.
3.5.2.1.AFMC MFOQA Steering Committee shall include representatives from operations, training,
maintenance, requirements and safety. The goal is to meet annually via teleconference or video
teleconference. If all AFMC aircraft are waived, AFMC MFOQA Steering Committee will not meet.
