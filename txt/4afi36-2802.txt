This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards and Decorations
Program. This instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-2803, The Air
Force Awards and Decorations Program. It establishes policies and procedures for awarding the
CMSgt James K. Clouse Trophy to the Fourth Air Force maintenance organization
demonstrating the most notable sustained superior performance for the year. It applies to all
Fourth Air Force Aircraft Maintenance organizations. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air
Force Form (AF Form) 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s
from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rds

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication has been substantially revised and must be reviewed in its entirety. Units that
want to be considered for the CMSgt James K. Clouse Trophy must submit a formal package to
HQ 4 AF/A4.
1. Purpose or Objective: Maintenance units represent a large segment of the Air Force
Reserve, providing vital support to the operational mission often under exceptionally adverse
conditions. The CMSgt James K. Clouse Trophy is presented to recognize excellence among
maintenance organizations within Fourth Air Force.

2. Award Period: Award period is 1 October through 30 September.
3. Submission: Units must submit nomination packages no later than 31 October to 4 AF/A4M.
4. Eligibility: All maintenance units assigned to Fourth Air Force.
5. Selection Criteria: Units must submit a formal package using an AF Form 1206,Nomination
for Award Formal package will include the five nomination categories outlined paragraph 6.
     5.1. Only the front page of the AF Form 1206 will be utilized and submitted.
     5.2. Use capital letters for the nomination category headings in the specific accomplishment
     block of the AF Form 1206.
     5.3. Use bullet statements. Each major bullet will consist of no more than five lines each
     (including sub-bullets). Single space the entire bulleted portion with no blank lines between
     major bullets and no blank lines between category headings.
     5.4. Write bullet statements in a style that describes a particular action, its result, and finally,
     its impact to the unit, Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC), Air Force, or beyond.
6. Nomination Categories:
     6.1. Mission Accomplishment: A description of mission accomplishments in bullet format
     for the competition period. Accomplishments shall stress maintenance efforts and the
     impacts of those efforts on the unit’s, or on any supported units, operational capability and
     mission accomplishment. Examples of areas that may be included are:
         6.1.1. Accomplishment of unit mission requirements.
         6.1.2. Weapon system or equipment materiel readiness status.
         6.1.3. Operational deployment participations and successes.
         6.1.4. Local or higher headquarters exercise involvement and successes.
         6.1.5. Special programs such as time compliance and technical order accomplishment.
     6.2. Innovations: Innovative actions taken by the unit that have significantly improved unit,
     Reserve or Air Force mission performance. An example that may be included is:
         6.2.1. Unit reorganized its entire mobility tasking plan using newly acquired computers.
             6.2.1.1. Plan was adopted command wide.
     6.3. Evaluations: Describe performance on local and higher headquarters evaluations. An
     example that may be included is:
         6.3.1. Received an “Outstanding ” score during HQ AMC ORI, June 2010.
     6.4. Awards Participation: Describe unit participation in Maintenance Award programs
     during the rating period. An example that may be included is:
         6.4.1. Eight maintenance awards were submitted to higher headquarters.
         6.4.2. Unit MXS Engine Shop was recognized as the AFRC “Maintenance Section of the
         Year” for 2009. Examples that may be included are:

   6.5. Safety: Describe innovative safety programs implemented and list the number of
   mishaps and lost time injuries incurred during the rating period.
       6.5.1. There were no lost duty hours in Fiscal Year 2010.
       6.5.2. Instituted a new AF Form 55 process that reduces errors by 25 percent.
7. Selection Procedure: The selection will be accomplished as follows:
   7.1. The A4 Director will appoint a chairperson who, in turn, will assemble a quorum of
   staff members (at least 6 people) from the Fourth Air Force Logistics Division for the award
   panel.
   7.2. The panel may convene during the November or December Unit Training Assembly
   (UTA). Deliberations will be in closed session.
   7.3. The chairperson will notify the A4 Director of the selection.
   7.4. The panel may decline to award this trophy.
8. Awarding the Trophy: Formal announcement will be made before 31 December.
   8.1. The A4 Director will notify the Fourth Air Force Public Affairs Officer of the selection.
   8.2. The Public Affairs Officer will ensure the trophy presentation receives the appropriate
   publicity.
   8.3. The Fourth Air Force Commander or A4 Director will present the trophy at the
   Raincross conference or an appropriate ceremony.
9. Trophy Display and Care: Fourth Air Force Logistics Division will display and maintain
the perpetual trophy engraved with the name of the awarded unit. The selected unit will also be
awarded an engraved trophy for permanent display at their unit.
   9.1. Prescribed Forms. None
   9.2. Adopted Forms. AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication
