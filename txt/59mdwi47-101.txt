This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive 47-1, Dental Services. This instruction
describes the proper completion and submission of Wilford Hall Medical Center (WHMC)
Forms: WHMC Form 3205, Lackland Dental Laboratory Services Quality Control; WHMC
IMT 5003, Prosthodontics Treatment Plan; WHMC IMT 5020, Patient Questionnaire – Adult;
WHMC IMT 5021, Orthodontic Clinical Exam; WHMC IMT 5022, Patient Progress Log;
WHMC IMT 5023, Patient Questionnaire-Child/Adolescent; WHMC IMT 5035, Oral and
Maxillofacial Surgery APV. This instruction applies to all personnel assigned, attached or under
contract to the 59th Medical Wing. This instruction does not apply to the Air National Guard or
Air Force Reserve. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
office of primary responsibility using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System Records
Disposition Schedule located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Added WHMC Form 143, 50% or Less N20 Minimal Sedation Clinical Record.
1. Form Overview WHMC Form 3205, Lackland Dental Laboratory Services Quality
Control, is used for technician/dentist comments on the quality/timeliness of products
received/fabricated in the dental laboratory. It indicates materials used to fabricate
appliances/prostheses and porcelain firing temperatures (if indicated). The top portion of the
form is filled out by the noncommissioned officer in charge (NCOIC) of the dental laboratory.

The individual technicians fill out pertinent information on materials and firing temperatures.
Technicians/section heads initial the back of the form to indicate who accomplished specific
procedures. When the case is received in the dental laboratory the form is filled out and placed
in the case pan. The form accompanies the case through completion and insertion/delivery and
then returned to the dental laboratory. The forms are reviewed by the dental laboratory NCOIC.
A quality control report is filled out based on the information received on the forms. The
information received is used to drive training/continuing education.
2. Form Overview WHMC IMT 5003, Prosthodontics Treatment Plan, is used to record
patient findings and document sequential plan of treatment. The form is completed by the
dental provider and placed in the patient’s record.
3. Form Overview WHMC IMT 5020, Patient Questionnaire – Adult, is used to record
initial adult patient interview prior to consideration for orthodontic treatment. The patient
completes the form and gives it to the provider/resident for diagnostic workup. It acts as
worksheet for definitive record documentation then is shredded.
4. Form Overview WHMC IMT 5021, Orthodontic Clinical Exam, is used to record patient
findings as a result of orthodontic exam. WHMC IMT 5022, Patient Progress Log is used to
record patient progress toward completion of orthodontic treatment. These forms are completed
by the provider. They act as a worksheet for definitive record documentation then are shredded.
5. Form Overview WHMC IMT 5023, Patient Questionnaire-Child/Adolescent, is used to
record initial interview for child/adolescent patient prior to consideration for orthodontic
treatment. The form is completed by the parent or legal guardian and given to the
provider/resident for diagnostic workup.       It acts as worksheet for definitive record
documentation then is shredded.
6. Form Overview WHMC IMT 5035, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery APV, is used to
record patient findings, history/physical, and treatment plan for patients experiencing less
than 23 hour stay. It is completed by the provider and placed in the patient’s record.
7. Form Overview WHMC Form 143, 50% or Less N20 Minimal Sedation Clinical Record,
is used to record clinical pediatric/adult N20-02 minimal sedation. The form is completed by
the provider and placed in the patient record.
