This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 23-1, Material Management and
provides guidance on the responsibilities and procedures relative to property disposal with the
Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) Disposition Services. This instruction is applicable to all units
assigned or attached to Whiteman Air Force Base (AFB). Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Changes made to paragraphs 2.1.3 and 2.1.4
1. Scope. As of 14 March 2006, DLA Disposition Services transferred all its DLA Disposition
Services storage responsibility to the Defense Reutilization and Marketing Office (DRMO) at
Fort Riley, Kansas. DLA Disposition Services will have a representative assigned to Whiteman
AFB that will service Whiteman AFB and several other locations.
2. Purpose. This instruction defines Whiteman AFB receipt-in-place procedures for the turn-in
of equipment, supply account items and other surplus items to DLA Disposition Services.
   2.1. Points of Contact (POC).
       2.1.1. DLA Disposition Services Representative at Whiteman AFB, Bldg 127, 687-5595.

3. DLA Disposition Services Representative Responsibilities.
     3.1. Will service all Whiteman AFB customers weekly on Monday and Friday from 0700-
     1330.
     3.2. Arrange pre-inspection on-site appointments with units requesting surplus item
     disposition upon request. This appointment will be used to make resale, disposal and scrap
     determination and to give guidance on proper shipping methods for property to be shipped to
     DLA Disposition Services.
     3.3. Brief generating unit custodians on their responsibilities for safe and secure storage for
     items received in-place. Inform custodians of any status change or disposition decisions on
     all items received in-place at the unit.
     3.4. All unserviceable tires (automotive and aircraft) will be delivered to the DLA
     Disposition Services receiving area for processing and placement in the tire container
     maintained by DLA Disposition Services.
4. Unit Responsibilities.
     4.1. Schedule pre-inspection appointment with DLA Disposition Services Representative for
     initial disposition determination. Units must provide escort for DLA Disposition Services
     personnel if items to be viewed are located within a restricted area.
     4.2. The generating unit will complete a DD Form 1348-1A, Issue Release/Receipt
     Document, to turn in surplus non-EAID property to include furniture and tires.
     4.3. Provide safe and secure storage for items to be received and sold in-place at the unit and
     maintain oversight of DLA Disposition Services items received in-place until picked up or
     DLA Disposition Services Representative provides further disposition instructions.
     4.4. Will ensure all specialized cleaning, draining and purging, drying and perseveration of
     items is accomplished prior to turn-in for shipment. Items that have been drained and purged
     require an AFTO Form 20, Caution And Inspection Record, indicating the type of purging
     agent used. All items will be placed on wooden skids, shrink wrap will be used in cases were
     banding alone does not properly secure the load. All packing materials, banding, wooden
     skids and plastic wrap will be provided by the customer. Assistance will be provided by
     509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Outbound Shipping Non-commissioned Officer in
     Charge (NCOIC) upon request.
     4.5. For proper procedures for filling out the DD Form 1348-1A, use the following link
     http://www.dispositionservices.dla.mil/etid.html.

   4.6. When cargo is identified as hazardous material, a completed material safety data sheet
   must accompany the cargo. Hazardous material will not be shipped to DLA Disposition
   Services.
   4.7. Initiate and complete investigative/report of survey procedures for missing DLA
   Disposition Services-accountable items received in-place at the unit.
   4.8. 509 CS will manage and be responsible for all ADPE equipment that has been properly
   received and processed as excess to be turned into DLA Disposition Services.
5. 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Responsibilities.
   5.1. 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Deployment and Distribution Flight (509
   LRS/LGRD).
      5.1.1. General cargo shipments will be accepted Monday and Friday, from 1000-1400.
      Cargo movement will transport palletized cargo to the DLA Disposition Services facility
      from building 139, cargo movement warehouse. Note: During wing down days,
      holidays, weekends, exercises and mobility contingencies, shipments will not be accepted
      and processed by the cargo movement section until the first available duty day.
      5.1.2. All locally prepared DD Forms 1348-1A will include a complete forwarding
      (shipped to) address: SZ3586, DLA Disposition Services Riley, ATTN: MEO, P.O. Box
      2490, Fort Riley, KS, 66442-2490. All certifications for special processing must
      accompany the DD Form 1348-1A when property is shipped.
      5.1.3. All DD Form 1149, Requisition and Invoice/Shipping Document, will be the
      automated version. Cargo movement will not accept handwritten forms. Cargo
      movement will assist in completion of the registration process if needed.
      5.1.4. Due to limited warehouse space, large quantities and bulk items will be
      coordinated through the NCOIC of packing and crating (687-2456/687-1316) prior to
      processing the shipment. In some instances, large amounts of cargo or extremely bulky
      items may have to remain in the owner/users' facility areas until proper shipment
      clearances have been made.
   5.2. 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Materiel Management Flight (509 LRS/LGRM).
      5.2.1. Unserviceable due-in from maintenance items will be picked up and processed by
      the 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Flight Service Center and then processed
      through the DLA Disposition Services Representative for the document to be scanned
      prior to turning the items over to 509 LRS/LGRD.
      5.2.2. Turn-ins of Equipment Authorized In-Use Detail (EAID) items must be processed
      through 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Customer Support to clear equipment detail
      records then processed through 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Central Receiving.
      5.2.3. All standard base supply system-produced DD Forms 1348-1A will be signed for
      in Supply Asset Tracking System (SATS) by the DLA Disposition Services
      Representative.
   5.3. 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Vehicle Management (509 LRS/LGRV).

        5.3.1. Vehicle turn-ins will be processed from vehicle management and analysis directly
        to the DLA Disposition Services Representative. The vehicles will be receipted in place
        and maintained in the excess vehicles storage area.
     5.4. 509 LRS/LGRM and 509th Logistics Readiness Squadron Individual Protective

        5.4.1. DLA Disposition Services will establish a receipt-in-place memorandum of

6. Withdrawal. All property withdrawals from DLA Disposition Services (regardless of
condition) must be for a valid, official reason. All requests must go through 509 LRS Customer
Support. If the requirement is for an emergency or Mission Capability (MICAP) requirement,
contact 509 LRS Customer Support for assistance.

Terms
Demilitarization— The act of destroying the military offensive or defensive advantages
inherent in certain types of equipment or material.
Equipment Authorized In-Use Detail— Items accountable in the SBSS and maintained on
custodian authorization/custody receipt listing.
Non-Equipment Authorized In-Use Details— Equipment items that are not accounted for in
the standard base supply system (SBSS), real property and other equipment management code 1
items.
Receipt-In-Place-DLA Disposition Services— Accepts accountability for turned in property
while generating organizations/units retain responsibility for safe and secure storage until sold or
disposed of by DLA Disposition Services Representative.
Scrap-Non-DEMIL— Items determined not to have resale value. Generating organizations will
coordinate with the local DLA Disposition Services Representative to get guidance on
classification of scrap.
