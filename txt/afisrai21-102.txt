This instruction implements AFPD 21-1, Air and Space Maintenance, and provides guidance to National
Air and Space Intelligence Center (NASIC) functions managing contractor and equipment support; con-
ducting Research and Development (R&D) of communications equipment; and conducting and support-
ing foreign materiels research, engineering, and data exploitation projects. It applies to NASIC activities
where AFSC 2AXXX, 2EXXX, and 2WXXX personnel are assigned. Maintain records created as a result
of prescribed processes IAW AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and dispose of them IAW the AF
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) at https://AFRIMS.amc.af.mil. Contact supporting records man-
agers as required.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Tool management duties have
been expanded. NASIC/MSFL responsibilities have been deleted from this publication. Uninterruptible
Power Supply (UPS) requirements established.


1. Introduction. Personnel are assigned to directly support NASIC missions by utilizing general aircraft,
avionics, supply, and communications/electronics maintenance skills and practices to conduct and support
R&D and foreign materials exploitation (FME) projects. They also monitor contract maintenance and
provide equipment for support communications and intelligence production systems, while providing
specialized supply support to NASIC activities. In order to perform these various functions, NASIC uti-
lizes personnel in AFSC 2AXXX, 2EXXX, 2WXXX, government civilian employees, and contractor per-
sonnel. Directorate of Logistics, Installations, and Mission Support (AFISRA/A4/7) will perform
functional assistance visits as needed or as requested to review NASIC logistics support programs.

2. AFISRA/A4/7 Responsibilities.
    2.1. AFISRA/A4/7 will:
       2.1.1. Perform functional management oversight for logistics support personnel.
       2.1.2. Conduct functional and staff assistance visits to NASIC when requested or as needed.
    2.2. AFISRA/A4M will:
       2.2.1. Provide appropriate guidance and assistance to logistics support activities.
       2.2.2. Develop inspection criteria and provide them to the AF ISR Agency Inspector General (IG)
       and NASIC.
    2.3. AFISRA/A4MY will:
       2.3.1. Be the focal point for Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) requirements. (AFI 32-1063,
       paragraph 2, Electrical Power Systems, 10 Jun 2005 and AFI 65-601, Volume 1, paragraph 47,
       Budget Guidance and Procedures, 3 March 2005)
       2.3.2. Coordinate requirements with the Power Conditioning and Continuation Equipment
       (PCCIE) office at Hill AFB, Utah to procure UPS sized at 125 kVa and larger via existing Ogden
       Air Logistics Center (OO-ALC) contract.
       2.3.3. Coordinate requirements with the PCCIE office to procure UPS sized larger than 1 kVa and
       less than 125 kVa via GSA schedule.
       2.3.4. Coordinate requirements with the PCCIE office for UPS Emergency/Preventative Mainte-
       nance via existing OO-ALC contract.

3. NASIC Responsibilities.
    3.1. NASIC will:
       3.1.1. Establish a technical order distribution office.
       3.1.2. Appoint a unit Test Measurement Diagnostic Equipment (TMDE) coordinator to oversee
       TMDE sub accounts.
       3.1.3. Deploy a data management system to satisfy internal NASIC historical and trend analysis
       requirements. The data management system will also be used for prioritizing, scheduling and
       tracking contract maintenance.
       3.1.4. May coordinate UPS requirements directly with the PCCIE office at Hill AFB Utah for
       emergency/preventative maintenance or to procure UPS via OO-ALC contract or GSA schedule.
       Wings must courtesy copy AFISRA/A4MY when coordinating requirements directly with the
       PCCIE office.
