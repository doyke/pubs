This DOVERAFBI is hereby entered into by the 436th Airlift Wing (AW), 512th Airlift Wing
(AW), Air Force Mortuary Affairs Operations (AFMAO), Armed Forces Medical Examiner
System (AFMES) and Joint Personal Effects Depot (JPED), Dover AFB, DE. In keeping with
AFMAO’s mission of fulfilling our nation’s sacred commitment of ensuring dignity, honor, and
respect to our Fallen and care, service, and support for their families, a solemn Dignified
Transfer (DT) is conducted from the aircraft to AFMES to honor those who have given their
lives in the service of our country. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS). Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air
Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

2. Dignified Transfer Host (DTH).
     2.1. Will be an O-6 from the 436 AW, 512 AW, AFMAO, or AFMES.
     2.2. Briefs Senior Service official(s) on the DT process.
     2.3. Presides over the DT Official Party.
     2.4. Will attend DT training conducted by AFMAO prior to serving as a DTH.
3. Reverse Dignified Transfer Hosts (RDTH)
     3.1. Will be a Military Deputy Group Commander (or equivalent), the JPED Commander, or
     a Squadron Commander, either O4 or O5, from the 436 AW or AFMAO.
     3.2. Presides over the RDT Official Party.
     3.3. Will attend RDT training conducted by AFMAO prior to serving as a RDTH.
4. Schedule for DTH/RDTH. DTHs will be scheduled from Friday at 1200L lasting through
Monday at 2359L. The next session begins Tuesday at 0001L and ends on Friday at 1159L.
There are a total of 104 sessions per year. The RDTH schedule will begin Friday at 0001L and
last until Thursday at 2359L. There are a total of 52 RDTH sessions per year. RDTHs will be
scheduled 6 months in advance. The individual within each organization is responsible for
meeting their scheduled session. If he/she cannot meet their scheduled session, he/she will
coordinate a replacement NLT 48 hours prior to the start of the duty period. The individual
originally tasked with the duty period will notify the 436 AW/CCS at DSN 445-4360 as soon as
the replacement is identified, but not later than 48 hours prior to the start of the duty period.
Overall, the owning commander for each organization is responsible for the scheduled sessions
within their organization and will ensure each shift is covered by a fully trained host meeting the
grade/position requirements listed above. The number of sessions for DTHs and RDTHs
assigned to each organization will be based on the following:
     4.1. DTHs – Number of authorized O-6s for each named organization.
     4.2. RDTHs –Number of authorized Deputy Group Commander (or equivalent), the JPED
     Commander, or Squadron Commanders O-4 or O-5 from the 436 AW and AFMAO.
     4.3. DTHs who do not live in the local area (within 35 miles of DAFB) will be scheduled for
     the Tuesday – Friday duty period only except with prior coordination with the member and
     his/her commander.
