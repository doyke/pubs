This instruction implements AFPD 36-30, Military Entitlements, and DODI 1300.18.,
Department Of Defense (DOD) Personnel Casualty Matters, Policies, and Procedures. It
establishes procedures for the effective execution of the Air Force (AF) Casualty Services
Program at Osan Air Base (AB). This instruction is consistent with guidance provided in AFI
36-3002, Casualty Services. It applies to all personnel assigned to the 51st Fighter Wing (51
FW) and subordinate units, geographically separated units, and personnel assigned or attached
to, or supported by Osan AB providing casualty assistance. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims. The
use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or
service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.

This instruction requires the collection and maintenance of information protected by the Privacy
Act of 1974 authorized by Title 10 United States Code (USC) Sections 1475 through 1480, 2771,
and 8013 and 37 USC, Sections 551 through 556. System of Records Notice F030 AFPC,
Casualty Files, applies.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Changes from 51 MSS
to 51 FSS (throughout); updates paragraphs 1, 2, and 3; deletes paragraph 4; adds attachment 1.
OPR has changed from 51 MSS/DPMPS to 51 FSS/FSF (throughout).
1. Policy. The casualty services program is a function requiring time and accurate reporting,
sympathetic yet dignified and humane notification, as well as thorough and compassionate
assistance. The full support of all individuals, agencies, and organizations attached or assigned
to Osan AB are required in a coordinated effort to achieve these goals. The Airman and Family
Services Flight (51FSS/FSF) via the Airman & Family Readiness Center (51 FSS/FSFR)
administers the program.
2. Responsibilities:
     2.1. All Personnel. Any individual, agency, or organization having knowledge of a casualty
     is required to immediately notify the 51st Fighter Wing Command Post (51 FW/OC) at DSN
     784-7000. The 51 FW/OC will contact the on call casualty assistance representative from the
     51st Force Support Squadron (51 FSS), 51 FSS/FSF, 51 FSS/FSFR at DSN 784-5440 or the
     on call casualty cell phone at 010-8688-5422.
     2.2. 51 FSS/FSFR (Airman & Family Readiness Center). The primary agency for all
     casualty services. Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:
        2.2.1. Immediately notify the Casualty Services Program Manager (HQ AFPC/DPWCS)
        DSN 312-665-3505 by phone of all reportable casualties.
        2.2.2. Notify 51 FW/OC, 51st Medical Group (51 MDG), wing and unit commanders,
        and if applicable, Mortuary Affairs (51 FSS/FSOX) of any casualties.
        2.2.3. Ensure the 51 FSS/FSF Chief (or designee, A&FRC Director) designates, in
        writing, a trained casualty representative for standby duties during non-duty hours. The
        standby roster will be furnished to assisting agencies quarterly.
            2.2.3.1. Ensure DoD Casualty Information Processing System (DCIPS-Forward) is
            loaded on to local computers and that those computers are made available whenever
            needed.
        2.2.4. Ensure the 51 FSS/FSF Chief (or designee, A&FRC Director) designates, on an
        AF Form 1075, Casualty Personnel Roster, a Casualty Augmentation Support Team
        (CAST), and the team is trained and periodically evaluated. This form must be submitted
        to HQ AFPC/DPWCS and HQ PACAF/A1SA.
            2.2.4.1. Submit a request for Virtual Record of Emergency Data (vRED) access to
            HQ AFPC/DPWCS for each CAST member.
               2.2.4.1.1. Ensure base inprocessing checklists have newly assigned members
               update and ensure accuracy of their current vRED on file.
            2.2.4.2. The CAST will be activated to assist in compiling, recording, and
            dispatching casualty reports as directed by the Casualty Assistance Representative
            (CAR).

     2.2.5. Train all casualty services personnel at serviced Geographically Separated Units
     (GSUs), Personnel Support for Contingency Operations (PERSCO) and CAST members,
     and squadron commanders IAW AFI 36-3002 using this instruction, sample messages
     and letters for reporting casualties and notifying Next of Kin (NOK).
     2.2.6. Render casualty assistance to eligible Primary Next of Kin (PNOK). If casualty
     assistance is provided by another base’s CAR due to geographical separation, etc, provide
     assistance as necessary.
     2.2.7. Coordinate payment of benefits with the Financial Services Office (death only) to
     the PNOK.
     2.2.8. Review and coordinate on all facts and circumstances/condolence letters prepared
     by the appropriate commander prior to dispatching to the PNOK. Receive copy of final
     letters from Commanders, before forwarding to NOK, to provide to HQ AFPC/DPWCS.
     2.2.9. Notify 51 FW/PA of the casualty and circumstances of the incident as soon as the
     information is available. IAW the National Defense Authorization Act of 2004, public
     release of casualty information cannot occur until 24 hours after notifying the NOK of the
     status of the member. Casualty information of ill or injured members may not be
     released without the member’s consent, unless release is otherwise authorized by the
     Privacy Act of 1974.
     2.2.10. Advise 51 FSS/FSMPD to process dependent movement orders (if applicable).
  2.3. 51 FW/OC:
     2.3.1. Serve as the primary point of contact on initial calls concerning casualties.
     2.3.2. On all calls concerning casualties, obtain the member’s name, social security
     number (SSN), place and time of incident, a brief synopsis of the circumstances, and the
     informant's name and phone number or other information that will facilitate subsequent
     contact in the event more information or verification is needed.
     2.3.3. Immediately contact 51 FSS/FSFR or the casualty assistance standby
     representative as reflected on the casualty standby roster.
     2.3.4. Immediately notify the Wing Commander (or designated representative) and
     member’s squadron commander and advise them to contact 51FSS/FSFR additional
     guidance.
     2.3.5. Do not release casualty information to any agency other than the CAR or the
     member’s Commander or First Sergeant. Direct all inquiries to 51 FW/PA.
  2.4. Commanders:
     2.4.1. Immediately notify 51 FW/OC and 51 FSS/FSFR when information is received on
     a casualty assigned to their unit.
     2.4.2. Ensure key personnel know and understand their responsibilities in support of the
     casualty services program and coordinate all actions through the CAR to ensure
     completeness and consistency. Direct all inquiries concerning the casualty to the CAR.
     Refer news media inquiries to 51 FW/PA.

       2.4.3. Wing, Group, or Squadron commanders make personal notification to the primary
       NOK (when available) as soon as possible after coordination with the CAR. Prepare
       circumstance and condolence letters per AFI 36-3002.
       2.4.4. Safeguard the personal effects of the casualty.
       2.4.5. If member is deceased:
          2.4.5.1. Within 24-hours notify the CAR of any awards or decorations for which the
          deceased will be recommended.
          2.4.5.2. Have 51 FSS/FSMPS pull a DD Form 1172, Application for Uniformed
          Services Identification Card-DEERS Enrollment, and DECOR 6, Recommendation
          for Award on the member within 12-hours after casualty occurs.
          2.4.5.3. Have 51 FSS/FSMP obtain a copy of all documents in member’s record from
          the Automated Records Management System (ARMS) on the Air Force Personnel
          Center (AFPC) Secure site.
          2.4.5.4. If the member has a line number for promotion (to SSgt and above), ensure
          the CAR is aware of this information. Contact the Promotions section of the Military
          Personnel Section (MPS), if necessary.
          2.4.5.5. Secure member’s Personal Information File (PIF), Unfavorable Information
          File (UIF), and Training Records as necessary, for potential investigations.
          2.4.5.6. Provide CAR a copy of condolence and/or circumstance letters before
          sending to NOK.
       2.4.6. Consider the effects of the casualty on unit. Conduct critical incident stress
       debriefings and obtain chaplain/life skills support for the Airmen. Other educational
       briefings may be appropriate, dependent upon the circumstances.
    2.5. 51 FW/HC:
       2.5.1. Provide a chaplain to accompany the notification officer on the initial death
       notification and offer spiritual support if needed.
       2.5.2. Ensure that a Chaplain Duty Roster is provided to 51 FW/OC and 51 FSS/FSFR.
    2.6. 51 FSS/FSOX
       2.6.1. Ensure current mortuary affairs officer appointment letter is provided to 51
       FSS/FSFR and 51 FW/OC.
       2.6.2. Assist 51 FW/CC in appointing a Summary Court Officer (SCO). 51 FSS/FSOX
       will instruct the SCO on their responsibilities and duties in accordance with AFI 34-244,
       Disposition of Personal Property and Effects, paragraph 1.4.4.1.
       2.6.3. Serve as the point of contact for obtaining autopsy and toxicology reports and
       provide 51 FSS/FSF a copy upon receipt.
       2.6.4. Provide a representative to brief mortuary entitlements and answer any questions
       the primary NOK or Person Authorized for Direct Disposition (PADD), whoever is
       charged with disposition instructions.

     2.6.5. If the NOK being assisted are in the local area, but the casualty was not assigned
     to Osan AB, assist 51 FW/CC in assigning appropriate Family Liaison Officer (FLO).
        2.6.5.1. Will notify the CAR of any death, once declared by the medical authority,
        and will include the following information:
             2.6.5.1.1. Deaths:
    Item A         Name of Casualty
    Item B         Grade
    Item C         Social Security Number
    Item D         Organization, Major Command (MAJCOM), Installation, and Duty Status
    Item E         Last Known Location of Individual
    Item F         Date and Time of Loss
    Item G         Circumstances

     2.6.6. During communications black outs, provide a runner to relay casualty information.
     2.6.7. Coordinate with USFK Mortuary Affairs (DSN 736-3003) for further instruction.
  2.7. 51 LRS:
     2.7.1. Dispatch a vehicle upon CAR or 51 FW/OC request.
     2.7.2. Assist the SCO with the processing and shipment of household goods, personal
     property, and movement of eligible dependents.
     2.7.3. Assist SCO with processing/shipment of household goods, personal property and
     movement of eligible dependents.
  2.8. 51 SFS:
     2.8.1. On all calls received pertaining to a casualty, obtain (as a minimum) the casualty's
     name, grade, SSN, and place of occurrence, a brief synopsis of the circumstances, and the
     informant's name and phone number or other information that will facilitate subsequent
     contact if more information or verification is required. Relay information immediately to
     the 51 FW/OC.
     2.8.2. Immediately contact 51 FSS/FSFR or the casualty assistance standby
     representative as reflected on the casualty standby roster and AF Office of Special
     Investigations (AFOSI), Detachment 611.
     2.8.3. Assist the CAR in obtaining casualty information from local civilian authorities.
     2.8.4. When necessary, accompany Casualty Notification Team and CAR to the
     residence of the NOK.
  2.9. 51 CPTS/FMF:
     2.9.1. Ensure a point of contact is available to prepare DD Form 397, Claim Certification
     and Voucher for Death Gratuity Payment, and process death gratuity payment to the
     primary NOK if NOK is located within the vicinity of Osan AB, ROK. If not, the CPTS
     & FSS located closest to the NOK is responsible for preparing and processing the
     paperwork. The point of contact (POC) should be designated in writing for duty and non-

       duty hours. The standby roster will be furnished automatically to the CAR and to other
       assisting agencies upon request.
       2.9.2. Advise the CAR of all monetary pay and allowances due to NOK and forward
       death certificates to Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS).
       2.9.3. Process claims for benefits when provided by 51 FSS/FSFR on behalf of the
       primary NOK.
    2.10. 51 MDG:
       2.10.1. Immediately notify 51 FW/OC to relay casualty information to 51 FSS/FSFR.
       2.10.2. Designate, in writing, a qualified medical representative to accompany the
       notification officer. The standby roster will be furnished to the CAR automatically and
       assisting agencies upon request.
       2.10.3. During mass casualty situations:
          2.10.3.1. In a timely manner, notify the CAR of casualties who are very seriously
          injured (VSI), seriously injured (SI), incapacitating illness or injury (III), and any
          death declaration by the medical authority, whether admitted to Osan AB hospital,
          121st Community Hospital, or any other medical facility, and will include the
          following information:
                2.10.3.1.1. Deaths:
       Item A         Name of Casualty
       Item B         Grade
       Item C         Social Security Number
       Item D         Organization, MAJCOM, Installation, and Duty Status
       Item E         Last Known Location of Individual
       Item F         Date and Time of Loss
       Item G         Circumstances

                2.10.3.1.2. VSI/SI/III/ Not Seriously Ill or Injured (NSI):
       Item A         Name of Casualty
       Item B         Grade
       Item C         Social Security Number
       Item D         Organization, MAJCOM, and Installation
       Item E         Medical Treatment Facility (MTF)
       Item F         Date of Hospitalization and Diagnosed as VSI, SI, or III
       Item G         Diagnosis of Illness or Injury
       Item H         Prognosis, Condition, and Treatment
       Item I         Cause and Circumstances
       Item J         NOK Invitational Travel

      2.10.4. Notify the CAR immediately if the attending physician determines that the
      primary NOK’s bedside presence is warranted for members in VSI or SI status and the
      primary NOK is not collocated with the member.
      2.10.5. Promptly notify the CAR when the status of a patient changes or the patient is
      evacuated to another hospital.
      2.10.6. During a communications black out provide a runner to relay casualty
      information.
   2.11. 51 FW/JA:
      2.11.1. Upon request, provide legal assistance to the PNOK of deceased members as
      provided for in AFI 51-504, Legal Assistance, Notary, And Preventive Law Programs.
      2.11.2. Advise the SCO in the legal aspects of administering the casualty’s personal
      belongings and finances.
   2.12. 51 FSS.
      2.12.1. Will ensure a POC is available and has access to civilian personnel records to
      release information to the CAR in the event of a civilian casualty consistent with the
      Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act. This POC should be designated in
      writing for duty and non-duty hours. The standby roster will be furnished automatically
      to the CAR and to other assisting agencies upon request.
      2.12.2. IAW Added Notification Requirement for Civilian Employee Death memo dated
      7 November 2005, civilian employee deaths will be reported in the same manner as
      military casualties. Interim reporting procedures for civilian deaths are:
          2.12.2.1. Complete Civilian Death Report worksheet.
          2.12.2.2. Provide completed worksheet to base CAR.
          2.12.2.3. CAR will update civilian death into DCIPS.
          2.12.2.4. CAR will ensure appropriate MAJCOM is notified as a cc addressee.
          2.12.2.5. HQ AFPC/DPWCS will ensure this information is entered into the HQ
          level DCIPS system and reflected on the next day’s Casualty Morning Report.
      2.12.3. Train all Contingency-Essential (C-E) and Emergency-Essential (E-E) personnel
      IAW 7 November 2005 memo.
      2.12.4. Load DCIPS on contingency and emergency essential computers.
      2.12.5. Ensure a local national employee is available to assist as a translator when none
      is available.
