This instruction provides procedures for implementing Luke AFB Form 210, 56th Fighter
Wing Volunteer Recognition Nomination Form (Attachment 2). The nomination form
provided at Attachment 2 is to be used for all base volunteers. Active duty and civilian members
must have performed their volunteer service outside of normal duty hours. Additionally, one
person out of the military retired, civilian, and youth categories will be selected to receive the Air
Force Chief of Staff Volunteer Excellence Award. This award recognizes one non-military
individual whose community service is of a sustained, direct, and consequential nature. This
publication does not apply to all Air Force, Air Force Reserve Command and to Air National
Guard Units. Luke AFB Form 210 is available digitally at http://www.e-publishing.af.mil/ and
https://luke.eis.aetc.af.mil/56MSG/CS/56_SCO/SCOK/SCOKP/LUKE%20FORMS/Forms/
AllItems.aspx. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office
of Primary Responsibility using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication;
route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.
Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in
accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.

1. Purpose
     1.1. Annually, Luke AFB recognizes members of our community who demonstrate
     compassion and service to others by performing volunteer work in either the local
     community or at Luke AFB. This initiative promotes volunteerism by providing an
     opportunity for Luke personnel and their families to find out about some of the many
     volunteer opportunities in the Valley.
2. Preparing Luke AFB Form 210
     2.1. Hand-written or type.
     2.2. Type using only 10 or 12-pitch font.
     2.3. May use computerized versions with 12-pitch Times New Roman font.
     2.4. Print or legibly write entries when computerized form, typewriter, or word processor is
     not available. Use only dark blue or black ink.
     2.5. Avoid using nicknames and code names.
     2.6. Do not enter classified information in any section of the form.
