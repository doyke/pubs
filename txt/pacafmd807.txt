This mission directive implements policy guidance in AFPD 38-1, Organization; AFI 38-101,
Air Force Organization; AFPD 10-1, Mission Directives, and AFI 10-101, Format and Content
of Mission Directives. It does not apply to the Air National Guard or the United States Air Force
Reserve. Its purpose is to prescribe unit mission, organization, and processes. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. See Attachment 1 for a glossary of references and supporting
information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes
include, section 2.4.2 HQ PACAF /SC changed to HQ PACAF/A6, section 4. Responsibilities,
section 6. Organization Chart, Abbreviations and Acronyms.
1. Mission. PACAF Air Postal Squadron (AIRPS).
1.1. Serves as the single service manager for air transportation of all DoD mail in USPACOM.

1.2. Provides operational guidance, transportation, personnel, and logistics support to ensure full
postal services during peace, war, and contingency situations.
1.3. Provides oversight and guidance for Pacific Air Forces postal and official mail matters.
2. Command. The Commander, PACAF AIRPS.
     2.1. As head of a Major Command direct reporting unit, is responsible to the Director of
     Communications, HQ Pacific Air Forces (HQ PACAF/A6) for functional management,
     including management of special-duty identifier 8M000--Postal Specialist, personnel
     assignments, and coordination with AFPC.
     2.2. Commands the PACAF AIRPS to include all geographically-separated detachments and
     operating locations.
     2.3. Exercises command, administrative, operational, functional, and technical control over
     assigned units and facilities, operating and maintenance budgets, and postal systems operated
     by PACAF.
     2.4. Is authorized direct communication:
        2.4.1. Between major and subordinate US Army, Navy, Marine Corps, and Air Force
        commands, Military Postal Service Agency and commanders of the appropriate
        subordinate detachments on operational and technical matters concerning AF postal
        functions.
        2.4.2. With HQ PACAF/A6, Secretary of the Air Force postal staffing elements, the
        USPS, and other government and civilian agencies on operational and technical matters
        pertaining to postal operations. Subordinate units may communicate directly with non-
        departmental level activities only as prescribed or directed by a departmental-level
        activity.
3. Assigned Forces. Not applicable to this mission directive.
4. Responsibilities. The Commander, PACAF AIRPS.
     4.1. Provides command postal oversight and guidance for all Air Post Offices, Postal Service
     Centers, and Official Mail Centers (OMC), including functional management, inspection
     oversight, policy interpretation and supplementation, and resource recommendations and
     review. Conducts annual postal operations inspections at each activity.
     4.2. Manages a USPACOM theater-wide air transportation network.
     4.3. Operates Aerial Mail Terminals (AMT), Mail Control Activities (MCA), and Aerial
     Postal Offices (APO) in PACAF locations (Antarctica, Australia, Japan, New Zealand,
     Republic of Korea, and Thailand).
     4.4. Performs regular site visits to squadron operating locations.
     4.5. Conducts regular functional manager site visits to non-squadron PACAF postal
     operating locations.
     4.6. Operates the USPACOM/J1 Joint Postal Cell, when required during contingencies, and
     conducts annual Pacific joint postal planning activities with components to ensure readiness.

   4.7. Deploys in support of AEF and internal taskings in conjunction with USPACOM
   OPLANs and civil support missions, as directed.
   4.8. Maintains contingency postal kits in support of UTC taskings.
   4.9. Attends theatre war planning workshops in relation to USPACOM, USFK, and USFJ
   OPLAN reviews.
   4.10. In addition to a significant morale and family support function, provides mission
   critical support. This includes transport of weapons and weapons system parts, High-Valued
   Shipments (such as cash), lab specimens, personnel and medical records, supplies and
   equipment, and classified up to secret documents and media.

Terms
