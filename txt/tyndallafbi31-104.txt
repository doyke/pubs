This instruction implements AFPD 31-2, Air Provost Operations, and supplements AFI 31-
101, Integrated Defense. . It outlines responsibilities and establishes the policies for which the
Ride-Along Program on Tyndall AFB. The Ride-Along Program is a cooperative effort between
the 325 SFS and the 325th Fighter Wing (325 FW) community. It applies to all Ride-Along
Program participants. This instruction requires collecting and maintaining information protected
by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by 10 U.S.C. 8013, DODI 6055.4, and AFI 31-218 (IP).
System of records notice F031 AF SP L, Traffic Accident and Violation Reports, applies. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Form 847s from the field through the appropriate functional chain of command. This
publication may be supplemented at any level, but all direct Supplements must be routed to the
OPR of this publication for coordination prior to certification and approval. It applies to
individuals at all levels who prepare, manage, review, certify, approve, disseminate and/or use
official Air Force publications and forms, including Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and
Air National Guard (ANG) units, except where noted otherwise. Ensure all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. General Information. This program is managed and monitored by the 325 SFS Operations
Office/Police Services as a crime prevention program. Police Services is responsible for the
proper coordination between agencies using the program. Ideally, this program will establish a
better understanding of Security Forces operations while enhancing the rapport between the
Security Forces personnel and the youth of Tyndall AFB. During Force Protection Condition
BRAVO or higher, there will be no Ride-Along Program.
2. Interested Parties. Participants/Guardians of minors will:
     2.1. Riders must be at least 16 years of age and complete Ride Along Program Application
     with General Release and Hold Harmless Agreement signed by themselves and/or their
     parent/guardian 72 hours prior to the requested date to participate in the Ride-Along
     Program. NOTE: Complete required paperwork each time applicant applies for program.
     2.2. The Police Services office will:
        2.2.1. Keep completed paperwork on file for a minimum of one year.
        2.2.2. Coordinate a date and time for the Ride-Along.
     2.3. The on-duty Flight Chief will:
        2.3.1. Once a date and time is agreed upon the on-duty Flight Chief for the specified day
        will contact the applicant with reporting instructions.
        2.3.2. There will be no more than one Ride-Along guest participating in flight operations
        at any given time.
        2.3.3. May select him/herself or select an experienced security forces patrolman as the
        representative for the guest to accompany.
     2.4. Program Requirements:
        2.4.1. The guest is not allowed to respond with the patrol to incidents involving the
        possibility of weapons, or situations determined by the patrol as dangerous. If the patrol
        has to respond to a potentially dangerous situation, the guest will be dropped off at an
        appropriate location and picked up by another patrol.
        2.4.2. At no time will the participant exit the patrol vehicle during traffic stops on
        Highway 98.
        2.4.3. The guest will follow the directions of patrol personnel at all times. Any deviation
        from instruction will result in termination of the Ride-Along.
        2.4.4. The patrol will brief the guest on general security forces duties and mission.
        2.4.5. The desk sergeant will be briefed on the exact location of the guest at all times.
        2.4.6. After the guest has been briefed on the proper radio procedures, they may be
        allowed, at the discretion of the patrol and the Flight Chief, to use the security forces
        radio for short routine transmissions.

   2.5. Criteria For Youth Applicants:
       2.5.1. Procedures for youth applicants will be the same as adult applicants (18 and over)
       with the exception of the Ride-Along Program Background Affidavit, which does not
       need to be completed.
       2.5.2. All applicable forms must also be endorsed by the applicant’s legal guardian.
       2.5.3. For applicants under the age of 18, the Police Services office or higher will
       personally meet with the applicant and guardians of the applicant to ensure proper intent
       and a full understanding of this operating instruction is in place.
3. Procedures For Other Interested Parties. The Ride-Along Program is primarily intended
for juvenile family members, active-duty, reserve component, retired members, and AF civilian
employees assigned to the Tyndall Air Force Base area who are interested in a career in law
enforcement. Sponsors of the interested party should contact the Police Services Office at (850)
283-4965 for guidance on completing the prescribed forms and scheduling an appropriate time
for participation.
   3.1. Background Investigations. A background check will be completed on all participants
   over the age of 18 before they are allowed to participate in the program.
   3.2. The Visitor Control Center will complete all background checks for this program.
