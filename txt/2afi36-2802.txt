This instruction implements AFPD 36-28, Awards and Decorations Programs, and establishes
procedures policy and responsibilities for selecting the Second Air Force Training Squadron of
the Year (TSY). This program is to recognize 2 AF training squadrons within the training wings
and the 381st Training Group. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS). Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functional chain of command. Attachment 1 is a glossary of
references and supporting information. Attachment 2 is revised to illustrate preparation example
AF Form 1206.

1. Purpose. TRAINING SQUADRON of the YEAR: To recognize the outstanding mission
accomplishment, professionalism, and dedication of the top training squadron in Second Air
Force. It also serves as a venue to improve morale and enhance esprit de corps for members
assigned to the training wing and groups. This award is named the General William R. Looney
III Training Squadron of the Year Trophy.
2. Award Period. The award cycle is 1 January through 31 December. Accomplishments must
have occurred during this period.
3. Program Responsibilities. The Second Air Force Vice Commander (2 AF/CV) is OPR for
the TSY award program.

4. Eligibility Requirements. Training wing and 381st Training Group nominees may consist of
only training squadrons. (i.e, BMT, tech training, etc...) and excludes training support
squadrons.
5. Nomination Officials. Training Wing/381st Training Group CV/CDs are nomination
officials.
6. Nomination Procedures. Nomination packages will be submitted on an AF Form 1206,
Nomination for Award (limited to one page, bullet format), citation (no more than 12 lines and
250 words), and nomination memo. Nominations will be submitted to 2 AF/CV, when requested
through Task Management Tool, as outlined in paragraph 7, via electronic media using the
following categories:
     6.1. Mission Statement: Mission statement and a short synopsis of impact, example,
     “Produce the world’s best-trained forces!”.
     6.2. Significant Unit Accomplishments: Describe significant leadership accomplishments
     and how well the unit performed assigned primary duties. Define the scope and level of
     responsibilities and the impact on the wing/group’s mission. Include any new technology,
     initiatives or techniques developed by the unit that positively impact the unit and/or mission.
     Include results of Air Force, MAJCOM and numbered air force-level inspections and
     evaluations (Not to exceed 20 Lines).
     6.3. Significant Unit Improvements: Show how the unit developed or improved skills
     related to primary duties; e.g., formal training programs, certifications, and so forth. Include
     completion rates of any professional military education, including number of awards earned
     during in-residence attendance. Include any off-duty education not directly related to
     primary duties; e.g., class, course, degree enrollment and/or completion as a unit, and overall
     grade point average. Cite any other relevant efforts that significantly enhanced mission
     effectiveness, personal development, and efforts to improve work areas or facilities (Not to
     exceed 9 Lines).
     6.4. Base/Community Support: Define the scope and impact of the unit’s positive leadership
     and involvement in both the military and civilian community. Include leadership,
     membership, or participation in professional military and civilian organizations, associations,
     and events (Not to exceed 9 Lines).

8. Selection Procedures and Announcement of Winners.
   8.1. Evaluation and selection board for TSY award will be chaired by 2 AF/CV and will
   consist of TRW/CVs, and 381 TRG/CD.
   8.2. All board members will complete a score sheet according to the scale listed. Scoring
   will be based on a scale of 1 to 10 (1/2-point increments) for:
      8.2.1. 8.5 to 10— Exceptional performance/contribution.
      8.2.2. 7.0 to 8.5— Outstanding performance/contribution.
      8.2.3. 5.5 to 7.0— Standard performance/contribution.
   8.3. Nominations will be assessed with considerations addressing.
      8.3.1. Nature and results of unit accomplishments/initiatives related to the work
      environment that set the unit apart from other AETC units.
      8.3.2. Unit efforts to improve mission effectiveness, personnel development, and efforts
      to enhance work areas or facilities.
      8.3.3. Benchmark processes demonstrating innovative or highly successful training
      procedures.
   8.4. In the event of a tie, large variance in scoring or an extremely close vote, the board
   president will make the final decision on a winning nomination.
   8.5. Winner Determination:
      8.5.1. Winner is determined to be the training squadron that receives the highest score
      from the evaluation board members.
      8.5.2. Board scoring sheets will be forwarded to 2 AF/CV for review. 2 AF/CV will
      forward results and recommendation of overall winner to 2 AF/CCC and 2 AF/CC who
      will coordinate final approval.
   8.6. The Second Air Force Commander will make a formal announcement and notify the
   wing/group commanders of TSY award winner.
   8.7. The Second Air Force Commander will present the TSY award trophy to the winning
   wing/group concurrent with site location visit or at the semi-annual AETC Spring Senior
   Leader Conference, NAF breakout session only.
