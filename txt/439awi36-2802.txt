This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards and Decorations
Programs. It establishes procedures and responsibilities for supervisors to recognize and reward
exemplary participation in the 439th Airlift Wing Mishap Prevention Program by individuals and
subordinate units. The 439th Airlift Wing Safety Office (439 AW/SE) administers the safety
award program. It applies to all units assigned or attached to Westover ARB. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847 to 439 AW/SE, 100 Lloyd Street, Suite 102, Westover ARB,
MA 01022-1825.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

The website for the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) has been updated. The name
of the current base commander has been updated. A Summary of Changes has been added.
1. Implementation. This instruction creates safety incentive awards for which individual
members may be recognized. The civilian and military awards are “Safety Person of the Quarter”
and, “Safety Person of the Year”. The recipients of these awards will receive a certificate of
commendation and recognition. Additional recognition may be specified at the discretion of the
commander, 439th Airlift Wing and/or subordinate commanders.
2. Safety Award Categories.

     2.1. Safety Person of the Quarter.
        2.1.1. The 4 quarters are:
            2.1.1.1. 1st Quarter, October-December (Selection made in January).
            2.1.1.2. 2nd Quarter, January-March (Selection made in April).
            2.1.1.3. 3rd Quarter, April-June (Selection made in July).
            2.1.1.4. 4th Quarter, July-September (Selection made in October).
        2.1.2. Submissions for any quarter must be received in 439 AW/SE not later than the end
        of the Unit Training Assembly (UTA) for each selection month.
        2.1.3. May be awarded quarterly to either a civilian or military member of the 439th
        Airlift Wing who has made significant safety contributions.
        2.1.4. Selection criteria include: Mishap prevention, hazards identification and
        abatement, ability to project and reinforce a sound safety attitude to others. Other safety
        related accomplishments, in the opinion of the nominator, which uniquely qualifies this
        individual for recognition.
     2.2. Safety Person of the Year.
        2.2.1. Individuals who have been selected as “Safety Person of the Quarter” during the
        year will automatically be placed into competition for the “Safety Person of the Year”
        Award.
        2.2.2. Selections for the Safety Person of the Year will be made in November and
        awarded during the December UTA.
        2.2.3. Safety Person of the Year recipients will be considered for submission to higher
        headquarters for awards referenced in AFI 36-2833, Safety Awards.
3. Nomination Procedures.
     3.1. Unit commanders, functional managers, and supervisors are responsible for nominating
     deserving members to the 439 AW/SE.
     3.2. Send nomination(s) to the 439 AW/SEG via e-mail or official memorandum (one per
     individual). Telephone nominations will not be accepted. If a candidate is not selected,
     supervisors may update the information and resubmit it for future consideration. Each
     submission is considered current for two quarters.
        3.2.1. Include in your nomination:
            3.2.1.1. Full Name (including middle initial).
            3.2.1.2. Grade/Rank, Job Series/AFSC, Unit or Organization.
            3.2.1.3. The basis used for your nomination of this individual; for example, the
            individual’s accomplishments, which resulted in implemented suggestions, improved
            work conditions, or changed procedures, which enhanced the safety of the workplace
            or encouraged safe work practices.
            3.2.1.4. Cite specific examples of the individual’s involvement in your unit’s safety
            program and any supporting information that would be applicable for consideration.
