This supplement implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 23-302,
Vehicle Management, 29 October 2007. It provides guidance and procedures for vehicle
maintenance management on Vance Air Force Base (Vance AFB). It applies to all Air Force
military, civilian, and contractor personnel under contract by the Department of Defense
assigned to or tenants on Vance AFB. This instruction requires collecting and maintaining
information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by Title 10, United States Code
(U.S.C.), Section 8013 and Executive Order 9397 (Numbering System For Federal Accounts
Relating to Individual Persons (SSN). System of records notice F031 AF SP L, Traffic Accident
and Violation Reports, applies. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and are disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS), located at https://afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.                 Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Form 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain of command. (AF Form
847 is prescribed in AFI 11-215, USAF Flight Manuals Program [FMP]. Refer to that
publication for guidance on filling out the form.)

1.20.5. (Added) Vehicle Control Officers (VCOs) will report all accidents involving military
or contractor-owned vehicles to the Transportation Manager. The Manager will notify HQ
AETC/LGTV by phone, E-mail or fax, followed by message, of any government/contractor-
owned vehicle accidents involving aircraft, extraordinary circumstances, hospitalization, loss of
life, and accidents, which may exceed the one-time repair limits.
1.20.5.1. (Added) The vehicle operator will report any vehicle accident on base to Security
Police, 580-213-7415, as soon as possible after the accident. The operator is required to make
immediate notification to his/her supervisor, director, or next in charge.
1.20.5.2. (Added)       When accidents occur during non-duty hours, report the following
information to the Transportation Manager:
1.20.5.2.1. (Added) Date, time, and location of accident.
1.20.5.2.2. (Added) Type of vehicles involved and registration numbers.
1.20.5.2.3. (Added) Estimated cost of accident damage to using organization.
1.20.5.2.4. (Added) Vehicles will not be moved until released by Security Forces or local
authorities, unless it is necessary for removal of the injured, or to prevent another accident.
1.20.5.3. (Added) For contractor employees, the VCO/VCNCO must notify the contractor
safety specialist the same day of the accident or the next duty day if the accident occurs after
duty hours.
1.20.5.4. (Added) The vehicles will be taken to Vehicle Maintenance the same day of the
accident, or the next duty day if it occurs after duty hours. If the vehicles cannot be driven for
safety reasons, notify Vehicle Maintenance for wrecker assistance.
1.20.5.5. (Added) The contractor's safety specialist investigates all vehicle accidents where a
contractor employee is involved. Results of the military unit investigation and completing
actions are reported to the Reports of Survey Monitor. The Reports of Survey Monitor then
submits a report to the 71 FTW Vice Commander IAW AFMAN 23-220_Vance Supplement 1.
1.20.5.6. (Added) The vehicle operator must submit a completed SF Form 91, Motor Vehicle
Accident Report, to unit VCO/VCNCO with original to Transportation Manager and a copy to
Wing Safety Office within 24 hours following the accident.
1.20.5.7. (Added) Complete DD Form 518, Accident Identification Card, and give it to others
involved in accident. If vehicle is parked, place in vehicle or secure to windshield.
1.20.5.8. (Added) The operator will report off-base accidents in accordance with state laws.
The operator will immediately notify Security Forces and any other law enforcement agency that
has jurisdiction.
1.20.5.9. (Added) Notify Command Post at 580-213-7384 that a military or contractor-owned
vehicle has been involved in an off-base accident.
1.20.5.10. (Added) Investigating law enforcement authority may be provided information on
circumstances of the accident, but do not admit to negligence or responsibility.
