This instruction implements Air Force, Policy Directive (AFPD) 33-1, Information Resources
Management and Air Force Instruction (AFI) 35-109, Public Affairs Visual Information, and
provides guidance for the Keesler AFB dedicated cable television channel. The service is
provided to personnel and family members assigned, attached or tenant to Keesler AFB, living or
working on base and subscribing to commercial cable service. This publication does not apply to
Air Force Reserve Command Units or the Air National Guard. Ensure that all records created as
a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Attachment 1 is a Glossary of
References and Supporting Information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Visual Information
AFI has change from AFI 33-117 to AFI 35-109. In para. 2.2 hours changed from 0700 - 1700
hrs to 0700 – 1600 hrs.
1. Terms for Commander’s Cable Access Channel.

     1.1. Official items are announcements or messages to be aired in the interest of the Air Force
     and the members and units of the 81st Training Wing. Other messages are those of interest
     to a significant portion of the Keesler population.
     1.2. Unofficial items are those which are in the nature of public service announcements.
     1.3. Emergency announcements are those which impact or have far reaching consequences
     on the mission or majority of people and units assigned to Keesler.
2. Procedures for Commander’s Cable Access Channel.
     2.1. The Keesler AFB dedicated cable television channel will operate on cable channel 14,
     on a 24-hour, seven day a week basis. Editing will be done during duty hours, or as needed
     in an emergency.
     2.2. Messages/announcements should be submitted to the Public Affairs office during
     normal duty hours, 0700 - 1600, Monday through Friday.
     2.3. Messages will be aired as indicated by the requester for seven days or more, depending
     on nature of the message.
     2.4. Units, staff agencies, clubs and individuals desiring to place announcements/messages
     must adhere to the following guidelines:
        2.4.1. A message may be either typed on plain bond paper and delivered to the Public
        Affairs office, 709 H Street, Rm 201A, during normal duty hours; FAX to PA at 377-
        3940; or e-mailed to 81trw.pa@us.af.mil at least three (3) duty days prior to the first day
        they are to appear.
        2.4.2. Commanders, deputy commanders, staff agency chiefs, and/or directors will
        indicate approval of official messages/announcements submitted.
3. Policy on Terms for Broadcast.
     3.1. Official messages/announcements will be given priority over all other messages.
     Department of Defense personnel will not be portrayed in an unfavorable or undignified
     manner.
     3.2. Unofficial items will be aired on a space-available, first-come first-served basis.
     3.3. Accuracy and proper coordination are the responsibilities of the submitting unit/agency
     prior to delivery to Public Affairs.
     3.4. Personnel recognition and items highlighting significant accomplishments by units and
     individuals may be submitted.
     3.5. Items of commercial interest, advertisements for services or products, or merchandise
     for sale, etc., will not be accepted.
     3.6. Informational videos may be broadcast when requested on a space available basis.
     Videos should be submitted during duty hours with a requested airing schedule no later than
     three (3) duty days prior to the first requested broadcast date.
     3.7. Videos submitted for broadcast must be from the base visual information library within
     the government; produced by government personnel for official purposes approved for
     broadcasting by the 81st Training Wing commander; approved by the Office of the Secretary
