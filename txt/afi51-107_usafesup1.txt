AFI 51-107, 1 April 1996, is supplemented as follows:
This supplement provides guidance on authority and procedures for the hiring of civilian attorneys within
USAFE. It reflects Staff Judge Advocate (HQ USAFE/JA) delegation of certain authority to hire civilian
attorneys to fill positions at Air Force legal offices within USAFE. This supplement applies to all US Air
Forces in Europe (USAFE) units. It does not apply to US Air Force Reserve or Air National Guard units.
1.3. For purposes of this supplement, “staff judge advoc ates of USAFE m ain operating bases (MOB),”
refers to the following offices:
31 FW/JA39 WG/JA48 FW/JA52 FW/JA86 AW/JA100 ARW/JA
1.3.1. Authority to approve qual ifications of candidates to positions graded at GS-13 and below, and to
approve appointments to those positions, when using fully competitive hiring procedures, is delegated to
numbered air forces (NAF) staff judge advocates and to the staf f judge advocates of USAFE MOBs, for
vacancies within their respective offices.
1.3.2. Authority to promote attorneys to positions graded at GS-13 and below, and to upgrade att orneys
to the target grade of a developmental position (when GS-13 and below), is delegated to NAF staff judge
advocates and to the staff judge advocates of USAFE MOBs, for positions within their respective offices.
1.3.3. Authority to approve qualifications and reassign attorneys to perform different functions within
equal or lower graded positions is delegated to NAF staff judge advocates and to the staff judge advocates
of USAFE MOBs, for positions within their respective offices.
2.4.1. All attorney qualification, appointment, reassignment, or transfer actions requiring HQ USAFE/JA
approval (e.g., those actions affecting GS-13 and below pos itions at other than M OBs, and proposals to
transfer attorney positions between different organizations or bases), or requiring Air Force Civilian
Attorney Qualifying Approval, shall be submitted through command staff judge advocate channels for
recommended approval or disapproval. N o further comment is r equired if the reviewing legal office
