AFI 31-113, 26 January 2012, is supplemented as follows:

This supplement applies to all activities assigned to Eglin AFB, Florida. This supplement also
applies to Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Units. This supplement requires the collection
and maintenance of information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974. System of Records
Notices (SORN) F031 AF SP F, Notification Letters to Persons Barred from Entry to Air Force
Installations, F031 AF SF B, Security Forces Management Information System (SFMIS) and
F031 AF SP O, Documentation for Identification and Entry Authority, applies. Send
recommended changes or comments for this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility
(OPR) using the Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS). This publication may not be supplemented.

1.5.12.10. Access control guidance for Eglin AFB is codified in EAFB Plan 31-101, Integrated
Defense Plan. Guidance set forth in this supplement will work in conjunction with Annex W of
the Integrated Defense Plan.

3.11.2.1. The collection and use of Social Security Administration Numbers (SSANs) is
necessary to facilitate the vetting process and has been deemed vital to complete an accurate
fitness determination for installation access.
3.11.2.2. SSANs will only be utilized to vet identity and security risk potential for access control
(protection/law enforcement) as explained in Title 50, United States Code, Section 797, Internal
Security Act of 1950.
3.11.2.3. Pursuant to references Homeland Security Presidential Directive (HSPD)-6,
Integration and Use of Screening Information, HSPD-11, Comprehensive Terrorist-Related
Screening Procedures, and HSPD-24, Biometrics for Identification and Screening to Enhance
National Security, disclosure/sharing can occur across the Federal government for the life of the
issued access credential.
3.11.2.4. All Personally Identifiable Information (PII) will be protected pursuant to the Privacy
Act of 1974, DoD and USAF privacy programs.
3.11.2.5. The supplied credential and related information will be used to identify and respond to
potential risks to DoD personnel, property, and national assets.
4.7.2.1. (Added) Eglin AFB uses EAFB Form 90, Eglin Air Force Base Access Affidavit to
facilitate the vetting process. This affidavit will be used in conjunction with the National Crime
Information Center (NCIC) and Terrorist Databases to make a fitness determination for
installation access.
