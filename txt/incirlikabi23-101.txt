This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 23-1, Materiel Management
Policy and Procedures and provides guidance on responsibilities and procedures for
participation in the Department of Defense Precious Metals Recovery Program (PMRP) and
outlines procedures according to the Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 23-110, Volume 6, Chapter 4,
Precious Metals Recovery Program for the recovery of precious metal scrap. This publication
does not apply to the Air National Guard (ANG) and the Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC)
and their units. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication. Requests for waivers must be submitted to the OPR listed above for consideration
and approval. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS).

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document was substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. This publication is
updated to reflect changes in guidance and procedures dealing with the Base Precious Metals
Recovery Program (PMRP). The major changes include the change in roles and responsibilities
of the PMRP Base manager, PM generating agencies and turn-in procedures.

1. Overview.
     1.1. Applicability. All activities at Incirlik AB will cooperate to the fullest extent with the
     installation PMRP Manager to ensure a successful program.
2. Roles and Responsibilities.
     2.1. The Chief Inspector will be the Base PMRP Manager.
     2.2. The Base PMRP Manager is responsible for the following:
        2.2.1. Ensuring a computer program/report is run at least quarterly to produce a listing
        identifying those National Stock Numbers (NSN) against which a precious metal
        indicator requiring monitoring is assigned.
        2.2.2. Review supply account records quarterly to identify stock numbers assigned a
        Controlled Item Code (CIC) “R” (fine precious metals or high precious metals content).
        item record review will be accomplished with a Discoverer script.
        2.2.3. Notify PM generating activities bearing materials that they are required to appoint
        a unit primary and alternate PMRP Monitor who will establish jacket file accounts and
        manage the unit program in accordance with regulation, AFMAN 23-110, Volume 6,
        Chapter 4; Precious Metals Recovery Program, Department of Defense (DOD) 4160.21-
        M, Defense Material Disposition Management.
        2.2.4. Providing adequate training to all PM generating agency monitors when new
        monitors are appointed.
        2.2.5. Ensure a copy of the designated Base PMRP is sent to all PM generating activities
        to be placed in their PM jacket file.
        2.2.6. Notifying each activity in the PMRP that they are required to initiate and maintain
        a self-inspection program to include as minimum requirements, periodic testing of hypo
        solution draining from electrolytic cartridge units to ensure units are operating properly
        and are in compliance with receipts, issue, and turn-in requirements.
        2.2.7. Being familiar with the contents of this instruction, AFMAN 23-110, Volume 6,
        Chapter 4 and DoD 4160.21-M.
     2.3. Each PM generating agency will:
        2.3.1. Commanders of the activities participating in the PRMP will appoint, in writing, a
        primary and alternate PMRM. These individuals will be familiar with the program
        directives and will monitor the program within the activity to ensure full program
        compliance and act as a point of contact for LRS. The name, location and phone number
        of the appointees will be provided annually, or when changes are made, to 39
        LRS/LGRMI.
        2.3.2. Be responsible for the harvesting of PM and subsequent turn-in to DLA
        Disposition Services.
        2.3.3. Prepare tags and documentation for PM being turned into DLA Dispostion
        Services. Coordinate with 39 LRS/LGRMCC Customer Service for an Electronic Turn-
        in Document (ETID).

     2.3.4. Maintain a jacket file accounting system, which will include the following: Note:
     Contain all items for a period of one year.
        2.3.4.1. TAB A: Monitor’s appointment letter. Documentation of organization and
        shop monitor’s training. Base PMRP appointment letter.
        2.3.4.2. TAB B: AFMAN 23-110 Volume 6, Ch 4 (stamped “for reference use
        only”), operating instructions (if applicable) and a copy of this instruction and the PM
        listing. Stock Group List (SGL)/Precious Metal Indicator Codes (PMIC).
        2.3.4.3. TAB C: Copies of self-inspection reports generated with self inspection
        checklist and PMRP manager semi-annual inspection report.
        2.3.4.4. TAB D: One-for-one swap log for CIC “R” National Stock Numbers.
        2.3.4.5. TAB E: Quarterly reports of turn-in material and control log for silver
        flake/sludge harvested from electrolytic units turned in to the DLA Disposition
        Services Office by troy weight.
     2.3.5. Appoint in writing , an individual (s) (if applicable):
        2.3.5.1. Other than PRMP monitor or alternate to receipt for issue and turn-in to the
        DRMO PM and PM bearing items assigned control code “R”.
        2.3.5.2. To harvest the silver flakes and sludge from electrolytic units and remove
        and replace recovery cartridges.
        2.3.5.3. Persons not involved in the PMRP to witness the harvesting and the cartridge
        removal, and to certify the weight.
     2.3.6. Provide a secure storage area for all PM bearing material at all times until
     transferred to DLA Disposition Services. The PM material will be locked away if not
     under the surveillance of a responsible person.
     2.3.7. Maintain and properly secure any electrolytic recovery unit(s) (ERU) issued by the
     Defense Property Disposal Region Europe.
     2.3.8. Ensure preventative maintenance on silver recovery units is completed:
        2.3.8.1. Day-to-day adjustments.
        2.3.8.2. Cleaning.
        2.3.8.3. Replacement of fuses, gaskets and hoses.
        2.3.8.4. Weekly tests of the electrolytic silver recovery unit and silver recovery
        cartridge with silver estimating strips. The test results and who performed the test
        will be annotated and filed in Tab E of the PMRP jacket file. Results of the reading
        cannot be higher than 1.75. If so, the unit must be cleaned or changed immediately.
        2.3.8.5. Establish a system to identify how much fixer has gone through the
        electrolytic or silver recovery cartridge unit. The unit will maintain a log of this
        quantity.
     2.3.9. PM monitors, during January and July of each year, will review his/her program to
     ensure overall compliance. The written results of this review will be forwarded to 39
     LRS/LGRMI no later than the 15th of the month.

        2.3.10. PMRP generating activities Monitor must brief all personnel on the PMRP and
        its procedures. As a minimum cover the procedures identifying PM and the turn-in of PM
        to the unit’s PMRP Monitor.
3. Turn In Procedures.
     3.1. At least quarterly turn in to DLA Disposition Services precious metals bearing
     scrap/waste and excess refined precious metals. Classify excess and condemned items
     bearing precious metals as turn-in items, unless they qualify for turn-in as scrap. Provide a
     quarterly report of all precious metals turned in to the DLA Disposition Services facility to
     39 LRS/LGRMI not later than the 15th day of the last month of each quarter.
