This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 31-1, Integrated Defense which
superseded AFPD 31-2 on 9 July 2007. It applies to and establishes procedures for curfew hours
on Incirlik Air Base (IAB ) for children unaccompanied by a parent, guardian, or any other adult
18 years of age or older authorized by a parent or a guardian. This instruction also applies to
tenant units. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. This publication may not be supplemented.

1. General Information. Parents and guardians must ensure their minor family member
children comply with the restrictions established in this instruction. It shall not constitute a
defense if the parent or guardian did not have knowledge of the presence of such minor upon the
streets, highways, roads, alleys, playgrounds, or other public areas on IAB during curfew hours.
2. Curfew Restriction. The following rules apply to personnel on IAB. Children under 18
years of age unaccompanied by a parent or guardian shall not loiter in the streets, highways,
roads, alleys, playgrounds, or other public areas on IAB during curfew hours.

3. Curfew Hours. School Days: School days curfew hours will be in effect starting the
evening before the first day of school until the morning of the last day of school for that week.
Normally, this would be from Sunday evening until Friday morning, while school is in session.
     3.1. Curfew for children 14 years of age and under is 2100 to 0600.
     3.2. Curfew for children 15 through 17 years of age is 2230 to 0530.
4. Weekends and Non-School Days. Weekends and non-school day curfew hours only apply
to those days, which do not precede a school day. For example, when Monday is not a school
day, weekend curfew hours listed below will apply. Single days off in the middle of the week
would also apply to the weekend curfew hours on the day preceding the day off.
     4.1. Curfew for children 14 years of age and under is 2200 to 0600 (Friday and Saturday).
     4.2. Curfew for children 15 through 17 years of age is 0001 to 0530 (Friday and Saturday).
5. Summer Vacation. Summer vacation curfew hours will be in effect starting the morning of
the last day of school until the evening before school starts in the fall. The summer vacation
curfew hours are as follows:
     5.1. Curfew for children 14 years of age and under is 2200 to 0600.
     5.2. Curfew for children 15 through 17 years of age is 0001 to 0530.
6. Exception to Curfew. Children 12 through 17 years of age, who are unaccompanied, are
permitted to proceed directly to their homes from authorized base activities, such as chapel
service, athletic events, theater, youth services, scout assemblies, etc. It is also permitted for
children to travel from their point of employment (such as babysitting) directly to their homes.
Children are allowed to camp out overnight in their yards, so long as they stay in the immediate
area of the campsite and do not cause a disturbance.
7. Youth Supervision. The ages specified in this instruction are maximum ages based on
demonstrated age-appropriate child behavior norms. Children who do not consistently
demonstrate behavior consistent with these norms should not be given the same degree of self-
management responsibilities. Notwithstanding the guidelines adopted by this base allowing
certain activities for children of certain ages, parents are always expected to exercise reasonable
judgment and will be held responsible for any incidents or mishaps (preventable) which may
occur. Adults who expose children to substantial risks of serious harm may be charged by
authorities with endangering the welfare of a minor.
8. Violations. Children found or suspected to be in violation of this instruction will be detained
by Security Forces and sponsor, First Sergeant and Commander will be notified. Multiple
violations could result in action under Incirlik Air Base Instruction 51-907, Family Member
Misconduct Board (FMMB).
