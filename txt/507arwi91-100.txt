This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 91-3 Occupational Safety and
Health, Air Force Instruction (AFI) 91-203 Air Force Consolidated Occupational Safety
Instruction It provides guidelines and designates responsibility for training, qualification, and
documentation for personnel operating, egress or ingress of hangar doors. This directive is
applicable to all 507th Air Refueling Wing (ARW) personnel and to all 137 ARW personnel who
maintain or operate ARC-Associated aircraft, systems, and equipment during periods of duty at
(or deployed to) Tinker AFB. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication
to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF IMT 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with the AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

This is a new instruction that incorporates 507th MXGI 91-100, dated 15 March 2009 and
applicability to Hangar 1053 at Tinker AFB, OK.

1. Responsibilities:
   1.1. Facility Managers shall:
       1.1.1. Ensure hangar door serviceability is in accordance with AFI 91-203, Paragraph
       24.14.
       1.1.2. Ensure warning and hazard identification signs are maintained as outlined in AFI
       91-203, Para 24.14.
 2                                                      507ARWI91-100 6 SEPTEMBER 2013


        1.1.3. Ensure door control panels are marked clearly and appropriately.
        1.1.4. Will develop and maintain door operating checklists and ensure checklists are
        posted at each hangar door.
     1.2. The 507th Maintenance Squadron Maintenance Superintendent, (507MXS/MXM) shall
     be the POC for outlining responsibilities and procedures for safe operation of hangar doors.
     1.3. Commanders of non-maintenance units shall forward a letter annually to 507
     MXS/MXM, listing hangar door operator qualified personnel and their date of annual
     training.
     1.4. Supervisors shall:
        1.4.1. Ensure personnel receive hangar door awareness training within 60 days of return
        from technical training.
        1.4.2. Provide annual hangar door awareness training to those personnel whose primary
        work center is in Hangars 1030, 1082 and 1053.
        1.4.3. Schedule all hangar door operator training through the applicable Unit Training
        Manager.
     1.5. Hangar Door Operators shall:
        1.5.1. Be familiar with procedures outlined in AFI 91-203, Para 24.14.
        1.5.2. Operate doors in accordance with local checklist posted on the hangar doors being
        operated.
2. Conducting Training:
     2.1. The 507th Maintenance Group Maintenance Training Flight, 507 MOF/MXOT, will
     provide hands-on hangar door operator training annually for any 507 ARW/137 ARW
     personnel who have a requirement to operate any of the 507 ARW powered and manual
     hangar doors. As a minimum, annual operator training will include hangar door hazards,
     emergency procedures, and hangar door operations.
     2.2. Hangar door awareness training will include hangar door hazards and emergency
     procedures.
     2.3. Lesson plans will be maintained by the 507th Maintenance Operations Flight Training
     Office, 507 MOF/MXOT.
     2.4. The hangar door operator training instructor will forward a class roster to the affected
     Unit Training Manager for input into the appropriate MIS.
     2.5. The affected Unit Training Manager will document hangar door awareness training and
     hangar door operator training in the appropriate Management Information System (MIS) for
     maintenance personnel.
