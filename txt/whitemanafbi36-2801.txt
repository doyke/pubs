This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards And Decorations
Programs, and references Air Force Instruction 36-2846, Financial Management And
Comptroller Annual Awards Program, and provides guidance on establishing eligibility criteria,
submission format, and selection procedures for management of Whiteman’s Resource Advisor
of the Year program. This program is designed to recognize the outstanding accomplishments of
financial management personnel on Whiteman Air Force Base. This instruction applies to base
organizations having an individual, military or civilian, performing the duties and responsibilities
of resource advisor. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33 -363, Management of Records and disposed of in
accordance with the Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.

1. AWARD CATEGORIES.
   1.1. Resource Advisor of the Year – RCM/CCM Level (Group/Squadron).
2. ELGIBILITY CRITERIA.
   2.1. Personnel must be serving in the nomination category as of the last day of the award
   period.
   2.2. Personnel must be nominated in the organization to which they are officially assigned.
2                                                WHITEMANAFBI36-2801 6 MARCH 2013


    2.3. Personnel must be a resource advisor, appointed in writing by their respective
    commander, and on file with 509 Comptroller Squadron/FMA (509 CPTS/FMA).
    2.4. Personnel must have been assigned to a 509 Bomb Wing (509 BW) unit a minimum of 7
    months during the year nominated to compete for this award.
    2.5. There will be one Whiteman Air Force Base yearly winner in the RCM/CCM category.
    The overall winner will represent the wing at the Financial Management and Comptroller
    Annual Awards competition at Air Force Global Strike Command.
3. NOMINATION PROCEDURES.
    3.1. Units may nominate one individual and do so by submitting one hard copy of the
    nominees completed AF Form 1206, Nomination for Award (see Attachment 2).
    3.2. Nomination packages must be received by 509 CPTS/FMA by 30 September of the
    current fiscal year.
    3.3. The nomination package should be limited to one page and submitted using the front-
    side of the AF Form 1206. Ensure the package is submitted in bullet format and prepared
    according to the provided attachments.
       3.3.1. Heading explanations are included to help determine where specific items should
       be placed:
           3.3.1.1. LEADERSHIP AND JOB PERFORMANCE IN PRIMARY
           DUTY: Describe significant accomplishments and how the nominee exceeded the
           scope and magnitude of assigned duties or the level of performance (quantify
           performance measures or performance standards) expected. Describe how member
           provided superior decision support to the war fighter. Describe how the nominee
           further attainment of the Financial Management vision, goals, and strategic
           objectives.      Also, consider awards and recognition, significant individual
           accomplishments, unique job achievements, processed or implemented suggestions,
           creative and innovative solutions. (70 Points)
           3.3.1.2. SIGNIFICANT SELF-IMPROVEMENT:                    Show how the member
           developed or improved skills related to primary duties; e.g., formal training, career
           development course enrollment or completion, On-the-Job Training, certifications,
           off-duty education related to primary duties, professional military education (PME),
           and so forth. (20 Points)
           3.3.1.3. BASE OR COMMUNITY INVOLVEMENT: Define the scope and
           impact of the member’s positive leadership and involvement in the military and
           civilian community. Include leadership, membership or participation in unit advisory
           councils, professional military organizations, associations and events. (10 Points)
           3.3.1.4. Individual award winners cannot be considered for awards in two
           consecutive fiscal years.
4. SELECTION PROCEDURES.
    4.1. Financial Management and Comptroller Awards Committee will be comprised of three
    voting members and one non-voting Board President. Note: The Financial Management and
    Comptroller Awards Committee will review Nominations.

     4.1.1. Board President: The Board President will be the 509th Comptroller Squadron
     Commander and will be a non-voting member. In the event of a tie, the Board
     President’s score will be used to break the tie.
     4.1.2. Other Members: The 509th Contracting Squadron Commander (509 CONS/CC),
     the Financial Analysis Flight Commander, and the Superintendent will serve on the board
     and will be voting members.
  4.2. Scoring: Scoring nomination submissions will be accomplished by package only and
  utilizing the provided score sheets. Board members will receive copies of all nomination
  packages with an attached score sheet. Board members are to score each package using the
  scoring scale provided on the score sheet and return to 509 CPTS/FMA by the end of the first
  full week in October.
5. AWARDS AND PRESENTATIONS.
  5.1. The RCM /CCM level annual award winner will be recognized at the first Financial
  Management Board (FMB) in the new fiscal year. The winner will receive an engraved
  plaque, clock, or similar item and their picture will be posted in the 509th Comptroller
  Squadron.
