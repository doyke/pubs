6.10.3.2. (Added) Test Security Requirements.
6.10.3.2.1. (Added) Control measurement items that can be compromised. Store written tests in
a locked cabinet, safe, or vault, including knowledge progress checks (PC) used in lieu of end-of-
block tests. Use Keesler AFB Form 11, Test Control, to sign tests in and out.
6.10.3.2.2. (Added) Maintain a current inventory which includes the location and the quantity of

6.10.3.2.2.1. (Added) Master copy of test.

6.10.3.2.2.3. (Added) Test keys (manual and computer keys)
6.10.3.2.2.4. (Added) Removable storage media.
6.10.3.2.2.5. (Added) Other data, e.g., test item pools, test analysis data, temporarily completed
answer sheets.
6.10.3.2.3. (Added) The TDE chief, unless delegated to the training specialist, authorizes
custodians to remove and return written tests from locked storage containers. Limit custodians
to the minimum number required for efficient operation. Test custodians will be identified in
writing. This document will be prominently displayed on or near the test storage cabinet.
6.10.3.2.4. (Added) If test information is stored on the hard drive of a computer, protect it from
compromise through passwords and limited student access. Develop back-up copies on
removable storage media or another hard drive to prevent loss.
6.10.3.2.5. (Added) Control classified tests under the provisions in previous paragraphs as well
as the safeguards outlined in applicable security directives.
6.10.3.2.6. (Added) Avoid test rotation patterns that might allow students to predict which test
version is to be administered next.
6.10.3.2.7. (Added) If a student is suspected of cheating or compromising a test, collect all test
materials in the student’s possession immediately and send the student to the instructor
supervisor. The instructor supervisor or other competent authority must advise the student of
his/her Article 31, UCMJ rights (AFVA 31-231, Advisement of Rights or AF Form 1168,
Statement of Suspect/Witness/Complainant, can be used for this purpose) if the student is
suspected of a criminal offense and the questioning is part of a law enforcement investigation or
disciplinary inquiry. Students who are caught cheating or willfully compromising a test are
subject to disenrollment for misconduct. Confirmed or suspected cases of test compromise may
cause that particular test to be suspended from classroom administration for an applicable
number of days, usually not less than 90 days. Under unusual circumstances, tests may be
suspended indefinitely. Course supervisors coordinate confirmed or suspected test compromise
situations with the TDE. The TDE chief determines length of suspension of the measurement
devices. Note: If any criminal action is taken, the rights of the individual should be advised on
