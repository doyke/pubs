This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Air and Space
Maintenance. This instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101,
AFRC Sup, Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance Management; Air Force Occupational Safety
and Health Standard (AFOSH Std) 91-100, Aircraft Flight Line - Ground Operations and
Activities and AFOSH Std 91-501, Air Force Consolidated Occupational Safety Standard. It
provides safety guidelines and designates responsibility for training, qualification and
documentation for personnel who operate hangar doors in the Aircraft Maintenance complex. It
applies to all 914th and 107th personnel assigned to the Niagara Falls Air Reserve Station, who
operate hangar doors. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force Information Management Tool AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication and route to MXS/MXMVE. Ensure that
all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and
disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Due to the applicability of this instruction to both 914th and 107th personnel, it is being revised as
a Niagara Falls Reserve Station Instruction and should be reviewed in its entirety.
1. Responsibilities. Supervisors will ensure only properly trained and qualified personnel
operate hangar doors. Building managers will ensure hangar door lesson plans and checklists
have been developed and monthly operational checks completed. Hangar door training lesson

will be located at each hangar door. Monthly hangar door operational checks will be
documented on the building managers locally developed monthly facility inspection checklist.
2. Procedures. The following items must be thoroughly understood and accomplished by
personnel authorized to operate hangar doors.
     2.1. Hangar doors will remain closed during adverse weather.
        2.1.1. For hangar doors equipped with snow melt/deicing system, activation is the
        responsibility of each building manager and the 914th Civil Engineer (CE) Base
        Operating Services (BOS) contractor.
     2.2. CE Requirements:
        2.2.1. CE will be responsible for the installation and repair of signage, floor safety
        markings and repair of hangar doors including electrical, mechanical and all warning
        devices.
        2.2.2. The CE Facility Maintenance Section will conduct annual inspections to ensure all
        signage, markings, and warning devices meet prescribed directives and will maintain
        inspection documentation.
        2.2.3. For door maintenance issues, building custodians will call the CE work order desk.
     2.3. Training Requirements:
        2.3.1. Qualified squadron training representatives or supervisors will conduct hangar
        door training instruction.
        2.3.2. Annual hangar door awareness training will be conducted for all personnel who
        routinely work in hangars (regardless of AFSC or duties). Documentation will be done
        on an AF Form 2426, Training Request and Completion Notification, and/or GO81
        course code AFRC 1100.
        2.3.3. Operator training is accomplished using a lesson plan. As a minimum,
        qualification training will include hangar door hazards, emergency procedure and hangar
        door operations.
        2.3.4. Supervisors will document initial and annual operator training on AF Form 2426.
        Maintenance Group personnel who operate hangar doors will be tracked in the MIS (GO-
        81) system using locally developed course codes NFIAP 000707, NFIAP 000850, NFIAP
        000917, NFIAP 00907. Personnel not tracked in (GO-81) will provide a roster from their
        respective Squadron Commander to the 914 MXG/CC. Building managers will control
        the roster of personnel authorized to operate the hangar doors.
