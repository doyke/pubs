This Instruction implements AFI 32-1024, Standard Facility Requirements; AFI 32-1032,
Planning and Programming Appropriated Funded Maintenance, Repair, and Construction
Projects; AFH 32-1084, Facility Requirements; AFH 32-9007; and Managing Air Force Real
Property. It establishes responsibilities and procedures for maintenance, sustainment and
modifications to AFWA facilities on Offutt AFB. It applies to all military, civilian, and
contractor personnel working in Building 185 assigned to the A-Staff, 1WXG, and 2WXG.
Maintain and dispose of records created as a result of prescribed processes in accordance with
AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records and dispose in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/. The use of the name or mark
of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication
does not imply endorsement by Air Force. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force (AF) Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the
appropriate chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

AFWA HOI 32-1: Facility Maintenance, AFWA HOI 32-2: Facility Planning and Utilization,
and AFWA HOI 32-6: AFWA Interior & Exterior Sign Policy, were consolidated and
incorporated into one publication.

General: AFWA facilities will be managed in accordance with Air Force (AF) and Department
of Defense (DoD) directives and instructions. Maintenance of the facility is a team effort led by
the Facility Manager, but dependent on participation from all building occupants.
1. Roles and Responsibilities:
     1.1. The 2d Weather Group (2WXG)
        1.1.1. Appoint in writing a Facility Manager and Assistant Facility manager
     1.2. The 2d Systems Operations Squadron (2 SYOS) Operations Flight (SYO):
        1.2.1. Operate a facility office as the focal point for all building and property issues.
        1.2.2. Liaison between AFWA and 55 Civil Engineering Squadron (CE) for all real
        property issues.
        1.2.3. Ensure adequacy, cleanliness, and good repair of all the AFWA facilities.
            1.2.3.1. Monitor Goodwill cleaning and American District Telegraph (ADT)
            contracts.
        1.2.4. Manage building key inventory.
        1.2.5. Maintain current copies of building drawings.
        1.2.6. Maintain facility SharePoint site as a “one stop shop” for all AFWA facilities
        information.
        1.2.7. Assist personnel in writing unfunded requirements for facility items and submit.
     1.3. Building Occupants:
        1.3.1. Ensure the cleanliness and safety of their work area and common areas. Offices
        need to be organized, free of clutter, dust and hazards.
        1.3.2. Coordinate ANY facility change (from desk moves to construction projects and
        everything in between) request through the Facility Manager.
2. Building Rules:
     2.1. Space Heaters are not authorized.
     2.2. Signs will not be posted without coordinating with the Facility Manager.
     2.3. All construction projects will place wiring in the wall, subfloor or ceiling; mounting any
     type of conduit to finished walls is not permitted.
     2.4. Microwaves, refrigerators, and toasters are only permitted in the centralized break areas.
     2.5. Publish building policy and procedures.
     2.6. Soda/snack bar funds are not authorized.

   2.7. All deliveries to the building will be made at the loading dock.
   2.8. All building occupants will follow DoD/AF guidance on energy conservation.
   2.9. Nails/screws to mount anything on the steel demountable walls are prohibited.
   2.10. Magnets must be used to mount items on the steel demountable walls.
   2.11. Pictures, calendars, whiteboards, etc may be nailed or screwed to the sheetrock walls.
   2.12. All building occupants may use the observation deck during favorable weather.
   2.13. Tacks, screws or nails to mount signs in the stairwells are prohibited.
   2.14. Purchase of chairs, furniture, etc for the new building will comply with comprehensive
   interior design plan.
3. Facility Maintenance and Repair:
   3.1. Emergency Actions: Immediately contact the AFWA Operations Center at 294-2586
   with any emergency situation.
   3.2. All non-emergency issues should be routed to the Facility Manager by the Facility
   SharePoint site: https://spx.afwa.af.mil/Utilities/support/facilities/default.aspx
4. Facility Change Management:
   4.1. Coordinate facility change (to include space allocation, demountable wall moves, desk
   moves, wall mounts, etc.). 2 SYOS is the focal point for facility change. All proposed
   changes will be coordinated with this office through the SharePoint facility request interface.
   4.2. The SharePoint electronic request will suffice as coordination for changes internal to
   each unit that do not require funding.
   4.3. When a change in the allocation of space is required between units or requires funding,
   the user is responsible for staffing an E-SSS through all 2-letters for AFWA/CC approval
   (attach 1). E-SSS must include a processed unfunded request (UFR) memo as an attachment,
   as required.
5. Prescribed and Adopted forms.
   5.1. There are no prescribed forms for this publication.
   5.2. Adopted Forms:
      5.2.1. AF Form 332, Base Civil Engineer Work Request
      5.2.2. AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.
      5.2.3. DD Form 1391, FY__ Military Construction Project Data
