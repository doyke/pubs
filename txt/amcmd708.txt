This mission directive implements policy guidance in AFPD 10-1, Mission Directives, AFI 10-101, For-
mat and Content of Mission Directives, and AFI 38-101, Air Force Organization. It defines the organiza-
tions and missions of Air Mobility Command (AMC) Communications Group and Squadrons. Its purpose
is to prescribe mission, organization, and processes.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
This interim change 2003-01 changes unit designations from 805th Computer Systems Squadron to 805th
Communications Support Squadron. Changed paragraph 3.2.2. to read Directs and controls C2 capabili-
ties supporting the AMC TACC’s global mission. Attachment 2, changed 805th Computer Systems
Squadron to 805th Communications Support Squadron. A bar ( | ) indicates a change since the last revi-
sion.


1. Mission. The HQ AMC Communications Group supports air mobility operations at 12 AMC bases
and 120 locations worldwide. Manages AMC-wide computer enterprise, operates and maintains AMC
command and control and transportation systems, provides Information Assurance for all AMC systems,
and manages and maintains consolidated servers across AMC and selected Air Force systems.
   1.1. The 805th Communications Support Squadron provides software development and maintenance,
   database engineering, administers AMC’s computer security programs, system integration testing and
   quality assurance for United States Transportation Command (USTRANSCOM), AMC, and Tanker
   Airlift Control Center (TACC).
   1.2. The 868th Communications Squadron provides command, control, communications and com-
   puter (C4) support to USTRANSCOM, AMC, and worldwide aerial ports. Operates the AMC Net-
   work Operations Security Center (NOSC). Installs, operates, and maintains metropolitan area
   network/local area network infrastructure.

2. Command. The HQ AMC Communications Group is assigned to HQ AMC/SC.

3. Responsibilities.
    3.1. HQ AMC Communications Group
       3.1.1. The HQ AMC Communications Group provides systems administration for all AMC com-
       mand and control (C2) and transportation systems.
       3.1.2. Tests and validates mission-critical software procured by the government to ensure interop-
       erability with Defense Transportation System assets.
       3.1.3. Operates and maintains the Scott AFB information technology (IT) infrastructure and sup-
       ports USTRANSCOM by providing Operations and Maintenance of its IT infrastructure.
       3.1.4. Develops and maintains AMC-unique software applications.
       3.1.5. Engineers and sustains AMC computer networks.
       3.1.6. Provides integrated attack warning and attack assessment while controlling and defending
       AMC networks.
    3.2. 805th Communications Support Squadron
       3.2.1. Manages and administers AMC’s computer security program.
       3.2.2. Directs and controls C2 capabilities supporting the AMC TACC’s global mission.
       3.2.3. Provides reliable C4 systems supporting the TACC Global Reach mission and AMC Crisis
       Support Staff.
       3.2.4. Provides premier IT support for USTRANSCOM, including C4 support, personal computer
       maintenance, and video teleconference/audiovisual support.
       3.2.5. Develops, tests, and maintains network applications for AMC.
       3.2.6. Maintains and administers Communications Security, Computer Security, Emission Secu-
       rity, and Security Awareness Training and Education for AMC.
       3.2.7. Manages radio frequency assignment for AMC airborne platforms and mission support
       forces operating worldwide.
       3.2.8. Provides independent system and interoperability testing for software applications.
       3.2.9. Maintains custom web applications, network centric software development and databases
       of Air Force portals.
       3.2.10. Manages tasking and deployment of AMC Global Mobility Task Force communica-tions
       assets.
       3.2.11. Oversees AMC IT enterprise planning efforts and resources management.
    3.3. 868th Communications Squadron
       3.3.1. Plans, develops, installs, operates, maintains, and protects AMC C2 communications sys-
       tems.
       3.3.2. Operates and maintains AMC’s NOSC.
       3.3.3. Provides information assurance for combat operations with network administration,
       defense, and infrastructure for global operations.

    3.3.5. Evaluates new hardware and software application for AMC systems.
    3.3.6. Maintains secure system administration and program management of Global Command and
