This instruction implements policy and guidance in support of the following Air Force
Instructions (AFI): AFI 10-712, Telecommunication Monitoring and Assessment Program, AFI
33-112, Information Technology Hardware Asset Management, AFI 33-590, Radio
Management, and AFI 64-101, Offutt Supplement (Sup) 1 Multi-Channel Video Programming
Distribution (Broadcast Cable), AFMAN 33-145, Collaboration Services and Voice Systems
Management. This instruction issues responsibilities, policies, and procedures for the
Telecommunications Control Officer (TCO) process. It applies to Air Force Weather Agency
(AFWA), 1st Weather Group (1 WXG) (Offutt AFB), and 2nd Weather Group (2 WXG) (Offutt
AFB). Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed
of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System Records Disposition
Schedule located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims.            Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the office of primary responsibility (OPR) using
AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field
to the appropriate functional chain of command. This Publication does not apply to the Air
National Guard or Air Force Reserve Command. The use of the name or mark of any specific
manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply
endorsement by the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This instruction is revised to reflect changes in organizational names, office symbols and TCO
management processes.

1. General. The TCO manages telecommunications for the AFWA, 1 WXG, and 2 WXG units
physically located on Offutt AFB, NE. The TCO acts as a liaison for the maintenance of existing
telecommunications and the coordination of new telecommunications between the
requester/customer and the 55 CS. This document defines the procedures and requirements for
acquiring new telecommunications equipment and systems.
2. Policies. Landline telephones are governed by AFMAN 33-145. Wireless communications
(i.e., cell phones and pagers) are governed by AFI 33-590. BlackBerry® devices are governed
by AFI 33-200, Information Assurance Management (paragraph.3.42), DoDD 8100.2 Use of
Commercial Wireless Devices, Services and Technologies in the DoD GIG (paragraph 4). Cable
television is governed by AFI64-101_OFFUTTAFBSUP_I and applicable contract.
3. Procedures.
     3.1. The Requester/Customer Will:
        3.1.1. Complete AFWA Information Technology (IT) Request (3215) for all
        telecommunications requirements. This can be found on the AFWA SharePoint site at
        https://spx.afwa.af.mil/Utilities/support/ITRequest/default.aspx. This includes the
        following:
           3.1.1.1. BlackBerry®, Cell phones, Video Teleconferencing, Fax Machine and
           Telephone: customer shall obtain approval from their staff director or group
           commander via the automated AFWA IT Request process and coordinate through
           AFWA/A6 to the AFWA TCO for technical solution.
           3.1.1.2. Cable television: customer shall complete an AF Form 9, Request for
           Purchase, and obtain approval from AFWA/DS.
        3.1.2. Submit completed documents to the AFWA TCO. This submission should be
        accomplished at least 60 days before the work is required to help ensure completion.
     3.2. The AFWA TCO Will:
        3.2.1. Review and validate all new telecommunications requirements to ensure
        completeness and compliance with all policies.
        3.2.2. Ensure all wireless electronic devices are on the Air Force Approved Products List
        (APL) and are on a vendor Blanket Purchase Agreement.
        3.2.3. Land, fax and video teleconferencing lines: use the Work Order Management
        System (WOMS) online program to submit requirements to 55 CS.
        3.2.4. Wireless devices and accessories: ensure funds are available prior to purchasing
        equipment.
        3.2.5. Cable television: submit requirements to the 55 CS using the WOMS online
        program, AF Form 9s to 55 CS/RA (cable television point of contact) and approval
        documentation.
        3.2.6. Monitor and track the requirement until completion.
        3.2.7. Maintain copies of all supporting documents such as AF Form 1297, user’s
        agreement, and AFWA Forms 64 (if applicable).

      3.2.8. Ensure all BlackBerry® handheld devices are added to the Personal Wireless
      Communication Systems IT Equipment account.
      3.2.9. Create WOMS ticket for Blackberry Administration Server (BAS) access for all
      Blackberry devices.
      3.2.10. Dispose of excess equipment IAW AFI 33-112, Information Technology
      Hardware Asset Management.
4. Funding.
   4.1. Landline Telephones:
      4.1.1. AFWA TCO will coordinate purchase of all telephone equipment.
      4.1.2. All telephone instruments must be approved by appropriate A-Staff Directorate or
      Group CC, via local IT request process to the A6 Directorate.
   4.2. BlackBerry® Devices and Cell Phones: AFWA/A6 will approve and fund the
   purchase of all BlackBerry® Devices and cell phones as approved by the requestor’s A-Staff
   Directorate or Group CC.
   4.3. Cable Television: Customer will fund for all installations and recurring monthly
   charges until the end of the fiscal year. AFWA/A6 will assume funding for subsequent fiscal
   year charges.
