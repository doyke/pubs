SUMMARY OF REVISIONS

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Adds reference to
81 TRW prohibitions on tobacco use; clarifies reference to limits of tobacco products in indoor
recreation facilities; clarifies designated tobacco use within the 81 TRW; adds clarification for
student and instructor prohibitions on use of tobacco products during the duty day; clarifies 81
TRW designated tobacco use areas; and adds Health Promotion Manager’s responsibility in
providing an on-going review of base-wide tobacco use activity and recommends policy.

2.1.1. (Added) The 81 TRW goal is to eliminate tobacco use.

2.2.3.1. (Added) At the direction of the 81 TRW Commander, all indoor recreation facilities will
be tobacco free except specific designated areas at Gaude Lanes, the enlisted and officer lounges.
These areas will phase into becoming tobacco free by 7 December 2002 in accordance with the
Department of Defense memorandum dated 7 December 1999.

2.2.8.1.1. (Added) (AETC Sup 1) The use of tobacco products by 81 TRW students is prohibited
at Keesler AFB during all aspects of training during the school duty hours. This is to include any
planned student activity during the normal duty hours. Non-prior service airman will follow the
guidance in AETCI 36-2216, Technical Training Administration of Military Standards and
Discipline Training, for their respective phase.

2.2.8.1.2. (Added) (AETC Sup 1) It is inappropriate for active duty instructors, civilian
instructors, other permanent party personnel and other civilians on Keesler to use tobacco
products during the school duty hours while within eyesight of 81 TRW students.

2.2.10.1. (Added) Local tobacco use areas will be designated by appropriate Wing/Group
Commanders and must be 30 feet away, as space allows, from the facility.

5.1.1. (Added) The Health Promotion Manager will report as a standard agenda item the 81
TRW tobacco use rates at the quarterly Health Promotion Council. The Health Promotion
Manager will chair a Tobacco Ad Hoc Committee in order to provide an ongoing review of base-
wide tobacco use activity and to recommend policy. The Committee will meet quarterly, or as
often as deemed necessary, and be represented by all Keesler Squadrons, Defense Commissary
Agency (DECA), Civilian Personnel, and Staff Judge Advocate.
