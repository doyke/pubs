1. Mission. The Deputy Chief of Staff (DCS) of the Air Force for Intelligence, Surveillance and
Reconnaissance (ISR) (AF/A2), pursuant to 10 USC §§ 8031-38, and as documented by
paragraph 4.3 of AFMD 1, Headquarters Air Force, and this Headquarters Air Force (HAF)
Mission Directive, assists the Secretary of the Air Force, other Secretariat offices, and the Chief
of Staff in carrying out the ISR mission of the Department of the Air Force. The AF/A2 serves
as the HAF focal point responsible for the end-to-end functional management of all Air Force
ISR capabilities; developing and implementing the Air Force policies and guidance for
developing and managing Air Force ISR activities; leading the organization, planning, and
programming of Air Force intelligence; providing planning, programming, and budgeting
oversight; and being responsible for professional development, training, education, readiness,
and deployment of the intelligence force. As Secretary of the Air Force, I retain ultimate
responsibility for all policies related to the Department of the Air Force. Within his/her areas of
responsibility, the AF/A2 prepares policies for my approval and issues official
guidance/procedures to ensure implementation of those policies. The AF/A2 also assists the
Chief of Staff of the Air Force in his role, pursuant to 10 U.S.C. § 151, as a member of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (JCS).

2. Organizational Relationships. The Secretary of the Air Force is responsible for, and has all
legal authority necessary to conduct, the affairs of the Department of the Air Force. The
Secretariat, the Chief of Staff of the Air Force, and Air Staff offices perform their Department of
the Air Force functions subject to the authority, direction, and control of the Secretary of the Air
Force.

2.1. The AF/A2 reports directly to the Chief of Staff of the Air Force, but provides support to
the Secretary of the Air Force, the Under Secretary of the Air Force, other Secretariat offices, the

Chief of Staff of the Air Force, and other Air Staff offices. The Secretary of the Air Force may
re-delegate authority to the AF/A2, but the Secretary of the Air Force, through the Chief of Staff
of the Air Force and, as appropriate, Assistant Secretaries of the Air Force, retains ultimate
responsibility for all matters affecting intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance programs of
the Department of the Air Force.

2.2. The AF/A2 is part of the Air Staff and as such works closely with other HAF offices to
assist the Secretary of the Air Force and the Chief of Staff in carrying out his or her
responsibilities. The AF/A2 and the Office of the AF/A2 work in close cooperation with the
HAF officials, and their respective offices, which are responsible, pursuant to Chapters 803 and
805 of Title 10 (10 U.S.C. §§ 8013-8023 and §§ 8031-8038), for assisting the Secretary of the
Air Force and the Chief of Staff in carrying out his or her responsibilities.

2.2.1. Pursuant to Headquarters Operating Instruction (HOI) 90-1, two or more HAF two-
letter/digit organizations, Field Operating Agencies, and/or Direct Reporting Units with
responsibilities in the same functional area are encouraged to develop "standard operating
procedures (SOP)" that set forth procedures enabling covered organizations to fulfill and carry
out their respective missions, roles, and responsibilities. There are no SOPs between AF/A2 and
any other organization.
2.3. Reporting to the AF/A2 are two Field Operating Agencies: the Air Force Intelligence
Analysis Agency (AFIAA) and the Air Force Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance
Agency (AF ISR Agency), whose missions are documented in AF Mission Directive (AFMD) 21
and Air Force Mission Directive (AFMD) 15, respectively.
3. Responsibilities.
The AF/A2 is specifically responsible for:
3.1. Serving as the AF ISR focal point across the Department of Defense (DoD) and other
government agencies.

3.2. Serving as the office of primary responsibility (OPR) for ISR to include planning,
programming, policy, guidance, and intelligence force development for both the active and
reserve components.

3.3. Developing ISR strategy, policy, guidance standards, and concepts of operations for ISR
capabilities and programming ISR flight plans/roadmaps.

3.4. Supporting development and overseeing implementation of AF ISR doctrine and
requirements.

3.5. Formulating intelligence policy and guidance, specifically to oversee policy, planning,
programming, resource allocation, and program evaluation for the National Intelligence Program
(NIP) and the Military Intelligence Program (MIP) in support of AF ISR.

3.6. Assisting the Chief of Staff in providing operationally ready forces in response to the needs
of the combatant commanders through measured analysis, assessment, and actionable decisions
based on current and predicted readiness indicators.

3.7. Formulating the Air Staff position on AF ISR matters under consideration by the JCS and
National Security Council (NSC) and acting as the single point of contact (POC) in HAF.

3.8. Developing AF/A2 guidance to implement policy in support of the capabilities-based
requirements process.

3.9. Developing Air Force policy and guidance to ensure ISR activities comply with domestic
and international law.

3.10. Developing policy to ensure proper supervision and control of Air Force intelligence
activities.

3.11. Publishing AF Intelligence Oversight instructions, ensuring training of AF/A2 personnel in
intelligence oversight procedures, complying with those procedures, and serving as a member of
the Air Force Intelligence Oversight Panel.

3.12. Serving as the AF senior official and OPR for all national-level Intelligence Community
matters, to include the Military Intelligence Board (MIB), the ISR Integration Council, and the
Intelligence Policy Advisory Group (IPAG).

3.13. Serving as the Air Force senior official responsible for the oversight, management, and
administration of the sensitive compartmented information (SCI) security program including
policies and implementation for the protection of sources and methods, special security office
system and security awareness and education programs.

3.14. Serves as Air Force representative in National Military Command Center/Joint
Operations/Intelligence Operations Center and National Operational Intelligence Watch Officers
Network (NOIWON).

4. Delegations of Authority: Attachment 1 lists my delegated authorities to the AF/A2. The
authorities delegated to the AF/A2 by this HAF Mission Directive may generally be re-delegated
to other Department of the Air Force officials unless the attached delegation or superseding law,
regulation, or DoD issuance expressly prohibits re-delegation. While the authorities are
delegated to the AF/A2, the exercise of the authorities remains subject to the oversight and
control of the Secretary of the Air Force, any Assistant Secretary of the Air Force having
oversight, and the Chief of Staff. Any re-delegation of authority made shall not be effective
unless in writing. Any person re-delegating authorities in accordance with this Directive may
further restrict or condition the authority being re-delegated.

A2.1. The Deputy Chief of Staff, Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance (AF/A2) is
responsible for the overall supervision of all matters pertaining to Air Force intelligence,
surveillance and reconnaissance (ISR) capabilities. The AF/A2 is the HAF focal point
responsible for the end-to-end functional management of all Air Force ISR capabilities;
developing and implementing the Air Force policies and guidance for developing, managing, and
operating Air Force ISR activities; leading the organization, planning, and programming of Air
Force intelligence, providing planning, programming, and budgeting oversight; and being
responsible for professional development, training, education, readiness, and deployment of the
intelligence force. Implements national-level security directives and personnel administrative
actions directed by the Secretary of the Air Force.

A2.2. Three-letter/digit subordinate offices include:

    A2.2.1. Directorate of Analysis and Estimates (AF/A2A). AF/A2A directs and manages
for USAF all overhead imagery requirements and collection. Serves as the AF/A2 focal point for
the global Tactics Analysis Reporting Program for theater air components, directs force
protection intelligence policy, and provides tailored products for special mission support and
time-sensitive indication and warning (I&W) intelligence to Air Force Senior leaders.
Represents AF/A2 on National Intelligence Estimates. Assists in the development of IC analytic
standards, represents AF in IC analytic standard workings/committees and directs
implementation for AF/A2.
    A2.2.2. Directorate of ISR Capabilities (AF/A2C). AF/A2C is the HAF lead for
intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance (ISR) capabilities used by the Air Force. Works
with OSD, ODNI, and Intelligence Community (IC) authorities to translate higher-level guidance
into Service specific policy and guidance on the utilization of ISR assets. Monitors the
HAFMD 1-33 4 SEPTEMBER 2009                                                                   11


performance and effectiveness of fielded ISR assets. Directs the implementation of plans and
procedures to enhance the collection, processing, exploitation, and dissemination of ISR data.
Focal point for furnishing subject matter expertise to forums on targeting and force level
applications, ground, airborne, and overhead ISR system/sensor capabilities. Interfaces with
Congressional, OSD, Joint, and MAJCOM staffs to provide AF ISR forces and assets to joint
commanders.

    A2.2.3. Directorate of ISR Strategy, Integration and Doctrine (AF/A2D). A2D develops
and promulgates AF ISR strategy and plans in support of National Security Strategy, National
Defense Strategy, National Military Strategy, and national intelligence community strategic
direction. Formulates and establishes AF ISR doctrine. Liaises with key strategy, plans, and
doctrine offices in the Joint Staff, OSD, and other Services. Guides Air Force mid and long-
range ISR capability and requirements development and leads End-to-End capability portfolio
management. Leads AF ISR Quadrennial Defense, Quadrennial Roles and Missions, and
Quadrennial Intelligence Community Reviews. Leads the AF Senior ISR Officer Symposiums
and ISR awards programs. Provides direction for management, readiness, and deployment
activities for Total Force ISR personnel. Provides management, policy, and guidance for ISR
personnel education, training, and career field development.

    A2.2.4. Directorate of ISR Resources (AF/A2R). AF/A2R represents Air Force ISR
policy, requirements, manpower and funding issues in National Intelligence Community (IC) and
DoD forums. Represents Air Force ISR in the IC Policy Advisory Group. Develops and
oversees execution of the Air Force ISR program and budget in the Military Intelligence
Program (MIP) and National Intelligence Program (NIP), except for space acquisition programs
managed by the Under Secretary of the Air Force. Develops Air Force ISR policy and positions
on Congressional issues, inquiries and actions. Provides management and oversight functions
for the Air Force Foreign Material Program in partnership with AF/TEZ as defined in AFI 99-
114, Foreign Material Program. Provides functional management of Air Force ISR intelligence
agreements with foreign nations. Guides Air Force special security programs.

    A2.2.5. Directorate of ISR Innovations (AF/A2U). AF/A2U manages rapid insertion of
advanced technological solutions to peacetime and combat Air Force operations. Orchestrates
quick response deployment of off-the-shelf imagery and geospatial capabilities, Unmanned
Aerial Systems (UAS) and targeting applications. Sponsors rapid prototyping and fielding of
technology applications to support combat, headquarters, and unit-level requirements. Manages
AF commercial satellite imagery activities with OSD, JCS and the Services.

    A2.2.6. Directorate of Special Programs (AF/A2Z).                AF/A2Z ensures effective
management, oversight, and integration of operational, research and development, and limited
access programs within the intelligence community. Ensures these capabilities and resources are
available to departmental operational and intelligence customers. It is also the AF/A2 focal point
for coordinating Joint and NSC matters. Guides Air Force intelligence oversight program.
