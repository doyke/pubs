This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-29, Military Standards. This
instruction extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-2907, Unfavorable
Information (UIF) Program. It provides guidance and procedures on establishing 507th Air
Refueling Wing (ARW) procedures for the Headquarters (HQ) Air Force Reserve Command
Vice-Commander (AFRC/CV) directed program to up-channel significant incidents of
misconduct or adverse actions in a timely manner. It applies to all 507 ARW units. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force Information Management Tool (AF IMT) 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF IMTs 847 from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual
(AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force
Records     Disposition    Schedule     (RDS)     located    at   https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims. This instruction requires collecting and maintaining information protected
by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by (5 U.S.C. 552a).

1. Responsibility:
   1.1. It is the responsibility of the Wing Commander to up-channel information concerning
   personal misconduct, inappropriate behavior, or Uniform Code of Military Justice
   (UCMJ)/civilian law violations committed by reserve members within twenty-four hours
   after the incident or upon learning of the allegations, to the AFRC/CV via e-mail using PIN
   format.

2. Unit Responsibility:
     2.1. It is the responsibility of the Group Commander, Squadron Commander, Squadron First
     Sergeant, supervisor, or highest-ranking on-scene individual to up-channel actions through
     the chain of command as soon as possible. All commanders should exercise their judgment
     concerning the severity/gravity of each incident, but when in doubt, forward the information.
     See Attachment 2 for the Consolidated Personnel, Security Forces and Judge Advocate
     Checklist.
3. Reportable Offenses:
     3.1. Allegations of misconduct include, officer misconduct, racial offenses, sexual offenses,
     unprofessional relationships, fraternization, arrests, or convictions by civilian authorities for
     anything except minor traffic violations.
4. Reporting Format:
     4.1. When reporting the action up the chain of command, use the format that follows. The
     Wing Commander will use a similar format when sending the report via e-mail:

      INFORMATION                                       EXAMPLE ANSWER
      Date                                              16 Dec 08
      Unit Organization                                 507 ARW Tinker AFB, OK
      Grade/Rank/Name                                   02/1 LT/Joe B. Donuts
      Summary of Incident                               Member arrested at 0200 hours and
                                                        charged with drunk driving
      Local Staff Agencies Aware/Notified               507 ARW/JA, 72 ABW/SFS


5. Program Intent:
     5.1. The intent of this program is to obtain early notification, not to review actions against
     reserve members.

                                 CONSOLIDATED CHECKLIST
                                     (DPM, SFS, AND JA)

I. DPM CHECKLIST
     A. OPR/EPR DUE? (Consider appropriateness of delaying close-out, making it a referral, or
     even doing one, etc.)
     B. Projected promotion? Next board? Line number for promotion? (Consider delaying
     removing from list, NQP, PRF, etc.)
     C. Decoration in the works? (Consider withholding it until case is decided)
     D. Training classes? (PME, formal school, etc.)(Consider withholding until case is decided)
     E. Reenlistment/Retirement? (Consider denial of reenlistment/voluntary extension if
     appropriate or delay in retirement)
     F. Relocation/PCS/Separation actions? (No IRR until issues decided)
     G. Intra-base moves pending?
     H. ART/traditional reservist?
     I. Temporarily relieved from position? (Commander, supervisor, etc.)
II. SFS CHECKLIST (Wing/CCs should follow the following items)
     A. Evaluate case against DoD 5200.2R, Personnel Security Program.
     B. Check with SPAR for prior record of offenses.
     C. Which investigative agency involved? (OSI, SPOI, CIV, etc.)
     D. Does subject occupy position in which he has access to classified info, unescorted entry
     into restricted areas or access to sensitive resources such as drugs, firearms, funds, etc.?
     (Consider withdrawing any or all of these.)
     E. Should SSF be established?
     F. Notify 497 IG/INS of subject’s security clearance being “pending adjudication” as
     necessary.
NOTE: AFRC/SP WILL EVALUATE THE INCIDENT AND IF APPROPRIATE WILL
CONTACT THE WING/CC.
