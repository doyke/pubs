AFI 10-2501, Air Force Emergency Management (EM) Program Planning and Operations, 24
January 2007/Incorporating Through Change 2, 6 April 2009 is supplemented as follows: The
purpose of this supplement is to establish policy and procedures for managing the Osan AB
Emergency Management Program. It provides guidance for conducting unit programs, outlines
responsibilities for supporting Osan AB Emergency Management related operations plans and
managing affiliated training programs. This supplement applies to all units assigned, attached, or
deployed to Osan AB. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through Major Command (MAJCOM)
publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

3.11.1.2.5.5. (Added) Block 2-4, and 7-8. Last Exercise Date represents the date individual

Readiness Exercise, Emergency Management Exercise, or as part of a Operational Readiness
Inspection or Compliance Inspection. Date Trained includes all training necessary to perform
assigned function, as outlined in AFI 10-2501, Chapter 6. Physical Address is where the person
resides while stationed at Osan AB. Building Assignment is the CPS Shelter Management Team
members are assigned to (for Tier I and Tier II facilities). SMT members assigned to Tier III
facilities (Dorms) will have “DORM” listed as building assignment. All other blocks are self
explanatory.
3.11.1.2.5.6. (Added) Block 5. CBRN Military represents all assigned military personnel.
CBRN Civilians represents emergency essential civilians only. EOC and UCC are those
respective members outlined in Blocks 2, 3, 7, and 8. Required block is the total number of
individuals assigned to either the unit or the position. Trained block is the total number of
required individuals who have been fully trained. Percentage block is the number of trained
personnel divided by the number required training, multiplied by 100 (Example: 8 personnel
trained/10 personnel required=.80x100=80%).
3.11.1.2.5.7. (Added) Block 6. Date Created should be no later than 30 days after the date
listed on the current Osan AB IEMP 10-2. Date Approved is the date the Unit Commander
approves the unit 10-2 checklist. Date Reviewed is the date 51 CES/CEX personnel review unit
checklist for adequacy.

3.12.1.2.1. (Added) Osan units operating facilities containing collective protective systems
(CPS) are required to maintain a minimum of three trained SMT personnel per shift at all times.
If SMT personnel are on TDY, leave, or otherwise cannot perform assigned duties (i.e., profile),
the unit is responsible for having a trained substitute available. Those personnel may be
augmented by additional unit personnel drafted to assist with surge operations for processing
personnel into shelters during shift changes or after a chemical attack. Newly appointed
personnel will have 6 months remaining until DEROS upon the time of appointment.
3.12.1.2.2. (Added) For collective protection shelter (CPS) facilities: A minimum of one
Shelter Supervisor per shift, minimum of two CCA Monitors per shift.
