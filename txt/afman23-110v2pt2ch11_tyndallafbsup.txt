11.2.1. Local purchase, Base Service Store, and Tool Issue items are not managed or stocked in
base supply. Normal method of obtaining these items is the Government Purchase Card (GPC)
or AF Form 9, Request for Purchase. GSA-managed weapon system parts may be processed
through Supply. Weapon system parts are parts identified in USAF Technical Orders, Parts
Manuals, or Illustrated Parts Breakdown. Civilian contractors not authorized GPC cards may
order GSA weapon system parts if the terms of their contract allow for GFE support. However,
Base supply does not stock or accept issue requests for GSA-managed, non-weapon system,
administrative or janitorial supplies.
11.2.1.1. On-base delivery is made using the sweep method. Priority 01-02 issues and due-out
releases will be delivered within one hour, priority 03 issue and due-out releases every two
hours, and priority 04-05 issue and due-out releases twice a day (morning and afternoon) with
delivery no later than the next duty day. Off-base issue and due-out releases are forwarded no
later than the close of transaction business day to Outbound for shipment to the off-base location.
11.2.2. Central Storage Local Checklist will be used for customer call-in issue requests in lieu of
the AF Form 2005. After verification of processing, completed checklists are retained in file for
30 days and then destroyed.
11.4.2.2.2. Bench Stock Support will mark the white labels produced by the Daily Bin Labels
(D38/NGV837) program in green for shelf life and blue for precious metals before forwarding to
organizational bench stock accounts. These labels will be replaced by the organization when
permanent colored labels are produced by Bin Label (R38/NGV789) program.

11B1.2.1. Central Storage Local Checklist will be used in lieu of the AF Form 2005 in Central
Storage. Pre-serialized AF Forms 2005, Issue/Turn-in Request, are used at the Customer Service
call-in point.

11B1.3. Note: Customer Service is the call-in point for non-EAID (EMC 1, Activity Code P)
items.

11B18.6.3. The requesting activity is responsible for the purchase, storage, accountability and
refill of compressed gas cylinders using GPC procedures. Gaseous cylinders are not managed by
Base Supply.

11B18.10.1.3.1. Urgency of Need B post-post issue requests will not be processed for items
under inventory.

11B18.22. Central Storage call-in point processes all customer issue requests for hazardous
materials (IEX 9) from base supply stocks. Customers will input hazardous issue request into
Enterprise Environmental Safety and Occupational Health-Management Information System
(EESOH-MIS) and then Central Storage processes the issue in ESSOH-MIS and Enterprise
Solution – Supply (ES-S).

11B18.22.3. If a customer processes a hazardous issue in ES-S without EESOH-MIS
authorization, Central Storage will suspend delivery, advise the customer to initiate EESOH-MIS
authorization approval with 325 CES/CEV, and suspend the issue. The material will not be
delivered unless EESOH-MIS authorization is obtained. Central Storage will reverse-post the
transaction or obtain Funds Manager approval to process a Credit Code ―Y‖ (GSD only) turn-in.
Turn-in of alpha budget coded items will not require Funds Manager approval or a Credit Code
―Y‖.

11C7.5. Maintenance will process cannibalization action through the CAMS/IMDS /IMDS and
perform necessary documentation IAW AETC Sup 1, AFI 21-101.

11C16.5. AWP end items will be stored in maintenance shops. The respective shop will
maintain an AWP suspense file. The Maintenance Activity will order bit and pieces through
CAMS/IMDS /IMDS .

11C16.7. The Supply Contractor Program Manager will direct a surveillance of maintenance
AWP monitors when the quarterly IREP meeting indicates deficiencies, concerns, or systemic
problems related to AWP management at the maintenance shop level or when requested by the
LRD.
