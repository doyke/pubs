Air Force Instruction (AFI) 31-601, Industrial Security Program Management, 29 June 2005 and
AFI 31-601_PACAFSUP 1, 7 March 2006, are supplemented as follows: The purpose of this
supplement is to establish wing guidance and unit responsibilities on proper handling, protection,
destruction and transmission of classified materials. The supplement applies to all assigned,
attached, Geographically Separated Units (GSUs), tenant units and staff agencies under the 51st
Fighter Wing (FW) security program. NOTE: Commanders of tenant units who have not
elected Information Protection (IP) support must establish a Memorandum of Understanding
(MOU) or staff a program “waive out” memorandum. The “waive out” memorandum should be
endorsed by the MAJCOM, DRU, or FOA who has security administrative control over the
tenant organization. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through Major Command (MAJCOM)
publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm . The authority to collect and maintain
the records prescribed in this instruction is Title 50 U.S.C. 797 and Title 10 U.S.C. 8012.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication has been substantially revised throughout and must be thoroughly reviewed.
This revision outlines the 51 FW/IP and Special Security Office (SSO) roles. Program “waive
out” procedures were added to clarify jurisdictional issues in relation to protection and
management of classified information. The Commander, 51 FW (51 FW/CC) retains
responsibility and authority for authorizing and/or granting all DoD contractors access to the
installation. Paragraph 1.5.9 has been deleted and PA warning statement has been added.
1.6.1.1. The 51 FW/CC designates contractor operations requiring access to classified
information on Osan Air Base (AB) and GSUs falling under the 51 FW as either intermittent
visitors (less than 90 days) or integrated visitor groups (more than 90 days).
1.6.1.2. The 51 FW/CC delegates Visitor Group Security Agreement (VGSA) approval to the
51st Fighter Wing, Chief, Information Protection (51 FW/IP), also known as the Information
Security Program Manager (ISPM).
1.6.1.2.1. (Added) Parent organizations of any Tenant or GSU that decline participation in the
Wing’s Information, Personnel, and Industrial Security programs inherit the responsibility of
Industrial Security Program Management and oversight.
1.6.1.2.2. (Added) Refer to AFI 31-401_PACAFSUP_51FWSUP, Information Security
Program Management (PA), paragraph 1.4.2.1., for the list of exempt tenant and GSU
organizations.
1.6.1.3. Contractors operating on Osan AB and GSUs that fall under Osan AB for parent support
will be integrated into and must comply with the installation and sponsoring unit’s security
program, as well as the VGSA.
1.6.1.4. The Chief, Information Protection is designated as the ISPM, see AFI 31-
401_PACAFSUP_51FWSUP, paragraph 1.3.4.
1.6.1.4.1. (51 FW) 51 FW/IP retains the Industrial Security oversight for all industrial security
operations to include program administration and the Special Security Officer will manage the
Sensitive Compartmented Information (SCI) portion of all on-base contractor operations.
1.6.2. Sponsoring Activity is synonymous with sponsoring unit/organization’s commander. The
commander of the sponsoring activity retains the overall responsibility for implementing the
industrial security program.
1.6.2.1. The unit security manager is the sponsoring activity’s point of contact for security
matters pertaining to industrial security contracts and will coordinate with the 51 FW/IP.
1.6.2.2. Sponsoring units are responsible for drafting the DD Form 254, Contract Security
Classification Specification Department of Defense. NOTE: A majority of the contracts are let
by contracting offices other that the servicing USFK contracting office. Upon receipt of a DD
Form 254, that was initiated at another installation or a visit request from a civilian contractor
requiring access to classified information, the sponsoring unit through their unit security

initial, refresher, and annual training as required by AFI 31-601, paragraph 3.1.4.

processing, and handling incoming visitor group requests.

contractor employees have security container combinations.
1.6.2.7.6. (Added) Monitor/track all contractor employees’ security clearances and notify the
company’s home office security representative if security clearances are within 6 months of
becoming out of scope.
1.6.3. The 411th Contracting Support Brigade, Korea, is the responsible office for the
establishment of all local USAF National Industrial Security Program Operating Manual
(NISPOM) contracts on Osan AB. The contracting office must coordinate all DD Form 254 with
the 51 FW/IP office.

9.1.1. COMSEC information or material will not be released to contractors without the approval
of the installation COMSEC manager. When a contractor requires access and/or stores
COMSEC material or documents, the sponsoring unit will coordinate and obtain approval
through the base COMSEC officer. Applicable EMSEC clauses will be referenced by the
procuring contracting office in DD Form 254, Item 11i. The DD Form 254 will be coordinated
with the base EMSEC manager before obtaining ISPM coordination.
9.1.2. Information Assurance office through unit work group managers will ensure contractors
accessing AIS and/or networks that process sensitive but unclassified and classified information
will abide by AFMAN 33-152, User Responsibilities and Guidance for Information Systems,
requirements. Specifically, email addresses must be clearly identified as contractors. Contractor
personnel with access to the unclassified network should be removed from general distribution or
Plain Language Addresses (PLA), and not given complete access to share drives. Contractors
must be restricted from certain folders that do not pertain to the performance of their contract.
Contractors will complete all Information Assurance Awareness Program requirements prior to
being granted access to the base computer network IAW AFMAN 33-282, Computer Security
(COMSEC).
9.1.2.1. Contractor employees requiring access to classified government Automated Information
System (AIS) networks, or information under the terms of a government contract must have a
security clearance. The facility security officer processes these clearances according to the
NISPOM. Contractor employees who require access to unclassified government information
systems require personnel security investigations and trustworthiness determinations processed
through their home office in accordance with DoD 5200.2-R, Personnel Security Program, AFI
31-501, Personnel Security Program Management, and AFMAN 33-282.
