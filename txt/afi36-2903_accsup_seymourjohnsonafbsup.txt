AFI 36-2903 is supplemented as follows: This supplement provides 4th Fighter Wing guidance
and information on the proper wear of the uniform items. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/.

Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Form 847s from the field through the appropriate functional chain of command.
1.4.12.1. (Added) 4 FW personnel may not wear the ABU, Fitness Center, Food Service, or
Aircrew Flight Dress uniforms while consuming alcohol at off-base establishments.
6.1.1. The authorized wear period for the cold weather accessories is from 1 October – 30 April.
6.1.10. Local (Squadron and higher) Commanders may determine wear of Sage Green Fleece
indoors as mission needs dictate. ABU blouse will be worn at all times underneath the Fleece.

This authority is only applicable during authorized wear period for cold weather accessories per

6.2.11. When to wear Headgear. No-hat areas are not designated as no-salute areas.
Commanders may designate no-salute areas where saluting would be highly repetitious or
otherwise unfeasible. The approved installation no-hat areas are: inside the security fence
surrounding the flightline and ammo yard, the routes between the Fighter Squadrons and the
flightline for aircrew only during flying operations, and the area between the Fire Department
(building 4601) and Jabara Ave.
6.3.3.1.1. (Added) Headphones and earphones are authorized for use inside the fitness center,
the trail located near the Medical Group, the 2 mile trail located near the fitness center, and the
fitness center track. Use during organized PT sessions or testing is at the discretion of the unit
commander.
7.1.2.1. (Added) Wear of the PTU/IPTU is required for Airmen engaging in the FIP, formal
physical fitness assessments, or organized unit PT sessions.
8.4.5.1. (Added) Nametag colors.          The following nametag lettering/embroidery and
background colors shall be utilized:
8.4.5.1.1. (Added) 333d Lancers. Silver lettering/embroidery. Red background.
8.4.5.1.2. (Added) 334th Eagles. Yellow lettering/embroidery. Blue background.
8.4.5.1.3. (Added) 335th Chiefs. White lettering/embroidery. Green background.
8.4.5.1.4. (Added) 336th Rockets. Black lettering/embroidery. Yellow background.
8.4.5.1.5. (Added) Wing/Group staff personnel have the option to wear colors as prescribed by
their respective flying squadron or white lettering/embroidery on a blue background.
8.4.5.2. (Added) Naming convention. The following naming convention shall be utilized by all
personnel, assigned or attached to the 4 FW, authorized to wear the FDU.
8.4.5.2.1. (Added) Monday – Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. For officers, first and last name
is mandatory. Nicknames/callsigns are prohibited. (e.g., member may use "SAMUEL JONES"
or “SAM JONES”) For enlisted personnel first name, last name and abbreviated rank are
mandatory. Nicknames/callsigns are prohibited. (e.g., member may use "TSGT SAMUEL
JONES," "TSGT SAM JONES)"
8.4.5.2.2. (Added) Friday: In addition to 8.4.5.2, members may choose to utilize
nicknames/callsigns in place of first name provided they are in good taste. For officers, last
name is mandatory. For enlisted personnel last name and abbreviated rank are mandatory. (e.g.,
Officers: “ACE JONES,” Enlisted: “TSGT ACE JONES”).
9.1.2. (Added) Maintenance Coveralls will be worn as required when performing aircraft
maintenance duties. Blue coveralls will be provided to each member as necessary and may have
USAF name tapes applied to the left breast and the member’s last name on the right breast. The
appropriate squadron patch may be applied to the right shoulder.
