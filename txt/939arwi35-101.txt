This instruction establishes procedures to ensure maximum compliance with intent and purpose of AFPD
35-1, Public Affairs Management. Due to the peculiarities in unit support and tenancy at Portland Interna-
tional Airport (ANG) and to ensure accurate and responsible release of information about accidents, either
on or off base, the following information is provided as guidance and is applicable to all members
assigned to the 939th Air Refueling Wing located at Portland IAP, Oregon.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS

This revision changes unit designation form 939th Rescue Wing to 939th Air Refueling Wing. A bar ( | )
designates revision from previous edition.


1. On-Base Accidents. The public affairs office for the Portland Air National Guard (PANG) Installation
is responsible for releasing information to news media on all military accidents occurring at Portland IAP.
In the event an Air Force Reserve (AFRC) aircraft is involved, the 939 ARW will coordinate all news
releases with the PANG Installation.

2. Off-Base Accidents:
   2.1. In the event of an off-base accident involving AFRC aircraft or personnel, individuals having
   knowledge of details will immediately provide information to the 939 ARW Command Post (CP) who
   will in turn follow appropriate quick reaction checklist for notification of wing agencies and higher
   headquarters.
   2.2. When other than AFRC aircraft or personnel are involved, details and information will be fur-
   nished to the host public affairs office by the 939 ARW/PA.

3. Responsibilities:
2                                                                     939ARWI35-101 1 APRIL 2003


    3.1. The 939 ARW/PA office will be responsible
       3.1.1. Working with and coordinating all news release actions regarding accidents involving
       AFRC aircraft or personnel occurring on Portland, ANG Base with the host public affairs office.
       3.1.2. Establishing a working relationship with local news media for dissemination and release of
       information on any off-base accident that involves AFRC equipment or personnel.
       3.1.3. Maintaining an up-to-date Crash Kit in accordance with AFI 35-103, Public Affairs Poli-
       cies And Procedures. It will be readily available and located within the 939 ARW/PA office.
       3.1.4. Providing semiannual accident/incident briefing to unit operations, legal, medical, and
       disaster preparedness personnel.
    3.2. The 939 ARW/CP will be responsible for alerting the 939 ARW/PA office of any accident involv-
    ing 939 ARW aircraft. If an accident occurs on other than normal duty hours, notification will be made
    to the 939 ARW/Public Affairs staff. The 939 ARW/PA will, in turn, notify the host public affairs
    office.
