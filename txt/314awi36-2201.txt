This instruction assigns responsibilities, and outlines procedures for management of training
programs in accordance with AFPD 26-22, 22 March 2004, Military Training and AFI 36-2201,
15 September 2010, Change 1, 8 March 2011, Air Force Training Program. The purpose of this
instruction is to outline the role that the 314 MXG Maintenance Training Section will have with
314 OG/314 AW Staff additional duty unit training managers. Maintain and dispose of records
created as a result of prescribed processes in accordance with records disposition available at the
Record Information System web site https://afrims.amc.af.mil. This instruction applies to all
314 AW personnel. This instruction does not apply to 19th Airlift Wing, USAF Combat Aerial
Delivery School, Air National Guard, Reserve, or any other associate organization assigned or
attached to Little Rock AFB, AR. This instruction may be supplemented at any level but all
direct supplements must be routed to the OPR for coordination prior to certification and
approval. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained In Accordance With (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of IAW with the Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from
the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

1. General. The 314 MOF/MXOT Maintenance Training Section will continue
management/oversight of all MXG personnel, while also aiding the 314 OG and Wing Staff with
the administration/oversight of all upgrade and ancillary training spread across the OG and Wing
Staff. The OG Unit Training Manager (UTM) will continue management/oversight of all OG
staff rated personnel and will now provide ancillary training oversight for all officers serving on
the Wing Staff. The 314 OG UTMs are still responsible for overall management of the training
program and serve as the training consultant to all unit members. They will perform the UTM
responsibilities stated in AFI 36-2201. The 314 OG UTMs will perform all AFI 36-2201
responsibilities that have not been deligated to 314 MOF/MXOT in this publication.
2. OJT Roster Management:
     2.1. Each 314 OG staff and squadron UTM is responsible for the following:
        2.1.1. Gain access to MilPDS and receive required training from the Base Training
        Manager.
        2.1.2. Annotate the OJT roster with each trainees current upgrade status to include task
        qualification, CDC completion by volume, and Craftsman Course attendance/completion
        if applicable. Once annotated, forward the OJT roster to 314 MOF/MXOT by the 8th
        duty day of the month for review.
        2.1.3. With the aid of 314 MOF/MXOT, brief the commander on the status of each
        trainee monthly.
     2.2. 314 MOF/MXOT is responsible for the following:
        2.2.1. Generate an OJT roster by the 5th duty day of each month and ensure the roster is
        annotated and briefed to the unit commander by the 10th duty day of each month.
        2.2.2. Forward the annotated and updated OJT roster to the Base Training Manager by
        the 10th duty day of January, April, July, and October.
        2.2.3. Provide recommendations to each 314 OG UTM about the status of each trainee in
        upgrade training and highlight any trouble areas that need to be mentioned at the status of
        training briefing with the unit commander.
3. Training In/Out-processing:
     3.1. Each 314 OG UTM is responsible for the following:
        3.1.1. Add 314 MOF/MXOT to their unit’s in/out-processing checklist.
     3.2. 314 MOF/MXOT is responsible for the following:
        3.2.1. Update personnel ADLS organization information upon the member in/out-
        processing. Exception: 714 TRS personnel in student status will report to the 714 TRS
        UTM for ADLS updates.
        3.2.2. Review each trainee’s training status code and identify needed changes to the 314
        OG UTMs.
4. Quarterly Unit Training Meetings:
     4.1. 314 OG Training Coordinator is responsible for the following:

      4.1.1. Unit quarterly training meetings will be consolidated into one 314 OG training
      meeting.
      4.1.2. 314 OG Training Coordinator will do an agenda, hold the meeting, and process the
      minutes for the 314 OG IAW AFI 36-2201.
      4.1.2.1. The meeting will be attended by 314 OG Training Coordinator, 314
      MOF/MXOT, 314 OG UTMs, Functional Leads, and Supervisors.
      4.1.3. Each Squadron UTM will provide inputs to the 314 OG Training Coordinator by
      the last duty day of the months of February, May, August, and November.
      4.1.4. Provide a copy of the minutes to 314 MOF/MXOT withing five duty days of the
      meeting.
   4.2. 314 MOF/MXOT is responsible for the following:
      4.2.1. Provide training topics and special interest items to the 314 OG Training
      Coordinator by the last duty day of the months of February, May, August, and November.
      4.2.2. Reveiw unit training meeting minutes and send to Base Training.
5. Ancillary Training.
   5.1. Each 314 OG staff and squadron UTM is responsible for the following:
      5.1.1. Use the Education and Training Course Announcement (ETCA) website to find
      course requirements for all ancillary items. Course requirements include: targeted
      audience, prerequisites, frequency of training, and method of instruction.
      5.1.2. Utilize the ancillary training report that 314 MOF/MXOT generates, along with
      the ETCA website, to notify their unit personnel of overdue training.
   5.2. 314 MOF/MXOT is responsible for the following:
      5.2.1. Pull and forward an updated ancillary training report by the 10th duty day of each
      month to the 314 OG UTMs.
