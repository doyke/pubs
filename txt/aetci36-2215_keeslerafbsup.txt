SUBJECT: Guidance Memorandum 2 to AETCI 36-2215, KeeslerAFB Supplement

Releasability: There are no releasability restrictions on this publication.

       By Order of Commander Headquarters 81st Training Wing (AETC), this 81st Training
Wing Guidance Memorandum immediately changes AETCI 36-2215, KeeslerAFB Sup, dated 22
February 2012. Compliance with this memorandum is mandatory to the extent its directions are
inconsistent with other Air Force publications, the information herein prevails in accordance
with AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management.

      This memorandum provides guidance changes that are effective immediately. Specific
changes appear on attachment 1 of this memo. An asterisk (*) indicates changes.

       Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS).

      This memorandum becomes void after one-year from the date of this memorandum, or
upon publication of an interim change or rewrite of AETCI 36-2215, KeeslerAFB Sup,
whichever is earlier.

12.21.10.1.1. (Added) 81 TRG Group Control Center (GCC) personnel will:

12.21.10.2. (Added) MARDET UCC will account for all Marine students attending Marine-
sponsored classes (including all students TDY to Keesler AFB) and report numbers to the GCC.

12.21.10.3. (Added) CNATTU will account for all Navy students (including students TDY to
Keesler AFB), attending Navy or Air Force courses and report numbers to the GCC.

12.21.10.4. (Added) IMSO will account for international students when students are not in class

AETCI 36-2215, 9 SEPTEMBER 2010, is supplemented as follows.

This supplement implements and extends the guidance in AETCI 36-2215. It provides guidance
for administering technical and basic military training, collecting, processing and managing
student accounting data; it applies to all squadrons, and geographically separated units (GSUs) of
the 81st Training Group. This supplement does not apply to Air Force Reserve (AFR) and Air
National Guard (ANG) units. Comply with DoDR 5200.1, Information Security Program,
Appendix 3 for documents containing For Official Use Only information." Comply with AFI
33-332, Air Force privacy Program, for documents containing Privacy Act information. Ensure
that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication;
route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.
Attachment 1 is a glossary of references and supporting information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Parent instruction AETCI 36-2215, Technical and Basic Military Training Administration, is a
new instruction substantially revised and renamed from Training Administration. Role-based
responsibilities and guidance on the training requirements process are now included in the parent
instruction vs. this supplement. This document is substantially revised and must be completely
reviewed.
10.2.2.1. (Added) Squadron MTLs will validate the BMT shipping list and forward the
compiled final report to 81 TRG/MTO and the registrar (81 TRSS/TSRE org box). Squadron
MTLs will also update TTMS Student Record Custom Tab Housing Field for students that reside
in a squadron other than the squadron where they are currently being trained, if applicable. In
the event that a student is housed in a squadron other than the assigned training squadron, the
MTL responsible for their housing will enter and update all applicable TTMS Student
Management codes as required.
10.2.2.2. (Added) The registrar’s office will consolidate and forward the squadron shipping list
to Student Processing Center (SPC), squadron chief MTL and TMs.
10.2.7. Officer course IS is responsible for ensuring ZP and awaiting training status codes are
entered in TTMS Student Management.
10.3. NPS students reclassified from different technical training locations will not receive a ZP
status code upon arrival. MTL will record an awaiting training status code if the student is not
immediately entered into training.
11.3.1. After the force gain action is complete, the student is entered into training. On the
following duty day, the TM verifies the training line number (TLN) has an “S-dash” number
visible on the class start/grad roster in TTMS; if no “S-dash” appears contact the registrar.
12.2.1. Course instructor supervisors will report discrepancies in the TTMS class
start/graduation roster to the TM.
12.2.2. MTLs will enter awaiting training status codes in TTMS Student Management of all
NPS enlisted students transitioning to follow-on courses at Keesler, if needed. MTLs and ISs
will coordinate to ensure student accountability between courses. ISs will enter awaiting training
status codes in TTMS Student Management of all students transitioning from officer course of
initial entry (COIE) to follow-on courses at Keesler, if needed.
12.5.1. Use Keesler AFB Form 10, Appointment Slip, to document and notify the student's MTL
of assignment to Special Individualized Assistance (SIA) outside the normal training day.

Table 12.13., Item 3. The course TM is the approving official for extensions to completion time
for Type 6 courses.
