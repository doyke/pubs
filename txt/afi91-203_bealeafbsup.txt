This publication supplements Air Force Instruction (AFI) 91-203, Air Force Consolidated
Occupational Safety Instruction, 15 June 2012. This supplement applies to all personnel
assigned or attached to the 9 RW. This publication does not apply to the Air National Guard
(ANG). This publication does not apply to Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Units. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).

35.1. General Information.
        35.1.3. Materials Handling Equipment (MHE) Not in Regular Service.
            35.1.3.5. (Added) Any permanently mounted crane, hoist or derrick that is inactive
            for a period of more than 6 months or not inspected in accordance with AFI 91-203
            may be administratively locked and placed into an inactive status. Permanently
            mounted cranes, hoists or derricks that are administratively locked and placed into an
            inactive status may only be reactivated after 9 CES conducts an inspection. 9 CES
            can be contacted at DSN 368-2604/2605 to schedule the inspection.
     35.6. Hoists.
            35.6.3.3. Qualification of Operators.
               35.6.3.3.1. (Added) It is the responsibility of the using agency to provide the
               required training to personnel assigned as permanently mounted crane, hoist or
               derrick operators. 9 CES will provide support to using agencies, when requested,
               to assist with establishing and conducting operator training.
               35.6.3.3.2. (Added) A current listing of qualified operators shall be maintained
               at each permanently mounted crane, hoist or derrick. This list must be kept with
               the AFTO 95 and include the current Unit Hoist Manager and his/her contact
               information.
            35.6.3.5. Inspections.
                     35.6.3.5.1.10. (Added) Frequent Inspections, as defined in AFI 91-203,
                     paragraph 35.6.3.5.1, are the responsibility of the using units. All daily and/or
                     pre-use inspections shall be documented on the AFTO 244. Inspection
                     procedures must be included as a part of the unit’s site-specific training plan.
                     35.6.3.5.1.11. (Added) It is the using unit’s responsibility to maintain all
                     historical data and inspections for the life of the permanently mounted crane,
                     hoist or derrick (including hooks).
                     35.6.3.5.2.15. (Added) Periodic Inspections, as defined in AFI 91-203,
                     paragraph 35.6.3.5.2, shall be performed by 9 CES. Additionally, 9 CES is
                     responsible for all required annual inspections, maintenance, and repairs of
                     permanently mounted cranes, hoists or derricks in accordance with AFI 91-
                     203. An annual inspection report will be provided to the Unit Hoist Manager.
                     Any unauthorized maintenance or modifications by using units will void MHE
                     certifications.
            35.6.3.6. Maintenance.
               35.6.3.6.6. (Added) 9 CES is the only organization to perform maintenance on
               any permanently mounted crane, hoist or derrick.
               35.6.3.6.7. (Added) 9 CES inspectors are the only personnel authorized to clear
               red Xs for any permanently mounted crane, hoist or derrick.

       35.6.3.9. (Added) The following are additional requirements and responsibilities of
       each unit that possess a permanently mounted crane, hoist or derrick:
       35.6.3.9. 1 (Added) Provide 9th Civil Engineer Squadron (9 CES) with 1 primary
       and 1 alternate Unit Hoist Manager and maintain current contact information.
          35.6.3.9.2. (Added) Develop and maintain a training plan which includes
          equipment specific data for operation, performing pre-use/monthly inspections,
          and all necessary items in accordance with AFI 91-203.
          35.6.3.9.3. (Added) Validate training plans with 9 CES before implementation.
          9 CES shall retain copies of each unit’s training plans.
          35.6.3.9.4. (Added) 9 CES shall chair the Beale Overhead Crane & Hoist
          Working Group. Each using organization will be represented when the working
          group meets. Meetings will be held semi-annually to cover such items as: training
          plans, operator qualifications, inspections, and maintenance items.
