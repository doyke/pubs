This Directive states the mission of Air Force District of Washington (AFDW), communicates
responsibilities of AFDW to subordinate commanders and assigned personnel and explains the
organizational relationships among HQ USAF, AFDW, Joint Force Headquarters National
Capital Region (JFHQ-NCR), and Joint Task Force National Capital Region Medical (JTF-
CapMed).

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This Mission Directive delineates missions and assigned duties applicable to AFDW in both its
worldwide Air Force service role and its JFHQ-NCR Air Force component role and incorporates
Joint Basing changes due to Base Realignment and Closure.
1. Mission.
   1.1. AFDW is a Direct Reporting Unit (DRU) to the Chief of Staff, USAF, and serves as the
   Air Force service component to JFHQ-NCR and a supporting command to JTF-CapMed.
   1.2. AFDW is the designated single Air Force voice for planning and implementing Air
   Force and joint solutions concerning the NCR. AFDW organizes, trains, equips, and
   provides forces    for operations within the NCR and Air Expeditionary Force (AEF)
   worldwide deployments. AFDW executes specified Military Department statutory

     responsibilities for administration and support of HQ USAF and assigned Air Force units
     and personnel within the NCR and        worldwide. AFDW prepares and, when directed,
     conducts homeland operations in the NCR.
2. Command. The AFDW Commander will:
     2.1. Respond to the Vice Chief of Staff (VCSAF) for Air Force administration and support
     functions.
     2.2. Execute Service responsibilities for administration and support to HQ USAF, assigned
     Air Force units, selected forward operating areas and DoD agencies, US federal executive
     departments, and Air Force personnel within the NCR and worldwide.
     2.3. Plan, coordinate, maintain situational awareness of, and, as directed, deploy and employ
     Air Force forces for approved homeland operations in the NCR Joint Operating Area (JOA).
     2.4. Serve as supporting commander to JTF-CapMed.
     2.5. Dual-hat as Commander, 320th Air Expeditionary Wing (AEW), upon activation, and
     exercise Administrative Control (ADCON) for all Air Force forces assigned or attached to
     the 320 AEW.
     2.6. Serve as Commander of Air Force Forces (COMAFFOR) for Air Force forces assigned
     or attached to JTF-NCR.
3. Responsibilities. AFDW will:
     3.1. Organize, train, equip, and provide forces for AEF deployments.
     3.2. Provide ceremonial support and conduct homeland operations as part of a Joint effort,
     within the NCR. Homeland operations include homeland defense, defense support of civil
     authorities (DSCA) and emergency preparedness (IAW AFPD 10-8). This will incorporate
     Air Force capabilities designed to detect, preempt, respond to, mitigate, and recover from the
     full spectrum of incidents and threats to the homeland, whether man-made or natural. It also
     encompasses support to National Special Security Events (NSSEs).
     3.3. Exercise UCMJ authority over and provide manpower, personnel, legal, chaplain,
     finance, logistics, contracting, security, and safety support for designated Air Force activities
     located within the NCR, selected Field Operating Agencies, and selected Air Force Elements
     performing duties in non-Air Force activities worldwide.
     3.4. Plan and coordinate all Air Force activities related to Counter-Chemical, Biological,
     Radiological, and Nuclear (C-CBRN) planning, analysis, and response for the NCR JOA;
     coordinate directly with appropriate Air Force Active, Guard, and Reserve units,
     installations, JFHQ-NCR, and other responsible agencies.
     3.5. Plan, coordinate, and execute Air Force emergency evacuation in support of Continuity
     of Government/Continuity of Operations (COG/COOP) within the NCR, coordinate directly
     with appropriate Air Force Active, Guard, and Reserve units, installations, JFHQ-NCR, and
     other responsible agencies.
     3.6. In coordination with the Air Mobility Command Unit at Joint Base Andrews, ensure
     availability and use of the Aerial Port of Debarkation/Embarkation (APOD/E) for joint
     reception, staging onward movement and integration (JSROI).
