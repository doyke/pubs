This instruction implements Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-101; Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC) Supplement 1 Aircraft and Equipment Maintenance Management and Air Force
Reserve Command Instruction (AFRCI) 21-165; Aircraft Flying and Maintenance Scheduling
Procedures. This Airlift Wing Instruction (AWI) applies to all personnel in the 934th Airlift
Wing (AW). Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication;
route AF Form 847s from the field through Major Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms
manager. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management
System (AFRMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www/my.af.mil/gess-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. The use of the name or mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial
product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.


1. Roles and Responsibilities.
   1.1. Plans, Scheduling & Documentation (PS&D) will coordinate with Chief of Current
   Operations (934OSS/OSO) and Aircraft Production Superintendant (Pro Super) to produce
   the Flying and Maintenance schedules.
2. Standardized Flying Window.

     2.1. Mission essential deviations to normal flying will be coordinated between Operations
     Group (OG) and Maintenance Group (MXG).
3. Flying Rules.
     3.1. Normal Flying.
        3.1.1. Scheduled cross country departures in addition to the local training flights will not
        exceed the daily sortie capability established in the Annual Flying and Maintenance Plan
        without negotiation.
        3.1.2. Deviations will be negotiated when they become known or by the weekly
        scheduling meetings.
        3.1.3. Operations aircraft utilization will normally not exceed 65% of possessed aircraft
        each day to include local, cross country, spare and deployed aircraft.
4. Spare Aircraft.
     4.1. MXG will normally provide 1 spare aircraft daily. Use of the spare as a primary line
     may be negotiated.
     4.2. Spare configuration will be the standard configuration unless otherwise requested.
     4.3. Spare aircraft will be included in the daily operations aircraft utilization formula of 65%
     and will be considered a committed aircraft.
     4.4. If a scheduled aircraft breaks for a maintenance issue, the OG/Supervisor of Flying
     (SOF) may elect to use the spare aircraft for any tail that day. The use of the spare will be
     coordinated between the Pro Super/Expeditor and SOF. Once utilized, MXG will not
     generate another spare aircraft.
5. Utilization.
     5.1. To determine the Operations daily aircraft utilization (up to 65% of possessed aircraft)
     the following formula and definitions will be used:
        5.1.1. Operations committed aircraft will include, but not limited to: local training
        sorties, cross country trip, JA/ATT support, and a daily spare.
     5.2. Maintenance and other committed aircraft.
        5.2.1. Maintenance committed aircraft will include ISO, HSC, Refurbishment, Time
        Change items, Time Compliance Technical Order (TCTO), scheduled down time for
        discrepancies, and Special Inspections. These aircraft will be identified on the annual,
        quarterly, monthly, and weekly schedule and are not available for other use.
        5.2.2. Other aircraft being used for a purpose other than flying or maintenance, examples
        include trainer, static display, and tours, may be considered other committed. Deviations
        may be negotiated.
     5.3. The Commitment Rates for possessed aircraft will not normally exceed 65% for
     operations and 35% for maintenance without agreement from MXG/CC and OG/CC. Each
     situation will be evaluated on a case by case basis.

7. Wing Flying and Maintenance Planning.
   7.1. Annual Flying and Maintenance Planning: The Baseline Allocation message will be
   provided to the PS&D office by 934OSS/OSO on the same day received to aid in the
   planning of the Annual Flying Schedule.
8. Flying Hour Accountability Procedures.
   8.1. Maintenance Operations Control (MOC) performs the debrief function. After MOC
   enters the flying hours, the AFTO Form 781 will be given to PS&D.
   8.2. PS&D will review the AFTO Form 781 against G081 for accuracy.
   8.3. PS&D will update their tracking flying hour excel.
   8.4. PS&D will give the AFTO Form 781 to the SARM for their review.
   8.5. On the first duty day of the month PS&D will run a flying hour accountability batch file
   in G081. PS&D will ensure the flying hour tracking sheet is up to date. After the batch file
   will be given to the SARM.
   8.6. SARM will contact PS&D after data verification.
   8.7. All corrections must be completed by the 4th of the month.
9. Procedures for Schedule Contributions.
   9.1. Weekly Schedule Contributions.
      9.1.1. All requests must be submitted to PS&D NLT 1500hrs on Wednesdays for the
      following week.

       9.1.2. Operations will give PS&D their requirements to include: take off time, sortie
       duration, fuel loads, and aircraft configuration.
       9.1.3. The Quality Assurance Technical order distribution account (TODA) custodian
       will submit a list of new or revised publications.
       9.1.4. Other base agencies that need to use an aircraft will submit a request by email. The
       request will include: point of contact, date and time needed, duration, configuration or
       special requirement, and reason.
       9.1.5. The weekly aircraft utilization meeting, also known as the WALMART, is
       normally held in the 96AS Blue Room; on Thursdays.
       9.1.6. PS&D will route the weekly schedule to Wing and Group Commanders for
       signatures once all information has been compiled.
       9.1.7. Schedules will be published and distributed NLT 1200hrs Fridays prior to the start
       of the scheduled week. UTA weekends NLT 1600 hrs on Sunday.
    9.2. Monthly Schedule Contributions.
       9.2.1. NLT the first Thursday of the month, 934OSS/OSO will give PS&D their
       estimation for requirements needed for the following month. Estimates must include:
       dates, hours, sorties, cross country trips, TDYs, Deployments, take off times, duration,
       exercise information, configurations, munitions requirements, and any other known
       information to accomplish their monthly flying.
       9.2.2. NLT the second Thursday of the month the Aircraft Maintenance Squadron
       (AMXS) and Maintenance Squadron (MXS), will give PS&D any requests for aircraft
       down time for the next month.
       9.2.3. NLT the third Wednesday of the month other base agencies requests need to be
       submitted to PS&D to be complied into the monthly schedule.
       9.2.4. PS&D will route the monthly schedule to Wing and Group Commanders for
       signatures once all information has been compiled.
       9.2.5. Schedules will be published and distributed NLT 1200hrs Fridays prior to the start
       of the next month.
    9.3. Quarterly Schedule Contributions.
       9.3.1. Quarterly schedule arrangement: January -March, April-June, July-September,
       and October-December.
       9.3.2. Any activity requiring use of a maintenance facility for commanderâ€™s calls,
       retirements, and ceremonies will be coordinated through PS&D NLT the second
       Thursday of the month prior to the next quarter.
       9.3.3. PS&D will route the quarterly schedule to Wing and Group Commanders for
       signatures once all information has been compiled.
       9.3.4. Schedules will be published and distributed NLT 1200hrs Fridays prior to the start
       of the quarter.
