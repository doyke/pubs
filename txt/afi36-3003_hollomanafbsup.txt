This publication supplements Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-3003, Military Leave Program,
26 October 2009. This supplement applies to all personnel assigned or attached to the 49th
Wing (49 WG). Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims/cfm. Contact supporting records managers
as required. Refer recommended changes and questions to the Office of Primary Responsibility
(OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, to 49th Wing
Knowledge Operations (49 WG/CCK), 490 First Street, Holloman AFB, NM 88330-8277. This
instruction requires collecting and maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act (PA) of
1974 authorized by Title 10, USC 8013, and Executive Order (E.O.) 9397. System of Records
Notice (SORN) F036 AF PC C, Military Personnel Records System, applies.

8. 4. Regular and Special Pass Guidelines. Unit commanders:
       8.4.10. (Added) Military members are subject to recall at their own expense during
       regular and special pass periods.
       8.4.11. (Added) The local area is defined as the place where 49th Wing members live
       and from which he and she commutes to the duty station. All passes must begin and end
       in the local area. At the beginning and the end of any pass period, the member must be
       physically present in the local area.

    8.4.12. (Added) The use of a regular pass or special pass is appropriate as long as
    members can return to their place of duty within 12 hours of notification of a recall.
    Notification begins from the time contact is made with the member, which includes, but
    is not limited to, leaving a voicemail message or text message. Failure to return to duty
    within 12 hours of recall is punishable under the Uniform Code of Military Justice.
    Members will regular and special pass travel to remain within 12 hours driving time of
    Holloman AFB or Alamogordo, NM, using web-based travel planners such as
    www.mapquest.com or www.maps.google.com.
    8.4.13. (Added) All military personnel who would be unable to return to their place of
    duty within 12 hours must be on leave. If the member’s authorized absence exceeds the
    pass period, the member shall be charged leave for absences not excused under DoD
    7000.14-R, Volume 7A, Department of Defense Financial Management Regulations.
    8.4.14. (Added) Commanders may impose more restrictive travel requirements
    consistent with AFI 36-3003 based on unit mission requirements.
    8.4.15. (Added) Members on pass period (able to return to duty within 12 hours of
    notification of recall) who depart their home for an overnight stay outside of a 2-hour
    driving radius must comply with the following additional requirements:
       8.4.15.1. (Added) Provide their supervisor with their location and a contact number
       to be reached for emergencies or recall actions. Members shall provide this
       information in person, via telephone or email before close of business (COB) on the
       last duty day (typically Friday) before the pass period begins.
       8.4.15.2. (Added) Discuss an Operational Risk Management (ORM) plan with their
       supervisor.
