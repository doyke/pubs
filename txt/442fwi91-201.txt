This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 91-2, Safety Programs. It establishes pro-
cedures for the safe operation of hangar doors at Whiteman Air Force Base Missouri. These procedures
apply to all personnel assigned to the 442d Maintenance Group (MXG). MXG supervisors will ensure
compliance of this instruction. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 37-123 (will convert to
AFMAN 33-363), Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force Infor-
mation Management Tool (AF IMT) 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF IMT
847s from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This revision removes the Integrated Maintenance Data System (IMDS) course code 00013. An (|) indi-
cates revisions from previous edition.


1. Responsibilities. It is the responsibility of all assigned 442d Maintenance Group supervisors and
applicable maintenance personnel to ensure compliance of this instruction.

2. Procedures for Electrically/Mechanically Operated Hangar Doors:
   2.1. Only qualified personnel identified by their shop/section supervisor will be authorized to operate
   hangar doors. Shop/section supervisors will keep their flight chief/section supervisor informed of per-
   sonnel changes.
2                                                                       442FWI91-201 1 AUGUST 2007


    2.2. Operators will be thoroughly familiar with operating instructions and precautions necessary for
    safe operation of hangar doors. A training course outline will be maintained in Training Management.
    Training will be documented in IMDS training will include Hangar Door Hazards, Emergency Proce-
    dures, and Hangar Door Operations. All personnel will have Awareness Level training annually. This
    training will be located in Item 2 of the AF Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record briefing
    guide and will include Hangar Door Hazards and Emergency Procedures. Documents for awareness
    level training will be included as part of the annual Air Force Occupational Safety and Health Stan-
    dards (AFOSH) briefing on the AF Form 55. No separate line entry is required for this training.
    2.3. A detailed hangar door operating checklist will be posted next to operating controls. These will
    include step-by-step procedures and list the name of the building custodian. Luminescent or reflective
    directional arrows will be placed adjacent to each operating switch and on horizontal sliding doors to
    indicate the direction of travel.
    2.4. Operators will perform a visual check of the area, including door tracks, prior to activation of any
    of the door switches. WARNING: Under no circumstances will hangar doors be opened to a
    width of less than ten feet.
    2.5. Operators will immediately notify Maintenance Operations Center (MOC) of any discrepancies
    found during operation of hangar doors.
    2.6. MOC will expeditiously notify the building custodian, who will initiate a work order for repair.

3. Prescribed Forms: No forms are prescribed.

4. Adopted Forms: AF Form 55, Employee Safety and Health Record
