This publication establishes responsibilities, procedures and operating instructions for dignified
transfer of human remains through Hill Air Force Base (HAFB) to a geographical area of
responsibility upon request of the family or the Department of Defense. This instruction will be
used in conjunction with AFI 34-242, Mortuary Affairs Program and AFPAM 34-504, Escorting
Deceased Air force Military Personnel. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW) AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of IAW AF Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located
at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm . Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air
Force Information Management Tool (AF IMT) 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF IMTs 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain of
command.

1. General.
   1.1. The dignified transfer of human remains instruction will be used to ensure the proper
   honors and respect are paid to deceased military members killed while on active duty. Every
   effort will be made to ensure proper customs and courtesies are maintained throughout the
   transfer events. All tasked organizations should be aware that notifications of dignified
   transfer are often short notice. It is critical to empower tasked personnel with responsibility
   for executing decisions without delay.

2. Responsibilities.
     2.1. The 75th Air Base Wing (ABW) Commander authorizes Hill AFB organizations to
     support dignified transfers and tasks group commanders and wing staff agencies to provide
     support per this instruction.
     2.2. Squadron unique responsibilities are outlined in Attachment 2 of this instruction.
3. Dignified Transfer Procedures.
     3.1. 75th Air Base Wing Public Affairs (75 ABW/PA) director will ensure maximum
     notification to base populace through the Director, Deputy, and Secretary (DIRDEPSEC)
     email address. It is customary for base personnel to line the motorcade route and render
     honors to the deceased.

Figure 1. Example Draft DIRDEPSEC Message




     3.2. 75th Operations Support Squadron (75 OSS) will track aircraft and keep senior leaders
     apprised of dignified transfer arrival.
     3.3. Between two and three hours prior to aircraft arrival, 75th Security Forces Squadron (75
     SFS) will establish a free zone for dignified transfer ceremony.
     3.4. Approximately one to two hours prior to aircraft arrival, the Hill AFB Honor Guard,
     Patriot Guard riders, and family members will begin arriving at Hill AFB and proceed to
     Building 1.
        3.4.1. Off base visitors (such as the Patriot Guard riders and family members) will be
        escorted to pre-determined locations by HAFB personnel identified for escort duty (see
        Attachment 3 and Attachment 4).
        3.4.2. Dignified Transfer Coordinator will identify senior ranking military personnel and
        take charge of directing military formation and guide civilian formation prior to and
        during the ceremony.
        3.4.3. Family members, casualty assistance officers, and distinguished visitors (DVs)
        will be escorted to the DV Lounge in Building 1 or to the third floor executive overflow
        area of the 75 OSS depending on size of party.

     3.4.4. The HAFB Honor Guard will use the 75 OSS Executive Conference Room to
     prepare for transfer events. The Hill AFB Honor Guard will provide a minimum of seven
     personnel to support the dignified transfer; six of which will act as pallbearers moving the
     casket from the aircraft to the next mode of transportation reference AFI 34-501,
     Mortuary Affairs Policy.
  3.5. Approximately one hour prior to aircraft arrival, civilian law enforcement and funeral
  home personnel will arrive at Hill AFB and proceed to Building 1.
  3.6. Base Operations will establish quiet hours approximately 30 minutes prior to aircraft
  arrival and will maintain quiet hours until completion of ceremony and departure of
  motorcade.
  3.7. Approximately 15 to 30 minutes prior to aircraft arrival, senior leaders will arrive, pay
  respects to the family, and prepare for aircraft arrival.
  3.8. 75 OSS will provide continuous updates to family and receiving party to ensure they are
  in place on the Red Carpet prior to aircraft arrival.
  3.9. Upon arrival, aircraft will be escorted to parking spot at the red carpet in front of Bldg 1
  by 75 OSS Transient Alert. 75 OSS will secure the aircraft prior to the ceremony
  commencement. 75 OSS will secure aircraft prior to ceremony commencement.
  3.10. Once aircraft is secure, preparations will be made to complete the dignified transfer.
  3.11. The commander of troops (COT) will announce command for “Present Arms”. COT
  will then call “Order Arms” after remains are properly received by Honor Guard and
  transferred to motorcade. (Military members in attendance will follow COT instructions and
  Patriot Guard echoes command for their personnel).
  3.12. Personnel will continue to maintain proper customs and courtesies while motorcade
  prepares and departs the airfield.
  3.13. 75 OSS will perform airfield foreign object damage (FOD) check prior to resuming
  normal flight line operations.
  3.14. 75 Logistics Readiness Squadron (LRS) will complete refueling operations.
  3.15. 75 OSS will prepare aircraft for departure.
