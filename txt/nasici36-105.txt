This instruction outlines processing responsibilities for all incoming and departing civilian
personnel assigned to the National Air & Space Intelligence Center (NASIC), Wright-Patterson
Air Force Base, Ohio. This publication implements guidance in AFI 31-401, Information
Security Program Management, AFI 36-1001, Managing the Civilian Performance Program,
and NASICI 31-103, Security Operations, as well as guidance from the Civilian Policy Advisory
Board. This publication applies to all NASIC civilian personnel. This publication does not apply
to the Active duty, Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) Units, or Air National Guard (ANG)
Units assigned to NASIC. This NASIC publication may be supplemented at any level, but all
supplements must be routed to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) listed above for
coordination prior to certification and approval. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field to NASIC
Publications Office (NASIC/SCOK), 4180 Watson Way, Wright-Patterson AFB, OH 45433-
5648 publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
http://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Contact supporting records managers as
required. If information is collected by one or more organizational components and transmitted
to other organizational components for management purposes, the Information Collection and
Reports (ICR). Manager must review for accuracy and compliance with AFI 33-324, The Air
Force Information Collections and Reports Management Program. The use of the name or mark
of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication
does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Changes office symbol from DPP to DPC and corrects other office symbols throughout the
instruction. Clarifies other in/out processing procedures.
1. IN-PROCESSING RESPONSIBILITIES:
     1.1. NASIC/SOS, Security Office is responsible for distributing the NASIC Form 26, NASIC
     In-processing Checklist. Security Office will notify NASIC/DPC that the employee is here
     and that their payroll paperwork is ready for pickup. Security Office will notify employee’s
     supervisor that the employee has arrived and is ready to be escorted to his/her duty location.
     1.2. Employee/immediate supervisor’s responsibilities are as follows; after initial in-
     processing actions are completed at the AFPC OL/DPIDOT (Base Civilian Personnel), to
     complete the checklist that is given to them by the Security Office; employee is responsible
     for providing their payroll paperwork to NASIC/DPC upon completion. Supervisor’s
     responsibility is to ensure that the checklist and payroll paperwork is complete and given to
     NASIC/DPC. Supervisor is responsible for getting the employee to his/her duty location.
     Employee returns the NASIC Form 26 to NASIC/DPC upon completion of all stops within
     ten days of receipt.
     1.3. NASIC/DPC, Civilian Personnel Division is responsible for verifying the completion of
     the AFPC OL/DPIDOT in-processing. Once employee completes the payroll paperwork,
     NASIC/DPC will input it into the payroll system. After the employee returns the NASIC
     Form 26, NASIC/DPC will file it in the new hire folder.
2. OUT-PROCESSING RESPONSIBILITIES:
     2.1. Immediate supervisors/employee requests NASIC/DPC complete a Request for
     Personnel Action (RPA) on individuals resigning, separating/transferring, retiring, or going
     on an extended leave without pay status at least three weeks prior to departure date of the
     employee. If employee is resigning or retiring they shall send an email on NIPRNET to their
     NASIC/DPC representative stating what they are doing and the effective date. If a short
     notice departure is required notify NASIC/DPC ASAP. All out-processing documentation
     must be completed by the employee prior to their supervisor signing NASIC Form 21.
     Civilian Out-Processing Checklist. If retiring once you receive your Retirement SF 50 you
     can schedule an appointment to get your retiree ID card at https://idcards.wpafb.af.mil.
     The Employee will contact NASIC/SOS for an out-processing appointment, and refer to
     NASICI 31-103, Security Operations, https://org.eis.afmc.af.mil/sites/NASIC/default.aspx
     for additional information. After all out-processing actions are completed; the employee’s
     NASIC Controlled Area Badge and out processing checklists must be turned in to
     NASIC/SOS.
     2.2. NASIC/DPC is responsible for ensuring that the employee picks up a copy of NASIC
     Form 21, Civilian Out-Processing, WPAFB IMT 1429, Civilian Separation Clearance
     Record and a computer account disposition letter. NASIC/DPC will accomplish the RPA
     and when completed will have the requesting office sign before releasing to AFPC.
     2.3. NASIC/SC (Communication and Information Directorate) is responsible for processing
     the NASIC Out-Processing Computer Account & External Database Access Removal.

  2.4. NASIC/SOS is responsible for providing the employee the AF IMT 2587, Security
  Termination Statement to be completed for employees that are separating (retiring/resigning)
  or employees that are being removed. Employees that are transferring to another location are
  not required to have the AF IMT 2587 completed. NASIC/SOS will debrief the employee
