This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Award and Decorations
Program and provides guidance on the 39th Air Base Wing (39 ABW) local quarterly safety
awards program and the purpose, eligibility, procedures, selection criteria and type of awards. It
applies to all groups, squadrons, staff agencies, and all other personnel assigned or attached.
This publication applies to all assigned 39 ABW personnel, tenant units, and to personnel
deployed to Incirlik Air Base, to include members of the Air National Guard (ANG) and the Air
Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and their units. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) 39 ABW/SE using the AF
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication. Ensure that all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records and disposed of in accordance with the Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.

1. Purpose. To promote a vigorous mishap prevention program through recognition of
outstanding individual safety acts or achievement in all areas, and to provide an incentive for
Incirlik and attached personnel to actively participate in the mishap prevention program.
2. Eligibility. All personnel assigned or attached to the 39 ABW are eligible for submission of
quarterly safety awards.
3. Awards. Awards consist of a certificate of recognition signed by the 39th Air Base Wing
Commander, available incentives and prizes, and appropriate recognition in base publications. If

an award submission meets requirements, or has the potential for a higher headquarters level
safety award, the 39 ABW Safety Office (39 ABW/SE) will forward accordingly. Awards for
Ground Safety, Weapons Safety and Flight Safety accomplishments will be given each quarter.
4. Determining Category of Award.
     4.1. Flight Safety involves flight or intent for flight.
     4.2. Weapons Safety involves the handling and storage of explosives or surety related
     materials and equipment.
     4.3. Ground Safety is anything not covered under flight or weapons safety.
5. Procedures. Commanders submit AF Form 1206, Nomination for Award, to the 39 ABW/SE
within two weeks from the end of the quarter (i.e. submit by 14 April for January to March
awards).
     5.1. AF Forms 1206’s should be limited to no more than ten lines, singled spaced and in
     bullet format. Information should include the necessary who, what, when, where, and
     impact, and should avoid use of local terms and abbreviations. Please spell out award
     specific acronyms. Standard acronyms do not need to be spelled out (e.g., USAFE, Air
     Force, etc.).
         5.1.1. Give a concise description of the specific achievement or action, how the
         nominee(s) accomplished them and how they contributed to mishap prevention. Include,
         if possible, a quantitative assessment of the Air Force personnel and/or equipment
         affected by the specified achievement or action.
     5.2. Units may submit more than one individual/team per award quarter.
6. Selection Criteria. 39 ABW/SE will determine the winners in each category using criteria
outlined in Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-2833, Safety Awards, as guide. There is no limit to
how many awards may be given per quarter. Once the recipients have been determined, 39
ABW/SE will return the signed certificates to the respective group executive officer.
Presentation of the awards will be at the discretion of the group and squadron commanders.
7. United States Air Forces in Europe (USAFE) Safety Awards. Those nominees identified
as exceptional or consistent safety performers can compete for USAFE-level safety awards. 39
ABW/SE will evaluate local award winners for Major Command (MAJCOM) award potential.
Qualified nominees will be forwarded to USAFE for consideration. For information on the latest
MAJCOM award package requirements, please refer to AFI 36-2833 and the USAFE
Supplement or contact 39 ABW/SE.
