This instruction implements AFPD 31-2, Law Enforcement. It establishes procedures, sets forth policies,
outlines responsibilities, and prescribes enforcement procedures for the control of animals within the con-
fines of Travis Air Force Base (TAFB), California (CA). This instruction pertains to all 60th Air Mobility
Wing (60AMW) and tenant units, as well as all personnel residing in base quarters.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes include
mandates for pet registration though microchip implants, updates leash laws and replaces administrative
hearing process with TAFBI 31-106, Travis Disciplinary Action Program, for animals deemed dangerous
or a nuisance to the Travis community.


1. General.
   1.1. Wild animals on Travis AFB will be allowed to live and roam free without harm or harassment
   unless they pose a direct threat to people or hamper mission accomplishment.
   Where possible, wild animals that do pose a threat will be caught by Humane Animal Services (HAS)
   or the Department of Fish and Game. Request for this service is through the Security Forces Control
   Center (SFCC).
   1.2. Exceptions. This instruction does not apply to Military Working Dogs, Service Animals, or Base
   Equestrian Center.

3. Responsibilities.

           3.1.1.2. Prepare a police report on all reports of animal bites or other violations of this instruc-

       3.1.2. Send police reports to the 60 CES/CEH and Travis Disciplinary Action Program (TDAP)
       presiding authority to determine disposition of the animal.
       3.1.3. Designate SFCC as the sole point of contact for contacting HAS per the base service agree-
       ment.
           3.1.3.1. Have HAS respond to reports of stray or dangerous domestic animals.
           3.1.3.2. Have HAS respond to reports of wild animals posing a threat to the public or opera-
           tions.
           3.1.3.3. Notify HAS to respond to reports of dead domestic or wild animals.
       3.1.4. Respond to reports of stray domestic animals during hours that HAS is not available, or
       anytime there is a threat to public safety.

4. Pet Registration and Immunizations.
    4.1. Registration.
       4.1.1. Dogs and cats four (4) months or older will be registered with the Base Veterinary Office,
       Bldg 543, within 10 days after acquisition or within 10 days after assignment to government quar-
       ters. All pets must be registered via AVID, HomeAgain, or other microchip registration service
TRAVISAFBI31-102 7 DECEMBER 2007                                                                           7


       within the continental United States. The Base Veterinary Office can provide this service for pets
       that do not have a microchip. Additional information on microchip registration can be found at
       http://www.avidmicrochip.com (AVID) or http://www.homeagain.com (HomeAgain).
       4.1.2. The pet owner of dogs or cats found without a microchip will be given five (5) duty days to
       show proof the pet had a chip implanted or the pet has been permanently removed from the instal-
       lation.
       4.1.3. Pets that cannot be identified will be taken to the Solano County Animal Shelter by HAS.
   4.2. Immunizations.
       4.2.1. Pet owners must have their pets immunized or show proof of current rabies vaccination
       upon visiting the Base Veterinary Office.
       4.2.2. All dogs and cats must wear a Travis AFB rabies tag and collar while on base.
       4.2.3. Rabies vaccinations are required for all dogs and cats over 16 weeks of age. A second
       immunization is required at one (1) year of age, and thereafter every three (3) years.
       4.2.4. Rabies vaccination can be accomplished through the Base Veterinary Office or civilian vet-
       erinarian. If a civilian veterinarian is used, pet owners must provide the original rabies certificate
       for verification. The original rabies certificate will be maintained in the pet’s record and a DD
       Form 2208, Rabies Vaccination Certificate, will be issued for the pet owner’s records.

5. Quarantine Procedures.
   5.1. Per AFI 48-131, Veterinary Health Services, any animal involved in an incident resulting in
   someone needing medical treatment at DGMC, the Base Veterinarian and SF will investigate.
       5.1.1. As required, the Base Veterinarian will determine a designated confinement area after
       examination of the animal.
          5.1.1.1. Privately owned dogs and cats will be confined under observation by order of Base
          Veterinarian (Chief, Travis Branch, Veterinary Services) until definite signs of rabies develop
          or until the animal has been retained in confinement for 10 days from time of incident.
       5.1.2. Animals showing no clinical signs of rabies may be released from designated confinement
       area after 10 days from time of incident.
          5.1.2.1. Animals developing signs of rabies during 10-day observation period will be eutha-
          nized and appropriate specimens submitted for laboratory examination to Fort Sam Houston,
          Armed Forces Institute of Pathology.

6. Definitions.
   6.1. Abused animal refers to any animal which is:
       6.1.1. Mistreated, beaten, tormented or teased.
       6.1.2. Deprived of water, food or shelter.
       6.1.3. Kept under unsanitary conditions.
       6.1.4. Abandoned.
8                                                           TRAVISAFBI31-102 7 DECEMBER 2007


    6.2. Dangerous animal refers to any animal which:
       6.2.1. When unprovoked, inflicts injury on or kills a human being.
       6.2.2. When unprovoked, engages in aggressive conduct requiring defensive action by any person
       to avoid bodily injury.
       6.2.3. Has killed or injured a domestic animal.
       6.2.4. Is trained for fighting or attacking other animals or human beings.
       6.2.5. Animals are not considered dangerous animals when provoked, tormented, teased, or
       abused.
    6.3. Nuisance animal refers to any animal which is found:
       6.3.1. Running loose.
       6.3.2. Incessantly howling, yelping or barking.
       6.3.3. Damages personal or government property.
       6.3.4. Defecates on common grounds.
       6.3.5. Defecates on neighbor’s yards.
       6.3.6. Disrupts the comfort, peace or quiet of nearby residents or has caused annoyance or dis-
       comfort to a reasonable person of normal sensitivity in the area.
    6.4. Enclosure means a fence or structure at least six feet in height forming or causing an enclosed
    area suitable to prevent the entry of young children, humanely confines the animal, has an adequate
    exercise area, posted with appropriate warning sign(s) and designed to prevent the animal from escap-
    ing.

7. Removal of Pets from the Installation.
    7.1. The 60 MSG/CC may direct the removal of dangerous or nuisance pets at any time or direct the
    TDAP convene to review cases involving animals that may be dangerous or a nuisance.
       7.1.1. If a TDAP review is directed, administrative hearings and appeals will be conducted per
       TRAVISAFBI 31-106, Travis Disciplinary Action Program.
           7.1.1.1. The TDAP presiding authority will consider the following when determining if a pet
           is dangerous:
               7.1.1.1.1. Any previous history of the animal attacking, biting or causing injury to a
               human being or other domestic animal.
               7.1.1.1.2. Nature and extent of injuries inflicted and the number of victims involved.
               7.1.1.1.3. Place where the bite, attack, or injury occurred.
               7.1.1.1.4. The presence or absence of any provocation for the bite, attack, or injury.
               7.1.1.1.5. The extent to which property has been damaged or destroyed.
               7.1.1.1.6. Whether the animal exhibits any aggressive characteristics or unpredictable
               temperament or behavior in the presence of human beings or other domestic animals.
TRAVISAFBI31-102 7 DECEMBER 2007                                                                        9


              7.1.1.1.7. Whether the animal can be effectively trained or retrained to change its temper-
              ament.
              7.1.1.1.8. Any other relevant evidence regarding the ability of the owner to protect the
              public safety in the future if the animal is permitted to remain on Travis AFB.
          7.1.1.2. The TDAP presiding authority will consider the following when determining if a pet
          is a nuisance:
              7.1.1.2.1. The nature of the alleged behavior.
              7.1.1.2.2. The number of previous complaints of inappropriate behavior.
                 7.1.1.2.2.1. Pets, which are a nuisance, will normally be removed from the installation
                 after two substantiated documented nuisance incidents filed with the Base Housing
                 Office.
      7.1.2. Based upon the facts, the TDAP will make a determination as to whether or not a pet is a
      danger to the public or a nuisance and provide a recommendation to the 60 MSG/CC for final
      determination. The 60 MSG/CC will consider the recommendation and render a final determina-
      tion as to the animal’s disposition.
          7.1.2.1. Should a pet be allowed to remain on the installation, owners will comply with para-
          graph 3.6.8.1. of this instruction.
          7.1.2.2. Should a pet owner be ordered to remove his or her pet, a deadline for removal will be
          provided and owners will comply with paragraph 8. of this instruction.
          7.1.2.3. The TDAP will document the results of the hearing.

8. Transfer of Dangerous Animals.
   8.1. No person will sell, transfer or in any other way dispose of an animal deemed dangerous pursuant
   to this instruction by the TDAP presiding authority to any person, entity, or place not residing or
   located within Travis AFB or member agency of the Solano Animal Control Authority (Fairfield,
   Suisun City, Dixon, Rio Vista, or Vacaville).
   8.2. The owner of an animal that has been deemed dangerous may sell, transfer, or otherwise dispose
   of such animal to a person, entity, or place not residing or located within Travis AFB or member
   agency of the Solano Animal Control Authority, provided the owner first notifies the hearing officer
   and HAS of the proposed sale, transfer, or disposition in writing.
      8.2.1. The notice shall be given and received by the TDAP presiding authority and HAS not less
      than 15 days in advance of the sale, transfer, or disposition and shall specify the name and address
      of the recipient person or entity. Upon receipt of such notice, the hearing officer or HAS may
      notify the governmental jurisdiction in which the recipient person or entity is located or resides.
