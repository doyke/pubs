This Laughlin Air Force Base Instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 91-2,
Safety Programs, and establishes procedures and responsibilities for providing Secure Holding
Area (formally Safe Haven, Refuge and Secure Holding Areas) and receiving of Class 1.1, 1.2.1,
1.2.2, 1.2.3, 1.3, and 1.4 non-nuclear munitions/explosives. It applies to all personnel assigned
to the Munitions Storage Section (47 FTW/MXMW), the Property Distribution Section
(47LRD/DJI/LGRTC), and Security Forces (47 SFS/S5R) on Laughlin AFB. The procedures in
this publication are directive in nature. Personnel in the 47 FTW will comply with these
procedures as written orders subject to military discipline under Article 92(1) [or 92(2)] of the
UCMJ, and subject to appropriate disciplinary actions for Civil Service employees. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF IMT 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication.
Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records,
and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. This change
incorporates revisions from IC-2 to AFI 24-203, Preparation and Movement of Air Force Cargo.
1. HOLDING AREA.
   1.1. Secure Holding Area. The primary entry/exit route is the Main Gate near Highway 90.
   47 SFS will hold the vehicle in the incoming vehicle inspection parking lot at the main gate

2. CLASS 1. 1, 1.2.1, 1.2.2, 1.2.3 AND 1.3 NON-NUCLEAR MUNITIONS/EXPLOSIVES.
    2.1. NORMAL DUTY HOURS: (0700-1600) The primary point of entry/exit to Laughlin
    AFB for vehicle(s) transporting DOT Class 1.1, 1.2.1, 1.2.2, 1.2.3 and 1.3 non-nuclear
    munitions/explosives is the Main Gate near Highway 90. 47 SFS will hold the vehicle in the
    incoming vehicle inspection parking lot at the main gate lot until properly inspected by
    LGRTC and under appropriate escort. The primary route to the Hot Cargo Pad is south on
    Liberty Drive to Alabama Avenue, east on Alabama Avenue and north on Kansas Avenue
    onto the north perimeter road. Vehicle(s) will proceed right, on the dirt road, to the inactive
    runway and then go east, on the inactive runway, to the Hot Cargo Pad holding area.
       2.1.1. For non-nuclear munitions/explosives delivered to Laughlin AFB, the cargo will
       be held at the Hot Cargo Pad until accepted by munitions storage personnel. The route of
       travel to Building 950 will be via the southern perimeter road or the “alternate route” if
       necessary.
       2.1.2. The alternate route for vehicle(s) transporting Class 1.1, 1.2.1, 1.2.2, 1.2.3 and 1.3
       non-nuclear munitions/explosives is the West Gate off Highway 277. Vehicle(s) will
       proceed NE on Laughlin Drive to Liberty Drive, SE to Arnold Boulevard, NE to Second
       Street, then SE on Second Street to Building 950 for storage.
    2.2. AFTER DUTY HOURS: During other hours, the normal entry/exit route is the Main
    Gate on Highway 90. 47 SFS will hold the vehicle in the incoming vehicle inspection
    parking lot at the main gate until properly inspected and under appropriate escort. The
    primary route to the Hot Cargo Pad is south on Liberty Drive to Alabama Avenue, east on
    Alabama Avenue and north on Kansas Avenue onto the north perimeter road. Vehicle(s) will
    proceed right, on the dirt road, to the inactive runway and then go east, on the inactive
    runway, to the Hot Cargo Pad holding area. Upon termination of Secure Holding, the
    routing off base will be reverse of the entry route.
       2.2.1. For non-nuclear munitions/explosives delivered to Laughlin AFB, the cargo will
       be held by 47 LRD/LGRTC at the Hot Cargo Pad until accepted by munitions storage
       personnel. The route of travel to Building 950 will be via the southern perimeter road or
       the “alternate route” if necessary.
       2.2.2. The alternate route for vehicle(s) transporting Class 1.1, 1.2.1, 1.2.2, 1.2.3 and 1.3
       non-nuclear munitions/explosives is the West Gate off Highway 277. Vehicle(s) will
       proceed NE on Laughlin Drive to Liberty Drive, SE to Arnold Boulevard, NE to Second
       Street, then SE on Second Street to Building 950 for storage. If the munitions/explosives
       are not designated for Laughlin AFB but are being temporarily held by the installation,
       transporting vehicles will continue on the south perimeter road to the hot cargo pad for
       storage.

3. CLASS 1. 4 NON-NUCLEAR MUNITIONS/EXPLOSIVES.
  3.1. NORMAL DUTY HOURS: Class 1.4 items received at Laughlin AFB will be
  delivered to the transportation dock, Building 77, via Liberty Drive for processing and
  movement to Building 950 for storage. The primary point of entry is the main gate. Property
  Distribution personnel may, at their discretion, secure incoming shipments of Class 1.4 non-
  nuclear munitions/explosives (not to exceed 25 lbs net explosive weight) in the explosives
  licensee holding area of TMO, Building 77. Storage times will not exceed 24 hours.
  3.2. AFTER DUTY HOURS: Contact 47 LRD/LGRTC Standby/On-Call personnel when
  47 FTW/MXMW personnel cannot be contacted for immediate delivery to Building 950.
  Class 1.4 non-nuclear munitions/explosives, not to exceed 25 lbs net explosive weight, may
  be delivered to Building 77, Supply Storage warehouse and held in the explosives licensed
  holding area for a period of not more than 24 hours. In the event that the Wing Command
  Post (WCP) is unable to contact Property Distribution and Munitions Storage personnel,
  entry of a non-nuclear munitions/explosive laden vehicle on to Laughlin AFB is not
  permitted.
4. TASKINGS.
  4.1. 47 LRD/LGRTC personnel will, when notified by WCP or the Security Forces Desk
  Sergeant, inspect in-bound munitions vehicles and documentation at the appropriate
  designated vehicle inspection area.
  4.2. 47 FTW/MXMW on-call personnel will, when notified by WCP or the Security Forces
  Desk Sergeant, proceed to the designated location and accept the freight after it has been
  released by LGRTC.
  4.3. 47 SFS/CC will ensure the primary and alternate munitions routes are marked on the
  base map at the Law Enforcement Desk and the weapons safety manager (WSM) reviews the
  routes annually. Security Forces providing escort duties for munitions vehicles will have a
  suitable scaled base map with marked munitions routes for reference.
