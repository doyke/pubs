SUBJECT: AETC Guidance Memorandum for Leave Enroute

RELEASIBILITY: There are no releasability restrictions on this publication.

1. By Order of the Commander, Air Education and Training Command, this Air Force Guidance
Memorandum provides leave enroute guidance for AETC Airmen deploying in support of
current operations regardless of Area of Responsibility. Compliance with this publication is
mandatory.

2. For personnel desiring to take leave enroute prior to any deployment or exercise, leave will
be authorized with approval from the home station commander.

3. For personnel desiring to take leave enroute returning from any deployment or exercise to a
location within the CONUS, the following rules apply:

   a. All leave requests must be approved by the deployed commander and home station
commander.

    b. Airmen deployed with a weapon must coordinate proper turn in and accountability for
their weapon with both their owning MAJCOM and deployed AFFOR staff.

    c. Airmen desiring leave must be assessed by a deployed medical health care provider
prior to approval. Personnel deploying under a medical waiver should include planned leave
enroute into their waiver package.

    d. Leave will be taken within CONUS and must be accounted for upon return to home
station.

   e. Airmen must provide appropriate leave location and contact information (commensurate
with what is documented in a LeaveWeb request) to home station.

   f. Leave will be limited to 14 days from the date of arrival in CONUS.
   g. Home station unit commanders will notify the Installation Personnel R eadiness (IPR)
and Unit Deployment Manager (UDM) of leave dates to ensure proper personnel
accountability and reporting.

  h. Upon approval by all parties concerned, deployed commanders will follow appropriate
COCOM and AETC Leave Enroute Policy Guidance.

    i. Airmen are responsible for moving themselves from the CONUS Port of Debarkation
(POD) to the leave address and back to the CONUS POD for movement to home station.
(Transportation lead at the deployed location will schedule flight back to home station from
CONUS POD.)

    j. Upon return to home station, Airmen will in-process the IPR and UDM. In addition, post
deployment stand-down time will be provided at the home station unit commanderâ€™s
discretion, following the AETC Post Deployment Stand-Down policy.

4. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained IAW Air Force Manual 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air
Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS).

5. If you have any questions about this policy, please contact HQ AETC/A1RR at DSN 487-
9712 or HQ AETC/A4RXR at DSN 487-3485.

6. This memorandum becomes void after one-year has elapsed from the date of this
memorandum, or upon publication of an Interim Change or rewrite of the affected publication,
whichever is earlier.
