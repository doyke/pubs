This publication implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 32-2001,
The Fire Protection Operations and Fire Prevention Program. This instruction sets policies,
defines procedures, provides criteria, and outlines responsibilities to schedule and maintain the
ARFF proficiency training program required for fire protection ARFF personnel. It applies to all
fire protection division personnel and assigned tenant organizations required to furnish support to
accomplish this training, (exception: fire fighters are utilized as aircrew members in lieu of
actual aircrew members). Training must be accomplished in accordance with AFI 32-2001,
paragraph 3.4. This publication may not be supplemented or further implemented/extended.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to 812 CES/CEX using the Air
Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s through
the appropriate chain of command. This instruction does not require tiers at or below the Wing
level. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS)
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS). The use of the name or mark of any specific
manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication does not imply
endorsement by the Air Force.

1. Responsibilities.
     1.1. Air Force Test Center (AFTC) Fire Chief (812 CES/CEX) manages the program for
     compliance.
     1.2. Life Support (412 OSS/OSCL) and Survival Evasion Resistance Escape (412
     OSS/OSCT), upon request from 812 CES/CEX, provides technical support to fire department
     rescue personnel in aircraft survival personnel equipment (ASPE).
     1.3. The 412 CE/CEX Assistant Chief for Training administers the ARFF proficiency
     training program.
        1.3.1. Ensures the training aids, support systems and equipment are appropriate.
        1.3.2. Provides 412 OSS/OSR with aircraft training needs to include aircraft type, date,
        time, and duration as required by Edwards Air Force Base Instruction 11-115, Scheduling
        Procedures for Aircraft and Air/Ground Support.
        1.3.3. Evaluates training and makes recommendations to the fire chief.
        1.3.4. Ensures realistic training is accomplished under Air Force, AFMC and
        departmental policies.
        1.3.5. Ensures proper documentation of training.
        1.3.6. Schedules remedial training sessions as appropriate.
        1.3.7. Develops training outlines for various aircraft types and systems based on Task
        Order (TO) 00-105E-Aircraft Emergency Rescue Information.
     1.4. 412 OSS/OSR
        1.4.1. Schedules aircraft as requested on AFTC Form 5016, Weekly Aircraft and Air
        Ground Support Requirements Schedule or an approved automated system.
        1.4.2. Schedules aircraft crew chief and egress systems specialist, if requested, for the
        aircraft concerned to provide technical support and instruction.
     1.5. 412 MXS/MXMW and 412 CE/CEXD
        1.5.1. Provides installation-specific explosive safety as outlined in AFMAN 91-201,
        Explosive Safety Standard
2. Frequency of Training. 812 CES/CEX will conduct ARFF proficiency training according to
AFI-32-2001, paragraph 3.4.
3. Evaluation. Assistant Chief for Training or designated representative evaluates and rates
each training exercise. Do not certify training as complete until a satisfactory level of proficiency
is attained. Fire protection management ensures that rescue crews are proficient in rescue
procedures outlined in local Division Management Plans, Standard Operating Guidelines and
TO-00-105E-9 on aircraft assigned or attached to Edwards AFB.
4. Records and Reports.
     4.1. Assistant Chief for Training uses Automated Civil Engineering System-Fire Department
     (ACES-FD) or like system, as the scheduling and controlling document to ensure all required
     personnel receive mandatory proficiency training, or other approved automated system.
