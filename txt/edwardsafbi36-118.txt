This instruction sets policies, defines procedures, provides criteria, and outlines responsibilities
to schedule and maintain the Aircraft Rescue and Firefighting (ARFF) proficiency training
program required for fire protection ARFF personnel. It applies to all fire protection division
personnel and assigned tenant organizations required to furnish support to accomplish this
training, (exception: fire fighters are utilized as aircrew members in lieu of actual aircrew
members). Training must be accomplished in accordance with AFI 32-2001, attachment 2, The
Fire Protection Operations and Fire Prevention Program; and AFMCI 11-301, AFMC Life
Support Program. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with AFPD 33-3 and Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Management System (AFRIMS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/rds_series.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES: Updates responsibilities (paragraphs 1, 2, 3, and 4); updates
organizations throughout. Changed AFFTCR 55-15 to AFFTCI 11-15 (paragraph 1.3.2)

1. RESPONSIBILITIES.

1.1.   AFFTC Fire Chief (95 ABW/CEF) manages the program for compliance.

1.2. Life Support (412 OSS/OSCL) and Survival Evasion Resistance Escape (412
OSS/OSCT), upon request from 95 ABW/CEF, provides technical support to fire department
rescue personnel in aircraft survival personnel equipment (ASPE).
1.3. The 95 ABW/CEF Assistant Chief of Training (A/C of Training) administers the ARFF
proficiency training program.

1.3.1. Ensures the training aids, support systems, and equipment are appropriate.

1.3.2. Provides 412 OSS/OSR with aircraft training needs to include aircraft type, date, time,
and duration as required by AFFTCI 11-15, Scheduling Procedures for Aircraft and Air/Ground
Support.

1.3.3. Evaluates training and makes recommendations to the fire chief.

1.3.4. Ensures realistic training is accomplished under Air Force, Air Force Materiel Command,
and departmental policies.

1.3.5. Ensures proper documentation of training.

1.3.6. Schedules remedial training sessions as appropriate.

1.3.7. Develops training outlines for various aircraft types and systems based on TO 00-105E-
Aircraft Emergency Rescue Information.

1.4.   412 OSS/OSR

1.4.1. Schedules aircraft as requested on AFFTC Form 5016, Weekly Aircraft and Air Ground
Support Requirements Schedule or an approved automated system.

1.4.2. Schedules aircraft crew chief and egress systems specialist, if requested, for the aircraft
concerned to provide technical support and instruction.

1.5.   AFFTC/SEF

1.5.1 Evaluates the safety of Edwards ARFF training proficiency program on a frequent no-
notice basis. A copy of the Fire Protection Division training and exercise schedules should be
sent to AFFTC/SEF as they become available.

1.6.   412 MXS/MXMW and 95 ABW/CED

1.6.1 Provides installation-specific explosive safety as outlined in AFMAN 91-201, Explosive
Safety Standard

2. FREQUENCY OF TRAINING. 95 ABW/CEF will conduct ARFF proficiency training
according to AFI-32-2001, atch 2.

3. EVALUATION: A/C of Training or designated representative evaluates and rates each
training exercise. Do not certify training as complete until a satisfactory level of proficiency is
attained. Fire protection management ensures that rescue crews are proficient in rescue
procedures outlined in local Division Management Plans (DMP), Standard Operating Guidelines
(SOG), and TO-00-105E-9 on aircraft assigned or attached to Edwards Air Force Base.

4. RECORDS AND REPORTS.

4.1. A/C of Training uses Automated Civil Engineering System-Fire Department (ACES-FD) or
like system, as the scheduling and controlling document to ensure all required personnel receive
mandatory proficiency training, or other approved automated system.

4.2. 95 ABW/CEF uses AF Form 1085, Fire Protection Training Report or like automated
document, to document the date, time, type, location, description, participants, and instructors for
all training that was scheduled and conducted.
