AFI11-301, Volume 1, Aircrew Flight Equipment (AFE) Program, 25 February 2009 and AMC
Supplement, 23 February 2010, are supplemented as follows. This supplement implements and
provides guidance for aircrew flight equipment and applies to all aircrew and flight equipment
personnel operating within the 375th Air Mobility Wing (AMW). Ensure all records created as a
result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-
363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/.
Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

1.3.1.1. (Added) Route all AFE related proposed amendments and recommended policy and
procedural improvements through the wing AFE office prior to submission.
1.3.2.1.1.1. (Added) Route all AFE related AF Forms 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication, through the wing AFE office prior to submission.
2.10.2.1.1. (Added) Due to manpower constraints, the AFE superintendent will act in the
capacity of quality assurance inspector. Any 7-level can perform quality control inspections
(QCI) and in-process inspections (IPI) on equipment as long as they meet the 6-month time in
unit requirement, are certified on the equipment, and appointed by the commander. QA
personnel will reside within the 375th Operations Support Squadron, 457th Airlift Squadron, the

311th Airlift Squadron, and the 906th Air Refueling Squadron AFE section, QA responsibilities
are in addition to supporting the daily workload of the AFE section.
2.10.15.1.1. (Added) All requests for local manufacture maintenance which directly support
aircraft maintenance or operations, will be sent to the AFES/flight chief for approval and
verification of manning availability.
2.11.14.1. (Added) Personnel authorized to perform IPIs will be designated be designated using
AMC Form 64, Request for Special Certification.
2.12.2.1.1. (Added) Technical Orders (T.O.), publication files, and read files: Check to ensure
T.O.s, publications, and read files are being maintained IAW applicable instructions and T.O.s.
Pay particular attention to T.O.s and instruction that have been recently filed. Check
publications against T.O. index to ensure currency. Check to ensure publication supplements are
current and properly posted.
2.12.2.1.2. (Added) Equipment Serviceability: Randomly check serviceable equipment on the
shelf; (i.e., rafts, preservers, masks, etc.) to ensure equipment is serviceable and properly tagged.
2.12.2.1.3. (Added) Ground Safety: Although safety checks will be conducted daily, a monthly
safety briefing and walk through will be conducted and documented. Check for unsafe
conditions; i.e., tripping hazards, safety items available and used, electrical outlets not
overloaded, etc.
2.12.2.1.4. (Added) The AF IMT 2420, Quality Control Inspection Summary, will be completed
by the QA noncommissioned officer (NCO). After the discrepancies have been corrected and
signed off, the form will be forwarded to the NCO In Charge or the assistant. A quality
assurance trend analysis memo for record will be generated each month by the 7th. The letter
will identify trends, if any. The entire shop will be required to review the letter before it is
posted in the shop. If negative trends are identified, they will be analyzed and procedures
implemented to prevent these trends from reoccurring.
2.12.2.1.5. (Added) On a monthly basis, compile the total discrepancies for the previous month,
within 7 days.
2.12.2.1.6. (Added) Definitions:       The following sets the standard for major and minor
discrepancies.
2.12.2.1.6.1. (Added) Major: This will include all write-ups that inhibit or preclude the use of
the item in a real world situation that may cause harm or death to a crewmember.
2.12.2.1.6.2. (Added) Minor: This includes all discrepancies that do not fit the above definition
of a major write up.
2.12.3.1. (Added) Only those trends that are systemic in nature and cannot be resolved by the
375 OSS/CC will be reported to the 375th Operations Group Commander. AFE QA trend
program associated with the inspection/maintenance of aircrew flight equipment will be outlined
in the AFE section Quality Assurance operating instruction.
3.7.6.1.1. (Added) Inspections will be recorded on AF IMT 2420.
3.7.6.3.1. (Added) Qualification: Before a person can perform QA duties, he/she must meet the
following criteria:
