AFI 25-201, Support Agreement Procedures, 1 May 2005, is supplemented as follows: The
purpose of this supplement is to establish policy and procedures unique for Incirlik Air Base
(AB), Turkey. This supplement defines training requirements, specific program administrative
procedures and timelines for support agreements affecting Incirlik AB resources.                This
supplement applies to all personnel assigned to the 39th Air Base Wing (39 ABW) and transient
personnel. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm..

1.2.3.1. (Added) When necessary, the Support Agreement Manager (SAM) will provide
guidance and assistance to the functional area OPRs in preparing functional area Memorandum
of Understanding (MOU), or Memorandum of Agreements (MOA).
2.2.1.3.1. (Added) When any wing or base activity is requested to provide supplies or services
to a DoD or non-DoD agency (other than a contractor), have the requestor provide an accurate
assessment of support needs to the SAM. The SAM will determine from the assessment if a
MOU or MOA is required.
2.2.1.5.1. (Added) The installation SAM manages the process of accomplishing support
agreements involving wing resources. They ensure agreements are coordinated with all
 2                                        AFI25-201_INCIRLIKABSUP 15 OCTOBER 2012


appropriate Functional Area Agreement Coordinators (FAAC), e.g., personnel, security forces,
vehicle maintenance, etc., and will initiate subsequent reviews. They ensure the agreements
contain all required information, are administratively accurate and are completed by the suspense
dates. Suspense actions will be tracked and follow-up actions taken as necessary. The SAM will
not negotiate on behalf of the FAACs.
2.2.1.5.2. (Added)      Support agreements will be accessible via SharePoint at:
https://ice.usafe.af.mil/sites/39ABW/MSG/LRS/LGRD/Forms/AllItems.aspx?RootFolder.
2.2.1.8.1. (Added) The SAM will conduct initial training for newly appointed FAACs within
60 days of their appointment (Attachment 11 (Added)). A designated member from 39
CPTS/FMA and 39 FSS/FSMM will assist the SAM with conducting training to address
financial or manpower issues. Training will contain the specific responsibilities of each
functional area.
2.2.1.8.3. (Added) The SAM will conduct refresher training when significant changes occur
within the support agreements program or annually at a minimum.
2.2.2.1.3. (Added) Post support agreement matrix to the HQ USAFE/A4RI Community of
Practice quarterly.
2.4.1.1.1. (Added) Catalog of support will be reviewed annually by FAACs.
2.4.1.4.1. If additional manpower requirements are needed by supplier to provide the support
requested by receiver, then the local Manpower Office (39 FSS/FSMM) will supply a manpower
annex to the support agreement. If additional manpower requirements are not valid, the
Manpower Office will annotate as such on DD Form 1144, Block 11 (General Provisions).
2.4.1.5.1. (Added) The wing or base activity providing the support will provide required data
to Financial Management (39 CPTS/FMA) for development of unit cost factors for relevant
categories of support. The financial manager will assist the wing or base activity providing
support in identifying what the receiver will reimburse, including its basis and estimated
reimbursement in the support agreement funding annex.
2.4.1.7.1. (Added) In conjunction with the unit Resource Advisor, annually review all support
agreement reimbursements and forward costs to 39 CPTS/FMA.
2.4.1.8.2. (Added) Include unit RA and unit manpower technicians in all FAAC training and
staff assistance visits.
2.6.1.1.1. (Added) SAM will request in writing for 39 CPTS/FMA to initiate annual budget
review NLT 1 Oct.
2.6.1.1.2. (Added) 39 CPTS/FMA will complete the annual budget review during the first
quarter of each new fiscal year.
5.2.1.2.1. (Added) Financial Management, Manpower, Civil Engineering (39 CES/CEA), Staff
Judge Advocate (39 ABW/JA) and Security Forces (39 SFS) will review and concur/ non-concur
with all proposed Inter-Service Support Agreements and review existing agreements.
5.2.1.4. (Added) All questions/concerns will be submitted to the SAM via email unless prior
coordination with the SAM has been made.

5.2.1.5. (Added) All requests for support will be submitted to the SAM. The request will
include type of support required, reasons for support, name of requester and point of contact, to
include voice/fax number, physical address and email address.
5.2.1.6. (Added) The SAM will draft all support agreements with supporting documents, to
include the unit’s concurrence letter (Attachment 10 (Added)), and forward the support
agreement package electronically to the group and/or wing staff agencies for coordination.
5.2.1.7. (Added) The FAAC will review all documents received and ensure that only the
original signed copy of the concurrence letter, applicable changes and cost computation
worksheet are forwarded back to the SAM.
5.2.1.8. (Added) Suspenses set by the SAM are firm; all FAACs must contact the SAM
directly for an extension. Extensions will be granted on a case-by-case basis.
5.2.1.9. (Added) Coordination for support agreements are built on a 120-day timeline
(Attachment 12 (Added)).
