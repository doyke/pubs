AFI 36-2845, 20 March 2006, is supplemented as follows: It specifies the Communications
and Information Annual Awards Program. This supplement applies to all United States Air
Forces in Europe (USAFE) units. It does not apply to Air National Guard (ANG) or Air Force
Reserve Command (AFRC) units. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located in Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS): https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.
SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This supplement implements the temporary policy issued with USAFEGM 36-1. The GM will
be obsolete as soon as this publication is publicly available.
1.10. Presenting Awards. Directorate of Communications and Information (HQ USAFE/A6)
will announce award recipients by message. HQ USAFE/A6 will determine the most appropriate
method to recognize the winners each year.

                                     Chapter 5 (Added)
                                USAFE UNIQUE AWARDS


5. (Added) Major Command (MAJCOM) Awards.
5.1. (Added) Work center awards. In conjunction with the Air Force level awards, USAFE
will recognize the outstanding small (2-10 personnel), medium (11-20 personnel), and large (21+
personnel) work centers. Nominations for these awards must meet the format specified in the
basic publication, paragraph 1.7. and Figure A4.1. for unit awards. The function of the work
center must be inherently communications and information related. The work center cannot be a
flight or larger (base level). The work center cannot be a division or larger (MAJCOM level).
5.2. (Added) Communications and Information Director’s Coin. HQ USAFE/A6 may
award the directorate coin for outstanding performance as he or she desires.
