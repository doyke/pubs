This instruction implements procedures for emergency delivery of firefighting foam in the event
of a major accident IAW Air Force Instruction (AFI) 32-2001, The Fire Protection Operations
and Fire Prevention Program, Attachment 3, Paragraph 3.3 and Air Force Policy Directive
(AFPD) 32-20, Fire Emergency Services. These procedures apply to personnel assigned to the
Material Management, Vehicle Management, Fuels Management and Management and Systems
Flights. It applies to all active duty personnel assigned or attached to Lajes Field. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the OPR using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publications; route AF Form 847s from the field through the
Lajes Field publications/forms manager. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS) located in Air Force Records Information Management System
(AFRIMS): https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. General. The following procedures have been developed to ensure prompt delivery of
firefighting foam in the quantity and to the locations requested by the Senior Fire Marshal/Base
Fire Marshal/On-Scene Commander. Material Management Flight is the Office of Primary
Responsibility for firefighting foam supply support.

2. Background. In the event of a major accident, the Senior Fire Marshal/Base Fire
Marshal/On-Scene Commander may request immediate delivery of firefighting foam to the
accident site or other location.

3. Responsibilities. Material Management Flight will ensure: Firefighting foam in storage is
clearly identified.

3.1. HAZMART (2-3405/2-1317) is the point of contact for delivery requests made during
normal duty hours.
3.1.2. Base Command Post, Fuels Resource Control Center, HAZMART, and Customer Service
are familiar with after-hours procedures for foam delivery and have a current list of contact
phone numbers for HAZMART/Storage and Issue personnel.
3.1.3. All HAZMART/Storage and Issue personnel are able to operate a forklift.

3.2. Base Command Post (2-4200) will be the point of contact for after-hours requests, as
outlined in Paragraph 5.

 3.3. Customer Service will ensure all personnel performing standby duty are familiar with the
after-hours foam delivery request procedures in Paragraph 5.

3.4. Vehicle Management will ensure a response vehicle, preferably a tractor-trailer, is available
for delivery.

4. Normal duty hour requests. All requests for firefighting foam during normal duty hours
will be handled by HAZMART (2-3405).

4.1. Upon receipt of a foam request, HAZMART personnel will notify Vehicle Dispatch (2-
3151) of the requirement for a vehicle and driver.

4.1.2. Personnel will then begin to select the quantity of foam (NSN 4210-01-144-0291) to be
delivered.

4.1.3. While property is being selected, process an issue request for the quantity of foam
requested, using organization account code 290FD.

4.1.4. Personnel will load foam onto vehicle and accompany vehicle in a forklift to the location
specified. If forklift cannot be driven to specified location, forklift can be taken by truck. Senior
Fire Marshal/Base Fire Marshal/On-Scene Commander will notify HAZMART if specified
location is not feasible for normal forklift operations.

4.1.5. Obtain signature for the foam being delivered after offloading. Return document to
Document Control.

5. After duty hour requests. All requests for firefighting foam made after duty hours will be
directed to the Base Command Post (2-4200).

5.1. Upon receipt of a foam request, Base Command Post personnel will notify the standby
person and HAZMART personnel of the request.

5.1.2. Upon arrival, the standby person will notify Vehicle Dispatch (2-3151) of the requirement
for a vehicle and driver and begin to select the quantity of foam (NSN 4210-01-144-0291) to be
delivered.

5.1.3. After property has been selected, prepare a post-post issue request for the quantity of foam
requested, using organization account code 290FD.

5.1.4. Personnel will load foam onto vehicle and accompany vehicle in a forklift to the location
specified by the Base Fire Marshal/On-Scene Commander.

5.1.5. Obtain signature for the foam being delivered after offloading. Return document to
HAZMART for processing the next duty day.
