AFI36-3002, Casualty Services, dated 22 February 2010, is supplemented as follows: This
supplement prescribes the responsibilities and procedures which are necessary to support the
Scott Air Force Base (AFB) Casualty Services program. It applies to all base agencies and
associate and geographically separated units (GSUs) supported by Scott AFB. This guidance is
applicable to the 932d Airlift Wing (AFRC) and the 126th Air Refueling Wing (ANG). Ensure
all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www. my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the
Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command.

1.1.1. It is imperative that each base agency with potential involvement in a casualty incident
understands the importance of that agency’s role and ensures compliance with this instruction
and base supplement.
1.1.3. The purpose of the Casualty Services Program is to ensure prompt, accurate reporting,
dignified and humane notification, and efficient, thorough, and compassionate assistance to the
next of kin (NOK) for all casualty cases.
1.1.9. (Added) The Scott AFB Casualty Services Program is administered by the Casualty
Assistance Representative (CAR). The CAR, alternate CAR, and/or Casualty Augmentation
Support Team (CAST) will provide guidance and assistance during casualty events.

1.1.10. (Added) For casualty reporting purposes, a casualty is defined as deceased, Very
Seriously Ill/Injured (VSI), Seriously Ill/Injured (SI), Duty Status Whereabouts Unknown
(DUSTWUN), or missing. In limited situations, a Not Seriously Ill/Injured (NSI) casualty may
be reportable.
1.1.11. (Added) Any agency that becomes aware of an active duty casualty will notify the 375th
Air Mobility Wing (AMW) Command Post (CP) at 618-256-5891 or DSN 576-5891. During
duty hours, the agency with the information will also contact the base Casualty Services Office at
618-256-6508 or DSN 576-6508/8668.
1.2.1. Ensure prompt and accurate reporting of all casualties (as defined in Table 1.1) that occur
in the Scott AFB area of responsibility or upon the request of Air Force Casualty Services
(AFPC/DPWCS).
1.2.2. Ensure dignified and humane notification, through official Air Force channels, to all NOK
in the Scott AFB area of responsibility.
1.2.3. Provide compassionate assistance to NOK until all claims and personal affairs have been
settled in accordance with (IAW) this instruction, eliminating delay of benefits and acting as a
liaison on behalf of the NOK.

1.7.2.2.3. (Added) Will make notification to NOK for any member in their unit that is declared
a casualty, to include active duty members who are declared deceased, VSI, SI, DUSTWUN, or
missing. Notification will be made as follows:
1.7.2.2.3.1. (Added) For NSI, SI, and VSI casualties, the member (if able), the member’s
commander, first sergeant, or attending physician may make notification by telephone to NOK,
IAW paragraph 3.25. Notification in person should only be considered if it is known the NOK
are in ill health. Commander will maintain close contact with the member’s attending physician
and ensure the family is kept informed of the member’s condition.
1.7.2.2.3.2.    (Added) For deceased, DUSTWUN or missing casualties, the member’s
commander will make notification in person to any NOK in the local area. If time allows, a
casualty notification team consisting of the casualty notification officer (member's commander),
a chaplain, and an attending medic will assemble with all members in service dress uniform.
Exception: The medic may wear the uniform of the day. If there is reasonable concern that the
NOK will be notified by other sources, the commander should make every effort to notify the
NOK as quickly as possible taking at a minimum a chaplain. The commander may invite
another person, i.e., a friend, co-worker, supervisor, etc., if it is known that person’s presence
may be comforting to the NOK.
1.7.2.2.3.3. (Added) For deceased, DUSTWUN or missing casualties, AFPC/DPWCS will
make arrangements regarding notification of NOK that do not live in the area of the member’s
home station.
1.7.2.2.3.4. (Added) Ensure no unauthorized notification of NOK. If at all possible,
commanders will not make notification to NOK regarding the death of an active duty member
without prior guidance from local CAR and/or HQ AFPC/DPWCS, Casualty Branch.
1.7.2.2.4. (Added) Send circumstance and/or condolence letters to NOK of deceased active duty
military members within five calendar days from the date the Air Force notifies the NOK of the
casualty. Commanders may have information pertaining to the casualty such as drug/alcohol,
disciplinary, personal, or mental problems which may be required for circumstance letters. If the
member’s conduct was improper, the facts should be related truthfully and tactfully, with as
much empathy as possible. These facts must be reported to the CAR as soon as the commander
is aware of them. The circumstance and/or condolence letters must be reviewed/approved by the
CAR before the commander sends them to the NOK.
1.7.2.2.5. (Added) Complete an AF IMT 348, Line of Duty Determination, on all active duty
deaths, on those injury cases that may qualify for the TSGLI and any other casualty case that
requires an LOD.
