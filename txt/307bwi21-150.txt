This publication implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 21-1, Air and Space
Maintenance. This instruction outlines policies and procedures for the safe operation of hangar
doors these local procedures are developed in accordance with Air Force Instruction (AFI) 21-
101, Air Force Reserve Command Supplement (AFRCSUP), Aircraft and Equipment
Maintenance Management, AFI 91-203, Air Force Consolidated Occupational Safety
Instruction. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change
of Publication; route AF Form 847’s from the field through the Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC) publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).

This is the initial publication and should be reviewed in its entirety to ensure compliance.


1. Purpose. The purpose of this instruction is to ensure the safety of all personnel performing
hangar door operations. It addresses hangar door hazards, responsibilities and training
requirements.

2. Hangar Door Hazards. Hangar door operation and movement creates a series of pinch
points and crush points. If proper precautions are not adhered to, damage to equipment and
injury or death to personnel can occur.
3. Responsibilities.
     3.1. Supervisors will:
        3.1.1. Ensure hangar door operator training is given only to personnel that have a
        responsibility to operate hangar doors. Personnel will receive operator training only by
        authorized trainers.
        3.1.2. Document hangar door training on trainees AF Form 55, Employee Safety and
        Health Record.
     3.2. Building Custodians will:
        3.2.1. Report any hangar door discrepancies immediately to 2nd Bomb Wing Civil
        Engineering.
        3.2.2. Ensure signage requirements outlined in AFI 21-101_AFRCSUP and AFI91-203
        are maintained.
     3.3. Hangar Door Trainers will:
        3.3.1. Perform on-the-job training with approved course lesson plans and demonstration
        of all safety items and emergency procedures.
        3.3.2. Submit an AF Form 2426, Training Request and Completion with appropriate
        course code to Maintenance Training Section for all maintenance personnel qualified to
        operate hangar doors.
     3.4. Quality Assurance will:
        3.4.1. Coordinate with the Maintenance Training section to develop a hangar door
        awareness training course and refresher course that establishes safe operating and
        emergency procedures for 307th Wing personnel. Hangar door operators will be trained
        using the refresher course material along with hands-on training by a qualified hangar
        door trainer. This course is designed to ensure all hangar doors are operated In
        Accordance With (IAW) locally developed checklists posted on the hangar doors being
        operated.
     3.5. Maintenance Training will:
        3.5.1. Coordinate with the Quality Assurance section to develop a hangar door awareness
        training course that establishes safe operating and emergency procedures for 307th Wing
        personnel.
        3.5.2. Ensure personnel who work in hangar facilities but are not required to operate
        hangar doors receive awareness training. As a minimum, awareness training will include
        hangar door hazards and emergency procedures. Annual hangar door awareness training
        will be documented in Integrated Maintenance Data System (IMDS) for all maintenance
        personnel who travel through aircraft hangars. Training is available on the 307th
