This publication implements and extends the guidance in AFI 48-116, 17 March 2004. This
instruction provides guidance for all Randolph AFB food facilities, sanitation inspection offices,
and organizations desiring a temporary food booth on Randolph AFB in preventing food-borne
illnesses. This instruction should be used with the most recent edition of the US Department of
Health and Human Services, Food and Drug Administration (FDA) Food Code (with AF
changes). This supplement also applies to associate units. Ensure all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFM 37-123,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (AF RDS) located at: https://afrims.amc.mil/rds_series.cfm. The use of the name or
mark of any specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this
publication does not imply endorsement by the Air Force.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. This revision
realigned paragraph numbers in accordance with AFI 48-116. Additional revisions include:
removed paragraph 2.2.2.3.; added “all potentially hazardous foods” (paragraphs 2.2.2.3.2. and
2.2.2.3.3. now paragraph 1.7.19.8.); the temperature of 140 degrees F was changed to 135
degrees F IAW the most current FDA Food Code (paragraph 1.7.19.9.); removed the statement
“If source of food items is in question, contact Public Health.” (paragraph 2.2.2.6. now
paragraph 1.7.19.13.); replaced the statement “a responsible individual,” with “the event POC.”
(paragraph 2.2.2.9. now paragraph 1.7.19.16.); replaced “12 ADS/SGGM (Public Health) with
“Public Health.” (paragraph 2.2.2.9. now paragraph 1.7.19.16.).


1.5.13. (Added-Randolph AFB) Facility managers must contact Public Health when the
following conditions exist:

1.5.13.1. (Added-Randolph AFB) Freezer outages: Public Health must inspect and provide
guidance on the disposition of distressed food items.

1.5.13.2. (Added-Randolph AFB)            Disruptions to normal base operations (water
outages/compromise of the base water system). To provide guidance/consultation ensuring all
food handling duties are in accordance with the standards of the FDA Food Code.

1.7.19.2. (Added-Randolph AFB) Temporary Food Facility defined: a facility intended to
operate for a limited time, examples are concession stands at sporting events, carnivals, base
open houses and organizational events; i.e., base-wide sausage sales.

1.7.19.3. (Added-Randolph AFB) Organizations operating a temporary food facility outside
of their organization for profit or non-profit will comply with this Wing Supplement. NOTE:
Functions held strictly within an organization (not open to the public) for fund raisers are not
required to follow these guidelines. The safety of these functions is the responsibility of the
event POC.

1.7.19.4. (Added-Randolph AFB) Organizations planning to operate temporary food service
facility as described above will:

1.7.19.4.1. (Added-Randolph AFB) Contact Public Health NLT 3 days prior to the scheduled
event.

1.7.19.4.2. (Added-Randolph AFB) Follow Public Health guidelines on operating Temporary
Food Facilities.

1.7.19.5. (Added-Randolph AFB) The sale of potentially hazardous foods such as cream-
filled pastries (cream, custards, and similar products), sandwiches or tacos containing meat,
poultry, eggs or fish, and salad dishes (egg, ham, tuna, etc.) is prohibited unless approved by
Public Health.

1.7.19.6. (Added-Randolph AFB) Potentially hazardous food items requiring preparation
must be prepared on-site unless otherwise approved by Public Health.

1.7.19.7. (Added-Randolph AFB) Keep food stored below 41 degrees F or above 135 degrees
F to prevent the growth of microorganisms.

1.7.19.8. (Added-Randolph AFB) Pork/ground meat must be cooked to at least 155 degrees F
(internal temperature).

1.7.19.9. (Added-Randolph AFB) Poultry must be cooked to at least 165 degrees F (internal
temperature).

1.7.19.10. (Added-Randolph AFB) Destroy potentially hazardous foods that are maintained in
the danger zone (between 41-135 degrees F) for more than 2 hours.

1.7.19.11. (Added-Randolph AFB) All foods must be purchased from an approved source; i.e.,
Commissary, Base Exchange or local supermarkets.

1.7.19.12. (Added-Randolph AFB) Obtain ice in single use bags made of plastic or wet
strength paper.

1.7.19.13. (Added-Randolph AFB) Use only single service items for patrons use.

1.7.19.14. (Added-Randolph AFB) Ensure the event POC receives training on proper
foodhandling techniques and personal hygiene from Public Health. This individual will be
responsible for educating other personnel who will be operating the temporary food
establishment.
