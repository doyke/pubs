This instruction establishes base policy and identifies required actions to be taken when a
drought warning or drought emergency is declared by the State of Delaware or the DRBC.
Section 4.03.D of the Regulations Governing the Allocation of Water promulgated by DNREC
requires Dover AFB to develop and implement, as necessary, a Drought Contingency Plan. This
Instruction shall act as the Dover AFB Drought Contingency Plan. All changes to this
instruction have been made IAW AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management, para
2.12.7.3. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change
of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command.

1. Purpose. As required by Section 4.03.D of the Regulations Governing the Allocation of
Water promulgated by DNREC, Dover AFB is required to develop and implement, as necessary,
a Drought Contingency Plan.
     1.1. Outlines actions to be taken in the event of a drought warning or emergency declared by
     the State of or the DRBC.
     1.2. A drought warning may request voluntary compliance with water conservation measures
     or may implement mandatory restrictions.
     1.3. A drought emergency requires mandatory compliance with the water conservation.
     Failure to comply with mandatory requirements can result in legal action by state or local
     enforcement officers against the offender.
2. Terms and Definitions:
     2.1. Terms and definitions used in this instruction are consistent with those defined in the
     Federal, state, and local environmental regulations.
     2.2. Some commonly used abbreviations and acronyms are listed in attachment 1.
3. Scope:
     3.1. This instruction is consistent with, and updates the previous Drought Contingency Plan
     dated 19 February 2008.
     3.2. This instruction is consistent with and augments the following Civil Engineering
     Operational Plans:
        3.2.1. Dover AFB Leak Detection and Control Plan.
        3.2.2. Dover AFB Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan.
4. Policy:
     4.1. Dover AFB policy is to comply with Federal, state, and local regulations pertaining to
     permitting, managing, and treating water. The above requirements take precedence if they
     conflict with this instruction.
     4.2. Develop consistent base-wide water conservation measures at all times, not only during
     drought conditions.

   4.3. Consider the long-term risk to the human and natural environment when making water
   management decisions to include environmental impact analysis and life cycle management
   costs.
   4.4. Implement the actions set forth in Sections 5 and 6 when under a drought warning or a
   drought emergency.
5. Organizational Responsibilities for a Drought Warning:
   5.1. 436 AW/CC.
      5.1.1. Acts as ESOH chairperson or appoints acting chairperson.
      5.1.2. Provides direction through ESOH to develop and maintain plans and regulations
      with specific guidelines for base environmental concerns.
   5.2. 436 CES.
      5.2.1. Through 436 CES/CEI.
          5.2.1.1. Establishes and publishes technical policy regarding water conservation
          measures.
          5.2.1.2. Prepares all correspondence to DNREC and EPA regarding water
          conservation measures.
          5.2.1.3. Prepares annual water summary and leak detection and control plan reports
          for submittal to DNREC.
      5.2.2. Through 436 CES/CEO.
          5.2.2.1. Prohibits street washing.
          5.2.2.2. Installs flow constrictors on hose bibs at vehicle wash areas at/near Building
          477, 635, 636, 706, and 914.
          5.2.2.3. Eliminates flushing of sewers or fire hydrants exCENt in the interest of
          public health.
          5.2.2.4. Restricts the watering of lawns and shrubs located around Dover AFB
          facilities. During a drought warning, watering of lawns and shrubs is restricted to two
          hours between 1600-0800 hours.
          5.2.2.5. Restricts the filling/refilling of swimming pools and wading pools. During a
          drought warning, swimming pools and wading pools may be filled/refilled between
          0500-0800 or 1600-2100 hours.
      5.2.3. Through 436 CES/CEN.
          5.2.3.1. Restricts the watering of lawns and shrubs associated with active projects
          located around Dover AFB facilities. During a drought warning, watering of lawns
          and shrubs is restricted to 1600-2100 hours.
   5.3. 436 FSS.
      5.3.1. Restricts golf course watering to tees and greens to two hours between 1600-0800
      hours.

        5.3.2. Serves water to customers in base eating establishments only when specifically

     5.4. 436 AW/PA.
        5.4.1. Publicizes the drought warning asking for voluntary support.

6. Organizational Responsibilities during a Drought Emergency:
     6.1. 436 AW/CC.
        6.1.1. Acts as ESOH chairperson or appoints acting chairperson.
        6.1.2. Provides direction through ESOH to develop and maintain plans and regulations
        with specific guidelines for base environmental concerns.
     6.2. 436 CES.
        6.2.1. Through 436 CES/CEI.
           6.2.1.1. Establishes and publishes technical policy regarding water conservation
           measures.
           6.2.1.2. Prepares all correspondence to DNREC and EPA regarding water
           conservation measures.
           6.2.1.3. Prepares annual water summary and leak detection and control plan reports
           for submittal to DNREC.
        6.2.2. Through 436 CES/CEO.
           6.2.2.1. Bans the washing of streets, parking lots and other paved surfaces including
           the washing of hangar floors exCENt in an emergency.
           6.2.2.2. Bans the flushing of sewers exCENt when deemed necessary in the interest
           of public health and safety.
           6.2.2.3. Bans the opening of fire hydrants for any purpose other than to fight fires.
           6.2.2.4. Bans the watering of lawns around Dover AFB facilities.
           6.2.2.5. Bans the filling/refilling of swimming pools and wading pools..
        6.2.3. Through 436 CES/CEN.
           6.2.3.1. Water can be applied to implement re-vegetation following new construction
           or alteration work to prevent soil erosion. This can be accomplished for two hours
           between 1600-0800 hours for a 60 day period after seeding or sodding.
     6.3. 436 FSS.
        6.3.1. Restricts golf course watering to tees and greens to two hours between 1600-0800
        hours.

     6.3.2. Serves water to customers in base eating establishments only when specifically

  6.4. 436 AW/PA.
     6.4.1. Publicizes the drought emergency indicating mandatory compliance with these

  6.5. ALL UNITS.
     6.5.1. The washing of government vehicles will be allowed only during the swing shift
