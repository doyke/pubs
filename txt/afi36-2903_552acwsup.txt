AFI 36-2903, Dress and Personal Appearance of Air Force Personnel, 18 July 2011,
(Incorporating Through Change 3 17 January 2014) and AFI 36-2903 ACC Supplement 1, Dress
and Personal Appearance of Air Force Personnel, 19 April 2013, is supplemented as follows.
This supplement provides further guidance regarding dress and personal appearance of Air Force
personnel and applies to all ACC active duty units assigned to the 552d Air Control Wing
located at Tinker AFB. Describes minimum standards of personal appearance of Air Force
members. Failure to observe the prohibitions and mandatory provisions of this supplement is a
violation of Article 92, Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ). Violations of these
provisions can be prosecuted under Article 92 of the UCMJ, as well as any other applicable
article of the UCMJ, when appropriate. Refer any changes and questions about this publication
to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change
of Publication; route AF Form 847s using existing routing procedures for correspondence.
Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are
maintained IAW Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
IAW Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS).

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

2.8.4. All personnel will have to wear the reflective belt on Tinker AFB flight line areas, active
runways, taxiways, and ramps associated during hours of low visibility/darkness in all uniform
combinations. Wear of the reflective belt in other areas is optional.
5.1.3. Squadron commanders may authorize Friday-wear of a single standardized morale
undershirt worn under either ABUs or flight duty uniform (FDU). SQ/CCs are the approval
authority of the design and color of the shirt.
       6.1.10. The Sage green fleece outer garment may be worn indoors 552 ACW buildings.
       6.2.6. The Green Winter Cap is authorized for wear with appropriate cold weather outer
       garment uniform items. The cap may be worn on the flight line. Headgear must be
       snapped either up or down to eliminate any FOD potential. During engine operation the
       chinstrap must be fastened and the appropriate headset or ear defenders will be worn
       under the cap against skin.
       6.2.7. Black Knit Watch Cap is authorized for wear with appropriate cold weather outer
       garment uniform items. The cap may be worn on the flight line.
       6.2.8. The Sage Green and Green Fleece Watch Cap is authorized for wear with
       appropriate cold weather outer garment (e.g. APECS or authorized/issued functional).
       The cap may be worn on the flight line
       8.3.6. SQ/CCs can authorize the wear of morale patches on the FDU on Fridays or
       during special events. Squadrons will have a single authorized morale patch that can be
       worn on the right shoulder of the FDU. SQ/CC is the approval authority for the design of
       the patch.
       8.4.5. Name tags on the FDU will be cloth and 2 x 4 inches in the colors indicated
       below. Naming convention will conform to the ACC standard for Officers and Enlisted:
