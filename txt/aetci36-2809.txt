This instruction implements AFPD 36-28, Awards and Decorations Programs. It establishes
the Air Education and Training Command (AETC) Order of the Sword and outlines procedures
to be used by AETC’s enlisted personnel to nominate and select outstanding leaders for
induction into this order. This publication applies to the AETC Air National Guard or AETC Air
Force Reserve Command units only upon mobilization. This publication may be supplemented at
any level, but direct Supplements must be routed to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
for coordination prior to certification and approval. Units must forward one copy of their
supplements of this instruction to the office of the AETC Command Chief Office. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the OPR using Air Force (AF)
Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, route AF Forms 847 from the field
through the appropriate chain of command to HQ AETC Command Chief Office. Ensure all
records created as result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance
with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in
accordance with (IAW) Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS).

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

Changes reference the removal of “19 AF” and adds “AFRS”, updating members of the AETC
Order of the Sword Committee (paragraphs 3. and 4.1.).
1. Objectives. The AETC Order of the Sword is designed to recognize and honor an
outstanding leader for conspicuous and significant contributions to the welfare and prestige of
 2                                                       AETCI36-2809 18 DECEMBER 2013


the enlisted force. Induction into the Order of the Sword is the highest honor and tribute enlisted
people can bestow.
2. Master Sword. The AETC Command Chief Master Sergeant maintains a symbolic master
sword on display at the Headquarters AETC. Individuals inducted into the AETC Order of the
Sword will have a brass plate showing their grade, name and date of induction affixed to the
master sword.
3. AETC Order of the Sword Committee. The AETC Command Chief Master Sergeant will
chair the committee. Command chief master sergeants from Air University (AU), Second Air
Force (2 AF), and Air Force Recruiting Service (AFRS) will serve as members of the committee.
4. Nomination and Selection. Any enlisted person or group of enlisted personnel in AETC
may nominate any individual for induction into the AETC Order of the Sword. Nomination
procedures are as follows:
     4.1. Submit the nominations in bullet format with a cover memorandum through the Wing
     Command Chief Master Sergeant and applicable AU, 2 AF, or AFRS Command Chief
     Master Sergeant to the AETC Command Chief Master Sergeant. The nomination should be
     concise but in sufficient detail to substantiate the recommendation. Attachments or
     additional endorsements are not authorized.
     4.2. The AETC Command Chief Master Sergeant will forward the nomination to committee
     members. After evaluating the nomination, committee members will respond in writing with
     their recommendation to approve or disapprove the nomination. A three-fourths affirmative
     vote of the committee is required for approval. The AETC Command Chief Master Sergeant
     will notify the nominating person (or group), by memorandum, of the committee’s approval
     or disapproval.
5. Induction Process. The AETC Command Chief Master Sergeant will consult with the
nominated person to plan the induction and ceremony and ensure AETC Protocol is in the
coordination process.
