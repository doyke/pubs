The directive states the mission, organization, and responsibilities for Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC).

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
This revision reflects AFRCâ€™s status as a major command (MAJCOM) of the United States Air Force
(USAF).


1. Mission. The mission of AFRC is to provide Citizen Airmen to defend the United States and to protect
its interests through air and space power. This organization is a MAJCOM of the USAF.

2. Command. The AFRC Commander:
   2.1. Is the Chief of Air Force Reserve.
   2.2. Commands the Air Force Reserve Command, composed of three numbered Air Forces: Fourth
   Air Force, Tenth Air Force and Twenty-Second Air Force and the Air Reserve Personnel Center
   (ARPC).

3. Responsibilities. The AFRC Commander:
   3.1. Organizes, trains, and equips combat-ready forces.
   3.2. Provides gaining major commands (GMAJCOMs) with combat ready forces that can operate as
   seamless members of the Expeditionary Air and Space Force.
   3.3. Coordinates and maintains liaison with HQ USAF and GMAJCOMs to ensure reserve training
   standards and qualification levels meet mobilization requirements.
   3.4. Establishes necessary priorities to ensure accelerated training programs and quotas for reserve
   individuals, especially in areas of skill shortages, in support of GMAJCOM requirements.

    3.5. Directs operation of Headquarters AFRC, the three numbered Air Forces and ARPC and pro-
    vides all required MAJCOM-level support for all AFRC units and individuals.
    3.6. Organizes additional units, including integrated with Regular Component and Air National
    Guard units, and trains personnel to achieve and maintain readiness as required by the AF.
    3.7. Maintains overall supervision of AFR matters supporting AF war plans, programming docu-
    ments, and mobilization actions.
    3.8. Ensures that AFRC units and individuals ordered to active duty during a call-up or mobilization
    are provided necessary supervision, training, family readiness and administrative support from exist-
    ing resources.
    3.9. Assists Regular Component forces by performing peacetime missions as an adjunct to, or corol-
    lary of, training, and assumes operations and missions assigned in whole or in part to the Reserve
    Component, and supports operations or missions performed by integrated units.
    3.10. Retains administrative control over all AFRC units called under the Presidential Reserve
    Call-Up Authority (10 U.S.C. 12304) and under Partial Mobilization (10 U.S.C. 12302), except for
    forces attached to the COMAFFOR; the COMAFFOR has specified ADCON over attached forces.
    3.11. Carries out wartime and contingency civil defense and disaster preparedness responsibilities.
    3.12. Provides management of Air Force representation for the State Reserve Facilities Boards in the
    Continental United States.

4. Direct Communication. HQ AFRC is authorized direct communications with MAJCOMs, field oper-
ating agencies, direct reporting units, specified military departments, federal and Department of Defense
agencies, and individual members on matters related to unit mission accomplishment. All communication
with AFRC units by non-AFRC agencies is through HQ AFRC.
