Releasability: There are no releasability restrictions on the publication.

    By Order Of The Commander Air Force Nuclear Weapons Center (AFNWC). This Center
Guidance Memorandum immediately supplements AFMCI63-403, Contract Support Services
(CSS) Requirements Approval Process, dated 08 March 2008. This GM is in accordance with
(IAW) AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management.

     The following supplemental guidance is effective immediately. Refer to the attachment for
implementation of AFNWC procedures. Attachment paragraph numbering format mirrors
paragraph numbering format listed in AFMCI63-403.

     The guidance in this memorandum becomes void after one year has elapsed from the date of
this memorandum, or upon publication of an interim change, publication or rewrite of AFMCI63-
403, Contract Support Services (CSS) Requirements Approval Process, whichever is earlier.

Please contact AFNWC/XPO at DSN 246-7411 with additional recommendations.

                                            Guidance

The following changes to AFMCI63-403, dated 08 May 2008, are effective immediately.

2.1. The acquisition of all services in excess of $150K (including services in a PEO portfolio)
with the following exceptions: (per Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) 2.101)
2.1.5.1. (Added) AFNWC includes acquisition of Contract Logistics Support (CLS) services
within the RAD process.
2.1.6.1. (Added) AFNWC includes acquisition of Performance-Based Logistics (PBL) services
within the RAD process.
2.1.7.1. (Added) AFNWC includes Interim Contractor Support (ICS) services within the RAD
process.
2.1.8.1. (Added) AFNWC includes Programmed Depot Maintenance (PDM) services within the
RAD process.
2.1.9.1. (Added) AFNWC includes Remanufacture and Repair services within the RAD process.
2.1.10.1. (Added) AFNWC includes A-76 Circular studies within the RAD process.
2.2. (Added) The requirement approval document process validates requirements for service
contracts and is not to be used for identifying program acquisition strategy.
3.3.1. (Added) Program Manager/requirements initiator will be present (via telecon) during
AFMC SSAP meeting to defend the program requirements.
4.1. Due to changes in the Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT) from $100K to $150K, per
Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) 2.101, all non-exempt, CSS requirements greater than or
equal to $150K shall be addressed in a RAD.
4.1.1. (Added) Approval of service acquisitions associated with a PEO portfolio greater than or
equal to $150K, but less than $100M, lies with the responsible Air Force Program Executive
Officer (AF PEO) per AFI 63-138, Acquisition of Services, para 3.5.1.
4.1.2. (Added) Approval of service acquisitions (non- PEO portfolio) greater than or equal to
$150K, but less than $10M, is AFNWC/CC (delegated to AFNWC/NC, AFNWC/NI and 377
ABW/CC) per AFMCI 63-403.
4.1.3. (Added) Approval of service acquisitions (non- PEO portfolio) greater than or equal to
$10M, but less than $100M, is AFMC/CC (through AFNWC/CC) per AFI 63-138 para 3.5.1.
4.1.4. (Added) Approval of service acquisitions, including those associated with a PEO
portfolio, greater than or equal to $100M, is the Service Acquisition Executive (SAE) per AFI
63-138 Table 1.1.
4.1.5. (Added) Table 1 applies only to non-weapon system RADs.
4.1.6. (Added) AFNWC shall treat approval by an Milestone Decision Authority (MDA) for an
Acquisition Category (ACAT) program of record captured within program milestone decision
documents as requirements approval for embedded services as defined in AFI 63-138, Table 1.1,
note 7. No further approval for those services is required.
4.3. Service Requirements Exceeding $150K. A flow diagram illustrating the AFMC Service
Requirements Approval Process is provided at Attachment 3. The following paragraphs expand
on this flow diagram.
4.3.4.1. (Added) Program Manager/requirements initiator will also attend the AFMC SSAP
meeting (via telecon) to explain and defend the requirement.
6.5.2.1. (Added) In coordination with the originating/requiring activity, Manpower (MO) will
determine whether proposed workload is inherently governmental as defined in the Office of
Federal Procurement Policy (OFPP) Policy Letter 11-01 and FAR Subpart 7.5.
6.5.3.1. (Added) All AFNWC RADs require Small Business (SB) coordination.
6.5.3.2. (Added) Include current contract information in the RAD if applicable.
6.5.3.3. (Added) AFNWC imposes additional mandatory coordination from Financial
Management (FM) and Contracting (PZ) representatives.
6.5.3.4. (Added) Final approval authority (AFNWC/CC, NC, NI or 377 ABW) for AFPEO/CM
RADs under $10M will ensure all questions and comments within the approvals page are
adjudicated before approval.
6.5.3.5. (Added) AFNWC/PZ will coordinate on all RADs before Wing approval and before
proceeding to senior leadership depending on dollar threshold.
