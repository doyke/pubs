AFMAN 23-220, 1 July 1996, is supplemented as follows:
11.7. Local Administrative Procedures:
11.7.1. Each squadron must appoint, in writing, a report of survey monitor and alternate. The letter must
provide full name, rank and duty phone of the primary and alternate.
11.7.2. (Added) The squadron ROS monitor’s responsibilities will include:
11.7.2.1. Appointing in writing and briefing an investigating officer for each ROS required in accordance
with the requirements of AFMAN 23-220, 1 July 1996.
11.7.2.2. Ensuring prompt completion of the initiation phase of reports of survey.
11.7.2.3. Receiving and reviewing the report of survey before sending it to the installation ROS adminis-
trator. The monitor will ensure all necessary blocks are completed and basic questions (who, what, when,
where, why and how) are answered. The investigation needs to include all available statements made by
involved individuals or witnesses.
11.7.2.4. Serve as focal point for corrections of administrative errors on report of surveys generated by
the squadron.
11.7.2.5. Completing all documentation necessary for voluntary payment in lieu of a report of survey.
11.7.2.6. Follow up to ensure items are removed from the squadron’s inventory account.
11.8. Scheduled Separation.
11.8.1. (Added) Clearance through Base Supply for individual equipment items should be accomplished
no less than 30 days prior to final separation date to allow for completion of report of survey for any lost
or damaged items.
11.8.2. (Added) When a member is being considered for separation under the provision of AFI 36-3208,
the squadron commander must initiate action to ensure proper accountability of all individual equipment

15.1.2.1. Lost or Destroyed Property:
15.1.2.1.1. In accordance with Air Force message R 1219172, Jun 96, depreciation of Automatic Data
Processing Equipment (ADPE) will be applied in accordance with the following guidelines. Depreciation
on Air Force ADPE equipment is applied with a five year average life.
15.1.2.1.1.2. The original cost will be used when the items are new or when it is known in advance that
the person involved is going to be relieved of responsibility and the only purpose of the survey is to give
accountability.
15.1.2.1.1.3. A replacement cost obtained from catalogs and local computer stores should be used if the
item is not the latest model but is still in the inventory.
15.1.2.1.1.4. The salvage value should be used if the item is old and is being held pending turn-in to the
Defense Reutilization Marketing Office (DRMO).
