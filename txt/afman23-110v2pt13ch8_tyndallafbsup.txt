This publication supplements USAF Standard Base Supply Customer's Procedures Equipment
Management. It applies to all Air Force, contractor operated, Air Force Reserve, and Air Force
Guard organizations supported by Tyndall Base Supply unless otherwise noted in contract or
support agreement terms or conditions. Ensure all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management
of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule
(RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Send recommended
changes and questions about this publication to 325 MSG/LRD using the AF IMT 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication. This publication requires collecting and
maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by 10 U.S.C. 9832 and
E.O. 9397. System of records notice F023 AF IL C applies.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

AFMAN 23-110, Volume 2, Part 13, Chapter 8, is supplemented as follows:

The summary of changes include who to notify when EAID, ADPE, or LMR items are identified
as being lost, damaged, or destroyed. Additionally, Requests for portable buildings must be
submitted to the CE Customer Service on AF Form 332, BCE Work Request, for Work Order
Review Committee (WORC) review for approval and architectural compatibility.

8.5.1.2. For government organizations (military and/or civil service), notify the organization
commander immediately when EAID, ADPE, or LMR items are identified as being lost,
damaged, or destroyed. The commander will initiate action as prescribed by AFMAN 23-220,
Reports of Survey for Air Force Property.
8.11. Submit TAFB Form 17, Delegate/Delete Property Custodians to the Customer Support,
Equipment Management Function to appoint or change primary and alternate equipment
custodians.
8.23.7. An AF Form 9 for systems furniture projects, modular furniture, and administrative
draperies will be routed directly to Base Contracting. When modular furniture or draperies are
required, the requesting activity will submit AF Form 9 directly to Base Contracting.
8.23.16. Locally procured multiple component issues are no longer submitted to supply for
processing. Utilize an AF Form 9 or Government Purchase Card (GPC).
8.23.19. (Added) Requests for portable buildings must be submitted to the CE Customer
Service on AF Form 332, BCE Work Request, for Work Order Review Committee (WORC)
review for approval and architectural compatibility. Upon approval the requesting organization
submits the AF Form 9, Request for Purchase, directly to Base Contracting for purchase.
8.24. The maximum processing times for equipment requests are as follows:

UND A         2 workdays
UND B         5 workdays
UND C         15 workdays

8.24.2. Forward requests for Non-EAID items (EMC 1), including IEX E (Individual
Equipment), to Customer Support, Customer Service Liaison for issue processing.
8.70. The organization commander may submit TYNDALLAFB Form 18, Request for Issue of
Individual Equipment/Clothing, to the Individual Equipment Function (IE) for issue of IE
managed items. Customers are allowed unrestricted access to all stocked merchandise except
pilferable assets.
