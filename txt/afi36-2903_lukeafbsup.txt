This supplement implements and extends the guidance of AFI 36-2903, Dress and Personal
Appearance of Air Force Personnel and AFI 36-2903_AETC Supplement, Dress and Personal
Appearance of Air Force Personnel. This supplement establishes Dress and Appearance policies
and standards for Luke Air Force Base (LUKEAFB). This guidance applies to all 56th Fighter
Wing (56 FW) personnel, tenant units and agencies. Violations of this instruction can be
prosecuted under Article 92 of the Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ), as well as any
other applicable articles of the UCMJ, when appropriate. This supplement does not change
required wear requirements established by AFI 36-2903. This publication applies to the Air
Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and to Air National Guard (ANG) and their units. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF
Form 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in
accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
in accordance with Air Force Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS). The authorities to waive wing/unit level requirements in this
publication are identified with a Tier (“T-0, T-1, T-2, T-3”) number following the compliance
statement. See AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management, for a description of the
authorities associated with the Tier numbers. This publication may not be supplemented or

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This revision updates Attachment 8. Update Operations Group; realign Medical Group alphabet
numbering sequence, and delete “Tenant Unit” from Attachment 8.
1.3.7. (Added) Dining Facility Dress Codes. The following dress code policy will be enforced
in all Luke Air Force Base dining facilities as applicable:
          1.3.7.1. (Added) The Falcon Inn (flight line kitchen) is considered an immediate
          work area for flight line personnel and flight line Airmen may wear Airman Battle
          Uniforms (ABUs) without shirt/blouse or hat while traveling directly to/from the
          facility for meals when approaching from the flight line west side (see Attachment 8,
          item m). The Hensman Dining Facility is not included in this authorization.
          1.3.7.2. (Added) Shirts and footwear must be worn. Headgear, du-rag/wave caps,
          sleeveless shirts, and pajama bottoms are prohibited.
          1.3.7.3. (Added) Fitness clothes drenched in sweat, filthy or tattered clothing,
          swimwear, extremely short shorts/skirts, or clothing with obscenities will not be
          permitted inside the dining facility. All clothing should be in good taste and
          appropriate for all military and civilian citizens.
          1.3.7.4. (Added) Personnel not dressed appropriately will be asked to leave and
          change to appropriate attire.
          6.2.7.4. (Added) Black Watch Cap. Between 1 Oct and 30 Apr of each year,
          personnel may wear the black wool watch-cap with the physical training uniform
          (PTU) only.
          6.2.8.4. (Added) Sage Green Watch Cap. Between 1 Oct and 30 Apr of each year,
          personnel who normally perform duties outside during cold or inclement weather may
          wear the sage green watch cap (only authorized with ABU) when performing those
          specific duties. Watch caps will not be worn outside the immediate work areas.
          Watch caps will not be worn with sleeves rolled up. Sage Green Watch Cap may also
          be worn with the PTU.
          6.2.9.5. (Added) To mitigate the potential for aircraft foreign object damage,
          headgear will not be worn in the following areas: All routinely used runways,
          taxiways, aircraft parking areas, and other areas where aircraft are routinely towed or
          parked, and the grounds immediately adjacent to these areas, as well as the areas
          listed are designated as “NO HAT” areas (see Attachment 8). Aircrew members,
          when dressed out to fly, who are proceeding directly to and from their primary work
          facility and the flight line will not wear a hat.
              6.3.3.1.1. (Added) Sidewalks and running paths on Luke AFB are
              approved/designated running areas. Head phones are authorized to be worn in
              these areas. For safety, head phones will be removed when crossing any roadway.

        21-107, Preventing Foreign Object Damage and AFI 21-101, Aircraft and Equipment

           9.1.1.2.2. (Added) Coveralls must be in good repair, zipped, buttoned or velcro.

    9.1.1.3. (Added) Name tape, United States Air Force/Occupational tape and grade

       9.1.1.3.1. (Added) Name tapes will be worn on the right side and contain the last

       9.1.1.3.2. (Added) USAF tape or Occupational tapes may be worn on the left
