This publication implements Air Force Instruction (AFI) 91-202, The US Air Force Mishap
Prevention Program. This instruction provides additional local guidance for Commanders, Unit
Safety Representatives (USR) and Motorcycle Safety Representatives (MSR) in the management
and administration of their unit’s safety programs. This instruction applies to all personnel
assigned to the 22d Air Refueling Wing. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Refer recommended changes and questions
about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command.

This instruction requires collecting and maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act of
1974 authorized by Title 10, U.S.C., Section 8013. System of Records notice F033 AF B,
Privacy Act Request File, applies. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR). Users of this instruction must notify
22 ARW/SE of conflicts between this instruction and other directives, instructions, or technical
orders.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This publication is updated to reflect changes in guidance and procedures dealing with the 22
ARW Ground Safety Mishap Prevention Program. The major changes include the new use of

AMC IMT 441 verses MAFB Form 33, the documents needed for Motorcycle Safety
Representatives, and requirements for the Pre-Departure Safety Briefings.
1. Unit Commanders will appoint, by letter, a primary and alternate safety
representative. This letter will include names, ranks, telephone numbers, office symbols and
date initially appointed.
     1.1. A Unit Commander’s Safety Guidance Memorandum will be published by each
     squadron. The guidance memorandum should discuss individuals’ safety responsibility both
     on and off-duty, Risk Management and mishap reporting procedures. It may also address the
     purchase of products and equipment. A copy of this memorandum will be posted on each
     safety bulletin board.
     1.2. Unit Safety Representative (USR) will maintain a list of all real property assigned to the
     unit. The list will be reviewed and updated prior to the scheduled annual safety inspection. A
     copy of the listing will be provided to the 22 ARW/SEG prior to your unit’s annual
     inspection.
     1.3. USRs and Motorcycle Safety Representatives (MSRs) will maintain safety program
     continuity in a manner most conducive to the unit’s mission. Attachments (2 and 3) are
     recommended USR/MSR program management structures to assist in development of a
     continuity process. It may be used in whole or in part and either as a binder or as part of a
     digital structure.
2. Safety bulletin boards will only contain safety-related material. If a separate bulletin
board for safety is not viable, then the safety material will be visibly separated from all other
material posted on the board. At a minimum, Unit must maintain at least one safety bulletin
board in a high pedestrian traffic area. If only one bulletin board is to maintained, virtual safety
bulletin boards will be used and discreetly placed on the units main sharepoint screen.
     2.1. Mishap reporting criteria: report all on and off-duty military injuries; report all on-duty
     injuries involving civilian workers; report all mishaps involving government vehicles; and
     report all mishaps where DoD property was damaged, i.e. aircraft, vehicles, buildings, and
     equipment. Unit will develop mishap reporting procedures that will include the minimum
     guidance listed below. Unit mishap reporting procedures may be combined with or
     published in squadron operating instructions.
        2.1.1. ARW/SEG will be notified of all mishap as soon as practical, but not later than the
        following duty day. Notification can be made telephonically to the 22 ARW Ground
        Safety Office or via email to the 22 ARW/SEG Org email box. Notification should
        contain as much information involving the mishap as possible (i.e. unit, name of
        individuals involved, extent of injuries or damage, and location.) During non-duty hours,
        notification may be made through the 22 ARW/CP to the 22 ARW/SEG on call Safety
        Investigator.
        2.1.2. An AMC IMT 441 will be completed by the injured employee for all mishaps and
        emailed to 22 ARW/SEG ORG email box. If the employee is unable to complete the
        AMC IMT 441, supervision will complete the form and forward appropriately.
        Instructions for completion of the AMC IMT 441 are included on the form. AMC IMT
        441 must be completed within 4 calendar days of the mishap. NOTE: If an employee
        does not seek medical attention, Section II is not required.

       2.1.3. The USR will maintain documentation on all mishaps experienced by their unit.
       The USR will also keep their respective commander informed on injury/illness trends,
       and preventative actions as necessary.
3. MSRs will verify the make, model and type of motorcycle to ensure appropriate
classification of motorcycle and individual training required. For example, some hybrid
cruiser motorcycles are also classified as sport bikes.
4. Unit commander or designated representative will conduct and document periodic
safety meetings (based on riding season, mishap trends, host base guidance, etc. ) with unit
riders to address seasonal, local area hazards, recent mishap trends, safe riding habits, and
personal risk management. Commanders are encouraged to conduct motorcycle “all calls” to
ensure face-to-face contact with assigned motorcycle riders and reinvigorate safety standards and
expectations.
5. Commanders shall ensure a Pre-Departure Safety Briefing is accomplished for all active
duty Airmen under the age of 26 when traveling distances greater than 350 miles from
McConnell AFB. Briefing will be documented on the AF Form 4392, Pre-Departure Safety
Briefing. The briefing will be maintained in a readily available area until the member completes
travel.

Terms
Mishap—An unplanned or unsought event, or series of events, resulting in death, injury,
occupational illness or damage to, or loss of, equipment or property.
Functional Manager—The senior operating officials at all levels exercising managerial control
over an activity or operation. They may include, but are not limited to: Agency Chiefs, Branch
Chiefs, Flight Chiefs, and Work Center Supervisors.
Risk Assessment Code (RAC)—An expression of the degree of risk in terms of hazard or
deficiency severity and probability of occurrence. The Wing Ground Safety Office, Fire
Department, and Bioenvironmental Engineers will assess and assign RACs.
