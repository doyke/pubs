This instruction implements DoDI 2000.14, DoD Combating Terrorism Program Procedures and
AFI 36-2848, Air Force Security Forces Annual Awards and AETCI 36-2801, AETC Recognition
Program. It explains the Goodfellow Air Force Base Antiterrorism Awards Program and the
purpose, eligibility, selection criteria, and nomination processes for each. Ensure all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN
33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Disposition Schedule (RDS), located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/rds_series.cfm. This instruction
applies to all organizations assigned to the Goodfellow AFB, including all tenant units.

1. Unit Antiterrorism (AT) Program Manager of the Year Award:
   1.1. Purpose. This award recognizes individual outstanding performance, stimulates
   competition, improves morale, and increases mission effectiveness. It is reserved for
   personnel who are currently performing Antiterrorism Officer (ATO) duties.
   1.2. Evaluation Period. Calendar year (1 January thru 31 December). Submit packages no
   later than 15 January of each year.
   1.3. Description. The award will consist of appropriate items designed for display purposes
   that are available at the time.
   1.4. Eligibility. Military and civilian appropriated fund employees assigned as unit ATOs
   are eligible for this award. This includes personnel assigned to Goodfellow AFB, to include
   tenant units.

     1.5. Nomination. Commanders and supervisors may nominate eligible personnel using the
     AF IMT 1206. All nominations will need to be routed through the unit commander prior to
     submission to the 17 TRW/CVA office. The information included in the AF IMT 1206 must
     be in bullet format, maximum of 20 lines including the following headings: (1) Significant
     highlights from most recent AT program review/unit exercise (2) Extraordinary antiterrorism
     training efforts, and (3) Innovative actions/processes. See attachment 2 for nomination
     format.
     1.6. Selection. The Wing Antiterrorism/Force Protection Office will make the selection and
     forward the winner to the 17 TRW/CV for final approval. The selected winner will be the
     individual whose accomplishments had the greatest positive impacts on the Installation’s
     Antiterrorism (AT) program. Other selection criteria can include, but is not limited to;
     validity and accuracy of information listed on the AF IMT 1206, compliance with standards,
     etc. If a tie occurs, the Wing Force Protection Officer will make the final decision.
     1.7. Publicity and Presentations. The Wing Force Protection Officer will ensure:
        1.7.1. Award submission period is well publicized.
        1.7.2. Timely notification of winner.
        1.7.3. Timely presentation of award.
2. Antiterrorism Achievement Award:
     2.1. Purpose. This award is presented at the discretion of the Wing Force Protection Officer
     to one or more individuals for their contributions to the AT program. A contribution can be,
     but is not limited to, a single event, action, specific contribution, and/or continued support to
     the AT program.
     2.2. Description. The award will consist of an AT coin (non-personalized) and/or printed
     certificate signed and presented by the Force Protection Officer.
     2.3. Eligibility. Military and civilian appropriated fund employees involved with the 17
     TRW antiterrorism/force protection program can receive this award.
     2.4. Nomination. Nominations will not be accepted for this award.
     2.5. Selection. Selection is non-competitive and is based on the sole discretion of 17
     TRW/CVA. This award may be given at any time.
