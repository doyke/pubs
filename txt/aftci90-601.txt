This publication implements Air Force Materiel Command Instruction (AFMCI), AFMC
Corporate Process, 90-601. This instruction outlines the Air Force Test Center’s (AFTC)
corporate governance structure and establishes the AFTC Council. Responsibilities and
procedures are identified for the management of AFTC financial, manpower, personnel,
facilities, and material resources aligning with the Test & Evaluation Enterprise. It applies to all
AFTC organizations and attached associated units which receive support from the AFTC
including the Air Force Reserve and Air National Guard (ANG), except where noted otherwise.
This publication may be supplemented at any level, but all direct Supplements must be routed to
the OPR of this publication for coordination prior to certification and approval. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route
AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional chain of command. Ensure that
all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).

1. Introduction. The primary objective of corporate governance is to ensure the
effective/efficient allocation of AFTC resources. The corporate governance process seeks to
maximize planning at a centralized level through the AFTC Council. The AFTC Council has a
broad range of experience and corporate knowledge concerning the Test and Evaluation
Enterprise. The AFTC Council operates under the guidelines specified by Headquarters Air
Force Materiel Command (AFMCI Corporate Governance 90-601).
2. AFTC Council Structure. The AFTC Council serves as the senior decision- making body
providing strategic direction for the AFTC. The AFTC Council identifies and finds solutions to

3. Roles And Responsibilities. The primary objective of the corporate process is to provide the
AFTC Commander and Staff with the capability to review Center-wide issues from a corporate
perspective and maximize the effectiveness of AFMC’s decision- making. The AFTC Corporate
Process also facilitates the transition of resource decision- making from a functional requirements
view to a corporate AFTC-framed, capabilities-focused process.
