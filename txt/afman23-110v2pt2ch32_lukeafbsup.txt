AFMAN 23-110, Volume 2, Part 2, Chapter 32, Contingency Processing: Manual Post-Post,
Automated Post-Post, Wartime Processing, and Contingency Planning, is supplemented as
follows. It applies to the 56th Fighter Wing and to all assigned and attached units. This
publication does not apply to Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and to Air National Guard
(ANG) Units. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of
Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of
command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in
accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/rds_series.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been revised and must be completely reviewed. Paragraphs 32.2.13.1 and
32.2.13.3 have been updated with new procedures. All other changes were administrative.
32.1. Due to the reorganization of the 56th Logistics Readiness Squadron (56 LRS), titles of
work centers have changed. Refer to the USAF PAD 08-01 for further guidance.
 2                               AFMAN23-110V2PT2CH32_LUKEAFBSUP 28 July 2011


32.2.13.1. Customer Liaison Element will maintain a current copy of DISCOVERER listings in
lieu of the Stock Number Directory (M14), Master Bench Stock Listing (S04), Mission Support
Kit/ Mobility Readiness Support Package (MSK/MRSP) Listing (R43), Other Asset List (R64),
and Part Number Directory (M21).
32.2.13.3. Customer Liaison Element will prepare one copy of AF Form 2005, Issue/Turn-in
Request, and enter information into the 56 LRS Post-Post database programs. This program will
build the flat file for recovery automatically and print a DD Form 1348-1, DOD Single Line Item
Release/Receipt Document, in the Central Storage Office for the pulling of property.
32.2.17. Cargo Movement Post-Post Control Team (PPCT) members will process all receipt
documents requiring release action.
32.2.17.9. Mission Capable (MICAP) PPCT members will screen all receipt documents for
possible release and annotate receipt documents with due-out information that require
processing. All receipts reviewed will include the reviewer’s initials, date, and time whether
they release or not.
