This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 41-2, Medical Support, and is
used in conjunction with Technical Order (TO) 00-35A-39, First Aid Kits. This instruction
establishes responsibilities and procedures for storage and refurbishment of Individual First Aid
Kits (IFAK) and Aircraft First Aid Kits (AFAK) for the 911th Airlift Wing (AW). It applies to
all personnel in the 911 AW. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rds/rds_series.cfm.”           Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms
847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s chain of command. See Attachment 1 for
a Glossary of References and Supporting Information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This instruction has been substantially revised and must be completely rewritten. Major changes
include the removal of the paragraph 4 along with sub-paragraphs and updates to the mobility
section that includes information about the Material Inventory Accounting System (MICAS).
1. General. The 911th Aeromedical Staging Squadron (ASTS) is the host medical material
supplier for all units of the 911 AW.

2. Responsibilities. All units assigned Individual First Aid Kits (IFAKs) have the responsibility
to identify and fund first aid kit requirements. The 911 ASTS refurbishes used or outdated
IFAKs based on these requirements and according to T.O.00-35A-39.
3. Guidelines:
     3.1. Mobility “A” Bags/IFAKs. The 911th Logistics Readiness Squadron (LRS) maintains
     all base military members Mobility “A” bags. IFAKs are inventoried as part of these
     mobility bags. The 911 LRS is responsible for maintaining the stored kits IAW Material
     Inventory Accounting System (MICAS) and bringing them to ASTS during the Unit Training
     Assembly (UTA) prior to the expiration date for updating. The ASTS will provide updated
     kits within two UTAs from the time the kits are turned in regardless of expiration status.
     3.2. "Hard" AFAKs: The 758th Airlift Squadron (AS) is the only unit authorized
     individual AFAKs at the 911 AW. These AFAKs are part of each assigned C-130H aircraft
     inventory. The 911 AMXS is responsible for maintaining these kits. AMXS will store the
     spares and distribute AFAKs to the aircraft and take responsibility to keep them current on
     the inspections. Upon request from the AMXS, the 911 ASTS will provide updated kits
     within one UTA from the time the kits are turned in regardless of expiration status.
     3.3. Life Raft and Survival Vest Kits: The 758 AS is the only unit authorized these
     specific kits at the 911 AW. The Aircrew Flight Equipment section (911 OSS/OSL) will
     maintain the kits at their facility, and are responsible for maintaining these kits. The 911
     OSF/OSL will work with the 911 LRS for refurbishment of first aid kit supplies.
     3.4. The 911 ASTS: The ASTS is not staffed full time with the appropriate Air Force
     specialty code (AFSC) to support refurbishment activities during normal business hours.
     IFAKs will be updated during regular UTA times.
