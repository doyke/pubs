This instruction establishes the Official Mail Management Program in the Head Quarters Air
Force Personnel Center (HQ AFPC). It provides procedures on properly preparing mail for cost
effective and timely delivery by the United States Postal Service (USPS) and commercial
carriers. It establishes the responsibilities for safeguarding accountable and controlled mail and
parcels. It does not replace DoD 4525-6-M, DoD Postal Manual, 15 Aug 02, DoDI 4525.8 / Air
Force Sup1, DoD Official Mail Management, 20 Mar 06, and DoDM 4525.8 / AFMAN 33-306,
DoD Official Mail Manual, 12 Oct 06. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847 to the AFPC Publishing Office,
550 C Street West Suite 48, Randolph AFB Texas 78150-4750, or email
afpc.publications@us.af.mil. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

Vigilance should be taken to protect Privacy Act (PA) and Personal Identifying Information (PII)
when submitting or sending nominations, applications or other documents to DoD agencies
whether through government Internet systems (e-mail), postal methods, faxing or scanning. As a
minimum review the following references and take appropriate actions to protect your PA/PII
that contains sensitive or For Official Use Only (FOUO) information before sending: AFI 33-
332, Air Force Privacy Program, Chapter 2 and 6, DoD 5400.11-R, Department of Defense
Privacy Program; AFI 33-119, Air Force Messaging, and AFI 33-129, Web Management and
Internet Use. Forms affected by the PA have an appropriate PA statement.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This revision is updated to add vigilance statement, updates publication/form references, and
office symbols.
1. General Information.
     1.1. Hours of Operations. Mail Center is staffed between 0700 and 1600 Monday through
     Friday.
        1.1.1. Accountable Mail Authorization designees are authorized to pick up accountable
        mail. Accountable mail must be claimed the day it is received in the Mail Center.
     1.2. Activity Distribution Office (ADO) Point of Contact (POC). ADO POC appointees
     become members of ADO distribution list. Individuals receive Mail Center, USPS, and
     commercial carrier policies.
        1.2.1. Outgoing SAME DAY mail must arrive no later than (NLT) 1300 Monday
        through Wednesday and Friday; NLT than 0900 on Thursday.
        1.2.2. Mail received after cutoff time is dispatched the following duty day.
        1.2.3. Privacy Act Material. Mail placed in Mail Center bins must be secured in sealed
        envelopes to avoid Privacy Act compromises.
2. Official Mail. Memorandums, publications, or parcels related exclusively to the business of
the United States (US) Government that are mailed at a cost to the government. Official
business items of the US Government are authorized to be sent as official mail in accordance
with (IAW) postal laws. Mail will be dispatched at the lowest postage and fee cost to meet
security and accountability requirements. Outgoing official mail must display a complete return
address, to include correct return organization and office symbol.
3. Personal Mail. Mail Center does not process personal mail.               Customers must use
commercial means to send and receive personal mail.
4. Overnight Mail Service. Before using an overnight mail service, determine if information
can be faxed, telephoned to recipient or sent by priority mail to accomplish the mission.
     4.1. Federal Express (FedEx).
        4.1.1. Regular Overnight. HQ AFPC uses FedEx to deliver overnight mail within the
        continental United States (CONUS) (including Alaska and Hawaii). Justify overnight
        mail by completing AFPC Form 444, Request for USPS Express Mail /Federal Express
        Service, (attachment 2). Complete AFPC Form 444, signed and deliver to Mail Center.
            4.1.1.1. Packages are delivered by end of the next business day, Monday through
            Friday.

        4.1.1.2. Use FedEx to dispatch mail when determined most cost-effective way to
        accomplish mission within time, security and accountability constraints.
        4.1.1.3. FedEx does not deliver to:
            4.1.1.3.1. Post Office boxes.
            4.1.1.3.2. Rural Routes.
            4.1.1.3.3. Army / Air Force Post Office (APO) or Fleet Post Office (FPO)
            addressees.
        4.1.1.4. FedEx will not be used:
            4.1.1.4.1. To respond to direct actions or requests for information except when
            imperative to accomplish the mission.
            4.1.1.4.2. To meet established suspense dates except when imperative to
            accomplish the mission.
            4.1.1.4.3. Solely because the package is addressed to higher headquarters.
     4.1.2. Classified Overnight. The Assistant Secretary of Defense for Command, Control
     and Communications, and Intelligence has authorized FedEx to transmit SECRET and
     CONFIDENTIAL material within the Continental United States (CONUS). Classified
     mail must fit in a FedEx container.
        4.1.2.1. Classified Mail Via FedEx. Comply with the following:
            4.1.2.1.1. Double wrap and tape each package IAW DoD 5200.1-R, DoD
            Information Security Program Regulation.
            4.1.2.1.2. Assign control number to each package (i.e., MSI 98-001).
            4.1.2.1.3. Prepare AF Form 310, Document Receipt and Destruction Certificate,
            and two copies of AFPC Form 444.
            4.1.2.1.4. Validate AF Form 310 information, e.g., reference number and control
            number against classified package.
            4.1.2.1.5. Apply FedEx package number used to ship document in the container
            number area on AF Form 310.
  4.2. USPS Express Mail.
     4.2.1. Use for correspondence requiring immediate delivery to an APO/FPO. Delivery is
     three to four day service to selected APOs. Not all APOs offer Express Mail service.
     Call Mail Center to verify which APOs are serviced. Express Mail requires customers to
     use AFPC Form 444.
