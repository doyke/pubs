This policy directive establishes the requirement for reporting the names, effective dates, and purpose of
visits for all distinguished visitors (DV) traveling to Headquarters Air Force Materiel Command (HQ
AFMC), any AFMC field unit, and all tenant organizations at Wright-Patterson AFB. It also sets up min-
imum standards of protocol to be accorded DVs and assigns areas of responsibility. This policy directive
does not apply to the Air National Guard or US Air Forces Reserve units and members.

SUMMARY OF REVISIONS
Policy series has changed from Public Affairs to Services.

1. The commander of AFMC expects all HQ AFMC directorates, AFMC field units, and all tenant orga-
nizations at Wright-Patterson AFB to extend all possible courtesies commensurate with the DVâ€™s grade
and position, and to manage all visits in a courteous and professional manner. See AFMCI 34-101,
Reporting and Monitoring Distinguished Visitors, for details of reporting and making arrangements for
these visits.

2. This policy directive applies to HQ AFMC directorates, AFMC field units, and all tenant organizations
at Wright-Patterson AFB.

3. All AFMC directorates, AFMC field units, all tenant organizations at Wright-Patterson AFB will
report weekly to the Headquarters AFMC Protocol Office (HQ AFMC/DSP) their projected visits of DVs
in the following grades:
   3.1. DoD. HQ AFMC directorates and all tenant organizations at Wright-Patterson AFB report briga-
   dier generals and civilian equivalents (DV-6) and higher, to include those who are retired in these
   grades.
   3.2. DoD. Field unit directorates report lieutenant generals and civilian equivalents (DV-4) and
   higher, to include those who are retired in these grades.
