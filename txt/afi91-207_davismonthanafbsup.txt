AFI 91-207, 22 May 2007, and ACC Sup, 15 January 2008, is supplemented as follows:
This supplement prescribes procedures for the implementation of the U.S. Air Force Traffic
Safety Program and is applicable to all units assigned, attached, or associated on Davis-Monthan
Air Force Base (AFB) including US Air Force Reserve and Air National Guard. Maintain
records created as a result of published processes IAW AFMAN 33-363, Management of
Records, and dispose of records IAW the AF Records Disposition Schedule (RDS), available
from the Air Force Portal at the AF Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) link.
Contact supporting records managers as required. Send comments and suggested improvements
on AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, through 355 FW/SE, 5275 E.
Granite St, Davis-Monthan AFB, AZ 85707.
SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This publication added reference to Base Instruction 21-42, Low Speed Vehicles (LSV)
Maintenance and restrictions for use of LSVs and requirement for motorcycle operators to
complete mentorship after the Basic Rider Course (BRC) and then complete the Experienced
Rider Course (ERC) after completion of mentorship requirements. More definitive requirements
for Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) for bicycle riders and formations were defined.
   2.2.3. (Added) Base Instruction 21-42 addresses the requirements for use and maintenance
   of LSV/Golf Carts. Commanders will use Operation Risk Management (ORM) process prior
   to authorizing the use of any utility-type vehicle not equipped with occupant protective
   devices (restraint devices, rollover protection, and crash protection) in a traffic environment.
   Usage is restricted to activities necessary for mission accomplishment only. When
   authorized, the following requirements apply:

       2.2.3.1. (Added) Use is restricted to senior personnel.
       2.2.3.2. (Added) Speed is confined to 15 miles per hour or less.
       2.2.3.3. (Added) A slow-moving vehicle emblem must be visible from the rear (Ref 29
       CFR 1910.145(D)(10)).
       2.2.3.4. (Added) If operating at night, these vehicles must be equipped with headlights
       and taillights.
       2.2.3.5. (Added) Reflective material will be used to ensure the vehicle is visible from
       both sides.
       2.2.3.6. (Added) Personnel must be seated while vehicle is in operation.
       3.4.2.9. (Added) Active duty motorcycle operators must complete the Basic Rider
       Course (BRC), then the Commander Mentorship Program, and finally complete the
       Experienced Rider Course (ERC). Afterwards they will contact 355th Fighter Wing
       Ground Safety office (355 FW/SEG) to be issued an ACC Form 15, ACC Motorcycle
       Trained, indicating that all motorcycle training requirements have been met.
           3.4.2.9.1 (Added) Active duty motorcycle operators must complete the BRC before
           operating a motorcycle on-duty, off-duty, on-base, or off-base. At no time will an
           untrained active duty member operate a motorcycle.
           3.4.2.9.2. Immediately upon graduation from BRC the active duty motorcycle
           operator will contact 355 FW/SEG and enroll in mentorship. The only exception is if
           the active duty motorcycle operator doesn’t own a motorcycle. In that case, he/she
           will contact 355 FW/SEG and enroll immediately upon purchase, lease, or rental of a
           motorcycle.
           3.4.2.9.3. (Added) Mentorship will be completed within 6 months.
           3.4.2.9.4. Active duty motorcycle operators who have completed mentorship will
           immediately contact 355 FW/SEG to enroll in the ERC.
           3.4.2.9.5. It is the responsibility of active duty motorcycle operators who loan their
           motorcycle out to other military riders to verify the person borrowing the motorcycle
           is fully trained and meets all requirements of AFI 91-207, The USAF Traffic Safety
           Program, and corresponding supplements.
    3.5.3. (Added) Wear of reflective belts is mandatory for all personnel engaged in base
    exercises during hours of reduced visibility and darkness. Training formations using base
    roadways must use road guards equipped with orange mesh vests augmented with reflective
    materials regardless of time of day, to include flashlights during periods of reduced visibility
    or darkness.
    3.5.4. (Added) Bicyclists must ride with the traffic flow (not against it) and wear reflective
    vests when riding during periods of darkness or times of reduced visibility. Additionally,
    bicycle headlamps, rear lights and/or rear reflectors must meet state and/or local
    requirements for visibility during periods of darkness or reduced visibility. These
    requirements must be briefed during newcomer’s orientation and included in the installation
    traffic code.

   3.6.5. (Added) Motorcycle operators must carry proof of Motorcycle Safety Foundation
   (MSF) course completion with them while riding and be able to produce same when requited
   by competent authority.
      4.6.8.2. (Added) ATV training will be conducted by certified Specialty Vehicle Institute
      of America (SVIA) instructors using the approved SVIA course curriculum. Using
      organizations will ensure sufficient SVIA-trained instructors are available to support their
      program. Funding for instructor training is the responsibility of the owning organization.
      ATVs will not exceed manufacturer recommended weight limits when towing equipment.
      ATVs should not be operated in hazardous locations where sparks or vehicle exhausts
      create an explosive or fire hazard.
