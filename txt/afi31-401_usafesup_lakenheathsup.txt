This supplement sets out policies and procedures relating to the Information Security program at
Royal Air Force (RAF) Lakenheath and applies to all 48th Fighter Wing (48 FW) organizations and
tenant units who participate in the 48 FW Information Security Program. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the
Air Force (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication, prescribed by Air Force
Instruction (AFI) 11-215, USAF Flight Manuals Program; route AF Form 847s from the field
through Major Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created
as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at:
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

This publication requires the collection and or maintenance of information protected by the
Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by Executive Order 9397. The applicable Privacy Act SORN
F031 AF SP           M,     Personnel     Security Access    Records     is  available   at
http://privacy.defense.gov/notices/usaf/.

2009 are supplemented as follows:

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This supplement clarifies the requirements for the unit Information Security Program and
transfers responsibility of Information Security Program Manager (ISPM) duties and
responsibilities from the Chief, Security Forces to the Chief of Information Protection. It deletes
retainability requirements for security managers, deletes requirement for providing 48 Security
Forces a copy of Operating Instruction and unit self-inspection, deletes requirement for
additional items in the Unit Security Manager (USM) handbook, and deletes requirement to
maintain the last two program reviews. Completion instructions for AF Form 2587, Security
Termination Statement, are deleted as they are already covered in AFI 31-401, its prescribing
directive.
1.3.4. (Added-LAKENHEATH)        Within the 48 FW, the statement “through Information
Security Program Manager (ISPM) channels” is defined as from the Unit Security Managers
(USM) to the Main Operating Base (MOB) ISPM to the HQ USAFE Information Security
Branch (HQ USAFE/IP). The Chief, Information Protection (48 FW/IP) is designated as the 48
FW ISPM.
1.3.6.11. (Added-LAKENHEATH) The security manager’s handbook (paper or electronic)
will include the list of Classified Contractors and key personnel listing, (if applicable), and a list
of classified computers, copiers, printers, and shredders, including make, identity (ID) number,
type, and location.
1.3.8. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Contact 48th Operations Group, Intelligence section (48
OGI) for connection with the USAFE/J2 FDO for any foreign disclosure concerns.
1.4.3. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Security self-inspections are to be conducted semiannually,
utilizing the most current 48 FW/IP Program Review checklist. Include industrial security
contractors in security self-inspections.
1.5.2.4. (Added-LAKENHEATH) The 48 FW NATO Sub-registry is under control of the
48th Communications Squadron, Base Records Management Section (48CS/SCOK).
2.5.2.1. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Coordinate all classification challenges through 48 FW/IP.
5.3.2.4. (Added-LAKENHEATH)             Immediately notify 48 FW/IP if a member refuses to sign
the SF 312.
5.5.1.1.1. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Verify clearance level is equal to or higher than the
access level being granted and the completion of SF 312, Nondisclosure Agreement (NdA)
prescribed by AFI 31-401. Complete by checking the Joint Personnel Adjudication System
(JPAS) for all unit members prior to commanders granting access to classified information.
5.8.1.1.3. (Added-LAKENHEATH)              Provide copies of Top Secret Control Officer (TSCO)
appointment letters to 48 FW/IP.
5.12.1.1. (Added-LAKENHEATH)              The overnight repository for the 48 FW is the Command
Post for up to US and NATO Secret.

5.17.5. (Added-LAKENHEATH) The AFTO Form 36, Maintenance Record for Security
Type Equipment, prescribed by TO OO-20F-2, Inspection and Preventive Maintenance
Procedures for Classified Storage Containers, will be affixed on the inside of the locking drawer
or in the top drawer of a multiple lock container.
5.18.2.3. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Notify 48 FW/IP prior to making modifications to rooms
approved for secure storage of classified information.
5.18.3. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Coordinate with 48 FW/IP during the design phase of any
program or system that requires the processing or storage of classified information.
5.18.4. (Added-LAKENHEATH) 48 FW/IP will accompany Civil Engineer personnel (48
CES) during these surveys. The ISPM will certify facilities for open storage of classified
information prior to use.
5.24.3.2. (Added-LAKENHEATH)            Post a letter of designated reproduction authorities near
reproduction equipment.
5.28.3. (Added-LAKENHEATH)             RAF Lakenheath does not have a Central Destruction
Facility.
6.8.1.1. (Added-LAKENHEATH) The DD Form 2501, Courier Authorization, prescribed by
AFI 31-401, will be used by personnel to hand-carry classified material off the installation.
6.8.1.2. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Normally, DD Forms 2501 are valid for RAF Lakenheath,
RAF Feltwell, and RAF Mildenhall areas or any combination of the three; however, unit
commanders can extend the coverage of the DD Form 2501 to the entire United Kingdom. This
authorization will be in writing and granting of such authorization will be considered on a case-
by-case basis.
6.8.2. (Added-LAKENHEATH)            A record of all issued and non-issued DD Forms 2501 will
be maintained by the USM.
6.8.3. (Added-LAKENHEATH) Individuals will be briefed on procedures for hand carrying
classified material upon initial issue of a DD Form 2501. USM will maintain a record of initial
briefings.
8.3.3.4. (Added-LAKENHEATH)            Unit information security training, along with TSCO
training, will be documented and maintained by the USM.
