This supplement implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 91-102,
Nuclear Weapon System Safety Studies, Operational Safety Reviews, and Safety Rules (24 Jun
10). This supplement describes Air Force Global Strike Command’s procedures for use in
conjunction with the basic AFI. This instruction applies to HQ AFGSC, subordinate units, and
all organizations receiving support from AFGSC assets that deal with nuclear weapon system
safety and security responsibilities, including Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and Air
National Guard (ANG) personnel. Refer recommended changes and questions about this
publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847s from the field through Major
Command (MAJCOM) publications/forms managers. Ensure that all records created as a result
of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363,
Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/. Contact supporting records managers as
required. This publication may be supplemented at any level, but all direct Supplements must be
routed to the OPR of this publication for coordination prior to certification and approval. Users
of this instruction must notify HQ AFGSC/SEW of conflicts between this instruction and other
directives, instructions, or technical orders. See Attachment 1 for a glossary of references and
supporting information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This revision adds applicability to AFRC and ANG personnel who receive support from AFGSC
assets that deal with nuclear weapon system safety and security responsibilities.
5.1.2. HQ AFGSC Weapons Safety Division (HQ AFGSC/SEW) is the voting member and
point of contact for all Nuclear Weapon System Safety Group (NWSSG) studies and actions on
AFGSC weapon systems.
5.3. HQ AFGSC/SEW will provide notification to the applicable AFGSC headquarters
directorate or staff agency to obtain qualified nonvoting technical advisors to participate in the
study. HQ AFGSC/SEW will forward requests for technical advisors outside HQ AFGSC to
HQ AFSC/SEW.
7.2. (Added) HQ AFGSC observers are requested to forward visit requests for the NWSSG
proceedings to HQ AFGSC/SEW 45 workdays prior to the meeting. Provide justification and
information as outlined in paragraph 7.1 of the basic instruction.
15. HQ AFGSC/A5RI is the office of primary responsibility for preparing and revising
Operation Plan Data Documents (OPDDs) and will provide a copy to the units concerned.
18.1.3. HQ AFGSC/SEW is the AFGSC OPR for keeping the Air Force Safety Center informed
of the status of Air Force approved NWSSG recommendations assigned to HQ AFGSC for
action. HQ AFGSC/SEW will review NWSSG study reports and forward applicable
recommendations to the appropriate HQ AFGSC agency for implementation. The appropriate
headquarters agency will provide HQ AFGSC/SEW a status report of action no later than
May 1st and November 1st of each year until complete. HQ AFGSC/SEW will consolidate the
information and forward to HQ AFSC/SEW.
24.2.2. HQ AFGSC/SEW will distribute schedules and agendas to affected HQ AFGSC
Directorates and Numbered Air Forces (NAF).
25.1.1. (Added) HQ AFGSC agencies developing plans or procedures involving nuclear
weapons will coordinate with HQ AFGSC/SEW, the applicable AFGSC Directorates, NAFs, and
other appropriate agencies to ensure compliance with Weapon System Safety Rules (WSSR).
25.2.1. (Added) Forward all proposed modifications, procedural changes, tests, or other
activities involving nuclear weapon systems to HQ AFGSC/SEW. The Headquarters Nuclear
Surety Working Group, consisting of members from applicable agencies, will make this
evaluation. Modifications, changes, etc., will be forwarded to HQ AFSC/SEW, if further
evaluation is required.
25.3.1. (Added) Changes must be coordinated through HQ AFGSC/SEW when OPDD
revision drives a special study or change to the existing WSSR. Identify the applicable WSSR,
the specific portion of the rule to be changed, specific wording of the recommended change, any
supporting background information, detailed justification for the proposed change, and rationale.
25.5.1. (Added) HQ AFGSC primary voting member for the NWSSG is the Chief of Weapons
Safety.
25.8.1. (Added) HQ AFGSC/SEW is the OPR for coordinating NWSSG studies and visits to
AFGSC units.
