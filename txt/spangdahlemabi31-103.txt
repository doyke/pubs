This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 31-1, Integrated Defense. It
applies to and establishes procedures for curfew hours on Spangdahlem Air Base (SAB) and
Eifel West community housing for children unaccompanied by a parent, guardian, or any other
adult 18 years of age or older authorized by a parent or a guardian. This instruction also applies
to tenant units. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 33-363_USAFESUP, Management of
Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF 847s from the field
through the appropriate functional’s chain of command.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

All mentions of Government Leased Housing have been removed from the document.


1. General Information. Parents and guardians must ensure their minor family member
children comply with the restrictions established in this instruction. It shall not constitute a

defense if the parent or guardian did not have knowledge of the presence of such minor upon the
streets, highways, roads, alleys, playgrounds, or other public areas on SAB and Eifel West
community housing areas during curfew hours.
2. Curfew Restriction. The following rules apply to personnel on SAB and Eifel West
Community Housing. Children under 18 years of age unaccompanied by a parent or guardian
shall not loiter in the streets, highways, roads, alleys, playgrounds, or other public areas on SAB,
Eifel West community housing during curfew hours.
3. Curfew Hours. School Days: School days curfew hours will be in effect starting the
evening before the first day of school until the morning of the last day of school for that week.
Normally, this would be from Sunday evening until Friday morning, while school is in session.
     3.1. Curfew for children 12 years of age and under is 2100 to 0500.
     3.2. Curfew for children 13 through 17 years of age is 2330 to 0500.
4. Weekends and Non-School Days. Weekends and non-school day curfew hours only apply
to those days, which do not precede a school day. For example, when Monday is not a school
day, weekend curfew hours listed below will apply. Single days off in the middle of the week
would also apply to the weekend curfew hours on the day preceding the day off.
     4.1. Curfew for children 12 years of age and under is 2200 to 0500 (Friday and Saturday).
     4.2. Curfew for children 13 through 17 years of age is 0030 to 0500 (Friday and Saturday).
5. Summer Vacation. Summer vacation curfew hours will be in effect starting the morning of
the last day of school until the evening before school starts in the fall. The summer vacation
curfew hours are as follows:
     5.1. Curfew for children 12 years of age and under is 2200 to 0500.
     5.2. Curfew for children 13 through 17 years of age is 0030 to 0500.
6. Exception to Curfew. Children 13 through 17 years of age, who are unaccompanied, are
permitted to proceed directly to their homes from authorized base activities, such as chapel
service, athletic events, theater, youth services, scout assemblies, etc. They may also travel
directly between the main gate and their homes. It is also permitted for children to travel from
their point of employment (such as babysitting) directly to their homes. Children are allowed to
camp out overnight in their yards, so long as they stay in the immediate area of the campsite and
do not cause a disturbance.
7. Youth Supervision. The ages specified in this instruction are maximum ages based on
demonstrated age-appropriate child behavior norms. Children who do not consistently
demonstrate behavior consistent with these norms should not be given the same degree of self-
management responsibilities. Notwithstanding the guidelines adopted by this base allowing
