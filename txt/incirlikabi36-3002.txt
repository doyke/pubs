This instruction implements Air Force Policy Directive 36-30, Military Entitlements; and
references Department of Defense Instruction 1300.18, Military Personnel Casualty Matters,
Policies, and Procedures; and Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-3002, Casualty Services. It
establishes responsibilities and procedures for casualty reporting, notification, and assistance to
the Next of Kin (NOK) of Air Force casualties (deceased, missing, captured, seriously ill/injured,
incapacitation illness, or injury). It specifically defines responsibilities of each agency involved
in casualty services to ensure effective and accurate notification and assistance in all casualty
matters and applies to all organizations assigned or attached to Incirlik Air Base, Turkey.
System of Records Notice F030 Air Force Personnel Center (AFPC), Casualty Files, applies.
This instruction applies to all 39th Air Base Wing personnel. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the OPR listed above using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the
appropriate chain of command. Requests for waivers must be submitted to the OPR listed above,
or as otherwise stipulated within this publication, for consideration and approval. Ensure that all
records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained IAW Air
Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW Air Force
Records Information Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS).
This publication requires the collection and or maintenance of information protected by the

This document is substantially revised and should be completely reviewed. The instruction

1. Responsibilities:
     1.1. Individual Responsibilities. Any individual having knowledge of a casualty will
     immediately notify the Casualty Assistance Representative (CAR) during normal duty hours,
     or the 39th Air Base Wing Command Post (39 ABW/CP) after duty hours with the following
     information if applicable/ known: name, grade and social security number of the casualty;
     date, time and place of casualty occurrence; circumstances of the situation; source of
     information (eye witness, a friend, radio/television, etc.) and the name, grade (if applicable)
     and method of contacting the person making the report.
     1.2. The Casualty Assistance Representative (39 FSS/FSFR). The CAR is responsible for
     casualty reporting, notification and assistance as outlined in AFI 36-3002. All reports of
     casualties or queries for assistance occurring during duty hours will be referred to the CAR.
     The 39 FSS/FSFR will furnish a roster to the 39 ABW/CP, 39th Medical Group, 39th Force
     Support Squadron Mortuary Affairs and 39th Security Forces Squadron listing casualty
     stand-by representatives to contact during non-duty hours. Casualty stand-by personnel are
     relieved from all additional stand-by duties that would interfere with casualty reporting,
     notification and assistance.
     1.3. The 39th Air Base Wing Command Post (39 ABW/CP):
        1.3.1. Will be responsible for receiving casualty information during non-duty hours and
        will immediately contact casualty stand-by personnel.
        1.3.2. Will record the following information if applicable/known: Name, grade and
        social security number of the casualty; date, time and place of casualty occurrence;
        circumstances of the situation; source of information (eye witness, a friend,
        radio/television, etc.); the name, grade (if applicable) and method of contacting the
        person making the report.
        1.3.3. Personnel will not discuss details of any casualty incident with NOK, but will
        advise NOK that a representative from the Casualty Services office will return their call
        as soon as practical.
        1.3.4. Will not release personal identifying information (PII) outside of the member’s
        chain of command and CAR (e.g. name, SSN). All notifications will use general
        information (i.e., rank and assigned unit).

     1.3.5. Will follow up with units to complete OPREP reporting. (Attachment 4)
  1.4. All Air Force tenant and Geographically Separated Units (GSUs):
     1.4.1. Will notify the CAR of all active duty casualties assigned to the tenant unit or
     GSU during normal duty hours or the 39 ABW/CP after duty hours.
     1.4.2. Will appoint a CAR using AF Form 1075, Casualty Personnel Roster. Units
     whose Field Personnel Records (FPRs) are not maintained by 39th Force Support

  1.5. 39th Medical Group (39 MDG):
     1.5.1. Will notify the CAR during normal duty hours, or the 39 ABW/CP after duty
     hours, when a reportable casualty requires major surgery or if there is a change in
     reportable status (death, very seriously ill or injured (VSI), seriously ill or injured (SI), or
     not seriously ill or injured (NSI)), regardless of service component.
         1.5.1.1. Will obtain the status of reportable casualties admitted to civilian hospitals
         and provide to the CAR.
         1.5.1.2. VSI: Very Seriously Ill or Injured. The casualty status of a person whose
         illness or injury is such that medical authority declares it more likely than not that
         death will occur within 72 hours
         1.5.1.3. SI: Seriously Ill or Injured. The casualty status of a person whose illness or
         injury requires medical attention and medical authority declares that death is possible,
         but not likely within 72 hours, and/or the severity is such that it is permanent and life-
         altering.
         1.5.1.4. NSI: Not Seriously Ill or Injured. The casualty status of a person whose
         illness or injury requires medical attention may or may not require hospitalization,
         and medical authority classifies as less severe than SI but still serious enough to
         expect a Next of Kin notification or putting into motion a process to engage benefits
         for the member beyond normal TRICARE benefits. Routine but serious medical
         events where full recovery is expected are not defined under NSI.
     1.5.2. Will provide the CAR with the name and grade of the physician who made the
     notification to NOK regarding casualty category and/or time of death, regardless of
     method (i.e. in person, if NOK is present of via verbal/written communication).
     1.5.3. Will provide the CAR an English translation of the autopsy report, when
     performed.
     1.5.4. Will provide the CAR a copy of the Department of Defense (DD) Form 2064,
     Certificate of Death Overseas.
     1.5.5. Upon request, MTF will provide the CAR with the prognosis of the patient and
     other pertinent information to accomplish the Emergency Family Member Travel
     (EFMT) for transporting the patient’s NOK. (Attachment 2)
     1.5.6. Will augment the notification team.

2. Casualty Notification Procedures for:
   2.1. Casualties assigned or attached to 39th Air Base Wing (39 ABW) with NOK in the
   immediate area: Notification of NOK will be made in person by squadron commanders or
   higher provided they are 1) a field grade officer and 2) equal or higher grade than the
   casualty.
      2.1.1. Casualties assigned or attached to 39 ABW and NOK is not in the immediate area:
      Notification of NOK will be made via available communications by squadron
      commanders or higher provided they are 1) a field grade officer and 2) equal or higher
      grade than the casualty.
   2.2. NOK of Air Force personnel not assigned or attached to 39 ABW residing in local area:
   The CAR or casualty stand-by person will form a notification team consisting of a field grade
   officer who is of equal or higher rank than the casualty, the duty Chaplain and a medical
   representative. The CAR or stand-by casualty person will brief the team on their

    responsibilities and furnish a copy of the original notification letter to be delivered to the
    NOK.
    2.3. Aircraft Mishaps during duty hours:
       2.3.1. 39 ABW/CP will immediately notify the CAR of all aircraft mishaps (military or
       civilian contract), whose originating or last departure point was Incirlik Air Base. After
       duty hours, 39 ABW/CP will notify the casualty stand-by person. The CAR will be
       notified immediately upon the death of any 39 ABW personnel resulting from an aircraft
       accident/mishap, regardless of its departure point or location. CAR will obtain a complete
       listing of crew members, including their organization of assignment, and passenger
       manifest from 728 AMS as necessary.
    2.4. Multiple Casualties Reporting in the event of a mass disaster:
       2.4.1. Procedures outlined in the current Medical Contingency Response Plan, (MCRP),
       39 ABW Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan 10-2, (39 ABW CEMP 10-2) and
       Quick Reaction Checklists will apply.
    2.5. Emergency Family Member Travel (EFMT). The responsibilities of base agencies as
    related to EFMT are outlined in Attachment 2.

                                         Attachment 2
                       EMERGENCY FAMILY MEMBER TRAVEL

A2.1. General. The Air Force provides round-trip transportation and per diem in accordance
with the JFTR (U5246) for not more than three designated individuals to the medical treatment
facility of a member listed as VSI or SI casualty when hospitalized in or outside the United
States, if the attending physician or surgeon and the commander or head of the military treatment
facility exercising military control over the member determine that the presence of the
designated individual may contribute to the member’s health and welfare for a period of up to 30
days (Title 37, U.S.C, Section 411h). In addition, EFMT applies to members who are deployed
on OEF/OIF CED orders and who are hospitalized and have been placed in a Hostile Not
Seriously Ill/Injured (NSI) casualty status.
A2.2. Responsibilities.
   A2.2.1. Medical Personnel Responsibilities. The attending physician initiates the request for
   EFMT.
   A2.2.2. Military Medical Treatment Facility (MTF) Commander’s Responsibilities. The
   MTF commander determines that the presence of the family member may contribute to the
   member’s health and welfare and concurs with the attending physician’s EFMT request. The
   term ―health and welfare, with respect to the member, includes a situation in which a
   decision must be made by family members regarding the termination of artificial life support
   being provided to the member. He/she must ensure that the CAR is immediately notified of
   the request and is provided all required information to effect EFMT approval and
   arrangement of transportation by HQ AFPC/DPWCS.
   A2.2.3. Unit Commander’s Responsibilities. When advised by the CAR of the NOK’s
   approved itinerary, the unit commander assigns the NOK an escort to arrange for the
   reception, lodging, and whatever assistance is needed. He/she ensures that the escort stays in
   contact with the NOK during the stay, and informs the CAR when return transportation is
   required.
   A2.2.4. CAR Responsibilities. The CAR is the overall focal point for the EFMT program.
   The CAR coordinates all actions with HQ AFPC.

      CASUALTY AUGMENTATION SUPPORT TEAM (CAST) RESPONSIBILITIES

A3.1. Purpose. The purpose of the CAST is to ensure casualties are reported to HQ AFPC
Casualty Operations Center in the shortest possible time to ensure prompt, accurate, and humane
notifications of NOK The CAST is composed of personnel assigned within the A&FRC, MPS
and members of FSXOI. All CAST members will perform stand by duty after duty hours to
include weekends and holidays.
A3.2. M&PF Responsibilities. When notified of a situation involving mass casualties, the
M&PF Commander will authorize recall of the CAST to augment the CAR as necessary. The
M&PF Commander will also determine if temporary duty manning assistance from other CARs
is required to provide additional support for casualty reporting, assistance, etc. When telephone
contact with HQ AFPC Casualty Operations Center cannot be established, the M&PF
Commander determines if abbreviated casualty reports are required.
A3.3. Training. CAST members will participate in quarterly training (conducted by the CAR)
and base level exercises that facilitate familiarization with casualty reporting procedures.
