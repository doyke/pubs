This 482d Fighter Wing Instruction establishes (482 FWI) procedures for the freezing of aircraft
records in event of aircraft mishap or accident in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Instruction
(AFI) 21-101, Aerospace Equipment Maintenance Management. Commanders and supervisors
are responsible for ensuring personnel comply with the provisions of this instruction. It applies
to all personnel assigned to the 482d Fighter Wing (482 FW) who are involved with flying
operations. Refer recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of
Primary Responsibility (OPR) using Air Force Form (AF) Form 847, Recommendation for
Change of Publication; route AF Form 847 from the field through the appropriate functional’s
chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this
publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363,
Management of Records and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Information
Management System (AFRIMS) Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

1. Responsibilities: The security of the aircraft records are the ultimate responsibility of the
482d Maintenance Operations Flight (MOF), Plans, Scheduling and Documentation (PS&D)
section.
2. Procedures: In the event of an aircraft accident mishap or impoundment, all work centers
shall immediately relinquish upon notification, all records pertaining to such aircraft.
   2.1. The 482d Maintenance Operations Center (MOC), upon notification of aircraft mishap,
   shall immediately notify 482d Maintenance Group/Quality Assurance (482MXG/QA).

    2.2. QA shall in turn notify PS&D to initiate the immediate freezing of all records associated
    with the mishap aircraft.
       2.2.1. PS&D shall then notify the following work centers to relinquish all records
       pertaining to the mishap aircraft:
           2.2.1.1. 482d Operation Support Squadron/Life Support (482 OSS/OSL)
           2.2.1.2. 482d Maintenance Squadron (482 MXS) Egress section.
           2.2.1.3. 482 MOF Engine Management section.
           2.2.1.4. 482d Operations Group (482 OG) Survival Equipment section
           2.2.1.5. 482 Maintenance Squadron (482 MXS) Non Destructive Inspection (NDI)
           section.
           2.2.1.6. 482 MXS/Aerospace Ground Equipment (AGE) (482 MXS/MXMG)section.
           2.2.1.7. 482d Logistics Readiness Squadron/ Petroleum, Oil, Lubricants (POL) (482
           LRS/LGRF).
           2.2.1.8. 482 MXS/Weapons Release section (482 MXS/MXMR).
           2.2.1.9. 482 MXS/Avionics section (482 MXS/MXMV).
           2.2.1.10. 482 MXS/ Munitions Flight (482 MXS/MXMW).
       2.2.2. Upon receipt of Aircraft Records, PS&D shall deliver to MOC until such time that
       QA can transfer all frozen records to the appointed On Scene Commander. The On Scene
       Commander shall determine where to store them for safe keeping for the Investigation
       Board.
    2.3. QA shall immediately impound any AGE that last serviced the mishap aircraft.
       2.3.1. MXG QA shall contact POL to have their QA impound fuel vehicle(s) that last
       serviced the mishap aircraft.
           2.3.1.1. POL QA shall also impound the Liquid Oxygen (LOX) cart and tank
           pending laboratory analysis.
    2.4. PS&D shall notify the 482 MOF/Analysis (MXOOA) section to freeze all Maintenance
    Information System (MIS) actions, on the mishap aircraft prior to the incident and advise that
    under no circumstances shall anyone be allowed to alter any information regarding the
    aircraft involved.
       2.4.1. PS&D shall notify the 482 MOF/Training (MXOT) section to freeze all Training
       Business Area (TBA) records on all personnel who performed maintenance on the
       mishap aircraft prior to the incident.
    2.5. The Analysis section shall initiate maintenance repair history reports for any recent
    significant malfunctions and related maintenance actions.
    2.6. All records shall be the property of the Aircraft Mishap Investigation Board.
