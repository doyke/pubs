6.2 Special Operations Weather Career field and pipeline. The decision was made to
separate our special operations weathermen into a new career field (1W0X2) with a new
initial skills pipeline. The SOWT pipeline consists of 3 new courses. The Special
Operations Weather Selection Course at Lackland AFB, TX will assess and train pipeline
students exiting BMT and preparing to enter the Special Operations Weather (SOW)
Initial Skills Course at Keesler AFB, MS. The core of SOWISC is the weather ISC
course. SOWISC adds routine, progressive fitness training and is not an AFSC-awarding
course. In week 29, the student will, in addition to meeting weather course graduation
requirements, complete an exit standard PT evaluation to assess his ability to meet fitness
requirements of enroute training and for the Special Operations Weather Apprentice
(SOWA) Course. After leaving Keesler AFB, the 1W012 student will attend Airborne,
SERE, Water Survival, and Underwater Egress training enroute to Pope AFB. SOWAC
is the 3–level AFSC-awarding course for 1W032 and will be where the student is
presented his beret.

                            Job Qualification Standard (JQS) for
                              Battlefield Weather Operations

1.         GENERAL.
1.1.       The purpose of this JQS is to document initial training requirements and standards for
           combat skills for weather personnel aligned against a Battlefield Weather (BW) unit
           type code (UTC). MAJCOMs will use the BW JQS in conjunction with the CFETP
           and local training requirements to determine tasks and proficiency levels for duty
           positions in a BW unit.

2.         TRAINING.
2.1.       Training references for this JQS are listed in Attachment 1.
2.2.       Minimum requirements to serve as a trainer:
2.2.1.     For Airmen, see AFI 36-2201v3, paragraph 6.2.
2.2.2.     The AF will recognize training/certification by Soldiers who are certified IAW Army
           standards in the tasks being taught.
2.3.       Airmen will serve as task certifiers IAW AFI 36-2201v3, paragraph 6.3.

3.         DOCUMENTATION. As prescribed in AFI 36-2201v3, the JQS is an information
           matrix arranged by column.
3.1.       Column 1 identifies tasks, knowledge, equipment, and technical references (TR) that
           are used by the Army to teach Soldiers basic combat skills and identifies the source of
           training or information for each task and knowledge training requirement. Weapons
           qualifying, to include qualifying in a chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear, and
           high-yield explosives (CBRNE) environment, will be accomplished with an
           individual’s primary weapon(s) as determined by theater reporting instructions, UTC
           mission capability statements (MISCAP), or unit line number (ULN) line remarks.
3.2.       Column 2 (OJT Task Certification Documentation).
3.2.1.     As a minimum for initial certification, complete the following columns:
3.2.1.1.   A: Training start date (day, month, year).
3.2.1.2.   B: Training complete date (day, month, year).
3.2.1.3.   C: Trainee initials.
3.2.1.4.   D: Trainer initials.
3.2.1.5.   E: Certifier initials.
3.3.       Column 3 (Proficiency Code).
3.3.1.     Tasks will be trained to the assigned proficiency level.
3.3.2.     Certification on tasks will be accomplished during OJT training to Go/No-Go
           standards by the individual’s home unit.
3.4.       This JQS is used for initial qualification training and is maintained on paper in the AF
           Form 623 or electronically in the Air Force Training Record or other locally-
           approved electronic mediums.
