This supplement implements and extends the guidance of AFI 31-601, Industrial Security Program
Management, 29 June 2005. This supplement applies to Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) units and
to the Air National Guard (ANG) only upon mobilization, and upon publication will be posted on the
online ANG Master Catalog at https://airguard.ang.af.mil/angpubs. Refer recommended changes and
questions about this publication to the office of primary responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Forms 847 from the field through the appropriate
functional’s chain of command. (AF Form 847 is prescribed in AFI 11-215, USAF Flight Manuals Pro-
gram (FMP). Refer to that publication for guidance on filling out the form.) Ensure that all records cre-
ated as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN
37-123, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition
Schedule (RDS) located at https://afrims.amc.af.mil/rds_series.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This revision includes new DD Form 254 procedures (paragraph 4.3.2.), updates office symbols (para-
graph 9.1.1.1. (Added) and 12.1.), adds information security program manager (ISPM) procedure (para-
graph 6.2.4.2.), adds requirement to validate security clearances using JPAS (paragraph 7.3.1.), and
requires background investigation standards be incorporated into the SOW/PWS/SOO contract language
(paragraph 9.1.3.). A bar ( | ) in the left margin indicates a revision from the previous version.
1.6.1.2. Ensure all AETC contractor support is designed as visitor groups using visitor group security
agreements (VGSA). On base contractor cleared facilities are not authorized for AETC activities.

1.6.2.3. AETC contracting activities are responsible for coordinating DD Forms 254, DoD Contract
Security Classification Specification, with Security Forces activities. (DD Form 254 is prescribed in DoD

1.6.2.5. The requesting functional manager (FM) is responsible for developing and coordinating the
VGSA.
1.6.3.2. Notify the information security program manager (ISPM) of any changes in classified contracts.
1.6.3.3. Notify the ISPM to ensure security requirements are incorporated into AETC contracts.
1.6.3.4. (Added) The contracting office (CO) and ISPM will develop local procedures to ensure security
requirements are incorporated into all applicable base contracts.
2.1.2. HQ AETC/A7S is the approving authority for CO-sponsored contractor interim top secret facility
security clearances.
2.3.4.3. HQ AETC/A7S is the approving authority for contractor requests for interim top secret personnel
security clearances.
3.2.3. The unit security manager (USM) will invite contractor-integrated visitor group security represen-
tatives to the installation’s information security manager meetings.
4.1.2. Statement of objectives (SOO) templates are available on the HQ AETC/A7S Web site located at
https://www-r.aetc.af.mil/sf/ (click on Information Security Branch tab) for both classified and unclassi-
fied contracts. Note: SOO is also commonly referred to as statement of work (SOW) or performance
work statement (PWS).
4.3.2. Accomplish a new DD Form 254 when changes are required.
4.4.2. COs will provide the ISPM at all performance locations with copies of all signed initial and revised
DD Forms 254.
4.5.5.1. (Added) Air Force program, project, or activity FMs will:
4.5.5.1.1. (Added) Sign the VGSA to validate sponsoring agency agrees with the security requirements.
4.5.5.1.2. (Added) Use annual program reviews and semiannual self-inspection reports to record contrac-
tor’s security compliance.
4.5.5.2. (Added) USMs will:
4.5.2.2.1. (Added) Ensure integrated visitor groups are included in the semiannual self-inspection pro-
gram.
4.5.2.2.2. (Added) Ensure a semiannual self-inspection is conducted on each integrated visitor group
using the VGSA requirements to evaluate compliance.
4.5.2.2.3. (Added) Provide a copy of the semiannual self-inspection to the ISPM. (A government military
or civilian employee will conduct the semiannual self-inspection.)
6.2.3. AETC integrated visitor groups, regardless of their level of access, will receive an initial program
review within 30 days after the contract start date. After the initial program review, visitor groups will be
checked as part of the sponsoring activity’s semiannual self-inspection program and the ISPM’s annual
program review schedule.
6.2.4.2. The ISPM will maintain a file copy of the VGSA.

6.2.4.5. The ISPM will maintain a file copy of the unit self-inspection reports.
7.3.1. Federal and contractor employees will use Joint Personnel Adjudication System (JPAS) as the per-
sonnel security system of record to validate security clearances.
9.1.1.1. (Added) Communications security (COMSEC) information or material will not be released to
contractors without approval of the command COMSEC manager (Headquarters Air Education and
Training Command, Enterprise Security Branch (HQ AETC/A6OS)). Direct requests for contractor
access or storage of COMSEC material or documents to HQ AETC/A6OS.
9.1.1.2. (Added) HQ AETC and wing COMSEC managers have functional responsibility for COMSEC
materials and assessments as follows:
9.1.1.2.1. (Added) HQ AETC/A6OS will provide the required COMSEC briefing to the contractor’s pri-
mary and alternate COMSEC managers for on-base visitor groups overseen by the ISPM at those loca-
tions where the contractor is responsible for a COMSEC account. The wing COMSEC manager will
provide the briefing when the contractor will be a COMSEC user of the wing account. HQ AETC/A6OS
and the wing COMSEC manager will document these briefings and re-accomplish them on an annual
basis.
9.1.1.2.2. (Added) HQ AETC/A6OS will conduct command COMSEC assessments of all contrac-
tor-operated COMSEC accounts and contractors who store COMSEC documents that are obtained from
the wing COMSEC account. AETC wing COMSEC managers will conduct assessments of all contractors
holding COMSEC material.
9.1.1.3. (Added) Reference applicable emission security (EMSEC) clauses in the DD Form 254, Item 11i.
Coordinate the DD Form 254 with the local wing EMSEC manager before obtaining ISPM coordination.
9.1.2. The contract document will outline contractor responsibilities for protection of government sensi-
tive unclassified information in contractor automated information system (AIS) equipment.
9.1.2.1. (Added) Computer security (COMPUSEC) procedures are as follows:
9.1.2.1.1. (Added) Integrated visitor group contractors will participate in the wing-level COMPUSEC
program to ensure all AISs meet standards for the protection of Air Force information.
9.1.2.1.2. (Added) All contractor-owned, contractor-operated AISs that process Air Force information
must meet the applicable criteria contained in AFI 33-202, Volume 1, Network and Computer Security,
Chapter 3.
9.1.2.1.3. (Added) The chief of the information assurance (IA) office will provide the ISPM a copy of all
annual staff assistance visits to the contractor.
9.1.2.2. (Added) EMSEC procedures are as follows:
9.1.2.2.1. (Added) Contractors will participate in the wing-level EMSEC program, where applicable.
Equipment used by contractors for the processing of classified information must be assessed and
approved, in writing, by the wing EMSEC manager.
9.1.2.2.2. (Added) Contractors must follow EMSEC countermeasures established by the wing EMSEC
manager.
9.1.2.2.3. (Added) The wing EMSEC manager will send the ISPM a copy of the approval authority for
equipment used by contractors for processing classified Air Force information. He or she will also send
the ISPM a copy of annual visits to contractor integrated visitor groups and cleared facilities.

9.1.3. The CO, ISPM, and activity FM will ensure background investigation standards are incorporated
into the SOW/PWS/SOO contract language.
9.1.4. The applicable wing IA office will review and coordinate on DD Forms 254 where the contractor
is required to hold or use COMSEC material or participate in wing-level COMPUSEC, EMSEC, and IA
awareness programs.
10.2. Sensitive Compartmented Information. In HQ AETC, the special security office (SSO) is
responsible for approving security attachments that outline contractor security requirements for sensitive
compartmented information (SCI), establishing SCI facilities, granting SCI access, and coordinating on
any DD Form 254 (including requests for bid or proposal, original DD Forms 254 forwarded contracts,
and revised and final DD Forms 254, if issued, that require SCI access). Note: Each AETC base with an
SSO will perform this function at the local level for contracts awarded for work at the base.
12.1. Applicability of Other Security Program Requirements. Base operations security (OPSEC)
program managers will provide guidance on OPSEC requirements and send contractor OPSEC plans to
HQ AETC/A2/3 for approval.
