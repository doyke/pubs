This instruction establishes responsibilities and issues policies and procedures for all
environmental issues or programs within Headquarters Air Force Weather Agency (HQ AFWA).
It applies to all military, civilians, and contractors assigned to the Air Force Weather Agency,
Offutt Air Force Base (AFB), to include the A-Staff, 1st Weather Group, and 2nd Weather
Group (WXG). This publication does not apply to Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) units.
Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication maintained
in accordance with AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance
with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Office symbols
were changed to reflect revised unit organization. Major changes include, responsibilities were
consolidated, reordered, and reworded.
1. General. The purpose of the environmental program is to protect the health and well being
of all individuals, provide a safe working environment, comply with federal, state, and base
regulations, and to conserve precious resources. See Attachment 1 for manuals and regulations
applicable to this program.
2. Roles and Responsibilities.
   2.1. Commander. The commander will appoint a primary and alternate environmental
   manager (EM) and environmental safety and occupational health (ESOH) council. Letters of

    appointment for the EM and ESOH will be submitted to the 55th Civil Engineer Squadron,
    Environmental Flight (55 CES/CEV).
    2.2. Directorates including 1 WXG, 2 WXG Commanders and A-Staff Directors.
    Directorates and commanders are responsible for appointing, in writing, a point of contact
    (POC) for recycling and hazardous waste programs. Letters of appointment will be submitted
    to the AFWA EM. Appointed individuals should have at least one year left on station. Letters
    of appointment for new POCs will be submitted to the AFWA EM no later than 30 days prior
    to departure, PCS/PCA, retirement, etc.
    2.3. Environmental Manager. Appointed EM is responsible for implementing policies and
    procedures for environmental compliance, hazardous materials, and recycling programs. The
    EM is the POC for all environmental issues and will work with appointed POCs to
    compliance throughout AFWA is accomplished. See Attachment 2, Attachment 3, and
    Attachment 4 for more information on paper and metal recycling and proper disposal of
    hazardous materials and waste.
    2.4. Environmental Safety and Occupational Health Member. Appointed EOSH Member
    will attend all base meetings, briefings, etc., with the AFWA Commander or representative.
    2.5. Contractors. Contractors will comply with all state, federal and base environmental
    laws and regulations, and coordinate issues through appropriate AFWA POC.
    2.6. AFWA Personnel. All AFWA members are expected to contribute to environmental
    awareness, recycling programs, reductions of hazardous waste, and related matters. Make
    any concerns known through appropriate division POCs to the AFWA ESOH.

                                         Attachment 2
                          PAPER RECYCLING INFORMATION

A2.1. Primary Goal. Reduce the amount of waste paper going to the landfill. Offutt generates
approximately 1,100 tons (2.2 million pounds) of paper per year or about 25% of the total waste
going to the landfill.
A2.2. Secondary Goal. C omply with Environmental Protection Agency regulations
A2.3. Paper Types. (NOTE: No carbon paper or plastic material):
    A2.3.1. High Grade White Office Paper. White paper generated from computer laser
    printers or office copier machines, includes bound documents (remove cardboard or leather
    covers before putting paper into the recycling bin). Most paper generated by Offutt AFB will
    fall into this category. NOTE: All “Privacy Act” and “For Official Use Only” paper must be
    shredded before recycling. Bags of shredded paper can be put into the bins located in
    designated areas or bins marked “Shredded Paper.”
    A2.3.2. Computer Paper. Track feed printouts from computer printers.
    A2.3.3. Newspaper. Air Pulse, Omaha World-Herald.
    A2.3.4. Mixed Paper. This includes colored high-grade office paper, charts and maps,
    publications, envelopes, manila folders, magazines, phone books, glossy advertisements,
    folders (without metal binders), instruction manuals (i.e., instructions for computer
    programs), message paper, and wrapping paper.

         HAZARDOUS MATERIAL AND WASTE DISPOSAL INFORMATION

AFWA EMs and appointed POCs are responsible for ensuring proper disposal of hazardous
materials and waste. The steps listed must be followed in order to comply with environmental
laws.
A4.1. Fluorescent Light Bulbs. Burnt white-end fluorescent light bulbs are collected in
approved hazardous waste containers located in the AFWA Mini-Store (Room 1222). EMs or
Directorate POCs must turn in full containers to the 55 CES/CEV 90 day storage facilities.
Maximum amount of full containers at one time is two. Burnt green-end fluorescent light bulbs
can be put in trash containers.
A4.2. Batteries. Kodak, Ray-O-Vac, Energizer, and Eveready alkaline batteries (AAA, AA, B,
C, and D) can be thrown in the trash. Other brands and types, such as lithium, 9 volt, etc., need to
be collected in approved containers. Containers are located in the AFWA Mini-Store (Room
1222). Full containers must be turned into 55 CES/CEV (located in Building 301D, extension
294-4087).
A4.3. Paint. Unused paint must be turned into 55 CES/CEV for disposal. Empty plastic and
metal paint containers must be left open to dry out residue; then peel off residue. Peeled off
residue can be put in the trash.
