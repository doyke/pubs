This instruction implements AFPD 36-26, Total Force Development, 1 January 2004. It
explains procedures and guidelines for the production processes for publications to support the
professional advancement and continuous education of Airmen. It applies to all active duty
personnel and to Air National Guard and Air Force Reserve Command units. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the AF Form 847, Recommendations for Change of Publication;
route AF Forms 847 from the field through the major command (MAJCOM) publications/forms
managers. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication
are maintained according to AFMAN 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of
according     to     Air     Force     Records       Disposition     Schedule      located      at
https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. The use of the name or mark of any
specific manufacturer, commercial product, commodity, or service in this publication does not
imply endorsement by the Air Force. See Attachment 1 for a glossary of references and
supporting information.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document is substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. It changes PDG
effective date note under (paragraph 1.3.1.4); includes changes required to develop and
administer the MKTS survey to support the enlisted promotion system (paragraphs 2.2 through
2.3.3); coordinates approval to publish PDG; updates the responsibilities to incorporate changes
within PDG production processes (paragraph 3.1); and CMSAF initiative to publish and print
Airman Handbook (paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4).

                                          Chapter 1

                    PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT GUIDE (PDG)

1.1. General Information. AFPAM 36-2241, Professional Development Guide, hereafter
referred to as the PDG, is developed and published to provide all active duty, Guard, and
Reserve Airmen a development guide for professional growth. Furthermore, the PDG provides a
single study source reference for active duty Airmen preparing for the promotion fitness
examination (PFE) and USAF supervisory examination (USAFSE). The production process is
based on input from Air Force-level subject-matter experts (SME) and research conducted by
Airman Advancement Division (AAD) PDG managers. Proposed development guide content is
forwarded to the Military Knowledge and Testing System (MKTS) advisory council for
consideration. The council consists of the Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force (CMSAF) and
command chief master sergeants (CCM) from all MAJCOMs, field operating agencies (FOA),
and direct reporting units (DRU) for consideration. This development guide is published every 2
years using the production process identified in Attachment 2.
1.2. Scope. The PDG provides Airmen a comprehensive guide to develop and improve
leadership, management and supervisory skills as they prepare for advancement through the
ranks. Furthermore, the development guide enhances the foundation built through basic, and
technical training, and professional military education.

                                          Chapter 2

                 MILITARY KNOWLEDGE TESTING SYSTEM (MKTS)

2.1. General. This chapter identifies and defines the components of the MKTS and assigns
responsibility for development and relationship to AFPAM 36-2241, Professional Development
Guide (PDG). The MKTS survey is critical to the Air Force enlisted promotion system.
2.2. Scope. The MKTS supports an individual study program for promotion testing and applies
to all active duty enlisted members in the grades of senior Airman (SrA) through technical
sergeant (TSgt) and master sergeant (MSgt)/senior master sergeant (SMSgt) preparing for
promotion testing under the Weighted Airman Promotion System (WAPS) and senior
noncommissioned officer (SNCO) promotion system, respectively. The MKTS also applies to
Airman first class (A1C) preparing for SrA below-the-zone promotion consideration. The MKTS
consists of two parts: development guide and promotion examinations.
     2.2.1. Development Guide. AFPAM 36-2241 guide provides the current basic military
     information Airmen need to prepare for advancement in their Air Force career.
     2.2.2. Examinations. The examinations consist of the PFE and USAFSE.
        2.2.2.1. All PDG questions are referenced to AFPAM 36-2241. This is the only guidance
        required to prepare for the PFE. The PFE assesses relative levels of noncommissioned
        officer military knowledge. The PFE provides a percentage score that is considered with
        other factors in the WAPS program.
        2.2.2.2. All USAFSE questions are referenced to AFPAM 36-2241. The USAFSE
        assesses relative levels of military knowledge for SMSgt and chief master sergeant
        (CMSgt). The USAFSE provides a percentage score that is considered with other factors
        in the SNCO Promotion Program.
2.3. Responsibilities:
     2.3.1. The MKTS Advisory Council:
        2.3.1.1. HQ AETC/A3/AADD personnel advise the CMSAF on matters pertaining to the
        MKTS.
        2.3.1.2. The CMSAF chairs the council with the following membership:
           2.3.1.2.1. MAJCOM Command Chief Master Sergeants (CCM) and selected FOA
           and DRU CCMs.
           2.3.1.2.2. HQ AETC/A3/AADD Branch Chief and PDG managers.
        2.3.1.3. The PDG manager serves as the permanent secretariat for the council, prepares
        minutes, collects and coordinates proposed changes to the MKTS, and archives related
        correspondence and documentation.
        2.3.1.4. In August of every year the council assembles at the Air Force Sergeant’s
        Association professional Airmen’s conference.
        2.3.1.5. The council acts upon proposed action items and sanctions a proposed change
        when a majority of the council approves the change.

     2.3.3.1. Every 2 years, enlisted members in the grades of staff sergeant (SSgt) through
     CMSgt are provided the opportunity to rate topics within the PDG on a 5-point scale
     ranging from 1 (extremely low need) to 5 (extremely high need) to identify the
     importance of general military knowledge required for their respective grade.
     2.3.3.2. The MKTS survey is administered via the Web site at
     https://www.omsq.af.mil/PD/index.htm, and is available during the months of January
     through March of every odd-numbered year.
     2.3.3.3. The survey has a cover letter from the CMSAF explaining the importance of
     volunteering to complete the survey as the results provide a guide for SMEs developing
     the PFE and USAFSE.

                                           Chapter 3

                                   AIRMAN HANDBOOK

3.1. Background. The Airman Handbook is a Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force
(CMSAF) initiative based on Air Force Core Values (Integrity First, Service Before Self,
Excellence in All We Do), Air Force Core Competencies (Developing Airmen, Technology to
War Fighter, and Integrating Operations). The Airman Handbook reminds us of our past,
reinforces the present, and allows us to envision the future through a common culture. This
handbook is published every 2 years using the production process identified in Attachment 3.
3.2. Scope. The Airman Handbook is revised every 2 years and is issued to United States Air
Force Academy, Reserve Officer Training Corps Detachments, Officer Training School, enlisted
Professional Military Education, and Civilian Acculturation and Leadership Training students. A
printed version of the handbook will be provided to the forementioned students when funding is
available.
