This supplement implements and extends the guidance of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 40-301,
Family Advocacy, 30 November 2009. It applies to all population assigned or attached to Vance
Air Force Base (AFB), OK. Civilian personnel assigned or attached to Vance AFB are strongly
encouraged to comply with this supplement. The purpose of the Family Advocacy Program
(FAP) is to support Air Force readiness. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes
prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN)
33-363, Management of Records, and are disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records
Information Management System (AFRIMS) Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located at https://www.my.af.mil/afrims/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm.           Refer recommended
changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR)
using the AF Form 847, Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF Form 847 from
the field through the VAFB Publications and Forms Manager. (AF Form 847 is prescribed in
AFI 11-215, USAF Flight Manuals Program [FMP]. Refer to that publication for guidance on
filling out the form.)

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This document has been substantially revised and must be completely reviewed. Major changes
include initial publication and incorporating information from Vance AFB Instruction 40-301,
Family Advocacy Program, 20 November 2007. It updates, clarifies, and streamlines previous
guidance with this newly revised basic instruction.
1.5.3.4.1. (Added) (VANCEAFB) A Family Advocacy Nurse is not assigned to Vance AFB, as
a result, there is no NPSP program.

1.7.2.1. (Added) (VANCEAFB) Commanders should ensure that active duty members are seen
within 3 duty days of a family maltreatment report.
1.7.5. (Added) (VANCEAFB) Commanders should deliver and obtain the signed receipt of the
Central Registry Board (CRB) determination letter by the active duty member within 3 duty days
following the CRB IAW Family Advocacy Standards, Central Registry Board for Family
Maltreatment, M1B.
1.10.4.1. (Added) (VANCEAFB) The SFS liaison for FAP will ensure that incident reports are
available to review and, if needed, photocopies provided to the Family Advocacy Officer or their
designee.
4.7.4. (Added) (VANCEAFB) During duty hours, initial reports of family maltreatment will be
made at the FAP office (213-7419) or the Base Defense Operation Center (213-7415). After
normal duty hours the reports will only be made to the Base Defense Operation Center which
will notify the FAP office by the next duty day.
4.7.5. (Added) (VANCEAFB) Law enforcement, FAP and DHS will do their best to coordinate
needed interviews and data collection, in order to avoid retraumatization of victims. All
information collected shall be accomplished in such a manner that will foster the victimâ€™s right to
privacy and maintain dignity. All interviews, especially of children, will be conducted in an
environment that is sensitive to dealing with the trauma of maltreatment and not in the presence
of the alleged offender.
