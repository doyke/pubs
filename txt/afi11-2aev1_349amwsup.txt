This supplement implements Air Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 10-2, Readiness, AFPD 11-2,
Aircraft Rules and Procedures, and AFPD 11-4, Aviation Service. It further extends the guidance
of Air Force Instruction (AFI) 11-2AEV1, Aeromedical Evacuation Aircrew Training. This
publication applies to Air Mobility Command (AMC)-gained and Air Force Reserve Command
(AFRC) Aeromedical Evacuation (AE) aircrew assigned or attached to the 349 Aeromedical
Evacuation Squadron (AES) for flying. It defines and implements policies specific to AE. Refer
recommended changes and questions about this publication to the Office of Primary
Responsibility (OPR) using the Air Force Information Management Tool (AF IMT) 847,
Recommendation for Change of Publication; route AF IMTs 847 from the field through the
appropriate functional’s chain of command. Ensure that all records created as a result of
processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with (IAW) Air Force
Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of IAW the Air Force
Records Disposition Schedule (RDS) located at https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-
af61a/afrims/afrims/.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES
This document is new. It has been numbered and aligned to supplement the entire basic
publication IAW AFI 33-360, Publication and Forms Management. This instruction implements
local directives related to Aircrew Training and establishes specific guidelines for aircrew
member compliance IAW AFI 11-2AE V1.

1.4.5.1.2. (Added-349 AMW) The 349 AES/CC may convene a Training Review Board (TRB)

assigned/attached personnel; review of training progression on initial, requalification and

1.4.5.1.2.1. (Added-349 AMW) The Officer In Charge (OIC)/Non Commission Officer in

briefing presentation. Attendees should include representatives from operations, aircrew training,
standardization/evaluation (Stan/Eval), chief nurse executive and chief enlisted advisor.

3.1.1. (Added-349 AMW) Qualification/Mission Training.

3.1.2. (Added-349 AMW) In unit Qualification Training Program (QTP) ensures AECM
students progress through and complete AECM FN/AET qualification training in a timely
organized manner. Aircrew Qualification Training is broken into three parts:

3.1.2.1. (Added-349 AMW) Mission Ready (MR) Training required by AFI 11-2AE V1
Table 3.1.

3.1.2.2. (Added-349 AMW) Didactic and hands on training required to satisfy Qualification
Standard in AFI 11-2AE V2 2.7. FN/AET Grading Criteria. Course content available on 349 OG
SharePoint website: http://132.33.129.173/Travis/349OG/349OST/default.aspx.

3.1.2.3. (Added-349 AMW) Flight Training.

3.1.3. (Added-349 AMW) In order to enter the qualification training program the student should
have completed the unit orientation process.

3.1.4. (Added-349 AMW) Medical Survival, Evasion, Resistance, & Escape (SERE) Training
and AE Technician Course or Flight Nurse Course should be completed before the student enters
in unit qualification training.

3.1.5. (Added-349 AMW) Qualification Training Checklist will be initiated and the tracking of
MR items will be accomplished. When qualification training is completed the AES Qualification
Training Checklist will be verified by OIC/NCOIC of Aircrew Training and placed in the
member’s 6 part training folder.

3.1.6. (Added-349 AMW) When qualification training is completed and a recommendation for
evaluation has been made, 349 AES Chief/Superintendent Stan/Eval will be notified via
AFI11-2AEV1_349AMWSUP 25 NOVEMBER 2008                                                       3

electronic mail and the AECM’s Air Force (AF) Form 4022, Aircrew Training Folder will be
provided to Stan/Eval for review and the scheduling of evaluation flight.

3.5. (Added-349 AMW) AECM Post-Qualification Mentor Program

3.5.1. (Added-349 AMW) Mentoring opportunities for newly qualified AECMs to shadow
experienced AE crewmembers on an Aeromedical Readiness Mission (ARM) during their
immediate post-qualification phase. Mentoring experiences may include briefings, flight/mission
paperwork, clinical skills, mission management, etc.

3.5.2. (Added-349 AMW) For up to six (6) months following initial qualification, AECMs may
be assigned to an experienced crewmember of the same Air Force Specialty Code (AFSC) during
ARM or operational missions for mentoring.

3.5.3. (Added-349 AMW) Pay status and logging of flying time will be according to current
regulations.

3.5.4. (Added-349 AMW) Flight scheduling should indicate on the mission certification sheet
when an AECM will be mentored by another AECM.

3.5.5. (Added-349 AMW) Mentoring activities accomplished as part of the post qualification
program will be documented on the Travis Air Force Base (TAFB) Form 73, Mission Itinerary.

4.3.2..1.1. (Added-349 AMW)Flight Level (FTL) “A” Highly experienced crewmembers
should be a flight instructor/evaluator with 500 flying hours or more and 10 or more years as a
qualified AECM.

4.3.2.2.1. (Added-349 AMW) FTL “B” Experienced crewmember should be a flight
instructor/evaluator and/or an AECM with 200 flying hours or more and between 5 and 10 years
as a qualified AECM.

4.4.1.1. (Added-349 AMW) Use date of evaluation as the date of accomplishment for all flying
training currency events that were trained during the qualification course.

4.5.1.4. (Added-349 AMW) Locally generated Mission Accomplishment Record (MAR)
available on 349OG SharePoint website:
http://132.33.129.173/Travis/349OG/349OST/default.aspx be utilized to report accomplished
training events to the unit SARM. Table 4.2. (Added-349 AMW) Note 1. Crewmembers should
update/review mobility folder annually and anytime applicable information changes (address,
emergency data, passport(s), etc).

5.2.1. (Added-349 AMW) The candidate may be recommended to the commander at the
Squadron Training Review Panel (TRP) for upgrade to instructor by an I/FE.
4                                      AFI11-2AEV1_349AMWSUP 25 NOVEMBER 2008

5.2.2. (Added-349 AMW) The recommendation is written on a preprinted Memo for
Record, Subject: Instructor/Examiner Recommendation available on the 340 OG SharePoint
website: http://132.33.129.173/Travis/349OG/default.aspx.

5.2.3. (Added-349 AMW) Upon the commander’s approval the recommendation is noted in the
TRP minutes and the recommendation is forwarded to the 349 AES Review and Certification
Board (R&C).

5.2.4. (Added-349 AMW) During R&C the commander may direct that the candidate receive a
no-notice spot evaluation if the recommendation was not made during a flight evaluation.

5.2.5. (Added-349 AMW) Upon R&C approval the candidate is scheduled in the next instructor
upgrade class.

5.2.6. (Added-349 AMW) After successful completion of the initial instructor evaluation and
open book test the candidate is scheduled to meet the 349 AES R&C board. Prior to squadron
R&C, upgrade packages will be sent to Operations Group Standardization/Evaluation (OGV) for
review. This package consists of Certification Worksheet, Flight Evaluation Folder (FEF) and
R&C minutes documenting recommendation for upgrade. After R&C, instructors may perform
instructor duties but must complete a Wing/CC interview within 90 days IAW AFI 11-202V2
TAFB Sup1, Aircrew Standardization/Evaluation Program.

5.9. Flight Evaluator Certification

5.9.1. (Added-349 AMW) Candidates for examiner may be selected based on an evaluator's
recommendation or by the R&C board. Minimum qualification for upgrade to examiner is a
mission ready flight instructor.

5.9.2. (Added-349 AMW) 349 AES instructors recommended by the R&C Board and approved
by the commander to be upgraded to examiner will be notified by the Stan/Eval section of their
selection. Candidates will not be upgraded involuntarily.

5.9.3. (Added-349 AMW) After notification of the commander’s approval to upgrade the
AECM to examiner, the Evaluator Upgrade Checklist, available on the 349 OG SharePoint
website: http://132.33.129.173/Travis/349OG/default.aspx, will be initiated and AF Form 4022,
Aircrew Training Folder created.

5.9.4. (Added-349 AMW) Evaluator candidates will receive an examiner upgrade orientation
from a Group Stan Eval Personnel (DOV) assigned examiner of like specialty to cover as a
minimum applicable publications, evaluator responsibilities, local procedures, and observation of
Emergency Procedures Evaluation (EPE) procedures. Candidates will receive a copy of the 349
OGV Evaluator Handbook and EPE Guide available on 349 OG SharePoint website:
http://132.33.129.173/Travis/349OG/default.aspx.
AFI11-2AEV1_349AMWSUP 25 NOVEMBER 2008                                                       5

5.9.4.1. (Added-349 AMW) Evaluator candidates will receive familiarization training regarding
all publications and supplements included in the Flight Evaluator (FE) Checklist available on the
349 OG SharePoint website: http://132.33.129.173/Travis/349OG/default.aspx.

5.9.5. (Added-349 AMW) Evaluator candidates will complete a minimum of one “over the
shoulder” flight. This should be performed while observing a qualified evaluator of like specialty
performing an evaluation/EPE.

5.9.5.1. (Added-349 AMW) Evaluator candidates should conduct a minimum of one simulated
flight evaluation and an EPE, with the evaluator acting as the examinee.

5.9.6. (Added-349 AMW) Evaluator upgrade training will be documented on the AF Form 4023,
Aircrew Training Progress Report.

5.9.7. (Added-349 AMW) Upon written recommendation of a DOV assigned examiner in the
AF Form 4022, and completion of all requirements per the Evaluator Upgrade Checklist, the
AES DOV section will forward an upgrade package to the OGV office no later than three
business days prior to the scheduled squadron R&C date.

5.9.8. (Added-349 AMW) The evaluator upgrade package will consist of the following: 349
OGV Certification Worksheet, a copy of the squadron R&C minutes verifying that candidate has
been recommended for upgrade. All attachments should be placed on the face of the candidate’s
FEF IAW AFI 11-202V2 TAFB S1, 9.8.6.1.

5.9.9. (Added-349 AMW) Examiner candidates will be scheduled to meet the squadron R&C
Board after receiving the reviewed FEF and upgrade package materials from 349 OGV.

5.9.10. (Added-349 AMW) The 349 AES will notify the OG/OGV office that the upgrade
candidate has met the squadron R&C Board and request a date for a Wing/CC interview. The
349 AES will re-submit the upgrade package in paragraph 5.9.8.

5.9.11. (Added-349 AMW) The examiner candidate may perform evaluator duties prior to
meeting with the Wing/CC. If the examiner candidate has not met with the Wing/CC within 90
days of the squadron R&C, the candidate will revert to prior certification status IAW AFI11-
202V2 TAFB S1, 9.8.6.1.

5.9.12. (Added-349 AMW) The 349 AES is responsible for ensuring the examiner upgrade
candidate’s availability for the Wing/CC interview.
